1. Which structural characteristic best describes the dynamic transition of the Indian economy over the past three decades?

[TRAP] Standing Deposit Facility

Unlike the traditional Reverse Repo window, the **Standing Deposit Facility (SDF)** allows the RBI to absorb banking system liquidity without placing government securities as collateral. This strengthens its capacity to address systemic cash surpluses.

13. Which statement accurately separates the legal framework of the Standing Deposit Facility (SDF) from the Fixed Reverse Repo rate?

B) SDF absorbs excess liquidity without requiring the RBI to provide government securities as collateral
C) SDF is controlled by the Union Ministry of Finance, whereas Reverse Repo is controlled by the RBI
D) SDF can only be accessed by regional rural banking networks
VIEW ANSWER & LIVE GEM

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: The SDF was introduced into the RBI Act framework in 2018 to optimize liquidity management. It replaced the fixed reverse repo rate as the floor of the Liquidity Adjustment Facility (LAF) corridor, operating without collateral constraints.

💎 Live Gem

The SDF operates at an interest rate 25 basis points below the prevailing Repo rate, while the Marginal Standing Facility (MSF) sits 25 basis points above the Repo rate.

19. To approve a regulatory policy resolution within the GST Council meeting, what statutory voting majority threshold is required?
A) Simple majority of members present and voting
B) Two-thirds majority of members present and voting
C) Three-fourths weighted majority of members present and voting
D) Unanimous consent with zero dissents permitted
VIEW ANSWER & LIVE GEM

Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Under Article 279A, decisions require a three-fourths weighted majority. The Central Government holds one-third of the total voting weight, while all State Governments combined hold two-thirds. This structure means neither block can pass resolutions independently.

💡 Elimination Tip

Because the Center holds 33.33% of the vote, no resolution can pass (requiring 75%) without the Center's consent, establishing an effective central veto.
