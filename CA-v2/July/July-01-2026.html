The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Harmony & Minorities
  • Revisiting the Ranganath Mishra Commission highlights the ongoing need for nuanced policy interventions that address not just religious, but also linguistic minority rights at the state-specific level.
Digital Economy & GST
  • As India celebrates GST Day, the integration of indirect taxes combined with robust e-Governance architectures (like TNeGA) has structurally formalized the economy and improved tax buoyancy.
Cooperative Federalism
  • The signing of the Yamuna Water Project between Rajasthan and Haryana showcases that active mediation by the Centre can resolve decades-old inter-state river water disputes outside lengthy tribunals.
Tech Diplomacy (UPI)
  • The expansion of UPI to Greece (the 10th nation) represents a strategic shift in India's soft power, moving from cultural exports to setting global digital public infrastructure (DPI) standards.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Inter-State River Dispute Resolutions

Pros: Bilateral Agreements

  • Swift Implementation: Direct agreements, like the Yamuna Water Project, bypass the notoriously slow Inter-State River Water Disputes Tribunals, allowing immediate infrastructural development and water sharing.
  • Political Consensus: Fosters genuine cooperative federalism where states compromise for mutual agricultural and industrial benefit rather than engaging in perpetual legal battles.

Cons: Fragility of Pacts

  • Political Volatility: Agreements are often brokered when the same political party rules both states and the Centre. A change in government can lead to immediate nullification of the pact.
  • Ecological Oversights: Hasty political agreements sometimes ignore long-term basin ecology and downstream impacts, prioritizing short-term electoral gains over sustainable water management.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Ranganath Mishra Commission (2004): Tasked to identify the criteria for socially and economically backward sections among religious and linguistic minorities, and to recommend measures for their welfare, including reservations.
