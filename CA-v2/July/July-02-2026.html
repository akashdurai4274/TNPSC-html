03

The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Rural Poverty Eradication
  • The DAY-NRLM structurally empowers rural women by integrating them into SHGs, ensuring that poverty alleviation is driven bottom-up through collective micro-entrepreneurship and financial literacy.
Financial Autonomy for PSUs
  • Upgrading entities like MECON to Miniratna-I enhances operational autonomy, allowing capital expenditure up to ₹500 crores without bureaucratic red tape, thereby making Indian PSUs globally competitive.
Redefining Federalism
  • The Punchhi Commission (2007) analyzed contemporary federal friction points, updating the discourse from the Sarkaria era to address newer issues like concurrent legislation and asymmetric federalism.
Ecology vs. Mega-Development
  • The Flamingo Revolution in Albania demonstrates how localized environmental movements against unchecked luxury development often escalate into nationwide political crusades for transparency and anti-corruption.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Granting Miniratna Autonomy

Pros: Corporate Agility

  • Rapid Decision Making: The ability to invest up to ₹500 Crores independently allows CPSEs to pivot swiftly into new market opportunities without waiting for prolonged ministerial approvals.
  • Talent & Innovation: Financial and operational autonomy helps PSUs adopt corporate structures, joint ventures, and technological upgrades, making them competitive against private sector giants.

Cons: Accountability Risks

  • Risk of Mismanagement: Reduced central oversight can lead to sub-optimal investments or mismanagement of public funds if the internal board governance is not strictly meritocratic.
  • Dilution of Public Mandate: Pushing for pure corporate profitability (to maintain the status) might force PSUs to abandon non-profitable societal mandates or rural infrastructure obligations.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Punchhi Commission (2007): Headed by Madan Mohan Punchhi. Mandated to review the working of the constitutional arrangements between the Centre and States. It submitted a 7-volume report in 2010 focusing heavily on internal security, communal violence, and the role of Governors.
