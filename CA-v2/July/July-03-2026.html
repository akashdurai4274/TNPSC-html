The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Electoral Representation
  • The foresight of the non-governmental Tarkunde Committee (1974) to recommend reducing the voting age from 21 to 18 drastically deepened democratic engagement, empowering millions of youths to participate in governance.
FDI & Infrastructure Growth
  • Japan's massive capital injection through Maruti Suzuki's Haryana plant (₹35,000 Cr) and the commitment to build 1,000 biogas plants bolsters India's 'Make in India' initiative while promoting green technology.
Biometric Integrity (SITA)
  • The introduction of the SITA Scheme under MeitY addresses the critical vulnerabilities in digital identity systems, fortifying the Aadhaar architecture against advanced technological frauds and deepfakes.
Ecological Preservation
  • Declaring the Borjuli wetland a Biodiversity Heritage Site ensures that critical natural habitats are legally protected against unchecked industrial encroachment, maintaining regional ecological balance.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Regional Concentration of FDI

Pros: Industrial Ecosystems

  • Agglomeration Economies: States like Haryana and Andhra Pradesh attracting massive FDI (like the Maruti plant) create robust supply chain ecosystems, lowering logistics costs and boosting rapid localized job creation.
  • Skill Specialization: Concentrated investments lead to specialized skill development centers, making the region a global hub for specific industries (e.g., automobile manufacturing).

Cons: Economic Disparities

  • Asymmetric Growth: If 25%+ of FDI flows to just one or two states, it exacerbates regional inequalities, leading to massive internal migration and strain on urban infrastructure in those specific areas.
  • Political Friction: Unequal distribution of central facilitation for foreign investments can lead to political discord between states demanding an equitable share of international mega-projects.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Tarkunde Committee on Electoral Reforms (1974): Appointed by 'Citizens for Democracy' under Jayaprakash Narayan. It operated outside the government and laid the intellectual foundation for the 61st Constitutional Amendment (lowering voting age) and the restructuring of the Election Commission into a multi-member body.
