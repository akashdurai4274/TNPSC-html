The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Equitable Skill Development
  • The PM Internship Scheme actively excludes elite-tier graduates to focus corporate training and stipends on marginalized youth (ITI, Diploma, non-elite UG), effectively democratizing high-level industrial exposure.
Defense & Soft Power Export
  • India's bilateral deals with Indonesia represent a dual approach: exporting hard power (BrahMos and Astra missiles) while expanding soft power through educational integration (IIM Bangalore campus in East Java).
Electoral Integrity
  • Revisiting the Indrajit Gupta Committee highlights the ongoing structural challenges of money power in politics, advocating for state funding to ensure that competent grassroots leaders aren't out-priced by corporate-backed candidates.
Educational Benchmarking
  • The Performance Grading Index (PGI) 2.0 fosters competitive federalism, compelling states to upgrade physical and digital school infrastructures to achieve higher national rankings, directly impacting foundational learning outcomes.
Pros & Cons Analysis: State Funding of Elections

Pros: Democratic Equality

  • Curbing Crony Capitalism: If the state funds campaigns (often in kind, like free media time, fuel, or printing), political parties become less reliant on corporate donations, reducing policy favors post-election.
  • Leveling the Playing Field: It allows qualified, honest individuals from economically weaker sections to contest against heavily funded legacy politicians, deepening the democratic talent pool.

Cons: Fiscal and Ethical Hurdles

  • Exchequer Burden: Funding elections for hundreds of candidates across state and national levels places a massive recurring financial burden on taxpayers in a developing economy.
  • Dummy Candidates: Unregulated state funding might incentivize non-serious "dummy" candidates to enter the fray purely to siphon off campaign funds, fragmenting the voting process.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Indrajit Gupta Committee (1998): Concluded that state funding of elections is fully justified in order to establish a fair playing field for parties with lesser resources. It recommended that state funding should be in kind (not cash) and restricted to recognized national and state parties.
