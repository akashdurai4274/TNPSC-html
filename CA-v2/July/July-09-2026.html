The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Indigenous Knowledge Preservation
  • The launch of the Tribex digital platform democratizes access to marginalized tribal arts and languages, preventing cultural erosion while integrating traditional knowledge into mainstream digital education.
Plantation Welfare Restructuring
  • The PM Chaa Shramik Protsahan Yojana represents a shift from mere wage protection to holistic socio-economic elevation (housing, health, education) for historically exploited tea plantation workers in the Northeast.
Ideological Shift in Governance
  • Renaming the Adi Dravidar Department to the Social Justice Department in Tamil Nadu reflects a semantic and policy evolution—moving away from caste-based nomenclature towards an egalitarian, rights-based governance framework.
Institutionalizing Biodiversity
  • Designating Mizoram's museum as the 21st Biodiversity Repository strengthens localized ecological mapping and legally protects regional flora/fauna under the Biological Diversity Act of 2002.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Agnipath Scheme (Short-Term Military Recruitment)

Pros: Modernization & Fiscal Prudence

  • Youthful Profile: Successfully lowers the average age of the armed forces from 32 to 26, ensuring a fitter, more technologically adaptable frontline combat force.
  • Pension Savings: By releasing the majority of recruits after 4 years without long-term pension liabilities, the defense budget can pivot from revenue expenditure to critical capital procurement.

Cons: Operational & Social Risks

  • Loss of Experience: Releasing highly trained personnel after just four years can hollow out the vital middle-tier leadership and institutional memory within combat units.
  • Post-Service Anxiety: Injecting thousands of militarily trained but unemployed youth back into civil society every year poses potential internal security risks and economic friction if horizontal reservations are not strictly enforced.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Balwant Rai Mehta Committee (1957): Appointed to examine the working of the Community Development Programme (1952) and the National Extension Service (1953). It famously recommended the establishment of the scheme of 'democratic decentralisation', which came to be known as Panchayati Raj.
