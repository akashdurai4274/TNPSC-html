The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

State Recognition of Art
  • The late singer S. Janaki’s rejection of the Padma Bhushan in 2013 highlights the friction between legendary regional artists and the delayed, often disproportionate, national recognition from state authorities.
Targeted Agrarian Upliftment
  • The PM Dhan Dhanya Krishi Yojana moves away from generic subsidies, focusing hyper-locally on 100 underdeveloped districts to fix foundational issues like farm irrigation to actively boost agricultural yield.
Constitutionalizing Grassroots
  • The retrospective look at the L.M. Singhvi Committee (1986) underscores the critical transition from viewing Panchayats as mere developmental agencies to constitutionally mandated, self-governing political institutions.
Urban Planning & Livability
  • The Global Livability Index reflects stark contrasts in urban planning; while Copenhagen leads globally, major Indian metropolises like Delhi and Chennai languish below the 100-mark, indicating urgent needs for sustainable civic infrastructure.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Defense Indigenization (Global Air Power)

Pros: Qualitative Superiority

  • Strategic Competence: India's 3rd rank in the WDMMA index, surpassing China despite having fewer aircraft, demonstrates a prioritization of high-quality, modern avionics and superior pilot training over sheer numerical volume.
  • Deterrence Value: A highly ranked, qualitative air force serves as a massive psychological and strategic deterrent against hostile neighbors in a two-front war scenario.

Cons: Numerical & Manufacturing Deficits

  • Fleet Depletion: The IAF is currently operating with dwindling fighter squadrons (well below the sanctioned strength of 42), which poses risks during sustained, multi-theater conflicts.
  • Import Dependency: Despite qualitative rankings, a significant portion of India's high-tech air assets (like Rafales and S-400s) are imported, exposing the strategic vulnerability of supply chain disruptions.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • L.M. Singhvi Committee (1986): Constituted to study the revitalization of PRIs. It forcefully argued that Panchayati Raj Institutions should be constitutionally recognized, protected, and preserved by the inclusion of a new chapter in the Constitution, fundamentally shifting the paradigm of local governance.
