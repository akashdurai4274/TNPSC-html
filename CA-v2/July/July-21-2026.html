The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Preventive Public Health
  • The approval of the Qdenga Tetravalent Vaccine marks a massive shift in combating endemic vector-borne diseases, drastically reducing hospitalizations and safeguarding vulnerable socio-economic groups during monsoons.
Financial Digitization
  • Initiatives like the Lucky Grahak Yojana (2016) successfully laid the behavioral groundwork for the massive UPI adoption seen today, pulling millions of micro-transactions into the formal digital economy.
Linguistic Heritage & Federalism
  • The establishment of Shabdalok (Museum of Words) and the expansion of the 8th Schedule (via 21st, 71st, 92nd CAAs) reinforce India's commitment to pluralism and protect minority linguistic identities from majoritarian assimilation.
Infrastructure Efficiency
  • The integration of the Digital Local Pass via Rajmargyatra by NHAI reduces toll-plaza idling times, thereby decreasing vehicular carbon emissions and streamlining rural-urban transit corridors.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Highway Toll Digitization

Pros: Seamless Transit

  • Administrative Ease: Locals living near highways can secure passes directly through their smartphones, eliminating bureaucratic red tape and physical queuing at toll booths.
  • Data Transparency: Real-time tracking of passes via systems like 'Marg Mitra' prevents revenue leakage and helps NHAI accurately assess traffic volume for future road expansions.

Cons: Digital Divide Limitations

  • Exclusion Errors: Rural commercial drivers and farmers lacking digital literacy or smartphone access may struggle to obtain digital passes, forcing them to pay non-subsidized rates.
  • Privacy Concerns: Centralizing localized vehicular movement data in a single app (Rajmargyatra) poses cybersecurity risks and concerns over mass surveillance without robust data protection laws.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • B.G. Kher Commission (1955): The First Official Language Commission constituted under Article 344 of the Constitution by the President. Its mandate was to make recommendations for the progressive use of the Hindi language for official purposes while safeguarding regional linguistic interests.
