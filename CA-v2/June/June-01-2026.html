The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • Initiatives like the 'Shine' app in the Indian Railways create secure grievance mechanisms, specifically addressing workplace harassment and ensuring a safer occupational environment for women.
  • Kerala's establishment of a dedicated Senior Citizens Welfare Department points towards an institutional recognition of an aging demographic structure.
Economic Impact
  • Taiwan overtaking India as the 5th largest global stock market highlights how deep investments in specialized tech manufacturing—specifically semiconductors—can drastically amplify a nation's macroeconomic valuation.
Political Impact
  • The evolution of the Railway Budget—separated in 1924 by the William Acworth Committee and merged back with the General Budget in 2017 via the Bibek Debroy Committee—demonstrates a shift towards consolidated fiscal federalism.
Environmental / Technological Impact
  • The SVAMITVA Scheme leverages unmanned aerial vehicles (Drones) for precision mapping of rural landscapes, modernizing land records and drastically reducing property disputes at the grassroots level.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • Consolidated Budgeting: Merging the Railway and General Budgets (2017) eliminated a standalone colonial legacy, allowing the Finance Ministry greater flexibility in allocating capital expenditure for infrastructure without political grandstanding.
  • Sports Administration: The Lodha Committee (2015) guidelines enforced transparency, age caps, and tenure limits within the BCCI, attempting to professionalize the wildly lucrative cricket economy.

Challenges

  • Manufacturing Deficits: India losing its 5th place stock market ranking to Taiwan is a harsh indicator that India's domestic manufacturing ecosystem (especially in deep tech and hardware) still lags significantly behind East Asian hubs.
  • Institutional Vacancies: Handing over critical institutions like DRDO via 'Additional Charge' (to Rajesh Kumar Singh) rather than full-time appointments can temporarily stall strategic R&D continuity.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • William Acworth Committee (1924): Recommended the separation of the Railway Budget from the General Public Budget.
  • Bibek Debroy Committee (2015): Recommended merging the Railway Budget back into the General Budget, implemented in 2017.
  • Lodha Committee (2015): Tasked by the Supreme Court to analyze and recommend structural administrative reforms within the BCCI (Board of Control for Cricket in India).

Mains Descriptive Questions

15-Marker · Mains Question 1
Trace the evolution of the Railway Budget in India from its separation by the Acworth Committee to its subsequent merger with the General Budget by the Bibek Debroy Committee. Discuss the macroeconomic advantages of this merger.
Hint: Mention the historical context (1924 separation due to railways being the bulk of colonial revenue). Explain the 2017 merger's rationale: railways were no longer the dominant revenue source, the merger saves the railway from paying dividend to the center, and it allows streamlined capital expenditure via the consolidated fund, eliminating populist announcement pressures.
15-Marker · Mains Question 2
Taiwan replacing India as the 5th largest global stock market underscores the critical value of deep-tech and semiconductor industries. Analyze the current challenges India faces in hardware manufacturing and suggest policy measures to build a self-reliant semiconductor ecosystem.
Hint: Contrast India's service-sector dominance with Taiwan's hardware (TSMC) dominance. Discuss challenges: high capital requirement, lack of silicon fabrication plants (fabs), water/electricity logistics, and intellectual property deficits. Suggest measures: PLI schemes, DLI (Design Linked Incentive) pushes, and international joint ventures. (Link to SDG 9).
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
Discuss the objectives and technological framework of the SVAMITVA Scheme. How does drone-based mapping of rural properties resolve long-standing socio-economic challenges in Indian villages?
Hint: Mention launch date (April 24, 2020) and Ministry (Panchayati Raj). Discuss how rural properties often lack clear title deeds, leading to land disputes and preventing farmers from using land as collateral for loans. Explain how Drones create exact 'Record of Rights', facilitating precise taxation, institutional credit access, and easing judicial backlog regarding property disputes.
