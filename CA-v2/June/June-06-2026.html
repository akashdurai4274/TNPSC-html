Agencies, Acts & Schemes

  • Ramsar Convention: Signed on February 2, 1971, in Ramsar, Iran. Implemented globally on Dec 21, 1975. India joined on February 1, 1982.
  • PM PRANAM Scheme: Launched in 2023 under the Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers. The objective is to reduce the usage of chemical/synthetic fertilizers and promote sustainable agriculture.
  • National e-Vidhan Application (NeVA): A Mission Mode Project under the Digital India Programme to make all legislative houses paperless (One Nation, One Application).

⬡ Tamil Nadu Connect

  • Wetland Dominance: Despite the recent additions in Uttar Pradesh, Tamil Nadu retains the top position in India for hosting the maximum number of recognized Ramsar sites.
  • E-Governance Leadership: Tamil Nadu was among the early adopters of making its legislative assembly operations paperless, a model that states like West Bengal are now adopting via NeVA.
03

The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • The introduction of a distinct Vegan logo by FSSAI ensures rigorous dietary transparency, protecting the lifestyle choices of consumers against misleading packaging.
Economic Impact
  • The PM PRANAM scheme not only fosters sustainable agriculture but significantly reduces the national fiscal deficit by cutting down massive government subsidies on imported chemical fertilizers.
Political Impact
  • The nationwide rollout of NeVA standardizes legislative procedures across states, fulfilling the "One Nation, One Application" vision while centralizing digital legislative archives.
Environmental Impact
  • Achieving the milestone of 100 Ramsar sites demonstrates India's aggressive diplomatic and domestic commitment to wetland conservation amidst escalating climate volatility.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • FDI in Education: Permitting prestigious institutions like the University of Liverpool to open campuses in Bengaluru curtails brain drain and retains foreign exchange within India.
  • Ecological Documentation: Declaring wetlands as Ramsar sites mandates strict international monitoring, preventing illegal land encroachments and industrial pollution in those sensitive zones.

Challenges

  • Federal Friction: Centralized digital initiatives like NeVA are sometimes viewed with suspicion by opposition-ruled states as potential infringements upon their legislative autonomy.
  • Agricultural Transition: Shifting abruptly from heavily subsidized synthetic fertilizers (targeted by PM PRANAM) to bio-fertilizers can cause short-term yield drops, leading to farmer distress.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Fraser Commission (1902): Appointed by Lord Curzon under the chairmanship of Sir Andrew Fraser. It was a crucial body tasked with examining and recommending comprehensive reforms for the British Indian Police system.

Mains Descriptive Questions

15-Marker · Mains Question 1
Evaluate the objectives of the PM PRANAM scheme. How can reducing the dependency on synthetic fertilizers transform India's agricultural economy and environmental footprint?
Hint: Mention its launch in 2023 by the Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers. Discuss the fiscal burden of fertilizer subsidies. Explain the ecological benefits: preventing soil degradation, protecting groundwater from nitrate pollution, and fostering bio-fertilizers. Link to SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption) and SDG 15 (Life on Land).
15-Marker · Mains Question 2
Discuss the significance of the National e-Vidhan Application (NeVA) in modernizing the legislative processes in India. What are the potential challenges in its nationwide adoption?
Hint: Outline NeVA as a Digital India Mission Mode Project aimed at creating paperless assemblies (recently adopted by WB as the 33rd legislature). Pros: Transparency, eco-friendly, central data repository. Cons: Cyber-security threats, digital literacy among older legislators, and states' fear of central overreach.
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
India recently declared its 100th Ramsar site. Analyze the role of the Ramsar Convention in wetland conservation and its importance for India's climate resilience strategy.
Hint: Trace the history (1971 Iran, India joining in 1982). Mention the 100th site: Jayaprakash Narayan Bird Sanctuary. Explain wetlands as "kidneys of the landscape" — flood mitigation, carbon sinks, and biodiversity hotspots. Highlight Tamil Nadu's leading role in hosting the maximum sites.
