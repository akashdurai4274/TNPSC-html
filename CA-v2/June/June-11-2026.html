The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • Removing the 2-child norm in Madhya Pradesh government exams aligns with changing demographic realities and removes discriminatory barriers to employment for rural populations.
Economic Impact
  • Project Ganga in UP bridges the massive digital divide, fostering rural e-commerce, digital literacy, and expanding the gig economy footprint into the hinterlands.
Political Impact
  • PM Modi surpassing Nehru's elected-tenure record symbolizes a massive shift in India's political continuum, cementing the BJP's structural dominance over multiple electoral cycles.
Health & Environmental Impact
  • The recurring outbreaks in Kerala (e.g., Shigella, Nipah) highlight the urgent need for robust state-level epidemiological surveillance, clean water access, and stringent food safety protocols.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Subsidized Public Transport

Pros: Schemes like Priyadarshini / Vidiyal Payanam

  • Workforce Participation: Drastically reduces commuting costs, encouraging more women to join the formal and informal urban workforce.
  • Financial Independence: Savings generated directly boost household consumption, health, and education expenditures.

Cons: Schemes like Priyadarshini / Vidiyal Payanam

  • Fiscal Strain: Places an immense recurring financial burden on already debt-ridden State Transport Undertakings (STUs).
  • Infrastructure Deterioration: Without timely government reimbursement, STUs struggle to maintain fleets, leading to overcrowding and poor service quality.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Campbell Commission (1866): Appointed under Viceroy John Lawrence to probe the catastrophic 1866 Odisha famine. Concluded that the disaster was caused by administrative failure and lack of rapid relief measures.
  • Subsequent Famine Commissions: Richard Strachey Commission (1880), Lyall Commission (1897), and MacDonnell Commission (1900) further formalized India's Famine Codes.

Mains Descriptive Questions

15-Marker · Mains Question 1
Evaluate the socio-economic impact of state-sponsored free public transport schemes for women, citing examples like Kerala's Priyadarshini and Tamil Nadu's Vidiyal Payanam.
Hint: Discuss the rise in female labor force participation by reducing commute barriers. Highlight financial independence (savings redirected to household health/education). Conversely, address the massive fiscal deficit these create for State Transport Undertakings (STUs) and the necessity for sustainable funding models.
15-Marker · Mains Question 2
The recurring outbreaks of infectious diseases like Shigella in Kerala necessitate a robust epidemiological framework. Analyze the causes behind such outbreaks and suggest measures to fortify the public health infrastructure.
Hint: Outline Shigella (bacterial, affects under-5 children, water/food borne). Mention Kerala's unique topography and high-density habitation. Measures: Real-time surveillance, stringent food safety audits, clean drinking water access, and public hygiene campaigns in alignment with Article 47 and SDG 3.
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
Examine the role of the Campbell Commission in shaping the famine relief policies of British India. How do modern infrastructure initiatives like 'Project Ganga' and 'Saubhagya Yojana' prevent systemic rural collapse in contemporary times?
Hint: Campbell Commission (1866) investigated the Odisha Famine and exposed administrative lethargy. Contrast this historical neglect with proactive modern schemes: Saubhagya (100% electrification) and Project Ganga (rural broadband in UP) which ensure rural populations have access to essential services, economic opportunities, and disaster warnings.
