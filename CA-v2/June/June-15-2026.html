The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • Odisha's groundbreaking KG to PG Free Education policy fundamentally shatters economic barriers, extending the spirit of the RTE Act and enabling unprecedented upward social mobility for marginalized youth.
Economic Impact
  • The BRICS Indore Declaration on Agriculture signifies a pivot towards multi-lateral cooperative agrarian frameworks, aiming to insulate member economies from global food supply chain shocks.
Political Impact
  • Revisiting the Muddiman Committee (1924) provides a historical lens on administrative transitions, paralleling modern debates on Center-State power-sharing and the constant evolution of federal governance.
Scientific / Heritage Impact
  • The discovery of an Iron Smelting Furnace at Keezhadi decisively proves the sophisticated metallurgical capabilities of the ancient Sangam era civilization, challenging previous chronological assumptions about Indian industrial history.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Universal Free Higher Education

Pros: State-Funded KG to PG Education

  • Eradicating Inequality: Eliminates tuition fees as a structural barrier, ensuring that higher education (Masters, Engineering, etc.) is not a privilege restricted to the wealthy.
  • GER Boost: Directly spikes the Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER), converting a massive youth demographic into a formally skilled knowledge workforce.

Cons: State-Funded KG to PG Education

  • Fiscal Hemorrhage: Sustaining free postgraduate education places a colossal, recurring financial burden on the state exchequer, risking budget deficits.
  • Quality Dilution: Without proportional investment in infrastructure and faculty, funding might be stretched too thin, resulting in degraded educational quality.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Muddiman Committee (1924): Officially the Reforms Enquiry Committee. Constituted to assess the dysfunctions of the diarchy system introduced by the Government of India Act, 1919 (Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms). Its report exposed the inherent flaws of divided provincial governance.

Trace the significance of the archaeological findings at Keezhadi, particularly the recent discovery of an iron smelting furnace. How do these findings alter the historical narrative of early industrialization in ancient Tamil civilization?
Hint: Mention the 11th phase of excavation. Finding an iron furnace implies advanced metallurgy and tools/weaponry manufacturing capability. It proves the Sangam era society was not merely agrarian but highly industrialized, pushing back the chronological timeline of urban development in South India.

Hint: Define Reforms Enquiry Committee formed to assess the Govt of India Act 1919. Discuss the failure of Diarchy (transferred vs. reserved subjects) exposing flawed provincial autonomy.
