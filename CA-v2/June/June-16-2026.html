The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • The phased extension of the Morning Breakfast Scheme (up to Class 8) decisively fights early childhood stunting and malnutrition, historically ensuring higher retention rates in government schools and fostering intergenerational social mobility.
Economic Impact
  • Awarding GI Tags to tribal and regional crafts like Bhagaiya Silk and Munda Jewelry protects indigenous intellectual property, preventing corporate exploitation while opening direct global export corridors for local artisans.
Diplomatic Impact
  • Establishing an India Chair for AI in Slovakia and spearheading 'Bharat Innovates' in France signifies India's shift from being a technology consumer to a strategic technology exporter and global influencer in AI governance.
Defense & Security Impact
  • The courageous counter-terrorism actions by Kirti Chakra awardee Meenakshi Sundaram underline the rigorous operational readiness of the Indian Army in highly volatile border regions like Kulgam, Kashmir.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Free Welfare vs Targeted Schemes

Pros: Targeted Nutrition (Breakfast Scheme)

  • Cognitive Development: Addressing hidden hunger directly boosts student concentration levels, improving the overall quality of primary learning and educational outcomes.
  • Women's Labor Participation: Relieves the morning cooking burden on working-class mothers, encouraging higher female participation in the formal and informal workforce.

Cons: Broad Expansion

  • Fiscal Burden: Expanding large-scale feeding programs to upper-primary levels requires massive, recurring budget allocations, straining the state exchequer.
  • Logistical Bottlenecks: Maintaining daily hygiene, supply chain integrity, and uniform food quality across thousands of dispersed government schools is an administrative challenge.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Chatfield Commission (1939): Constituted before WWII, its core mandate was to suggest comprehensive reforms for the modernization, mechanization, and reorganization of the Indian Army, moving it away from obsolete cavalry strategies to modern motorized infantry and artillery.
