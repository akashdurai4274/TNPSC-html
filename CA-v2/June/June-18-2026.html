The Fact-Sheet (Prelims Focus)

Category Extracted Data Points & Details
Economy & Inflation Wholesale Price Index (WPI): New Base Year is 2022-2023 (previously 2011-12). Released by the DPIIT (Dept for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade) under the Ministry of Commerce. It is calculated monthly and uniquely tracks inflation for goods only (services are excluded).
Startup Ecosystem Unicorn Milestone: A startup reaches 'Unicorn' status when its valuation crosses $1 Billion USD as a private entity. Term coined by Aileen Lee.
India's Stats: India's 130th Unicorn is Sarvam AI. Global Rank: USA (1st), China (2nd), India (3rd). Karnataka (Bangalore) holds the highest number of startups in India. Subcategories: Gazelles (reaching unicorn status in 3 years) and Cheetahs (within 5 years).
Science & Technology CODIEN: India's first Nuclear Engineering Design Center, set up by IIT Hyderabad.
IIT Genesis: Established based on the recommendations of the Sarkar Committee. The first IIT was IIT Kharagpur (currently there are 23 IITs).
Appointments & Polity National Statistical Commission: Saibal Chattopadhyay appointed as the new Chairman. The NSC was established in 2005.
Polity: Article 176(1) empowers the Governor to deliver the Special Address at the commencement of the first session after each general election and the first session of each year.
Important Days June 18: Autistic Pride Day; International Picnic Day. (Note: World Tourism Day is Sept 27).
June 5, 2020: Launch date of the Nagar Van Yojana (aimed at increasing urban green cover).

Schemes & Acts

  • Startup India: Launched on 16 January 2016. (Jan 16 is now celebrated as National Startup Day).
  • Stand-up India: Launched on 5 April 2016. Provides loans (₹10 Lakh to ₹1 Crore) specifically to Women, SC, and ST entrepreneurs to establish Greenfield Enterprises.
  • Whitley Commission (1929): Also known as the Royal Commission on Labour, set up under the chairmanship of John Henry Whitley to inquire into the conditions of labor.

The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • The Stand-up India scheme actively bridges the financial inclusion gap by mandating targeted institutional credit for SC, ST, and Women entrepreneurs, fostering grassroots socio-economic empowerment.
Economic Impact
  • Revising the WPI base year to 2022-23 ensures that India's macroeconomic inflation tracking accurately mirrors current industrial structures and wholesale consumption patterns, moving away from outdated 2011-12 metrics.
Political & Governance Impact
  • The constitutional mandate under Article 176(1) acts as a mechanism of executive accountability, where the Governor formally outlines the state government's policies and legislative agenda to the assembly.
Environmental & Tech Impact
  • Initiatives like the Nagar Van Yojana address critical urban ecological degradation, while tech infrastructure like IIT Hyderabad's CODIEN propels India toward self-reliant, clean nuclear energy solutions.
Pros & Cons Analysis: The Unicorn & Startup Ecosystem

Pros: High-Growth Startups

  • FDI and Economic Value: Rapidly scaling startups attract massive Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) and build critical domestic intellectual property, positioning India as a global tech hub.
  • Employment Generation: Ecosystems like Bangalore create substantial high-skilled employment and drive secondary economies in logistics, real estate, and services.

Cons: The Unicorn Bubble

  • Valuation vs. Profitability: Many unicorns operate on severe cash burns with inflated valuations without clear paths to actual profitability, creating volatile financial bubbles.
  • High Mortality Rate: Aggressive scaling demands often lead to sudden mass layoffs or complete business closures, disrupting local employment stability.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Sarkar Committee: Tasked with the development of higher technical education in India, its recommendations directly led to the establishment of the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), starting with IIT Kharagpur.
  • Whitley Commission (1929): The Royal Commission on Labour, chaired by John Henry Whitley, historically shaped early labor welfare regulations and industrial relations in India.

15-Marker · Mains Question 2
Analyze the rationale behind revising the base year of the Wholesale Price Index (WPI) to 2022-23. How does capturing accurate inflation data impact national macroeconomic policymaking?
Hint: Explain that DPIIT calculates WPI only for goods. Updating the base year from 2011-12 to 2022-23 aligns the index with current consumption and wholesale trading structures. Accurate data prevents faulty monetary policy adjustments by the RBI and ensures realistic fiscal planning by the government.
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
Discuss the constitutional role of the Governor with specific reference to the Special Address delivered to the state legislature under Article 176(1). Does this mandate symbolize nominal executive power or substantive policy direction?
Hint: Detail Article 176(1) requiring the Governor to address the first session after an election and the first session of the year. Argue that while the Governor delivers the speech, the content is drafted by the elected Council of Ministers, thereby symbolizing the nominal executive's constitutional duty to outline the elected government's substantive legislative agenda.
