Economic Impact
  • Securing IPR/Trademarks for religious institutions (Puri Jagannath) prevents the unauthorized commercialization and corporate exploitation of sacred heritage symbols.

Environmental & Tech Impact
  • Joint aerospace missions like the Indo-French 'Trishna' Satellite provide critical, high-resolution earth observation data necessary for global climate change mitigation and disaster management.
Pros & Cons Analysis: IPR for Religious & Cultural Heritage

Pros: Trademarking Heritage

  • Protection against Misuse: Legally restricts private entities from exploiting sacred logos and slogans (like the Neelachakra) for profit, preserving institutional sanctity.
  • Quality Control: Ensures that goods (like prasadam or souvenirs) bearing the mark maintain a standardized level of authenticity and hygiene.

Cons: Commercialization Risk

  • Cultural Enclosure: Trademarking aspects of public faith may infringe on the traditional rights of local artisans and small vendors who have historically relied on creating these religious artifacts.
  • Administrative Friction: Enforcing IP laws over deeply rooted cultural practices can lead to unnecessary legal battles between the temple trusts and devotees.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • A.L. Mudaliar Commission (1952-53): Also known as the Secondary Education Commission, established on 23 September 1952. It played a fundamental role in shaping the modern structure of secondary and higher-secondary education in post-independence India.

Evaluate the socio-economic and ethical implications of securing Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) for religious institutions in India, with reference to the recent trademarking by the Puri Jagannath Temple.
Hint: Discuss the Trademark Act 1999. Pros: Prevents corporate exploitation of sacred symbols, standardizes quality. Cons: Commodification of faith, marginalization of traditional artisans who use these symbols. Balance required between IPR and public cultural heritage.
15-Marker · Mains Question 2
"The expansion of the New Development Bank (NDB) into Central Asia marks a decisive shift towards a multipolar financial world order." Analyze this statement in the context of Uzbekistan joining the NDB.
Hint: Define NDB's origin (BRICS 2015, Shanghai HQ). Discuss Central Asia's strategic importance (resources, connectivity). Explain how NDB provides an alternative to Bretton Woods institutions (IMF/WB), fostering South-South cooperation without Western conditionalities.
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
In light of recent legislative actions by countries like the UAE and Indonesia, discuss the necessity and feasibility of implementing strict social media bans for minors to protect adolescent mental health.
Hint: Address the rising cases of cyberbullying, screen addiction, and anxiety among youth. Mention global precedents (Australia, UAE under 15, Indonesia under 16). Discuss feasibility challenges: age verification mechanisms, right to information/expression, and the role of parental controls vs. state bans.
