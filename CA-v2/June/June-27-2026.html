The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Child Welfare & Social Justice
  • Mission Vatsalya consolidates fragmented child welfare initiatives into a single umbrella, ensuring streamlined funding and targeted rehabilitation for vulnerable children across states.
Economic Engine (MSME)
  • The revised MSME classification (allowing medium enterprises up to ₹250 Cr turnover) prevents the 'dwarfism' of companies, encouraging scaling, exports, and massive employment generation.
Federal Diplomacy
  • Appointing a dedicated Special Representative in Delhi enables the Tamil Nadu government to effectively lobby for state funds, navigate bureaucratic red tape, and represent state interests without constant Chief Ministerial intervention.
Trade & Geo-Economics
  • Designating Haldia Port as an immigration check post facilitates seamless international maritime passenger and cargo movement, significantly boosting West Bengal's coastal economy.
Pros & Cons Analysis: State-Appointed Federal Committees

Pros: Advocacy for Autonomy

  • Decentralization Push: Committees like the Rajamannar Committee provided a structured intellectual framework for states to demand equitable financial devolution and administrative autonomy from a highly centralized Union.
  • Historical Blueprint: They successfully highlighted the misuse of Article 356 (President's Rule) and the partisan role of Governors, sparking a nationwide debate on true cooperative federalism.

Cons: Lack of Statutory Binding

  • Ignored Recommendations: Since these committees are constituted by the State, the Union Government is not constitutionally bound to accept or debate their reports in the Parliament.
  • Political Posturing: Often, the formation of such committees is viewed merely as political posturing by regional parties against the ruling party at the Center, leading to immediate dismissal of their findings.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • P.V. Rajamannar Committee (1969): Set up by the TN Government, it recommended the immediate constitution of an Inter-State Council (Article 263), the deletion of Articles 356 and 357, and transferring subjects from the Concurrent List to the State List to ensure robust fiscal and political federalism.

Mains Descriptive Questions

15-Marker · Mains Question 1
Critically evaluate the role of the Rajamannar Committee (1969) in shaping the discourse on Center-State relations in India. Discuss why its recommendations were largely ignored by the Union Government.
Hint: Discuss the context of its formation by the TN Govt (M. Karunanidhi). Highlight key recommendations: limiting Article 356, forming an Inter-State Council, and increasing state autonomy. Explain the limitations: constituted by a state (not binding on the Center), viewed as regional political posturing during the era of a strong central government.
15-Marker · Mains Question 2
Analyze the significance of the MSME sector in driving India's economic growth. How does the revised composite classification of MSMEs aid in preventing the 'dwarfism' of enterprises?
Hint: Define MSME importance (employment generation, export contribution). Explain the old vs. new criteria (incorporating both Investment and Turnover). Highlight how allowing Medium enterprises up to ₹250 Cr turnover removes the fear of losing MSME benefits, thereby encouraging scaling, formalization, and technological upgrades.
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
Discuss the constitutional and social imperatives behind umbrella schemes like 'Mission Vatsalya'. How do such integrated approaches improve the efficacy of child protection services in India?
Hint: Connect to Article 39(e) and (f) regarding child welfare. Discuss Mission Vatsalya's role (MoWCD) in streamlining fragmented child protection schemes. Emphasize the shift from institutional care to family-based non-institutional care, standardized funding, and better monitoring mechanisms at the district level.
