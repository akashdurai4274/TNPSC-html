The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social & Health Impact
  • The PM Jan Aushadhi Scheme fundamentally democratizes healthcare by breaking the monopoly of expensive branded drugs, drastically reducing out-of-pocket medical expenditures for the poorest households.
Economic & Statistical Planning
  • Commemorating P.C. Mahalanobis highlights the critical role of robust statistical modeling (the Mahalanobis Model of the 2nd FYP) in pivoting a nascent agrarian India toward heavy industrialization and self-reliance.
Political & Federal Dynamics
  • The Sarkaria Commission's insistence on restricting Article 356 and ensuring Governors are 'outsiders' laid the theoretical groundwork for modern Cooperative Federalism, even if implementation remains politically contentious.
Environmental Diplomacy
  • Extreme climate anomalies (heatwaves in France) and seismic events (earthquake in Venezuela) underscore the necessity of global disaster diplomacy, as executed via India's Operation Amistad.
Pros & Cons Analysis: Implementing Sarkaria Commission Reports

Pros: Strengthening Federalism

  • Institutionalized Dialogue: The recommendation to form an Inter-State Council under Article 263 provided a constitutional forum to resolve friction between the Centre and States.
  • Curbing Executive Overreach: By strictly defining the parameters for the imposition of President's Rule (Article 356), it aimed to stop the arbitrary dismissal of elected state governments for political gain.

Cons: Lack of Binding Authority

  • Advisory Nature: As the commission's recommendations are non-binding, successive Union Governments have selectively ignored critical guidelines, particularly regarding the partisan appointment of State Governors.
  • Outdated Nuances: Drafted in the 1980s, the report does not adequately address modern federal challenges like GST revenue sharing or the asymmetric use of central investigative agencies.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Sarkaria Commission (1983): Established by the Ministry of Home Affairs to review the working of the existing arrangements between the Union and States. Headed by R.S. Sarkaria. Submitted its final report in 1988 with 247 recommendations, notably leading to the formal establishment of the Inter-State Council in 1990.

Hint: Discuss Mahalanobis as the 'Father of Indian Statistics'. Detail the Mahalanobis Model of the 2nd FYP (1956-1961), which shifted focus from agriculture (1st FYP) to rapid industrialization, capital goods, and public sector dominance, laying the foundation for India's self-reliance in heavy industries.
