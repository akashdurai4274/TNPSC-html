Constitutional Articles & Acts

  • Article 165: Deals with the Advocate General of the State. Appointed by Governor; must be qualified to be a High Court Judge; holds office during the pleasure of the Governor; remuneration decided by Governor (on CM/Council advice).
  • Article 177: Grants the AG the right to speak, take part in legislative proceedings, and be a member of assembly committees, but without the right to vote.
  • 14th Constitutional Amendment Act (1962): Made Puducherry a Union Territory.
  • Article 239A: Inserted via 14th CAA, allowing the creation of local legislatures or Council of Ministers for specific UTs like Puducherry. (Part VIII, Articles 239-242 deals with UTs).

The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • The Supreme Court's "SUSAGAI" AI chatbot democratizes legal access, helping citizens and litigants navigate complex filing procedures and judicial jargon.
  • Mission Mausam directly impacts agrarian societies by providing highly accurate, localized weather forecasting, preventing crop losses.
Economic Impact
  • The sudden hike in gold import duty to 15% is a classic application of a "Tight Money Policy". It actively disincentivizes non-essential imports to prevent the rapid depletion of India's foreign exchange reserves, defending the Rupee.
Political Impact
  • The constitutional parameters around the Advocate General (Articles 165 & 177) underscore the necessity of non-partisan legal counsel for state executives.
  • The revocation of the unconventional OSD appointment reflects the tension between executive discretion and the demand for merit-based administrative continuity.
Environmental Impact
  • Recognizing and protecting ecosystems like the Saddle Peak National Park ensures the preservation of unique, endemic biodiversity in the fragile Andaman and Nicobar archipelago.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • Digital Judiciary (One Case One Data): Eliminates redundant filings across hierarchical courts (Taluk to SC), drastically reducing case pendency and saving administrative time.
  • Tight Money Policy on Gold: Stabilizes domestic inflation by curbing the outflow of US Dollars.

Challenges

  • AI in Justice Delivery: Risk of "AI hallucinations" in the SUSAGAI chatbot could mislead rural litigants lacking digital literacy.
  • Gold Tariff Hikes: Historically, excessive import duties directly catalyze black market operations and illegal gold smuggling across porous borders.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Supreme Court E-Committee: The nodal body driving the integration of technological tools like the "One Case One Data" centralized registry and AI chatbots into the Indian justice system.
