The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • Electoral Trust: The historical evolution from ballot papers to EVMs in 1982, and subsequently to VVPATs in 2013, highlights the Election Commission's commitment to ensuring transparency and voter confidence.
  • Cultural Diplomacy: Project Mausam re-establishes India's historical maritime ties across the Indian Ocean, leveraging soft power.
Economic Impact
  • Sugar Export Ban: Banning sugar exports until September 2026 acts as an anti-inflationary measure to prioritize domestic market availability, though it compromises agricultural export revenues.
Political Impact
  • Coalition Dynamics: The formation of a minority government in Kerala (Congress with 63 seats supported by IUML's 22 seats out of 140) demonstrates complex floor management in state legislatures.
  • Agency Autonomy: The extension of tenure for the CBI Director using executive discretion reignites debates over the independence of investigative agencies governed under the archaic DSPE Act.
Environmental Impact
  • Energy Conservation: Top executive leadership reducing convoy sizes and promoting EV adoption signals a high-level policy shift towards averting fuel crises and mitigating carbon emissions.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • VVPAT Integration: Provides a physical paper trail (lasting ~7 seconds) confirming the electronic vote, crucial for dispute resolution.
  • Sugar Ban: Protects low-income domestic consumers from price volatility caused by global shortages.

Challenges

  • CBI's Statutory Basis: The lack of a dedicated, independent constitutional or modern statutory framework makes the CBI vulnerable to allegations of political partisanship.
  • Coalition Instability: Minority governments are inherently fragile and often require ideological compromises to maintain the legislative majority.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Election Commission Reforms: The phased introduction of EVMs and VVPATs stems from various electoral reform committees aiming to eliminate booth capturing and ballot stuffing.

Mains Descriptive Questions

15-Marker · Mains Question 1
Trace the evolutionary milestones of electronic voting technology in India, from the first use of EVMs to the integration of VVPATs. How do these technological interventions safeguard the democratic process?
Hint: Mention the 1982 North Paravur by-election (EVM debut) and the 2013 Noksen assembly election (VVPAT debut). Discuss how EVMs prevent ballot stuffing and invalid votes, while VVPATs ensure physical auditability, directly enhancing electoral transparency and voter trust.
15-Marker · Mains Question 2
Critically analyze the statutory backing and autonomy of the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI). Why does the dependence on the Delhi Special Police Establishment (DSPE) Act, 1946 raise concerns regarding executive interference?
Hint: Outline that the CBI is not a statutory body itself but derives power from the DSPE Act. Discuss how reporting to the Ministry of Personnel limits its operational independence, citing recent debates over tenure extensions of Directors. Suggest the need for an independent constitutional or statutory framework.
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
Differentiate between "Project Mausam" and "Mission Mausam". Evaluate the significance of Project Mausam in bolstering India's cultural diplomacy in the Indian Ocean Region.
Hint: Mission Mausam (Ministry of Earth Sciences) focuses on climate/weather forecasting. Project Mausam (Ministry of Culture, launched 2014) focuses on archaeological and historical maritime trade routes. Discuss its role in countering regional geopolitical influences through soft power and shared heritage.
