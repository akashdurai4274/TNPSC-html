Why in News

  • Polity: The newly formed Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly elected its Speaker and Deputy Speaker, invoking critical discussions surrounding Constitutional Articles 178–181.
  • Economy: The Union Government announced the 'VPG RAMJI' scheme, structurally overhauling and replacing the iconic MGNREGA.
  • Macroeconomics: The Prime Minister advised citizens to minimize gold imports for a year to stabilize India's depleting Foreign Exchange (Forex) reserves and defend the Rupee.

Constitutional Articles

  • Article 178: Mandates the offices of the Speaker and Deputy Speaker for the State Legislative Assembly.
  • Article 179: Vacation, resignation, and removal. Crucial Note: The Speaker submits their resignation to the Deputy Speaker, and vice versa.
  • Article 180: Deputy Speaker's power to perform Speaker's duties in their absence.
  • Article 181: The Speaker cannot preside over the Assembly while a resolution for their removal is pending. They cannot cast a deciding/tie-breaking vote.

Schemes & Acts

  • VPG RAMJI: Viksit Bharat Guarantee for Rojgar and Ajeevika Mission Gramin. Replaces MGNREGA. Increases guaranteed workdays from 100 to 125. Implemented from July 1, 2026.
  • Annapurna Bhandar: West Bengal scheme announced to provide ₹3,000/month to eligible women (replacing Lakshmi Bhandar).
  • PM RAHAT: Launched Feb 13, 2026 (MoRTH). Free medical treatment up to ₹1.5 Lakhs for 7 days for accident victims.

⬡ Tamil Nadu Connect

  • Political Transition: C. Joseph Vijay assumed office as the Chief Minister. Key cabinet inductees include Bussy Anand, Venkat Ramanan (School Education), and Arun Raja (Ex-IRS).
  • Fiscal Strain of VPG RAMJI: The new scheme alters the central funding pattern to a 60:40 ratio for major states. For a project worth ₹10,000 Crores, the TN exchequer must now bear ₹4,000 Crores.
  • Civic Admin (PYQ Drill): In the Greater Chennai Corporation, the executive wing is administratively headed by the Commissioner (IAS), not the Mayor.

The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • Extending employment guarantee to 125 days bolsters rural safety nets against agrarian distress.
  • State schemes like West Bengal's Annapurna Bhandar (₹3000/month) reflect a nationwide pivot towards direct cash transfers for women, aiming to recognize unpaid domestic labor.
Economic Impact
  • Heavy gold imports require US Dollar payments, rapidly depleting India's Forex reserves.
  • This dollar outflow weakens the Rupee (trading below ₹93-94), leading to imported inflation (e.g., higher crude oil prices).
  • The VPG RAMJI scheme's 60:40 funding mechanism acts as a fiscal penalty for developed states, heavily stressing their localized capital expenditure.
Political Impact
  • The election of the Speaker brings focus to the neutrality of the presiding officer.
  • Article 181 safeguards democratic propriety by barring the Speaker from wielding tie-breaking powers when their own removal is debated, preventing conflict of interest.
Environmental Impact
  • The Litchi crisis in Bihar is a direct consequence of erratic climatic patterns—deficient rainfall coupled with severe pest attacks, emphasizing the vulnerability of mono-crop horticulture to climate change.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • VPG RAMJI: Provides 25 additional days of guaranteed work compared to MGNREGA, offering superior livelihood protection during non-agricultural seasons.
  • PM MITRA: Consolidates the fragmented textile supply chain into single mega-parks (e.g., Warangal), enhancing global export competitiveness.

Challenges

  • Fiscal Federalism: Altering the rural employment funding ratio to 60:40 disproportionately burdens performing states like Tamil Nadu.
  • Forex Management: Asking citizens to curb gold consumption via advisory is a temporary fix; the structural challenge of domestic gold demand requires robust financial alternatives (like Sovereign Gold Bonds).

Relevant Committees & Frameworks

  • Ministry of Rural Development Framework: Guidelines overseeing the transition and financial restructuring from the 2005 MGNREGA model to the 2026 VPG RAMJI model.
  • Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH): Operational protocols for the PM RAHAT scheme ensuring immediate, cashless trauma care during the 'Golden Hour' of accidents.

Why did the Prime Minister recently advise citizens to minimize the purchase of gold for a period of one year?
  • ATo prevent domestic hoarding and illegal smuggling of yellow metal.
  • BTo preserve Foreign Exchange (Forex) reserves and prevent the devaluation of the Indian Rupee against the US Dollar.
  • CTo promote the newly launched Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB) scheme exclusively.

✔ Answer: B

Gold imports are paid for in US Dollars. Heavy importation depletes India's Forex reserves, forcing the RBI to buy Dollars with Rupees, which depreciates the Rupee's value (currently sliding below ₹93) and causes imported inflation.
