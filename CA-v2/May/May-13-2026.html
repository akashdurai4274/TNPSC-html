Constitutional Articles & Acts

  • Anti-Defection Law (10th Schedule): Invoked if an MLA disobeys the directions (whip) issued by their party.
  • Article 76: Deals with the Attorney General of India (Note: The Solicitor General, who assists the AG, is a statutory post, not constitutional).
  • Navya Scheme: Launched June 24, 2025. Jointly implemented by the Ministry of Women & Child Development and Ministry of Skill Development. Targets adolescent girls (16-18 years) in Aspirational Districts for vocational training.

The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • The Navya Scheme empowers adolescent girls in backward (Aspirational) districts, bridging the gender skill gap and reducing early marriage dependencies.
  • Recognizing nursing professionals with the Florence Nightingale Award highlights the essential caregiving foundation of the healthcare system.
Economic Impact
  • Vocational training through the Navya scheme transitions unskilled youth into the formal workforce, directly contributing to the nation's demographic dividend.
Political Impact
  • The office of the Whip maintains party discipline and prevents political instability. It operates purely on British parliamentary conventions, lacking direct constitutional mention.
  • Appointing political loyalists or non-bureaucratic experts (like astrologer Ratan Pandit as OSD) reflects the Executive's discretionary powers, often leading to ideological friction in public administration.
Environmental Impact
  • Protecting biodiversity in regions like the Amrabad Tiger Reserve (Nallamala Hills) is vital for ecological balance and preserving the traditional habitat of the Chenchu tribes.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • The Whip System: Ensures that legislative bills supported by the ruling party pass smoothly without internal fragmentation or cross-voting.
  • Navya Scheme: Targets a highly specific, vulnerable demographic (16-18-year-old girls) precisely when they are at risk of dropping out of the education system.

Challenges

  • Anti-Defection Overreach: The strict enforcement of the whip stifles intra-party democracy and prevents legislators from voting according to their conscience or constituents' specific needs (except during Presidential elections).

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Aspirational Districts Programme (NITI Aayog): The underlying geographical framework used to identify backward districts for the targeted implementation of the Navya scheme.

Critically analyze the objectives and significance of the 'Navya' Scheme. How does targeting adolescent girls in Aspirational Districts contribute to India's long-term demographic and economic dividends?
Hint: Highlight the joint implementation by the Ministry of Women & Child Development and Ministry of Skill Development. Focus on the 16-18 age group, vocational training, and how it prevents dropouts and early marriages, aligning with SDG 4.

Discuss the role of the Executive in making discretionary non-bureaucratic appointments (such as Officers on Special Duty) to assist the political leadership. Does such a parallel advisory structure conflict with the traditional civil services machinery?
Hint: Use the recent TN appointment (OSD) context. Analyze the pros (domain expertise, political strategy alignment) vs. cons (ideological friction, bypassing merit-based TNPSC/UPSC recruitment). Conclude with the necessity of accountability in public administration.
