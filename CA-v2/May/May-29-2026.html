Agencies, Acts & Schemes

  • ULLAS Scheme: Understanding of Lifelong Learning for All in Society. Aims to impart basic education and promote complete literacy across states.
  • UDAN Scheme: Ude Desh ka Aam Naagrik. Launched in 2016 by the Ministry of Civil Aviation to make flight tickets affordable and improve regional connectivity for Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities.
  • Demographic Change Committee: Formed by the Ministry of Home Affairs, headed by Justice Prakash Prabhakar.

⬡ Tamil Nadu Connect

  • Inter-State Disputes: D.K. Shivakumar, previously Karnataka's Irrigation Minister and a strong proponent of the Mekedatu Dam, becoming CM raises concerns for Tamil Nadu, as TN CM firmly opposes the dam construction.

The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • Educational frameworks like the ULLAS initiative expand the definition of literacy beyond formal degrees, ensuring foundational reading and writing skills reach marginalized adult populations across states like Sikkim.
Economic Impact
  • The massive ₹2.87 Lakh Crore RBI dividend transfer acts as a crucial fiscal buffer for the Central Government, aiding in managing fiscal deficits and enabling higher capital expenditure without aggressive market borrowing.
Political Impact
  • Leadership transitions in neighboring states, such as D.K. Shivakumar assuming the CM office in Karnataka, directly impact the geopolitical negotiations of inter-state river water disputes (Cauvery-Mekedatu).
Environmental Impact
  • State-specific agricultural drives like Mission Queen Pineapple in Tripura or Kiwi promotion in Arunachal Pradesh encourage localized, climate-resilient farming, boosting rural economies sustainably.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • Aviation Connectivity: The UDAN scheme has successfully decentralized air travel, connecting Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities which acts as a catalyst for regional economic and tourism growth.
  • Macroeconomic Stability: Surplus RBI dividends demonstrate strong central bank asset management and foreign exchange stability.

Challenges

  • Demographic Shifts: Rapid demographic changes necessitate forming bodies like the Demographic Change Committee, indicating underlying socio-political tensions and potential delimitation concerns.
  • Population Hurdles: Achieving 95% literacy in heavily populated states like Tamil Nadu (~8 crore) remains logistically and economically far more complex than in smaller states like Sikkim or Mizoram.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • Demographic Change Committee: Headed by Justice Prakash Prabhakar (Ministry of Home Affairs).
  • R.N. Malhotra Committee: Core committee related to the Insurance sector.
  • L.C. Gupta Committee: Associated with SEBI, focusing on derivative trading.
  • Y.H. Malegam Committee: Focused on Microfinance sector regulations.
  • Note: These financial/economic committees were prominently featured in the UPSC Civil Services Examination 2026.

Mains Descriptive Questions

15-Marker · Mains Question 1
Analyze the parameters required for a state to be declared "Fully Literate". How does the implementation of the ULLAS scheme contribute to achieving comprehensive adult education, and what challenges do high-population states like Tamil Nadu face in reaching this metric?
Hint: Define literacy (7+ years, read/write basics). Mention the 95% threshold for "Fully Literate". Discuss ULLAS (Understanding of Lifelong Learning for All in Society). Explain that while Sikkim and Mizoram have succeeded due to smaller populations, TN (~8 crore pop) faces logistical hurdles in adult literacy outreach despite a strong ~88% current rate. (Link to SDG 4).
15-Marker · Mains Question 2
Discuss the economic implications of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) transferring a surplus dividend of ₹2.87 Lakh Crore to the Central Government. How does this impact the fiscal deficit and public expenditure?
Hint: Explain RBI's income sources (currency operations, forex). Highlight that a large dividend transfer provides the Centre with non-tax revenue. This helps bridge the fiscal deficit, reduces the need for market borrowing, and creates fiscal space for infrastructural capital expenditure without triggering inflation. (Link to SDG 8).
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
"Inter-state river water disputes are often deeply intertwined with regional political transitions." Evaluate this statement in the context of Karnataka's leadership change and its impact on the Cauvery-Mekedatu Dam dispute with Tamil Nadu.
Hint: Discuss D.K. Shivakumar's elevation to CM (formerly Irrigation Minister and strong Mekedatu advocate). Analyze how domestic electoral compulsions drive unilateral state actions (building dams). Contrast this with TN's existential reliance on Cauvery delta farming. Suggest the need for apolitical, robust mechanisms under Article 262 to ensure cooperative federalism.
