The Mains Analytical Breakdown

Dimensional Analysis

Social Impact
  • Kerala's introduction of a 3-day menstrual leave for school girls is a radical step in normalizing biological realities, destigmatizing women's health, and preventing educational dropout rates during puberty.
Economic Impact
  • Transitioning lower denomination currency (₹10, ₹20) to Polymer/Plastic notes dramatically reduces wear-and-tear. This increases the circulation lifespan and curtails the recurring macroeconomic costs of printing replacement currency.
Political Impact
  • The history of Goa's integration via Operation Vijay (1961) and subsequent Constitutional Amendments (12th and 56th CAAs) showcases India's post-independence territorial consolidation and constitutional flexibility.
Environmental Impact
  • Safeguarding vital ecological hotspots like the Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary ensures the survival of climate-resilient Mangrove forests, acting as natural barriers against coastal erosion while protecting Olive Ridley nesting sites.
Pros & Cons Analysis

Advantages

  • Gender Equity: Mandated menstrual leaves (supported by compensatory weekend classes) create an equitable educational environment, previously pioneered by Karnataka for working women.
  • Currency Durability: Polymer notes are significantly cleaner, harder to counterfeit, and waterproof compared to traditional cotton-paper blends.

Challenges

  • Implementation Hurdles: Menstrual leaves without strict academic catch-up frameworks risk girls missing out on core curriculum, potentially creating an unintended academic disparity.
  • Ecological Costs of Plastic: While polymer notes last longer, their initial production costs are higher, and their end-of-life disposal poses specialized environmental recycling challenges.

Relevant Committee Reports & Frameworks

  • UN Human Rights Frameworks: The addition of nations to the UN Blacklist on Sexual Violence acts as a global diplomatic pressure tool to enforce the Geneva Conventions during armed conflicts.
  • Police Reforms (Prakash Singh Guidelines): The UPSC empanelment process for selecting the state DGP ensures merit-based tenure stability, insulating police administration from arbitrary political shuffling.

"Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas (PURA) is critical to stalling distress migration." Analyze the core objectives of the PURA scheme conceptualized by Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam and its relevance in modern rural governance.
Hint: Mention the launch in 2004 under MoRD. Discuss the four connectivities: Physical (roads), Electronic (communication), Knowledge (education/health), and Economic (employment). Argue how upgrading rural infrastructure curbs urban crowding and fosters balanced regional development.
15-Marker · Mains Question 3
Critically examine the economic rationale behind shifting lower denomination currency notes (like ₹10, ₹20) from traditional paper-cotton blends to polymer (plastic) material.
Hint: Outline India's proposal to print polymer notes. Discuss Pros: vastly improved lifespan, waterproof nature, difficulty in counterfeiting, and long-term cost reduction in printing. Discuss Cons: High initial setup cost, incompatibility with existing ATMs/counting machines, and environmental concerns regarding plastic disposal. Cite Australia as the global pioneer.
