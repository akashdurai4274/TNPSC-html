Section 03
Planning Commission (Yojana Aayog) — Historical Background
RULE 01
Origins & Formation

Before NITI Aayog, the Planning Commission governed India's development planning. The KC Neogi Committee (1946) first recommended setting up a Planning Commission. Earlier, in 1938, the Indian National Congress formed a National Planning Committee — Subhash Chandra Bose was Congress President, and Jawaharlal Nehru was made its Chairman. The formal Planning Commission was established on 15 March 1950.

🏆 PYQ ALERT

The National Development Council (NDC) was established on 6 August 1952 — asked in TNPSC Group 2 twice. The NDC is where State Chief Ministers participated to review Plans. (Note: NDC ≠ Planning Commission ≠ NITI Aayog Governing Council — three separate bodies.)

🧠 MEMORY TRICK

NDC = "New Decision Club" formed August 6, 1952. Think: NDC gave CMs a seat at the table — but they couldn't really speak! (States had no real role, which led to NITI Aayog's creation.)

RULE 02
Soviet Inspiration — Five-Year Plans

The Five-Year Plans were inspired by Soviet Russia's planning model introduced by Joseph Stalin in 1928 (7-Year Plans). India implemented 12 Five-Year Plans in total under the Planning Commission. Plans were made at the top and pushed down to states (Top-Down approach).

Interesting aside from lecture: Former TN CM Stalin's name was given in memory of Joseph Stalin — the Soviet leader who launched the 7-year plans.

⚠️ TNPSC TRAP

Trap Question: "Who chaired the National Planning Committee formed in 1938?"
❌ Wrong answer students choose: Subhash Chandra Bose
✅ Correct: Jawaharlal Nehru. Bose was the Congress President who formed the committee; Nehru was appointed its Chairman.

Section 04
NITI Aayog — Establishment & Core Identity
RULE 03
Formation & Full Form

NITI Aayog was established on 1 January 2015 (New Year's Day) replacing the Planning Commission. NITI = National Institution for Transforming India. HQ: New Delhi.

PM Modi, having been the Gujarat CM for three terms, witnessed firsthand that State CMs had no meaningful role in the Planning Commission process. On assuming office in 2014, abolishing the Planning Commission was one of his first priorities.

🔴 CRITICAL FACT
  • NITI Aayog is purely an Advisory body — it has NO power to allocate funds to states
  • It functions as a Think Tank (Policy Research + Innovation body)
  • Planning Commission was an Executive body with fund allocation powers
  • Planning Commission: Top-Down | NITI Aayog: Bottom-Up
🏆 PYQ ALERT

The full form of NITI Aayog — "National Institution for Transforming India" — has been asked multiple times in TNPSC Group 2, Group 4, and VAO exams. Do not confuse with "National Integration…" or "National Implementation…"

⚠️ TNPSC TRAP

Trap: "NITI Aayog replaced the Five-Year Plan system."
❌ Trap answer: Yes, NITI Aayog runs Five-Year Plans
✅ Correct: NITI Aayog does NOT run Five-Year Plans. It uses a 3-year Action Agenda, 7-year Strategy, and 15-year Vision instead.

Section 06
Objectives, Approach & Strategic Framework
RULE 04
Cooperative Federalism — Core Objective

India is a federal country. NITI Aayog's primary goal is Cooperative Federalism — Centre and States working together as partners, not top-down command. The term "Cooperative Federalism" was coined by Granville Austin — the same scholar who called Fundamental Rights + Directive Principles the "Conscience of the Constitution."

Competitive Federalism (states competing to attract investment) is the healthy opposite — not to be confused with cooperative.

🏆 PYQ ALERT

"Who coined the term 'Cooperative Federalism'?" — Asked in TNPSC Group 1 (2019). Answer: Granville Austin. He also said Fundamental Rights and DPSP are the "Conscience of the Constitution."

Section 08
Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) — NITI Aayog Role
RULE 08
SDG Framework

SDGs were adopted at the New York Summit, 2015 by the United Nations. 17 Goals, 169 Targets. Deadline: 2030. Covers Economic + Social + Environmental dimensions.

In India, NITI Aayog is responsible for SDG Localisation — distributing SDG targets to states and monitoring progress.

NITI monitors 16 of 17 goals (Goal 17 = Global Partnerships — not actively monitored), using 70 targets and 113 indicators.

🔗 CONNECT

SDG Goal 4 (Quality Education) connects to NIPUN Bharat, Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan, NEP 2020, PM SHRI — all education initiatives monitored through NITI Aayog's Education Quality Index. TN's opposition to NEP 2020 (three-language policy) connects to state-level policy autonomy under Cooperative Federalism.

🪤 EXAMINER'S TRAP MCQ — The Sneakiest Question

Q: "The National Planning Committee was formed in 1938 with _______ as its Chairman."

A) Jawaharlal Nehru   B) Subhash Chandra Bose   C) Sardar Patel   D) B.R. Ambedkar

Why it's a trap: Most students know Bose was Congress President in 1938 and connect the NPC to him. But Bose FORMED the committee and appointed NEHRU as its Chairman. The question asks for "Chairman" not "who formed it."

✅ Correct Answer: A) Jawaharlal Nehru

B) 1 January 2015

D) 2 October 2014
✅ Answer: B — 1 January 2015
📌 Why: Established on New Year's Day 2015, replacing the Planning Commission.

Q2. What does NITI stand for in NITI Aayog?
A) National Integration Transforming India
B) National Institution for Total India
C) National Institution for Transforming India
D) New India Transforming Initiative
✅ Answer: C — National Institution for Transforming India
📌 Why: Official full form. "Transforming" not "Total" or "Integration."
🪤 Trap: Option A substitutes "Integration" — a very common wrong answer in TNPSC exams.
Q3. Who chaired the National Planning Committee formed in 1938?
A) Subhash Chandra Bose
B) Jawaharlal Nehru
C) Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel
D) B.R. Ambedkar
✅ Answer: B — Jawaharlal Nehru
📌 Why: Bose (Congress President) formed the committee; Nehru was appointed Chairman.
🪤 Trap: Most students say Bose — he was President but NOT Chairman of the NPC.
Q4. The National Development Council (NDC) was established on:
A) 15 March 1950
B) 1 January 1951
C) 6 August 1952
D) 26 November 1949
✅ Answer: C — 6 August 1952
📌 Why: NDC formed two years after Planning Commission to give States a (limited) review role.
🪤 Trap: Option A (15 March 1950) is Planning Commission's date. NDC came 2 years later.
