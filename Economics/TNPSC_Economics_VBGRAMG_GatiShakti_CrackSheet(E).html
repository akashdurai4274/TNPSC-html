Section 03

VBGRAMG Architecture, Functional Rules & Constraints

The Vikshit Bharat Guaranty for Rozgar and Ajeevika Mission Gramin (VBGRAMG) establishes a critical administrative change in the operational delivery of rural safety nets.

RULE 01 Mandatory Target Shift in Guaranteed Employment Volume

VBGRAMG legally scales up the annual employment guarantee threshold per rural household from the historic 100 days format to an upgraded 125 days framework, executed to shield agrarian households from seasonal underemployment cycles.

RULE 02 The Institutional Mandatory Harvest Exclusion Window

Out of the 365 days in a calendar year, a fixed 60-day exclusion window is enforced during local crop harvesting periods. No public works are executed during these days to safeguard the agricultural private sector from labor shortages. Public distribution of the 125-day allocation must fit tightly into the remaining 305 operational days.

RULE 03 Structural Redefinition of Funding Mechanisms

Moves away from the historical 100% centrally-backed unscheduled wage accounts. VBGRAMG introduces Normative Funding Parameters with a rigid structural split: 60% Central Contribution and 40% State Contribution. This system replaces unpredictable demand-driven spending with predefined, rules-based budgetary envelopes.

RULE 04 Unemployment Compensation Penalization Penalties

If an eligible worker demands employment, the administration must provide work within 15 days. Failure triggers a mandatory state-funded unemployment allowance. The financial burden falls completely on the State Treasury:
• First 30 Days: 1/4 (25%) of the legal standard daily wage rate.
• Day 31 Onwards: Escalates to exactly 1/2 (50%) of the daily wage rate for the remaining duration.

Practice Metric Matching: The national average daily wage baseline under the updated directive scales up to exactly Rs. 240.00 Rs. 327.40.
[CRITICAL FACT] Core Pillars of VBGRAMG Allocation Asset Design
The four strategic functional pillars of public works permitted under the VBGRAMG infrastructure blueprint are:
  1. Water Security: Focused on groundwater table recharge, traditional water body restoration, and desilting irrigation networks.
  2. Core Rural Infrastructure: Constructing all-weather farm-to-market access roads and public storage links.
  3. Livelihood Diversification: Civil works aiding aquaculture development, inland fisheries, and secure agricultural storage infrastructure.
  4. Extreme Weather Mitigation: Reinforcing protective bunds, checking environmental degradation, and managing micro-climatic flash flood controls.
[TNPSC TRAP] Unemployment Allowance Funding Allocations

Wrong Assumption: Students frequently generalize that since standard VBGRAMG wages are split 60:40 between Centre and State, unemployment penalties follow the same ratio.

Correct Fact Structure: Waging assets follow a 60:40 split, but if local state officials fail to assign a task within 15 days, the resulting unemployment allowance must be funded 100% exclusively by the State Government. The Centre pays 0% for administrative delays at the local block level.

[PYQ ALERT] 🏆 Gender Equity Mandate in Welfare Schemes
In historical public wage schemes (MGNREGA 2005), a mandatory statutory quota of minimum 1/3 (33.33%) participation was strictly reserved for women workers. This has been repeatedly queried by the Board in state administration exams. Under VBGRAMG, existing baseline worker job-cards remain valid while modern verification layers are introduced.
Section 04

Historical Evolution of Wage Employment Programmes

Rural poverty mapping dates back to historical investigations from early planning committees (such as the P.D. Ojha Study (1962), Bardhan Group, Dandekar & Rath Study, Alagh Task Force (1979), and Lakdawala Committee), all aiming to address the rural nutrition benchmark of 2400 Calories/day. This evolution led to a sequence of wage employment mergers:

Launch Year Official Program Name Structural Paradigm / Operational Mandate Evolutionary Lifecycle Node & Merger Matrix
1960s Rural Manpower Programme (RMP) Pilot decentralized community infrastructure production. Earliest post-independence experimentation with state-led rural wage structures.
1971 Crash Scheme for Rural Employment (CSRE) Assigned urgent employment to exactly 1,000 individuals per district for 10 months. Introduced targeted public employment quotas during periods of food scarcity.
1980 National Rural Employment Programme (NREP) Merged localized rural development works into a national structural plan. Embedded inside Samacheer textbooks as a major anti-poverty pillar. Merged in 1989.
1983 Rural Landless Employment Guarantee Programme (RLEGP) Provided explicit institutional job guarantees for completely landless families. Merged alongside NREP in 1989 to streamline rural funding streams.
1989 Jawahar Rozgar Yojana (JRY) Formed by merging NREP and RLEGP under the leadership of Rajiv Gandhi. Urban Split Strategy: JRY focused on rural blocks, while NRY (Nehru Rozgar Yojana) targeted urban areas.
1993 Employment Assurance Scheme (EAS) Introduced guaranteed wage options during lean agricultural months. Ran alongside the revised rural development programs throughout the decade.
1999 Jawahar Gram Samridhi Yojana (JGSY) Restructured version of the earlier JRY framework, focusing on village-level asset creation. Merged into the next phase of comprehensive rural programming.
2001 Sampoorna Grameen Rozgar Yojana (SGRY) Combined the funding channels of both JGSY (1999) and EAS (1993). Launched under A.B. Vajpayee. Later absorbed into the legislative framework of MGNREGA.
[MEMORY TRICK] Chronological Compression Mnemonic
To master the sequence of mergers for TNPSC sequence ordering questions, use this structural keyword path:
[RMP '60s] → [Crash '71] → [National '80] → [Landless '83] → [Jawahar '89] → [Assurance '93] → [Samridhi '99] → [Sampoorna '01] → [MGNREGA '05] → [VBGRAMG '2026]
Shortcut visualization: Remember that Jawahar (89) unites the 80s programs, which then evolve through Assurance (93) and Samridhi (99) into Sampoorna (01), clearing the path for the legislative formats.
[CONNECT] Contextual Macroeconomic Import Models

During the 1950s–1970s, India faced severe food deficits, relying on wheat shipments from the United States under the PL-480 (Public Law 480) program, paid for in Indian Rupees. Rice imports were similarly secured from Rangoon (Myanmar).

This vulnerability drove the adoption of the Green Revolution, building a secure buffer stock handled by the Food Corporation of India (FCI). This historical shift enabled the state to pivot from emergency food-for-work relief to structured, cash-backed wage employment models like VBGRAMG.

Section 05

PM Gati Shakti National Master Plan Framework

The PM Gati Shakti National Master Plan is a digital, tech-driven platform designed to break down silos across ministries and optimize India's multi-modal logistics network.

ENGINE SYSTEM The 7 Engines Driving Logistics Optimization

The entire physical master framework of the plan is structurally powered by exactly 7 functional thematic infrastructure engines:
1. Roadways | 2. Railways | 3. Airports | 4. Ports | 5. Waterways | 6. Mass Transport | 7. Logistics Infrastructure

PILLAR SYSTEM The 6 Constitutional Pillars of Gati Shakti Planning
  1. Comprehensiveness: Centralizes mapping data from all existing departments (such as SAGARMALA, BHARATMALA, and UDAN) into a single master layout.
  2. Prioritization: Allows cross-sectoral planners to evaluate and prioritize projects based on regional demands.
  3. Optimization: Reduces logistics bottlenecks by selecting the most cost-effective and efficient transit routes.
  4. Synchronization: Aligns work schedules across ministries to prevent repetitive construction delays (e.g., digging up a newly paved road to install fiber-optic cables).
  5. Analytical Support: Leverages satellite imagery and geospatial data to automate site selection and route planning.
  6. Dynamic Execution: Provides real-time progress updates through integrated dashboard monitoring.
[CRITICAL FACT] Technical Grounding and Budget Envelopes

• Total Capital Investment Target: Evaluated at a historic Rs. 100 Lakh Crore ($1.2 Trillion) layout scale.

• Digital Platform Host: Developed and managed by BISAG-N (Bhaskaracharya National Institute for Space Applications and Geo-informatics), located in Gandhinagar, Gujarat. It incorporates over 1600 geospatial data layers, coordinating 44 Central Ministries and 36 States/UTs.

• Logistics Cost Target: Designed to lower logistics overheads from their current share of 13% - 14% of GDP down to globally competitive single-digit levels.

[TNPSC TRAP] Architectural Engines versus Strategic Pillars

The Commission frequently creates trick questions by mixing the 7 Engines and the 6 Pillars of Gati Shakti.
• The Engines represent physical transport networks (e.g., Railways, Waterways, Mass Transport).
• The Pillars describe administrative planning concepts (e.g., Comprehensiveness, Optimization, Synchronization).

Do not mistake an operational planning pillar for an infrastructure asset engine during option elimination.

[CONNECT] Timeline of Integrated Sectoral Schemes
Gati Shakti acts as a unified digital framework that integrates several key infrastructure initiatives:
  • Jal Marg Vikas Project (2014): National inland waterways infrastructure development framework.
  • Sagarmala Programme (2015): Modernizing ports to drive port-led coastal economic growth.
  • UDAN (Ude Desh Ka Aam Nagrik) (2016): Expanding regional airport connectivity to underserved areas.
  • Bharatmala Pariyojana (2017): Developing primary highway corridors across India.
  • National Logistics Policy (2022): Formulated to complement Gati Shakti by aligning regulatory frameworks with digital infrastructure.

Section 08

High-Yield MCQ Drill (Exam-Style Practice)

Q1. What is the standard funding pattern split adopted for standard wage assets under the newly introduced VBGRAMG framework?
A) 100% Entirely Funded by Central Sector Accounts
B) 75% Central Government : 25% State Government Accounts
C) 60% Central Government : 40% State Government Accounts
D) 50% Central Government : 50% State Government Accounts
  ✅ Answer: C) 60% Central Government : 40% State Government Accounts
  📌 Why: VBGRAMG shifts funding to a Normative Funding Model with a structured 60:40 split between the Centre and States.
  🪤 Trap: Unskilled labor under the old MGNREGA model was 100% centrally funded, a common point of confusion.
Q2. Which digital infrastructure institute is directly responsible for developing and hosting the geospatial platform engine for the PM Gati Shakti Master Plan?
A) National Informatics Centre (NIC), New Delhi
B) BISAG-N, Gandhinagar, Gujarat
C) Indian Institute of Remote Sensing (IIRS), Dehradun
D) ISRO Telemetry Tracking Network, Bengaluru
  ✅ Answer: B) BISAG-N, Gandhinagar, Gujarat
  📌 Why: BISAG-N provides the core geospatial database, managing over 1,600 unique data layers for the project.
  🪤 Trap: Candidates often default to general tech agencies like NIC instead of specialized geospatial institutes.
Q3. Under the operational guidelines of VBGRAMG, how many calendar days are designated as the mandatory harvest exclusion window?
A) 30 Days
B) 45 Days
C) 60 Days
D) 90 Days
  ✅ Answer: C) 60 Days
  📌 Why: The rules mandate a 60-day freeze on public works during harvesting seasons to protect private agricultural labor supply.
  🪤 Trap: Missing the connection that 365 days minus 60 exclusion days leaves 305 operational days for project delivery.
Q4. Arrange the following historical rural employment initiatives in correct chronological sequence based on their launch years: 1. JRY, 2. NREP, 3. CSRE, 4. SGRY
A) 3 - 2 - 1 - 4
B) 2 - 3 - 1 - 4
C) 3 - 1 - 2 - 4
D) 1 - 2 - 3 - 4
  ✅ Answer: A) 3 - 2 - 1 - 4
  📌 Why: The timeline runs from CSRE (1971), to NREP (1980), Jawahar Rozgar Yojana (1989), and Sampoorna Grameen Rozgar Yojana (2001).
  🪤 Trap: Mixing up JRY (1989) and NREP (1980) due to their overlapping focus on infrastructure.
Q5. What is the national average daily wage rate set under the updated guidelines of the VBGRAMG scheme?
A) Rs. 280.50
B) Rs. 311.20
C) Rs. 327.40
D) Rs. 350.00
  ✅ Answer: C) Rs. 327.40
  📌 Why: The standard baseline wage across the scheme's national accounts is explicitly indexed to Rs. 327.40.
  🪤 Trap: Avoid rounding to clean numbers like Rs. 350; TNPSC relies heavily on exact decimal accuracy.
Q6. Which of the following is NOT categorized as one of the 7 functional engines driving the PM Gati Shakti National Master Plan?
A) Digital Telecommunication Connectivity Infrastructures
B) Mass Urban Transport Systems
C) National Inland Waterway Routes
D) Civil Aviation and Airport Gateways
  ✅ Answer: A) Digital Telecommunication Connectivity Infrastructures
  📌 Why: Gati Shakti focuses on physical transit logistics; telecommunications serves as an analytical mapping tool rather than a core transit engine.
  🪤 Trap: Telecommunications is a critical part of the digital platform, leading many to incorrectly categorize it as a primary transport engine.
Q7. If a worker under VBGRAMG is not assigned employment within 15 days, what is the mandatory unemployment allowance penalty for the first 30 days?
A) 1/10th of the standard daily wage rate
B) 1/4th of the standard daily wage rate
C) 1/2 of the standard daily wage rate
D) 100% of the complete standard daily wage rate
  ✅ Answer: B) 1/4th of the standard daily wage rate
  📌 Why: The statute requires the State Treasury to pay exactly 1/4 (25%) of the base wage during the first 30 days of delayed assignment.
  🪤 Trap: It scales up to 1/2 (50%) after day 31, which is often mistakenly applied to the initial 30-day period.
Q8. Which Industrial Policy Resolution is historically referred to as the "Economic Constitution of India"?
A) Policy Resolution of April 6, 1948
B) Industrial Policy Resolution of 1956
C) Industrial Policy Shift Statement of 1977
D) New Economic Policy Framework of 1991
  ✅ Answer: B) Industrial Policy Resolution of 1956
  📌 Why: The 1956 policy laid out the structural classification of public and private sector industries, earning this title.
  🪤 Trap: Candidates often confuse it with the 1991 LPG reforms due to their massive structural impact on modern markets.
Q9. The Jawahar Rozgar Yojana (JRY), launched in 1989, was formed by merging which two existing rural development initiatives?
A) Rural Manpower Programme & Crash Scheme
B) NREP & Rural Landless Employment Guarantee Programme (RLEGP)
C) Employment Assurance Scheme & JGSY
D) Small Farmers Development Agency & MFAL
  ✅ Answer: B) NREP & Rural Landless Employment Guarantee Programme (RLEGP)
  📌 Why: Under Rajiv Gandhi's administration, NREP (1980) and RLEGP (1983) were combined into JRY in 1989.
  🪤 Trap: SGRY (2001) represents a later phase of consolidation, often confused with the 1989 merger.
Q10. What is the total projected investment scale earmarked for the execution of the PM Gati Shakti National Master Plan infrastructure system?
A) Rs. 50 Lakh Crore Accounts Envelope
B) Rs. 75 Lakh Crore Accounts Envelope
C) Rs. 100 Lakh Crore Accounts Envelope
D) Rs. 150 Lakh Crore Accounts Envelope
  ✅ Answer: C) Rs. 100 Lakh Crore Accounts Envelope
  📌 Why: The Gati Shakti plan outlines a total investment layout of Rs. 100 Lakh Crore to transform national connectivity.
  🪤 Trap: Do not mistake this with the total annual Union Budget envelope, which currently sits near Rs. 50 Lakh Crore.
