1. Introduction — What is the Finance Commission?

The Finance Commission (நிதி ஆணயம் / நிதிக்குழு) is a Constitutional body established for the entire country of India. It is set up once every five years by the President of India (குடியரசு தலைவர்) under Article 280 of the Indian Constitution.

It was first established in 1951 — after the Constitution came into force on 26 January 1950. Since 1951, a new Finance Commission has been constituted every five years. As of May 2026, a total of 16 Finance Commissions have been set up, and the 16th Finance Commission is currently in operation.

CRITICAL The Finance Commission is a Constitutional Body — it is explicitly provided for in the Constitution under Article 280. This distinguishes it from NITI Aayog (earlier Planning Commission), which has NO constitutional basis. NITI Aayog is a non-constitutional body.
TRAP Students often confuse Finance Commission (நிதி ஆணயம்) with NITI Aayog (நிதி ஆயோக்). Remember: Finance Commission = Constitutional (Article 280). NITI Aayog = Non-Constitutional (no article in Constitution).

Core Purpose

The Central Government collects a large number of taxes (income tax, customs duty, surcharges, etc.). From the revenue collected through certain specified taxes, a share must be distributed to the State Governments. The Finance Commission determines what percentage of that collected revenue should be shared with the states, and how it should be distributed among them.

TIP Think of it simply: Central Govt earns money → Finance Commission decides how much % to give to states → States receive funds. The FC is the "referee" of fiscal federalism.

2. Constitutional Basis

Article 280

The President shall, within two years from the commencement of this Constitution and thereafter at the expiration of every fifth year or at such earlier time as the President considers necessary, by order constitute a Finance Commission which shall consist of a Chairman and four other members.

CRITICAL Article 280 — This is the ONLY article you need for Finance Commission. It authorises the President to constitute a Finance Commission every 5 years. The Constitution came into force on 26 January 1950, and the first FC was constituted in 1951.

4. Functions & Mandate

The Finance Commission recommends:

Function 1
Tax Revenue Sharing (வரி வருவாய் பகிர்வு) — What percentage of Central tax revenue should be distributed to states (Vertical Devolution)
Function 2
Distribution Among States (மாநிலங்களுக்கிடையே பகிர்வு) — How to distribute the states' share among all 28 states (Horizontal Devolution)
Function 3
Grants & Loans (மானியங்கள் மற்றும் கடன்கள்) — Recommendations for grants-in-aid and loans to state governments
Function 4
Centre-State Financial Relations Study (மத்திய-மாநில நிதி ஒப்பந்தங்கள் ஆய்வு) — Study of all financial arrangements between Centre and States
Function 5
Panchayati Raj & Urban Local Bodies (பஞ்சாயத்துராஜ் மற்றும் நகராட்சி) — Recommending funds for local self-government bodies at grassroots
Function 6
National Plan Targets (தேசிய திட்ட இலக்கு) — Suggesting methods to achieve national financial targets

CRITICAL The 16th Finance Commission recommended 41% of the Central divisible pool be devolved to states. States had demanded 50%, but the FC maintained the same 41% as the 15th FC.

The Divisible Pool (பிரிக்கக்கூடிய நிதி தொகுப்பு)

Not ALL central tax revenue is shared. Only revenue from specific taxes forms the Divisible Pool. The formula is:

Divisible Pool =

Gross Tax Revenue MINUS Cess + Surcharge + Cost of Collection

For example, your ₹100 petrol purchase has an "extra tax" (cess/surcharge) embedded in it — that portion goes directly to the Centre and is NOT shared. Only the remaining "divisible" portion forms the pool from which 41% is given to states.

TRAP The 41% is NOT of the total central tax revenue. It is 41% of the Divisible Pool (gross tax revenue minus cess, surcharge, and collection costs). Many students incorrectly think it's the full revenue.

6. Criteria for Horizontal Distribution (16th FC)

The 41% Divisible Pool is further split among all 28 states based on the following criteria. The total adds up to 100% of the states' share:

Income Distance / Revenue Gap (வருமான இடைவெளி) 42.5%

States with lower income get a larger share. Rich states get less.

Population (மக்கள் தொகை) 17.5%

States with larger populations (e.g., UP, Bihar) receive more funds.

Demographic Performance (ஜனசொல்பர்ஃபார்மன்ஸ்) 10%

Rewards states for demographic achievements (e.g., fertility rate control).

Area / Geography (பரப்பளவு) 10%

Larger states (by geographic area) receive proportionally more.

Forest & Ecology (வனம் மற்றும் சுற்றுச்சூழல்) 10%

States with more forest cover and better ecological indicators are rewarded.

GDP Contribution (ஜிடிபி பங்களிப்பு) 10%

Based on the state's contribution to national GDP.

TIP Mnemonic for 16th FC Criteria: "Income People Demo Area Forest GDP" → IPDAFG (42.5 + 17.5 + 10 + 10 + 10 + 10 = 100%)

7. History of Finance Commissions — All 16

TRAP Year of Constitution ≠ Year of Operation. Each FC is set up ~2 years BEFORE its operational period begins. The exam can ask EITHER the year of formation OR the 5-year operational period. Don't confuse them!

How the Cycle Works

Example: 15th FC was constituted in 2017. After 2 years of study, it submitted its report in December 2019. Its recommendations were operational from 2020–21 to 2025–26 (5 years). The 16th FC was then constituted in 2023 (2 years before 15th's term ended) and its recommendations are operational from 2026–27 to 2030–31.

CRITICAL Revenue Deficit Grants (ரெவென்யூ டிபிக்ட் கிராண்ட்ஸ்) — Grants given to states that have a revenue deficit (where expenditure > revenue). These are non-repayable transfers from Centre to States.

Fiscal Deficit (நிதி பற்றாக்குறை) explained simply: If you earn ₹10,000 but spend ₹15,000, your deficit is ₹5,000 = 50% of income. The FC sets a target: Centre should not exceed 3.5% fiscal deficit-to-GDP; States should not exceed 3%.

11. PYQ Section — Previous Year Questions

PYQ Under which Article of the Indian Constitution is the Finance Commission established?
✓ Answer

Article 280 — The Finance Commission is established under Article 280 of the Indian Constitution. It mandates the President to constitute a Finance Commission every 5 years.

PYQ How often is the Finance Commission constituted, and by whom?
✓ Answer

The Finance Commission is constituted every 5 years by the President of India under Article 280 of the Constitution.

PYQ How many Finance Commissions have been constituted in India so far (as of 2026)?
✓ Answer

16 Finance Commissions have been constituted from 1951 to 2026. The 16th Finance Commission (2026–2031) is currently in operation.

PYQ Who is the Chairman of the 16th Finance Commission?
✓ Answer

Arvind Panagariya (அரவிந்த் பனாகரியா) is the Chairman of the 16th Finance Commission.

PYQ What is the operational period of the 16th Finance Commission?
✓ Answer

The 16th Finance Commission's recommendations are operational from April 1, 2026 to March 31, 2031 (i.e., financial years 2026–27 to 2030–31). The FC was constituted in 2023 and submitted its report on November 17, 2025.

PYQ What percentage of the divisible pool has the 16th Finance Commission recommended for devolution to states?
✓ Answer

41% of the divisible pool (Central tax revenue minus cess, surcharge, and collection costs) to be devolved to all states. This is the same as the 15th FC. States had demanded 50% but were given 41%.

PYQ What is Vertical Devolution (செங்குத்து பகிர்வு)?
✓ Answer

Vertical Devolution refers to the distribution of Central tax revenues downward from the Central Government to the States as a whole. Since Centre is hierarchically above States, this top-to-bottom distribution is called "vertical." The 16th FC recommended 41% vertical devolution.

PYQ What is Horizontal Devolution (கிடைமட்ட பகிர்வு)?
✓ Answer

Horizontal Devolution refers to the distribution of the states' share (the 41%) among all 28 states based on various criteria (income gap, population, area, forests, GDP, demographic performance). Since all states are equal in status, the sharing is "horizontal." The criteria weightages add up to 100%.

PYQ What is the Divisible Pool (பிரிக்கக்கூடிய நிதி தொகுப்பு)?
✓ Answer

The Divisible Pool is the pool of Central tax revenue from which states' share is distributed. It equals Gross Tax Revenue MINUS (Cess + Surcharge + Cost of Collection). Not all Central taxes are shared — only the divisible pool is distributed between Centre and States.

PYQ Is the Finance Commission a Constitutional body? Is NITI Aayog a Constitutional body?
✓ Answer

Finance Commission = YES, Constitutional Body — established under Article 280.
NITI Aayog = NO, Non-Constitutional Body — there is no specific article in the Constitution for it. NITI Aayog (formerly Planning Commission) is a purely executive/administrative body.

PYQ When did the 15th Finance Commission submit its report, and for which period were its recommendations applicable?
✓ Answer

The 15th Finance Commission (Chairman: N.K. Singh) was constituted in 2017 and submitted its report in December 2019. Its recommendations were applicable for 2020–21 to 2025–26 (5 years, ending March 31, 2026).

PYQ When was the first Finance Commission constituted and who was its Chairman?
✓ Answer

The first Finance Commission was constituted in 1951. Its Chairman was K.C. Neogy (கேசி நியோகி). Its recommendations were operational from 1952 to 1957.

PYQ What are the fiscal deficit targets recommended by the 16th Finance Commission?
✓ Answer

The 16th Finance Commission recommended:
• Central Government: Fiscal deficit not to exceed 3.5% of GDP
• State Governments: Fiscal deficit not to exceed 3% of GDP
• Combined Debt: Target of 77.3% of GDP

PYQ The 16th FC report was tabled in Parliament on which date and along with what?
✓ Answer

The 16th FC report was tabled in Parliament on 1 February 2026 by Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman, along with the Union Budget 2026–27.

12. 💎 Live Classroom Gems

These are key insights from the live classroom discussion — beyond the standard textbook content:

💎 GEM 1 — FC vs NITI Aayog Distinction

The teacher specifically called out that many students confuse Finance Commission (Constitutional — Article 280) with NITI Aayog (Non-Constitutional). If asked in an interview or exam whether FC is a Constitutional body, always say YES and cite Article 280. NITI Aayog has NO article in the Constitution.

💎 GEM 2 — The 2-Year Pattern (Critical Pattern!)

A Finance Commission is set up approximately 2 years BEFORE its operational period begins. It spends those 2 years studying, then submits its report. The 5-year "term" people cite is the operational period of recommendations, NOT the period from when the FC was constituted. This distinction is EXAM-CRITICAL and books often don't explain it clearly.

💎 GEM 3 — Petrol Price Analogy for Cess

When you pay ₹100 for petrol, you don't see the "cess" embedded in it. The Centre collects this hidden extra tax. That cess/surcharge money is NOT shared with states — it stays with the Centre. Only the "divisible pool" (gross tax minus cess/surcharge/collection costs) is shared. This is why states always demand more — they don't get the cess revenue.

💎 GEM 4 — States Demanded 50%, Got 41%

All 28 State Governments collectively demanded 50% devolution from the 16th FC. But the FC recommended only 41% — the same as the 15th FC. "மாநிலங்களின் பங்கு 15வது குழுவை போலவே மாற்றமின்றி தொடர்கிறது" — the states' share continues unchanged from the 15th FC. This is a hot current affairs point for exams.

💎 GEM 5 — Why "Vertical" and "Horizontal"?

The teacher explained this visually: Centre is "above" States in India's power hierarchy → Centre-to-States distribution = top-to-bottom = VERTICAL. All 28 states are "equal" to each other → distribution among them = side-to-side = HORIZONTAL. This is why these simple geometric terms are used for fiscal devolution.

💎 GEM 6 — "Year of Constitution" vs "Year of Operation" Trap

The teacher warned: "Books often don't clarify this clearly." Exam questions may ask EITHER when the FC was constituted OR when its recommendations were operational. These are TWO DIFFERENT years. Example: 12th FC constituted 2003, operational 2005–2010. Always know BOTH dates for each FC.

💎 GEM 7 — Income Tax is a Central Subject

The teacher reminded students: Income Tax is collected by the Central Government (not states). "இன்கம் டாக்ஸ்ங்கற டிபார்ட்மெண்ட் வந்து மத்திய அரசோடது." This is why the Central revenue sharing formula matters — Income Tax revenue goes to Centre first, then is partly shared with states through the divisible pool mechanism.

💎 GEM 8 — Future 17th FC Timing Prediction

Based on the pattern: The 16th FC is operational until March 31, 2031. Therefore, the 17th Finance Commission will likely be constituted around 2029 — two years before the 16th FC's term ends. This is how you can predict future FC timelines!

💎 GEM 9 — 16th FC Report Tabled WITH Budget

The 16th FC report was tabled in Parliament on February 1, 2026 — the same day as the Union Budget. Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman presented both together. "பட்ஜெட்டோட சேர்த்தே ரிலீஸ் பண்ணாங்க" — This is a contemporary facts point likely to appear in current affairs-based questions.

💎 GEM 10 — Re-eligibility of FC Members

The teacher specifically mentioned that members of a Finance Commission ARE eligible for reappointment. "இவங்க மறுபடியும் தேர்ந்தெடுப்பதற்கான வாய்ப்புகள் உண்டு." This is a lesser-known fact that could appear as a true/false or MCQ option.
