★ Special Section: 1991 LPG Reforms
★ The 1991 Economic Reforms — LPG (Liberalisation · Privatisation · Globalisation)
July 1991 · PM: P.V. Narasimha Rao · FM: Dr. Manmohan Singh · Crisis: BOP — Forex = $1.2B only
PYQ TNPSC 2018, 2019, 2021, 2022, 2023 — VERY HIGH FREQUENCY REFORM
Context: Why the Crisis?
BOP Crisis 1991: Forex reserves fell to $1.2 billion — barely 2 weeks of imports. India was forced to pledge 67 tonnes of gold (Bank of England + Union Bank of Switzerland) to secure IMF loans. PM = P.V. Narasimha Rao, FM = Dr. Manmohan Singh. Rupee devalued by ~25%.
Three Pillars of LPG
L — LIBERALISATION
  • Abolition of Licence Raj (industries freed)
  • Industrial de-licensing — from ~800 to 18
  • MRTP Act restrictions reduced
  • FDI limits raised (51% auto-approval in 34 sectors)
  • Import licensing abolished (capital goods)
  • Reduced government control over economy
P — PRIVATISATION
  • Disinvestment of Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs)
  • Private sector entry in reserved areas
  • SEBI given statutory status (1992)
  • FIPB (Foreign Investment Promotion Board) established
  • Public sector reservation reduced to 8 industries
G — GLOBALISATION
  • Integration with world economy
  • Rupee made convertible on current account (1994)
  • WTO membership (1995)
  • Import duties reduced significantly
  • Export promotion policies enacted
  • Quantitative restrictions removed

★ Planning Commission → NITI Aayog
Planning Commission (1950–2014) → NITI Aayog (2015–)
🏛️ Planning Commission (1950–2014)
  • Established: March 15, 1950 (executive resolution — NOT by law)
  • Chairman: Prime Minister (ex-officio)
  • Effective head: Deputy Chairman
  • Constitutional status: NONE (extra-constitutional body)
  • Role: Formulate Five Year Plans, allocate resources to states
  • Approach: Top-down planning
  • States = recipients of allocated funds
  • Last Deputy Chairman: Montek Singh Ahluwalia
🚀 NITI Aayog (from Jan 1, 2015)
  • Full form: National Institution for Transforming India
  • Replaced Planning Commission on January 1, 2015
  • Chairman: Prime Minister (ex-officio)
  • CEO: Appointed by PM (not a minister)
  • First CEO: Sindhushree Khullar | Current: B.V.R. Subrahmanyam
  • Role: Think tank — policy advisory, NO fund allocation
  • Approach: Bottom-up, cooperative federalism
  • Plans: 3-yr Action Plan, 7-yr Strategy, 15-yr Vision
  • Promotes competitive federalism among states
