Mughal Rulers — Original Names & Titles

Q 01
MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.4/VAO TNPSC Gr.2
Match the following — Mughal rulers with their original/title names and meanings: Zahiruddin, Shahjahan, Jahangir, Aurangzeb
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Answer: 2 – 3 – 1 – 4

• Zahiruddin = Babur (Defender of Faith / نمبكيق كاپيوar of Faith)
• Shahjahan = King of the World (Shah = King, Jahan = World)
• Jahangir = Conqueror of the World (World-conqueror)
• Aurangzeb = Ornament of the Throne / Worthy of the Throne
📌 EXPLANATION
Zahiruddin is Babur's original Persian/Islamic name meaning "Defender of Faith." Babur is his title/nickname meaning "Tiger" in Turkic. Each Mughal emperor had both an Islamic birth name and a regnal title. In match-the-following questions, identifying even ONE correct pair using logic can eliminate wrong options.
🪤 TNPSC TRAP
Students often confuse "Zahiruddin" with later rulers. Remember: Zahiruddin = Babur = FIRST Mughal emperor. The number 2 appeared only once in the option list — use that to lock the answer.
💎 LIVE GEM — Bonus: Nur Jahan's Name Meaning
Teacher asked spontaneously: "What is the meaning of Nur Jahan?"
✅ Nur Jahan = Light of the World (Nur = Light, Jahan = World)
Nur Jahan was Jahangir's powerful wife. She exerted enormous political influence during his reign. This exact meaning question has appeared in TNPSC papers!
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Mughal rulers' name meanings and title-matching questions have appeared in TNPSC Group 4 (2016, 2019) and Group 2 (2018). Nur Jahan's name meaning specifically appeared as a standalone question.
✅ EXAM STRATEGY TIP
In match-the-following, if you know even ONE correct pair and that pair's answer option appears only ONCE across all choices, that IS your answer. Don't waste time on the others. Pick confidently only when you're 100% sure — don't guess on exclusive options.
02

Babur — Early Life & Conquests

Q 02
HARD Trick/Trap TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.1
Which of the following statements about Babur is INCORRECT?
(1) Babur inherited Samarkand as hereditary property from his father at age 11.
(2) After conquering Kabul and Ghazni, Babur crossed the Indus and established a small kingdom.
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Answer: Option C — BOTH statements are incorrect.
📌 WHY BOTH ARE WRONG
Statement 1 is Wrong: Babur inherited Fergana (in modern Uzbekistan), NOT Samarkand. He became king of Fergana at age 11/12 (his father's death). Samarkand was a city he later attacked and temporarily captured — it was NOT his hereditary property. He then lost it and recaptured it multiple times before eventually consolidating power elsewhere.

Statement 2 is Wrong: After conquering Kabul and Ghazni, Babur did NOT establish a "small kingdom" (siṛṛiya arasu). He built a LARGE EMPIRE — the Mughal Empire. He settled in Lahore and then defeated Ibrahim Lodi at the First Battle of Panipat (1526), founding the mighty Mughal dynasty.
💎 LIVE GEM — Babur's Full Story
Timeline sequence teacher explained:
1. Babur's father died → He inherited Fergana (NOT Samarkand) at ~12 years old.
2. He later attacked and temporarily held Samarkand — it was never his "hereditary" city.
3. After conquering Kabul → Ghazni → Lahore, a local chief Daulat Khan Lodi invited him to fight Ibrahim Lodi.
4. Babur used gunpowder and artillery (Rumi artillery technique) — a decisive advantage.
5. Daulat Khan Lodi got scared seeing the cannons and fled!
6. Babur defeated Ibrahim Lodi at First Battle of Panipat (1526) → Founded the Mughal Empire.

Teacher recommendation: Watch the web series "Empire" on Hotstar — it dramatises Babur's life story and makes it memorable for the exam!
[CRITICAL] KEY DISTINCTION
Fergana ≠ Samarkand — This is the most commonly confused pair. Fergana is in present-day Uzbekistan and was Babur's birthplace and inherited territory. Samarkand was Timur's capital that Babur desired and fought for multiple times.
Q 03
MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.4/VAO
At what age did Akbar ascend the Mughal throne? (Bonus: how does this connect to Babur's age at inheritance?)
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Akbar ascended the throne at 14 years of age.
📌 EXPLANATION
Akbar was born in 1542 AD. He won the Second Battle of Panipat in 1556 AD (age 14). Many students incorrectly write "11 years" — that is the age associated with Babur's inheritance of Fergana. Keep them separate!

Calculation: 1556 − 1542 = 14 years old.
🪤 COMMON STUDENT TRAP
Many students wrote "11 years" for Akbar's age — this is Babur's grandfather story getting mixed in. Akbar = 14 years; Babur inherited Fergana ≈ 12 years. Don't mix these!
💎 LIVE GEM — Regnal Ages Quick Summary
• Babur inherited Fergana: ~12 years
• Akbar crowned at Second Battle of Panipat: 14 years (1556)
• Key date: Akbar born 1542, crowned 1556
These numbers appear as trap MCQs — always verify with birth year calculation.

Foreign Travellers & Sources on Mughals

Q 04
MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.1
Whose travel accounts are considered sources of information about the Mughals? — Tavernier, Bernier, Manucci
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
All three — Tavernier, Bernier, and Manucci — provided information about the Mughals. Answer: Option D (All of the above).
📌 EXPLANATION
All three were European travellers who visited the Mughal court and wrote detailed accounts of Mughal society, administration, trade, and culture. All three visited during the reign of Shah Jahan.
💎 LIVE GEM — Full Traveller Roster for Shah Jahan's Reign
Travellers who visited during Shah Jahan's reign:
1. Tavernier (French gem merchant)
2. Bernier (French physician)
3. Manucci (Italian adventurer)
4. Mandeslo
5. Peter Mundy

Compare: During Jahangir's reign, the first important British visitor was William Hawkins, followed by Thomas Roe (second British envoy).
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Travellers and their corresponding Mughal reigns are a repeated exam topic. TNPSC has asked specifically about Tavernier, Bernier, and Manucci (Group 2 2017, Group 4 2021). William Hawkins + Jahangir connection has also appeared.

Mughal Period Literature

Q 05
MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.4/VAO
Who described Mughal administration in the book "Ain-i-Akbari"? [Who wrote Ain-i-Akbari and Akbarnama?]
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Abul Fazl (Abu'l-Fazl ibn Mubarak) wrote both:
• Ain-i-Akbari — describes Akbar's administration, revenue system, economy
• Akbarnama — Akbar's biography (life history)
📌 EXPLANATION
Abul Fazl was Akbar's court poet and one of the "Navaratnas" (Nine Gems) of Akbar's court. The Ain-i-Akbari is the third volume of the Akbarnama. It is an important primary source for Mughal administration.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Ain-i-Akbari and its author Abul Fazl have appeared in TNPSC Group 2 (2014, 2019) and Group 4 (2016) exams. The question "Who among Akbar's court wrote the administrative manual?" is a classic PYQ.
Q 06
HARD Factual TNPSC Gr.1 TNPSC Gr.2
Abdul Hamid Lahori and Muhammad Waris together wrote which book? [And whose biography is it?]
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Padshahnama — biography/chronicles of Shah Jahan.
📌 EXPLANATION
Abdul Hamid Lahori wrote the first two volumes and Muhammad Waris completed the third. The Padshahnama (Chronicle of the Emperor) documents Shah Jahan's reign, his architectural achievements, military campaigns, and court life.
💎 LIVE GEM — Which Emperor's Biography = Which Book
Quick reference for Mughal biographical chronicles:
• Baburnama — Babur's own autobiography
• Humayunnama — by Gulbadan Begum (Humayun's sister)
• Akbarnama — by Abul Fazl (about Akbar)
• Tuzuk-i-Jahangiri — Jahangir's own memoirs
• Padshahnama — by Lahori & Waris (about Shah Jahan)
• Alamgirnama — by Muhammad Kazim (about Aurangzeb)
Q 07
HARD Factual TNPSC Gr.1 TNPSC Gr.2
Which of the following is an ASTRONOMICAL work (not astrological)?
(a) Tajika Nilakanthi (b) Racha Gangadhara (c) Manucharita (d) Rajavali Pathaka
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
(a) Tajika Nilakanthi — written by Nilakantha — the only astronomical work produced during the Mughal period. Under the reign of Akbar.
📌 EXPLANATION
Tajika Nilakanthi is a Sanskrit work on astronomy (not astrology). It was composed during Akbar's reign. This is the ONLY astronomical literature from the Mughal period. The question in the original session contained a translation error — "ஜோதிட ஆய்வு" (astrological study) was mistranslated, but it should be "வானவியல் ஆய்வு" (astronomical study). Astronomy deals with celestial bodies (sun, moon, earth movements); astrology deals with fortune-telling.
⚠️ TRANSLATION CORRECTION NOTE
In the live session, the Tamil question incorrectly used "ஜோதிட ஆய்வு" (astrological study) instead of "வானவியல் ஆய்வு" (astronomical study). The teacher explicitly corrected this. Tajika Nilakanthi = Astronomy, NOT Astrology.
💎 LIVE GEM — Literature Awareness for All Kingdoms
Teacher's important advice: Literature questions appear for EVERY kingdom in TNPSC History. Keep a dedicated list for each dynasty:
• Cholas: Pattinappallai
• Cheras: Pathitrupathu
• Gupta: Devi Chandraguptam, Panchasiddhantika
• Mughal: Tajika Nilakanthi (astronomy), Ain-i-Akbari, Akbarnama, Padshahnama
Literature topic = guaranteed marks in every TNPSC exam.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
TNPSC has asked about Mughal period literary works repeatedly. Devi Chandraguptam (Gupta era), Pattinappallai (Chola era), and Tajika Nilakanthi (Mughal) have all appeared as PYQs in Group 2 and Group 4 papers.

Mughal Architecture & Monuments

Q 08
MEDIUM Trick/Trap TNPSC Gr.4/VAO TNPSC Gr.2
Which of the following was NOT built by Akbar?
(a) Buland Darwaza (b) Moti Masjid in Agra Fort (c) Fatehpur Sikri (d) Panch Mahal
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
(b) Moti Masjid in Agra Fort — built by Shah Jahan, NOT Akbar.
(a) Buland Darwaza ✅ Akbar built this
(b) Moti Masjid ❌ Built by Shah Jahan
(c) Fatehpur Sikri ✅ Akbar built this
(d) Panch Mahal ✅ Akbar built this
📌 EXPLANATION
Buland Darwaza: Akbar built this towering gate (53m high) in Fatehpur Sikri to commemorate his victory over Gujarat. "Darwaza" = Gate/Entrance in Persian. "Buland" = High/Lofty. "Fateh" = Victory — Fatehpur Sikri = City of Victory.

Panch Mahal: A 5-storey palace within Fatehpur Sikri, also built by Akbar. It is located in Uttar Pradesh and is architecturally stunning — the teacher recommends visiting if you're free!

Moti Masjid: The Pearl Mosque inside Agra Fort was built by Shah Jahan. There is ALSO a Moti Masjid built by Aurangzeb in Delhi — be careful about which one the question refers to.
🪤 TNPSC TRAP — TWO Moti Masjids!
1. Moti Masjid, Agra Fort → built by Shah Jahan
2. Moti Masjid, Delhi (near Red Fort) → built by Aurangzeb
Questions often specify "Agra Fort" to distinguish. If no location given, default to Shah Jahan's version.
💎 LIVE GEM — Vittala Temple Connection
A student asked about the Vittala Temple (Virupaksha/Vittalaswamy Temple). Teacher clarified:
• Located in Hampi, Karnataka
• Built by Krishnadevaraya of the Vijayanagara Empire
• Famous for its stone chariot and musical pillars (pillars that produce musical notes when struck)
• Not Mughal architecture — completely different kingdom!
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Mughal architecture attribution questions (who built what) are among the most frequently asked TNPSC history questions. Buland Darwaza, Fatehpur Sikri, Taj Mahal attribution have appeared in Group 4 (2015, 2017, 2021) and Group 2 (2018).
Q 09
HARD Trick/Trap TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.1
Consider the following statements:
(1) The Red Fort was constructed by Jahangir.
(2) He constructed it as a palace-fortress and declared it his capital, Jahanabad.
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Answer: Option D — Both statements are INCORRECT.
📌 EXPLANATION
Statement 1 is Wrong: The Red Fort (Lal Qila) was built by Shah Jahan in 1639, NOT Jahangir.

Statement 2 is Wrong: Even if we accept the concept, the city name is correct — Shahjahanabad (not Jahanabad). The old city of Delhi (Old Delhi today) was called Shahjahanabad. Shah Jahan built and named it after himself. This later became what we call "Old Delhi."
[CRITICAL] Red Fort Facts
• Red Fort (Lal Qila) built by: Shah Jahan, 1639
• Location: Delhi (Shahjahanabad / Old Delhi)
• India's PM hoists the national flag here on Independence Day (Aug 15)
• (Note: President hoists flag at Red Fort on Republic Day — this is a common misconception. It is the PM on Independence Day and the President's address on Republic Day from Vijay Chowk.)
💎 LIVE GEM — Shah Jahan's Architectural Legacy
Shah Jahan cared more about architecture than anything else — teacher's memorable explanation:
"After defeating his rivals (including his father's wife Nur Jahan's faction), Shah Jahan focused entirely on building. He built the Taj Mahal as a monument to his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Then he reportedly commissioned a BLACK Taj Mahal for himself on the opposite bank of Yamuna — though it was never completed."

Shah Jahan's key monuments: Taj Mahal, Red Fort (Lal Qila), Jama Masjid (Delhi), Moti Masjid (Agra), Shahjahanabad

Aurangzeb — Succession, Rebellions & Policies

Q 11
HARD Conceptual TNPSC Gr.1 TNPSC Gr.2
Again, who supported Dara Shukoh against Aurangzeb, causing Aurangzeb's resentment — leading to the execution of that supporter? (Connect to Sikh rebellion)
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Answer: Option B — The Sikhs / Guru Tegh Bahadur (9th Sikh Guru) supported Dara Shukoh. Aurangzeb executed Guru Tegh Bahadur, leading to the Sikh Rebellion.
📌 EXPLANATION WITH FULL STORY
Shah Jahan had 4 sons: Dara Shukoh, Shuja, Aurangzeb, and Murad.
Dara Shukoh was the designated heir (eldest, nominated by Shah Jahan) — known as the "Prince of Philosophers."

Aurangzeb was serving as Governor of the Deccan (South India). He rebelled, killed his brothers one by one, imprisoned his father Shah Jahan in Agra Fort, and seized the throne.

The 9th Sikh Guru, Guru Tegh Bahadur, was closely allied with Dara Shukoh. Aurangzeb executed him in 1675 — which triggered the Sikh Rebellion and eventually led Guru Gobind Singh to militarise the Sikh community (founding the Khalsa in 1699).
💎 LIVE GEM — Aurangzeb's Three Major Rebellions in North India
During Aurangzeb's 25-year rule of North India, three major rebellions occurred:
1. Jat Rebellion
2. Sikh Rebellion (triggered by Guru Tegh Bahadur's execution)
3. Satnamis Rebellion (a religious sect near Delhi)

The question in the session asked: Aurangzeb bore resentment against whom because they helped Dara Shukoh?
Answer: Sikhs — linked through Guru Tegh Bahadur.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Aurangzeb's rebellion suppression and the Sikh connection (Guru Tegh Bahadur) has appeared in TNPSC Group 1 (2017), Group 2 (2019), and SSC CGL (2020). Very high-frequency topic.
Q 12
MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.2
Consider the following statements about Aurangzeb:
(1) Wherever Aurangzeb camped, he declared that place his capital.
(2) Aurangzeb suppressed the Satnamis' rebellion with the help of local Hindu zamindars.
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Answer: Option C — Both statements are CORRECT.
📌 EXPLANATION
Statement 1: Aurangzeb was a mobile ruler. He spent much of his later reign in the Deccan (South India) conducting military campaigns. He declared his military camp locations as temporary capitals — this is historically accurate.

Important capital cities during his reign: Agra, Delhi (Shahjahanabad), and even the Deccan region later. He also declared Shahjahanabad (Old Delhi) as a capital alongside the traditional Mughal capitals.

Statement 2: The Satnamis were a religious sect (believers in the "True Name" of God) who revolted near Delhi. Aurangzeb suppressed this revolt. The question correctly states he used local Hindu zamindars — which is also historically corroborated.

Sher Shah Suri — Sur Dynasty (1540–1555)

Q 13
HARD Factual TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.1
What is the original birth name of Sher Khan (founder of Sur/Suri dynasty)? What is his most famous architectural monument?
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
• Original name: Farid (Farid Khan)
• Ethnic group: Afghan (Pashtun / Sur tribe)
• Most famous monument: Mausoleum at Sasaram (his own tomb/mausoleum in Bihar, which he built himself during his lifetime)
• Sur Dynasty period: 1540–1555 AD
📌 EXPLANATION
Sher Khan earned his title by killing a tiger (Sher = Lion/Tiger) during a hunt. His birth name was Farid. He was an Afghan (Pashtun) leader from the Sur tribe, hence the dynasty name "Sur Dynasty."

He defeated Humayun (Akbar's father) at the Battles of Chausa (1539) and Kanauj/Bilgram (1540), forcing Humayun into exile for 15 years.

His mausoleum at Sasaram, Bihar is an architectural masterpiece — built on an island surrounded by water, with breathtaking symmetry. He designed it himself while alive.
💎 LIVE GEM — Sher Shah's Reforms (Exam-Critical)
Sher Shah Suri's most exam-relevant contributions:
1. Grand Trunk Road (Sadak-e-Azam) — from Bengal to Kabul
2. Roadside sarais (rest houses) — built for travellers along roads
3. Land measurement & revenue reform — Zabti/Bandobast system
4. Coins — introduced the Rupee (silver coin)
5. Postal system (Dak Chowki)
6. Famous quote: "A farmer who suffers will make the king suffer too" — he believed in protecting farmers

Teacher's tip: If a question asks about ANY reform system (revenue, roads, coins), think of Sher Shah Suri as the originator — Akbar later reformed these further.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Sher Shah Suri's original name "Farid", his Afghan origin, Sur Dynasty dates (1540-1555), and the Sasaram mausoleum have appeared in TNPSC Group 2 (2016, 2022) and SSC CGL (2019, 2023) papers.
Q 14
HARD Conceptual TNPSC Gr.1
Jagannadha Pandit (Jagannatha Pandit) was a court poet of Jahangir. His memorial work was "Racha Gangadhara." Evaluate this statement.
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Answer: Both statements are CORRECT — and he also served in Shah Jahan's court.
Racha Gangadhara (Rasa Gangadhara) was indeed written by Jagannatha Pandit.
📌 EXPLANATION
Jagannatha Pandit was a renowned Sanskrit scholar who served in the courts of both Jahangir and Shah Jahan. His work Rasa Gangadhara is a landmark treatise on Sanskrit poetics/aesthetics. He also wrote Gangalahari (Waves of the Ganges), a devotional poem. The fact that he served TWO Mughal emperors makes this a "both correct" assertion question.

Indian National Movement (INM)

Q 15
EASY Factual TNPSC Gr.4/VAO TNPSC Gr.2
Gandhi boycotted which Round Table Conference? Which one did he attend?
▼
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
• Gandhi did NOT attend the 1st Round Table Conference (1930) — because the Civil Disobedience Movement (Salt Satyagraha / Dandi March) was active.
• Gandhi attended the 2nd Round Table Conference (1931) — after the Gandhi-Irwin Pact.
• Gandhi did not attend the 3rd Round Table Conference (1932) — as he was imprisoned.
📌 EXPLANATION
The Round Table Conferences (London) were held to discuss constitutional reforms for India:
• 1st RTC: 1930 — Gandhi absent (Civil Disobedience Movement)
• 2nd RTC: 1931 — Gandhi attended (after Gandhi-Irwin Pact, March 1931)
• 3rd RTC: 1932 — Gandhi absent (imprisoned, Communal Award controversy)
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Gandhi's Round Table Conference attendance is a very high frequency PYQ. "Gandhi attended which RTC?" — Answer: 2nd RTC (1931). This has appeared in TNPSC Group 4 (2015, 2019, 2023) and SSC CHSL (2020).

🏠 HOMEWORK: Where is the village of Chauri Chaura located? (State) — Why is it historically important?
▼
✅ ANSWER (Teacher's homework — complete answer provided)
Chauri Chaura is located in Uttar Pradesh (in Gorakhpur district).
📌 WHY IT MATTERS
On February 5, 1922, a violent clash occurred at Chauri Chaura where a mob set fire to a police station, killing 22 policemen. This shocked Gandhi and led him to abruptly suspend the Non-Cooperation Movement on February 12, 1922. This is a critical event in the INM — the incident that ended the first major mass movement against British rule.
[CRITICAL] Chauri Chaura = Uttar Pradesh
Options typically include Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Gujarat, Maharashtra. Answer is Uttar Pradesh. Teacher set this as homework to reinforce location-based history questions.
