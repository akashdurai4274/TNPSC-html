Indian History · Unit 3
Q 01
Medium Trap / Matching
Which of the following is an INCORRECT pair? (Cave temple site ↔ Dynasty/Period)
A) Ellora — Rashtrakutas   B) Mahabalipuram — Narashimhavarman I   C) Elephanta Caves — Ashoka   D) Pattadakal — Chalukyas
பின்வருவனவற்றில் தவறான இணை எது? (குகைக் கோயில் ↔ வம்சம்/காலம்)
அ) எல்லோரா — ராஷ்டிரகூடர்கள்   ஆ) மாமல்லபுரம் — முதலாம் நரசிம்மவர்மன்   இ) எலிபண்டா குகைகள் — அசோகர்   ஈ) பட்டடக்கல் — சாளுக்கியர்கள்
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option C — Elephanta Caves ↔ Ashoka is INCORRECT.
Elephanta Caves were built during the Rashtrakuta dynasty period, NOT during Ashoka's reign.
📌 Explanation — All Four Pairs Ellora Caves → Rashtrakuta dynasty ✅
Mahabalipuram (Mamallapuram) → Pallava king Narasimhavarman I (also called Mamallan — town named after him) ✅
Elephanta Caves → Rashtrakuta period (NOT Ashoka) ❌ — This is the trap!
Pattadakal → Chalukya (Salukkiyas) period ✅ — Famous Ramayana scenes carved in temples here.
💎 Live Gem — Elephanta Caves Origin Story Elephanta Caves' original local name was Śrīpurī. The Portuguese renamed it "Elephanta" because the island (located between Mumbai and Navi Mumbai in the sea) had a rock in the shape of a lying elephant. The Portuguese used the caves for rifle/gun training practice. Inside the caves there is a famous Trimurti (three-faced) Shiva sculpture. The Portuguese named it; the Rashtrakutas built it.
💎 Live Gem — Pattadakal Temple Detail One of the temples at Pattadakal (Badami Chalukya capital) has the entire story and characters of the Ramayana carved as sculptures. This specific fact was asked in a TNPSC Group 1 exam.
🏆 PYQ Alert TNPSC Group 1 (Previous Year): "Which temple at Pattadakal contains Ramayana sculptures carved on its walls?" — Rashtrakuta/Chalukya temple at Pattadakal. The cave-dynasty matching is a repeated TNPSC question type.
🚨 Critical Trap Both Ellora AND Elephanta belong to the Rashtrakuta period. Students often wrongly assign Elephanta to Ashoka or Maurya era. Ashoka built stupas and pillars — not cave shrines of this type.

Q 02
Medium Factual
Which Vedic (Aryan) deity served BOTH as the God of War AND the God of Weather / Climate?
A) Indra   B) Varuna   C) Vayu   D) Rudra
ஆரிய கடவுள்களில் போர்க்கடவுள் மற்றும் காலநிலை கடவுள் என்ற இரண்டு பணிகளையும் செய்த கடவுள் யார்?
அ) இந்திரன்   ஆ) வருணன்   இ) வாயு   ஈ) ருத்திரன்
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option A — Indra (இந்திரன்) served as both the God of War and the God of Weather/Climate.
📌 Explanation In the Vedic age, Indra was the most prominent deity:
• God of War — led armies of gods against demons
• God of Weather/Climate — controlled rain, thunder, storms
• Also called "Purandhara" (Destroyer of Cities / Fortresses) — because he destroyed the mountain fortresses of enemies, which in turn flooded cities.

A previous TNPSC Group 4 exam asked: "Who is called the Destroyer of Cities (Purandhara)?" — Answer: Indra.
🚨 Critical — Why Not Rudra? Rudra is wrong. Rudra in Vedic age is associated with storms and destruction, but is NOT described as the war god. The clear dual-role deity is Indra. Rudra later evolved into Shiva in the Puranic tradition.
🏆 PYQ Alert TNPSC Group 4 (Previous Year): "Who is called the Purandhara (Destroyer of Cities)?" — Answer: Indra. This topic appeared in TNPSC and is confirmed to have surprised many students in the exam hall.
💎 Live Gem — 9th Mandala of Rig Veda The teacher noted: "In SP Academy classes on the Vedic Age, we covered which deity is described in the 9th Mandala (book) of the Rig Veda." The 9th Mandala is dedicated to Soma — the ritual drink deity. This is a frequently asked TNPSC fine-detail question.

Q 05
Hard Conceptual
After defeating Harshavardhana, which title did Pulakesi II (2nd Pulakesi) assume?
A) Sathyashraya   B) Sri Prithvi Vallabha   C) Maharajadhiraja   D) Paramesvara
ஹர்ஷவர்தனனை தோற்கடித்த பிறகு இரண்டாம் புலிகேசி எந்த பட்டத்தை சூட்டிக்கொண்டார்?
அ) சத்தியஸ்ரயன்   ஆ) ஸ்ரீ பிரித்வி வல்லவன்   இ) மகாராஜாதிராஜன்   ஈ) பரமேஸ்வரன்
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option D — Paramesvara (பரமேஸ்வரன்). After defeating Harshavardhana, Pulakesi II took the title "Paramesvara."
📌 Full Context — Who was Pulakesi II? Pulakesi II (also known as Sathyashraya — meaning "one who speaks only truth") was the greatest ruler of the Chalukya (Salukkiya) dynasty. The Aihole Inscription (ஐகோல் கல்வெட்டு) provides details about his reign and achievements, including his war against Harshavardhana of the Vardhana dynasty.

Battle site: The war was fought on the banks of the Narmada River (நர்மதை நதிக்கரை).
After defeating Harsha, Pulakesi II claimed the title Paramesvara.
💎 Live Gem — Historical Contradiction in Sources The teacher revealed a fascinating historiographical problem: "The Aihole Inscription says Pulakesi II defeated Harsha. But if you read Harsha's own court records (Banskhera plates / Karawal inscription), they say Harsha defeated Pulakesi II!" This is a real contradiction in ancient Indian history — both rulers claimed victory. Teacher's conclusion: "We have no choice but to accept what is written. That is the nature of ancient history."
💎 Live Gem — Banabhatta Connection Banabhatta (பானபட்டா) was the court poet of Harshavardhana and wrote the famous biography Harshacharita. The popular Tamil comedy film "23rd Pulikesi" is a parody based on Pulakesi II's story, and the character "Banabhatta Uganandi" in that film is inspired by the real Banabhatta of Harsha's court.
🏆 PYQ Alert Previous TNPSC exams have asked: "On the banks of which river did Pulakesi II defeat Harshavardhana?" — Narmada River.

Q 07
Easy Trap — Textbook vs Exam
The largest number of Harappan (Indus Valley) Civilization seals were made of which material?
A) Terracotta (Burnt Clay)   B) Bronze   C) Copper   D) Steatite (Soapstone)
ஹரப்பா நாகரிகத்தின் மிகப்பெரிய எண்ணிக்கையிலான முத்திரைகள் எதனால் உருவாக்கப்பட்டன?
அ) டெர்ரகோட்டா (சுடுமண்)   ஆ) வெண்கலம்   இ) செம்பு   ஈ) ஸ்டீடைட் (சாம்பான் கல்)
▼
✅ Correct Answer (TNPSC Official)
Option A — Terracotta (சுடுமண் / Burnt Clay) — as per TNPSC's official question paper.
🪤 TNPSC TRAP — Textbook vs Exam Paper The Tamil Nadu school textbook says the seals were made of Steatite (ஸ்டீடைட்). However, in an official TNPSC examination paper, the answer given was Terracotta (சுடுமண்). This discrepancy has confused many students.

Exam strategy: Follow the TNPSC official answer paper — answer Terracotta. The famous Pashupati Seal (with three-headed figure surrounded by animals) and many other seals were indeed found in terracotta.
💎 Live Gem — Pashupati Seal Description The most famous Harappan seal is the Pashupati Seal — it shows a figure with three faces, two horns, seated in a yoga posture, surrounded by animals (elephant, tiger, rhinoceros, buffalo). This is believed to be a proto-Shiva figure. This specific seal's description is asked in exams.
🏆 PYQ Alert TNPSC Group 4 exam directly asked this question — "Large number of Harappan seals were made of?" and the official answer key marked Terracotta.

Q 10
Medium Factual / TN Admin History
The Staff Selection Board (forerunner of TNPSC) was established in Tamil Nadu under the Pankalam Cabinet. In which year was it established?
A) 1916   B) 1920   C) 1924   D) 1927
பணகல் அமைச்சரவையால் பணியாள தேர்வு வாரியம் (TNPSC முன்னோடி) எந்த ஆண்டு தோற்றுவிக்கப்பட்டது?
அ) 1916   ஆ) 1920   இ) 1924   ஈ) 1927
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option C — 1924. The Staff Selection Board was established in 1924 during the Pankalam Raja government.
📌 Full TNPSC History Timeline 1924: Pankalam Raja's cabinet → established Staff Selection Board to recruit government employees.
1929: Renamed to Public Service Commission (பொதுப்பணியாளர் தீர்வாணையம்).
After Tamil Nadu got its name: Renamed to Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission (TNPSC).

🔑 Key Fact: TNPSC is the FIRST public service commission established in India — even older than UPSC! This is a critical exam fact.
💎 Live Gem — TNPSC is Older Than UPSC The teacher emphasized: "We are all studying for TNPSC — and TNPSC itself has a great history. TNPSC (in its original form as Staff Selection Board) was formed in 1924 — it is the FIRST Public Service Commission in India. UPSC came later! This very board that tests you is older than UPSC."
🏆 PYQ Alert This question appeared directly in TNPSC examinations. "In which year was the Tamil Nadu Staff Selection Board established?" — 1924.

Q 11
Medium Trap — Date Confusion
Which of the following is NOT correct regarding the Poona Pact and the Second Round Table Conference?
A) Poona Pact was signed in 1932   B) Second Round Table Conference was held in 1931   C) Indian Government Act — 1935   D) [Implied: check the year pairings]
பூனா ஒப்பந்தம் மற்றும் இரண்டாம் வட்ட மேசை மாநாடு பற்றிய கூற்றுகளில் தவறானது எது?
▼
✅ Correct Answer — The NOT correct pair
Option A — "Poona Pact was signed in 1932" is CORRECT → So the pair with a wrong year for 2nd Round Table Conference is the trap.

The question had TWO incorrect items in a tricky format. The answer option with BOTH errors is Option A as presented in the question context.
📌 Key Facts — Poona Pact • Poona Pact: September 24, 1932 — signed between Dr. B.R. Ambedkar and Mahatma Gandhi.
• Background: Ambedkar demanded separate electorates (தனித்தகுதி வாக்காளர்) for Untouchables (Scheduled Castes). Gandhi opposed this and went on a fast unto death.
• Result: The Poona Pact gave reserved seats (not separate electorates) for SCs in the general electorate.
• Second Round Table Conference: 1931 (November, in London).
🪤 TNPSC TRAP — Year Mixing Exams frequently swap the years 1931 and 1932 between the Round Table Conferences and the Poona Pact. Remember:
• 2nd RTС = 1931 (Gandhi attended)
• Poona Pact = 1932 (Sept 24)
The question format "which is NOT correct" combined with two errors in the same option is a classic TNPSC trap.

Q 12
Easy Factual
Who was elected as the President of the Indian National Congress in 1938?
A) Rajaji   B) Kamarajar   C) Jawaharlal Nehru   D) Subhash Chandra Bose
1938ஆம் ஆண்டு இந்திய தேசிய காங்கிரஸின் தலைவராக யார் தேர்ந்தெடுக்கப்பட்டார்?
அ) ராஜாஜி   ஆ) காமராஜர்   இ) ஜவஹர்லால் நேரு   ஈ) சுபாஷ் சந்திர போஸ்
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option D — Subhash Chandra Bose (சுபாஷ் சந்திர போஸ் / நேதாஜி).
📌 The Haripura Congress 1938 — Full Story • Haripura Congress session (1938) — Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose was elected INC President.
• In 1939, he was re-elected defeating Gandhi's candidate (Pattabhi Sitaramayya) — the only instance of a Gandhi-backed candidate losing.
• But Bose's ideology clashed with Gandhi's — Bose wanted armed struggle ("Freedom is not begged, it is won by fighting").
• He resigned, formed the Forward Bloc, and eventually created the Indian National Army (INA / Azad Hind Fauj).
🚨 Critical Memory Point 1938 = Haripura = Netaji. Do NOT confuse with Lahore 1929 (Nehru), Tripuri 1939 (Bose re-elected). The question targets confusion between Rajaji and Netaji — Rajaji was never INC President.

Q 13
Hard Application — Chronological
Arrange the following events in correct chronological order:
1. Chauri Chaura Incident   2. Russo-Japanese War   3. Gandhi's Return from South Africa   4. [Question in session format]
Which sequence is correct? A) 1-2-3   B) 2-3-1   C) 3-2-1   D) 2-3-1
பின்வரும் நிகழ்வுகளை காலவரிசைப்படி சரியான வரிசையில் அமைக்கவும்:
1. சவுரி சவுரா சம்பவம்   2. ரஷ்ய-ஜப்பான் போர்   3. காந்தியின் தென் ஆப்பிரிக்காவிலிருந்து திரும்புதல்
▼
✅ Correct Answer
The correct chronological order is: 2 → 3 → 1
i.e. Russo-Japanese War (1904) → Gandhi Returns from South Africa (1915) → Chauri Chaura Incident (1922).

Answer Option: D — 2, 3, 1
📌 All Three Events with Years 1. Russo-Japanese War — 1904: Japan (Asian nation) defeated Russia (European superpower) — this shocked and inspired Indian nationalists and extremists who saw that Asians could defeat European powers.

2. Gandhi's Return from South Africa — January 9, 1915: This date is celebrated as Pravasi Bharatiya Divas (Non-Resident Indians Day / வெளிநாட்டு இந்தியர் தினம்). Gandhi returned and began his Indian freedom movement.

3. Chauri Chaura Incident — February 4, 1922: A mob of protesters set fire to a police station in Chauri Chaura, UP, killing 22 policemen. Gandhi suspended the Non-Cooperation Movement because of this violence.
💎 Live Gem — Russo-Japanese War's Impact on India The teacher noted: "The Russo-Japanese War of 1904 did not happen in India — but its impact on India was massive. When radical nationalists like Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Lal Lajpat Rai saw that an Asian nation (Japan) could defeat a European power (Russia), they were inspired. This became a fuel for the Extremist wing of Indian nationalism."
💎 Live Gem — January 9, 1915 Gandhi arrived in India on January 9, 1915 from South Africa. This date is now officially observed as Pravasi Bharatiya Divas (Non-Resident Indian Day). TNPSC has asked: "Which day is celebrated as Pravasi Bharatiya Divas?" — January 9.

Constitution of India · Unit 4
Q 03
Medium Conceptual
Which Article of the Constitution states that all Ministers (Council of Ministers) are appointed by the President on the advice of the Prime Minister?
A) 77   B) 78   C) 75   D) 74
அனைத்து அமைச்சர்களும் பிரதமரின் ஆலோசனையின் பேரில் குடியரசு தலைவரால் நியமிக்கப்படுகின்றனர் என்பதை எந்த விதி கூறுகிறது?
அ) 77   ஆ) 78   இ) 75   ஈ) 74
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option C — Article 75. The President appoints the Prime Minister and, on the PM's advice, appoints other ministers. The PM takes oath before the President.
📌 Articles 74 & 75 — Two Critical Articles Article 74: Council of Ministers with Prime Minister as head shall aid and advise the President. The President works under the advice of the Council of Ministers + PM.

Article 75: Deals with appointment of ministers. President appoints PM; on PM's advice, President appoints other ministers. PM takes oath before President.

🔑 Shortcut: "75 + 89 → State Government equivalent = Articles 163 + 164"
(Article 163 = Governor's Council of Ministers; Article 164 = State Ministers' appointment)
💎 Live Gem — Shortcut for State Equivalent The teacher gave a brilliant shortcut: "At the central level, Articles 74 and 75 govern the PM-President relationship. At the STATE level, the same roles are played by Articles 163 (Governor's advisory body) and 164 (State ministers' appointment by Governor). Simply remember: 74→163, 75→164. If a question asks about Tamil Nadu's Chief Minister taking oath — it's the Governor under Article 164."
🏆 PYQ Alert TNPSC — Repeated Question: Articles 74 and 75 are two of the most frequently tested polity articles in all TNPSC exams. Article 75 specifically about PM's appointment and ministers is a staple question.

Q 04
Medium Trap — 543 vs 550
How many members are elected to the Lok Sabha from the Union Territory of Delhi?
A) 3   B) 5   C) 6   D) 7
ஒன்றிய பிரதேசமான டெல்லியில் இருந்து மக்களவைக்கு எத்தனை உறுப்பினர்கள் தேர்ந்தெடுக்கப்படுகின்றனர்?
அ) 3   ஆ) 5   இ) 6   ஈ) 7
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option D — 7 seats. Delhi sends 7 members to the Lok Sabha.
📌 Lok Sabha Seat Structure — Full Concept Total Lok Sabha seats: 550 (maximum) — NOT 543!

How 550 breaks down:
• 543 = seats filled through election (currently in use)
• 2 = Anglo-Indian seats (nominated by President) — ABOLISHED by the 104th Constitutional Amendment Act
• So maximum is 550, but currently only 543 are elected.

Tamil Nadu: 39 Lok Sabha seats
Delhi (UT): 7 Lok Sabha seats

Only 2 Union Territories have Lok Sabha representation:
1. Puducherry (Pondicherry) — 1 seat
2. Delhi — 7 seats
🚨 Critical Trap — 543 vs 550 The most common wrong answer students give here is 543. This is wrong because:
• 543 = currently elected seats (out of maximum 550)
• 550 = constitutional maximum (post-104th Amendment removing Anglo-Indian seats)
• If the question asks "total seats / maximum seats" → answer 550
• If the question asks "currently elected seats" → answer 543
💎 Live Gem — 104th Constitutional Amendment The 104th Constitutional Amendment Act abolished the reservation of 2 seats for Anglo-Indians in the Lok Sabha and state legislatures. This was passed in 2019. Before this amendment, the President could nominate 2 Anglo-Indian members. Now those 2 seats are gone — reducing the elected maximum from 552 to 550.
🏆 PYQ Alert TNPSC frequently asks about Lok Sabha seat distribution. "How many seats does Tamil Nadu have in Lok Sabha?" (39), "How many total seats?" (550 max / 543 elected) are both commonly tested.

Which Schedule of the Indian Constitution deals with Languages?

இந்திய அரசியலமைப்பின் எந்த அட்டவணை மொழிகளைப் பற்றி பேசுகிறது?
அ) 4வது அட்டவணை   ஆ) 6வது அட்டவணை   இ) 8வது அட்டவணை   ஈ) 11வது அட்டவணை
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Option C — 8th Schedule. The 8th Schedule lists the 22 official languages of India.
📌 All Four Schedules Discussed
Schedule Subject

🏆 PYQ Alert Schedules of the Constitution is a high-frequency topic in all TNPSC levels. The 8th Schedule's language list and the 11th/12th Schedule (Panchayati Raj) are most commonly asked.

Modern India · Indian History Unit 3
Q 08
Medium Factual
Who called Muhammad Ali Jinnah the "Ambassador of Hindu-Muslim Unity"?
A) Mahatma Gandhi   B) Muhammad Ali Jinnah   C) Jawaharlal Nehru   D) Annie Besant
முகமது அலி ஜின்னாவை "இந்து முஸ்லிம் ஒற்றுமையின் தூதுவர்" என்று அழைத்தவர் யார்?
அ) மகாத்மா காந்தி   ஆ) முகமது அலி ஜின்னா   இ) ஜவஹர்லால் நேரு   ஈ) அன்னி பெசன்ட்
▼
✅ Correct Answer

Q 14
Hard Factual — TN History
[Homework Question] Which Pandya king brought an end to the Kalabhra rule and revived the Pandya dynasty?
A) Kadungon Pandiyan   B) Veerabandiyan   C) Koon Pandiyan   D) Varakunan
[வீட்டுப்பாடம்] கலபரர் ஆட்சியை முடிவுக்கு கொண்டு வந்து பாண்டிய வம்சத்தை மறுபடியும் உருவாக்கியவர் எந்த பாண்டிய அரசன்?
அ) கடுங்கோன் பாண்டியன்   ஆ) வீரபாண்டியன்   இ) கூன் பாண்டியன்   ஈ) வரகுணன்
▼
✅ Correct Answer
Kadungon Pandiyan (கடுங்கோன் பாண்டியன்) ended the Kalabhra interregnum and revived the Pandya dynasty.
📌 Kalabhra Dynasty — Key Facts • Kalabhra Period: Approximately 300 CE to 600 CE (about 300 years of rule).
• The Kalbhras disrupted the rule of the three Tamil kingdoms (Chera, Chola, Pandya) — this period is sometimes called the "Dark Age" of Tamil history.
• The Kalbhras were later overthrown by Kadungon Pandiyan from the south and Simhavishnu (Pallava) from the north.
• After Kadungon Pandiyan, the Pandya dynasty was revived.
💎 Live Gem — Kalabhra "Dark Age" Concept The teacher gave this as a homework question and asked students to post the answer in the comments. The Kalabhra period (300-600 CE) was the only phase where the classical Tamil three-dynasty rule was interrupted for about 300 years. This "Kalabhra Interregnum" is a unique Tamil Nadu History topic that only appears in TNPSC Unit 6 and is frequently asked.

📋 Session Topic Summary — 10 Key Takeaways
  • Cave Temple Dynasties: Ellora = Rashtrakuta; Elephanta = Rashtrakuta (NOT Ashoka); Mahabalipuram = Pallava; Pattadakal = Chalukya
  • Vedic Indra: God of War + God of Weather/Climate; also called "Purandhara" (Destroyer of Cities/Fortresses)
  • Pulakesi II: Greatest Chalukya ruler; defeated Harsha on Narmada River banks; took title "Paramesvara"; Aihole inscription is key source
  • Harappan Seals: TNPSC official answer = Terracotta (though textbook says Steatite); Pashupati Seal has three faces surrounded by animals
  • Articles 74 & 75: 74 = President acts on advice of Council of Ministers+PM; 75 = PM appointed by President, other ministers on PM's advice. State equivalents: 163 & 164
  • Lok Sabha Seats: Maximum = 550; Currently elected = 543; 2 Anglo-Indian seats abolished by 104th Amendment; Delhi UT = 7 seats; TN = 39 seats
  • 8th Schedule = 22 Official Languages (originally 14); 4th = Rajya Sabha seats; 6th = Tribal areas (ATMM); 11th = Panchayati Raj
  • Hindu-Muslim Unity Ambassador: Sarojini Naidu called Jinnah this title, referencing his role as Chief Architect of the 1916 Lucknow Pact
  • TNPSC History: Staff Selection Board established in 1924 under Pankalam Raja; became Public Service Commission in 1929; FIRST public service commission in India
  • Chronological Order: Russo-Japanese War (1904) → Gandhi returns from S. Africa (1915, January 9 = Pravasi Bharatiya Divas) → Chauri Chaura (1922)
