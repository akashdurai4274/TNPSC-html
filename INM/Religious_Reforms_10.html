1. Early Reform Movements in Bengal

Reformer Organisation / Movement Key Milestones & Dates
Raja Rammohan Roy Brahmo Samaj (Aug 20, 1828) Anti-sati campaign led to its abolition by Lord William Bentinck in 1829.
Maharishi Debendranath Tagore Adi Brahmo Samaj Took over after Roy's death in 1833; laid down 4 core articles of faith.
Keshab Chandra Sen Brahmo Samaj of India Joined 1857, split from Tagore in 1866.
Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar Widows' Remarriage Reform Pioneered the Widows’ Remarriage Reform Act of 1856 and first Age of Consent law in 1860.
[CRITICAL] The Brahmo Samaj Splits A major split occurred in 1866, leading Keshab Chandra Sen to found the Brahmo Samaj of India, while Debendranath Tagore's group became the Adi Brahmo Samaj.
[TRAP] The Sadharan Samaj Genesis Do not confuse the Brahmo Samaj of India with the Sadharan Samaj. The Sadharan Samaj was started specifically by opponents of child marriage after Keshab Chandra Sen married off his own fourteen-year-old daughter to an Indian prince, violating his own society's rules.
[PYQ] Age of Consent Evolution Vidyasagar's efforts ensured the first age of consent was included in the 1860 Indian Penal Code, fixing the marriage age at 10 years. It was later raised to 12 in 1891 and 13 in 1925.

2. Maharashtra, Hindu Revivalism & Anti-Caste Movements

💎 [GEM] Reformist vs. Revivalist Categorization For Mains mapping: Reformist movements include Brahmo Samaj, Prarthana Samaj, and Aligarh Movement. Revivalist movements include Arya Samaj, Ramakrishna Mission, and Deoband Movement.
Movement Founder / Leader Key Actions / Publications
Prarthana Samaj (1867, Bombay) Dr. Atma Ram Pandurang Joined by M.G. Ranade (founded Widow Marriage Association 1861, Poona Sarvajanik Sabha 1870, Deccan Ed Soc 1884).
Arya Samaj (1875, Punjab) Swami Dayanand Saraswati Wrote Satyarthaprakash; slogan "Go back to Vedas"; launched Suddhi counter-conversion movement.
Ramakrishna Mission (1897) Swami Vivekananda Based on Ramakrishna Paramahamsa's teaching "Jiva is Siva" (all living beings are God); active in disaster relief.
Theosophical Society (1875, USA) Blavatsky & Olcott Shifted to Adyar (Chennai) in 1886; Annie Besant launched New India and Commonweal.
Satyashodak Samaj Jyotiba Phule Maharashtra anti-caste leader; opened first untouchable school in Poona (1852); authored Gulamgiri (Slavery).
SNDP Yogam Narayana Guru Kerala anti-caste movement; established an inclusive temple at Aruvipuram.
[TIP] Quick Recall for Kerala Leaders Narayana Guru = Aruvipuram Temple & SNDP. Ayyankali (inspired by Narayana Guru) = Venganoor, founded Sadhu Jana Paripalana Sangam (1907) for basic rights and school access.

3. Social Reformers of Tamil Nadu

[HIGH YIELD AREA FOR TNPSC UNIT 8 & 9]

A. Ramalinga Swamigal (Vallalar) (1823–1874) Born in Marudhur near Chidambaram.
  • 1865: Established the Samarasa Vedha Sanmarga Sangam (later renamed Samarasa Suddha Sanmarga Satya Sanga).
  • 1867: Opened a free feeding house at Vadalur for all castes during the terrible 1866 famine.
  • Core Concept: Jeevakarunya (compassion for all living beings, including plants).
  • Literature: His voluminous songs were compiled as Thiruvarutpa (Songs of Grace).
B. Pandithar Iyothee Thassar (1845–1914) Radical Tamil scholar, Siddha medicine practitioner, and pioneer of the Dalit-Buddhist movement.
  • 1882: Established the Dravida Kazhagam with John Rathinam.
  • 1885: Launched the magazine Dravida Pandian.
  • 1891: Founded the Dravida Mahajana Sabha (first conference held in Nilgiris).
  • 1898: Influenced by H.S. Olcott, converted to Buddhism in Sri Lanka and founded the Sakya Buddhist Society in Madras.
  • 1907: Started the crucial weekly journal Oru Paisa Tamilan, publishing it until his death in 1914.
  • Other Org: Founded the Advaidananda Sabha to demand temple entry for "untouchables".

4. Islamic, Parsi, and Sikh Movements

Community Key Movement/Leader Characteristics & Data Points
Islamic Aligarh Movement Led by Sir Sayyid Ahmed Khan (reformist). Founded Aligarh Mohammedan Anglo-Oriental college in 1875 (became Univ in 1920). Advocated western science.
Islamic Deoband Movement Revivalist orthodox movement led by Ulema (Qasim Wanotavi & Rashid Gangotri) in 1866; opposed English education.
Parsi Rahnumai Mazdayasnan Sabha Founded by Furdunji Naoroji in 1851 in Mumbai. Main voice was the journal Rast Goftar (The Truth Teller).
Sikh Nirankari Movement Founded by Baba Dayal Das. Stressed formless (Nirankar) worship; rejected idols.
Sikh Namdhari Movement Founded by Baba Ram Singh. Followers carried a lathi instead of a kirpan. Prohibited dowry and child marriage.
Sikh Singh Sabha Established to restore Sikh purity against Christian & Arya Samaj influence; forerunner of the Akali Movement.

5. Active Recall (Book-Back Q&A Data)

In which year was Sati officially abolished?
1829. Led by the campaign of Raja Rammohan Roy and enacted by Governor-General William Bentinck.
Who was the founder of the Poona Sarvajanik Sabha and Widow Marriage Association?
Mahadev Govind Ranade (M.G. Ranade). He founded the Widow Marriage Association in 1861 and Poona Sarvajanik Sabha in 1870.
Identify the authors of 'Satyarthaprakash' and 'Gulamgiri'.
Satyarthaprakash: Swami Dayanand Saraswati (Arya Samaj).
Gulamgiri: Jyotiba Phule (Satyashodak Samaj).
Match the literary works/journals: Oru Paisa Tamilan, Thiruvarutpa, Rast Goftar.
Oru Paisa Tamilan: A weekly journal started by Pandithar Iyothee Thassar in 1907.
Thiruvarutpa: "Songs of Grace", authored by Ramalinga Swamigal.
Rast Goftar: "The Truth Teller", the main voice of the Parsi reform movement.
Who established the Sadhu Jana Paripalana Sangam and in what year?
Ayyankali established it in 1907.
