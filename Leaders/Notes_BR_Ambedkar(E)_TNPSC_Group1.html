SECTION 03

Core Sub-Topic Structural Breakdown

MODULE 01 Early Life, Institutional Barriers & Educational Milestones

Born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar was the 14th and final child of Ramji Sakpal, a military official holding the rank of Subedar. Facing systemic discrimination that forced him to sit apart on a gunny sack at school, he broke barriers by becoming the first student from the Mahar community to pass the 10th standard at Mumbai's Elphinstone High School in 1897. In 1906, he married nine-year-old Ramabai before pursuing economics and political science at Elphinstone College in 1912. Sponsored by the financial patronage of the Maharaja of Baroda (Sayajirao Gaekwad III), he traveled to the United States to earn postgraduate credentials at Columbia University.

[PYQ ALERT] 🏆 The 1916 Anthropological Breakthrough

On May 9, 1916, Ambedkar presented his seminal research paper titled "Castes in India: Their Mechanism, Genesis and Development" at an anthropology seminar at Columbia University. This remains a highly targeted timeline in advanced TNPSC history and polity papers.

[MEMORY TRICK] Chronological Academic Trajectory

Remember the institutional sequence of his major research papers:
• 1916: Castes in India (Columbia University research presentation)
• 1923: The Problem of the Rupee: Its Origin and Its Solution (Doctoral thesis mapping the foundational principles of the RBI).

MODULE 02 Social Mobilizations, Civil Rights & Institutional Sabhas

Returning to India, Ambedkar faced ongoing discrimination, including being denied access to common drinking water jars by office peons. In response, he founded the Bahishkrit Hitakarini Sabha (Depressed Classes Welfare Association) in 1924 to champion the rights of marginalized communities. The organization adopted a clear three-word motto: "Educate, Agitate, Organize" (கற்பி, ஒன்று சேர், புரட்சி செய்).

[CRITICAL FACT] The Mahad Satyagraha Milestone

On March 20, 1927, Ambedkar led the historic Mahad Satyagraha in Maharashtra to assert the right of oppressed communities to access water from the public Chander Tank. This targeted intervention challenged the practice of untouchability at its core.

[CONNECT] Ideological Parity: Ambedkar & Periyar

Ambedkar's work closely mirrors that of Thanthai Periyar in Tamil Nadu. Both prioritized structural Social Justice (சமூகநீதி) over political independence, arguing that internal equality and the eradication of caste discrimination were essential prerequisites for true national freedom. On December 25, 1927, Ambedkar publicly burned the Manusmriti, an act that aligned closely with the anti-untouchability temple entry movements led by Periyar across South India, such as the 1930 Kalaram Temple Entry Movement in Nasik.

MODULE 03 The Roundtable Conferences, Communal Award & Poona Pact Matrix

Ambedkar was one of the few leaders to attend all three Roundtable Conferences (வட்டமேசை மாநாடு) in London (1930, 1931, 1932), chaired by British Prime Minister Ramsay MacDonald. He consistently advocated for Separate Electorates (தனி தொகுதிகள்) for marginalized communities, building on the case he had previously made to the Simon Commission in 1927. This position led to disagreements with Mahatma Gandhi during the Second Conference in 1931, as Gandhi argued that separate electorates would permanently divide Hindu society.

[PYQ ALERT] 🏆 The Poona Pact Compromise

Following the announcement of the Communal Award on August 16, 1932, Gandhi began a fast unto death at Yerwada Jail. To preserve political unity, Ambedkar signed the historic Poona Pact on September 24, 1932, replacing separate electorates with reserved seats within the general electorate.

[TNPSC TRAP] Signatories of the Poona Pact

Wrong Assumption: Believing that Mahatma Gandhi personally signed the Poona Pact document.
Correct Historical Fact: While negotiated to end Gandhi's fast, the document was formally signed by Madan Mohan Malaviya on behalf of caste Hindus, alongside Ambedkar, as Gandhi remained imprisoned without official status.

MODULE 04 Labor Reforms, Constitutional Governance & Resignation Context

Ambedkar founded the Independent Labour Party in 1936, which was later reorganized into the Scheduled Castes Federation in 1942. Serving as the Labor Member of the Viceroy's Executive Council from 1942 to 1946, he introduced landmark labor protections, including reducing standard working hours from 12-14 down to a statutory 8-hour workday, and establishing the framework for the Employees' Provident Fund (EPF) and national Employment Exchanges.

As independent India's first Law Minister and Chairman of the Drafting Committee, he coordinated the evaluation of over 60 constitutions to shape the founding principles of the republic. He also introduced progressive gender equality measures, such as the Maternity Benefit Act.

[CRITICAL FACT] The 1951 Cabinet Resignation

On September 27, 1951, Ambedkar resigned from the cabinet. His decision was driven by two main factors: the stalling of the Hindu Code Bill, which sought to secure equal property and inheritance rights for women, and the government's delay in introducing reservations for Other Backward Classes (OBC).

True to his 1935 declaration at Nasik—"I was born a Hindu, but I will not die a Hindu"—Ambedkar, along with nearly 500,000 followers, formally embraced Buddhism on October 14, 1956, at Nagpur, shortly before passing away on December 6, 1956, in Delhi.

High-Yield MCQ Diagnostic Drill

Q1. In which year did Dr. B.R. Ambedkar present his landmark paper "Castes in India: Their Mechanism, Genesis and Development" at Columbia University?
A) 1897
B) 1912
C) 1916
D) 1923

✅ Answer: C — 1916

📌 Why: Ambedkar presented this foundational research paper on May 9, 1916, during his studies at Columbia University.

🪤 Trap: Confusing the presentation date with his later doctoral thesis publication in 1923.

Q2. What was the core organizational motto adopted by the Bahishkrit Hitakarini Sabha founded in 1924?
A) Self-Respect and Liberty
B) Educate, Agitate, Organize
C) Destruction of Caste Hierarchies
D) Freedom Through Education

✅ Answer: B — Educate, Agitate, Organize

📌 Why: The association adopted this three-word call to action to drive social empowerment.

🪤 Trap: Confusing it with the themes of Periyar's Self-Respect Movement (சுயமரியாதை இயக்கம்).

Q3. Who among the following leaders from Tamil Nadu participated alongside Ambedkar in the Roundtable Conferences?
A) Thanthai Periyar
B) C.N. Annadurai
C) Rao Bahadur Rettaimalai Srinivasan
D) K. Kamaraj

✅ Answer: C — Rao Bahadur Rettaimalai Srinivasan

📌 Why: Rettaimalai Srinivasan attended the conferences, collaborating closely with Ambedkar to advocate for marginalized communities.

Q4. On which specific date did Dr. B.R. Ambedkar resign from his position as independent India's first Law Minister?
A) August 15, 1947
B) January 26, 1950
C) September 27, 1951
D) December 6, 1956

✅ Answer: C — September 27, 1951

📌 Why: He resigned on this date over delays regarding the Hindu Code Bill and OBC reservations.

Q5. What is the title of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar’s official autobiography detailing his early experiences with discrimination?
A) Annihilation of Caste
B) Thoughts on Pakistan
C) Waiting for a Visa
D) The Buddha and His Dhamma

✅ Answer: C — Waiting for a Visa

📌 Why: His biographical reflections are documented under the title Waiting for a Visa.

Q6. In which year was India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, posthumously conferred upon Ambedkar?
A) 1956
B) 1972
C) 1990
D) 2005

✅ Answer: C — 1990

📌 Why: The Government of India awarded him the Bharat Ratna in 1990 during his centenary year celebration.

Q7. The historic Independent Labour Party was founded by Ambedkar in which year?
A) 1920
B) 1927
C) 1936
D) 1942

✅ Answer: C — 1936

📌 Why: He established the Independent Labour Party in 1936 to support labor rights and representation.

Q8. Which public water source was the focal point of the Mahad Satyagraha led by Ambedkar on March 20, 1927?
A) Kalaram Tank
B) Chander Tank
C) Yerwada Reservoir
D) Nasik Aquifer

✅ Answer: B — Chander Tank

📌 Why: The protest successfully asserted the right to access water from the public Chander Tank in Mahad.

Q9. The famous journal 'Janata' was launched by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar in which year?
A) 1920
B) 1924
C) 1930
D) 1936

✅ Answer: C — 1930

📌 Why: Janata was established in 1930, following his earlier publications Mooknayak and Bahishkrit Bharat.

Q10. During his tenure as Labor Member of the Viceroy's Council, Ambedkar successfully reduced standard working hours to:
A) 6 Hours
B) 8 Hours
