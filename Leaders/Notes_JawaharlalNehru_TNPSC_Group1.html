Early Life & Background

Jawaharlal Nehru was born on 14 November 1889 in Anand Bhavan, Allahabad, in present-day Uttar Pradesh. Anand Bhavan was a grand family residence — and more than just a home, it is an important historical landmark. The family consisted of five members: father Motilal Nehru, mother Swarup Rani (Swaruprani), Jawaharlal himself (the eldest child), and his two sisters Vijayalakshmi Pandit and Krishna Nehru Hutheesingh.

His father Motilal Nehru was a renowned lawyer and a major influence in the Indian National Congress. Motilal Nehru was the founder of the Swaraj Party (Swarajya Katchi) and remained influential in Congress until 1929. His affluent background meant that young Jawaharlal was sent abroad for studies at just 15 years of age.

Nehru is remembered with several epithets: Raja of Roses (ரோஜாவின் ராஜா) — he always kept a rose in his lapel or pocket, a habit he reportedly drew inspiration from George Joseph, an advocate from Madurai, who was a close family friend of the Motilal Nehru family. He is also called the Architect of Modern India (நவீன இந்தியாவினுடைய சிற்பி) and the Jewel / Treasure of India.

Gem
Nehru's habit of wearing a rose in his pocket was inspired by George Joseph, an advocate from Madurai (Tamil Nadu), who was a close family friend and major influence on the Motilal Nehru family.
Trap
Most textbooks mention only one sister — Vijayalakshmi Pandit. But Nehru had TWO sisters: Vijayalakshmi Pandit AND Krishna Nehru Hutheesingh. Krishna married a Jain named Hutheesingh (a prominent freedom fighter in the Jain community), hence the surname Hutheesingh was added.

Tip
Nehru went to Britain (England) — NOT America. He studied at Trinity College, Cambridge. Don't confuse with the USA. His father Motilal was also a lawyer, which is why the family could afford to send him abroad at just 15.

While in Europe for his studies, Nehru was deeply influenced by the Italian nationalist leader Garibaldi (Garibaldi — National Leader of Italy). He read about Garibaldi's speeches, which were full of passionate nationalist fervour. He also encountered the spirit of Bismarck — notably, Bismarck is remembered as the German unifier, and Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel is called the "Bismarck of India." Mussolini and Hitler were also European contemporaries Nehru studied. This European exposure planted in the young Nehru the dream of freeing India from British rule.

Political Career & Contributions

Nehru's active political life formally began in 1916 when he attended the Lucknow Conference (Lucknow Session). At this historic session, the Muslim League and Congress came together, and moderates and extremists also gathered under one roof. It was here that Nehru first met Mahatma Gandhi — a meeting that shaped Indian history. That same year, 1916, he married Kamala Kaul.

In 1919, following the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, Nehru established a newspaper — The Independent. That same year he was elected Vice-President of the Kisan Sabha (Kisan = Farmer/Agriculturalist in Hindi/Sanskrit; also used as "Kisaan Jam" in Tamil). Nehru participated in the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920), gaining his first experience of imprisonment, and spent approximately 9 years in jail during his lifetime.

By 1923, he was elected General Secretary of the Indian National Congress. One year later in 1924, Gandhi assumed Congress presidency at the Belgaum Session. In 1925, Sarojini Naidu became Congress president — remember this order: 1923 (Nehru — Secretary), 1924 (Gandhi — Belgaum), 1925 (Sarojini Naidu).

In 1929, at the historic Lahore Conference, Nehru and his father Motilal together spearheaded the Purna Swaraj (Complete Independence) resolution. Jawaharlal was elected Congress President and the famous Purna Swaraj declaration was made. By this time Nehru had effectively become the head of the party, with his father Motilal also prominently involved.

In 1937, Nehru presided over the Faizpur Congress Session — notable as the first Congress session held in a village. This is a frequently tested fact. Faizpur (also spelled Baispur/Paispur in the transcript) is significant precisely because it broke the urban tradition of Congress sessions.

In 1940, Gandhi launched the Individual Civil Disobedience movement. Acharya Vinoba Bhave was the first person selected by Gandhi to offer individual satyagraha; Nehru (along with Pattabhi) was the second to be arrested in this movement. Pattabhi and Nehru, though holding different ideologies, were close friends who sometimes quarrelled — but were ultimately political allies.

After independence on 15 August 1947, Nehru became India's first Prime Minister, appointed and sworn in by Lord Mountbatten, who was then the Governor-General. Nehru served as Prime Minister until his death on 27 May 1964 — making him the longest-serving Prime Minister of India to date. He served through approximately three election periods (1952, 1957, and onwards). During his tenure he established the IITs (including IIT Madras, IIT Delhi, IIT Bombay, IIT Calcutta), launched the Atomic Energy Programme, and positioned India as a modern, science-driven state. He also articulated the Non-Aligned Movement policy — refusing to align with either the American (USA) or Soviet (USSR) bloc during the Cold War.

Critical
Nehru is India's longest-serving Prime Minister to date. He served from 1947 to 1964 — approximately three full terms — until death. No other PM has served this long.

Critical
Three books are absolutely must-know: (1) The Discovery of India (1946), (2) Glimpses of World History (1934), (3) An Autobiography / Toward Freedom (1936). Memorise years too — exams test them.
Tip
Mnemonic for books order: Glimpses (1934) → Autobiography (1936) → Discovery (1946). Each is roughly 10 years apart: 34 → 36 → 46. "GAD" — Glimpses, Autobiography, Discovery.
Gem
Nehru wrote his autobiography in 1936 — very early in life, when he was only 46-47 years old and had decades of life ahead. This is unusual. Note: most people write autobiographies near the end of their life; Nehru's was written in the middle of his political career.
Trap
An Autobiography is ALSO called "Toward Freedom" (சுதந்திரத்தை நோக்கி). These are the SAME book with two names. Don't count them as two separate books.

Family & Connections

The Nehru family resided in Anand Bhavan, Allahabad. The five-member household comprised: Motilal Nehru (father, lawyer, Swaraj Party founder), Swarup Rani (mother), Jawaharlal Nehru (eldest, the future PM), Vijayalakshmi Pandit (sister), and Krishna Nehru Hutheesingh (sister).

Vijayalakshmi Pandit — India's first woman diplomat; she had an excellent alliance with the USA, representing India in America. She was also the first woman to participate in foreign affairs at an international level.

Krishna Nehru Hutheesingh — married a Jain named Hutheesingh, a prominent freedom fighter in the Jain community. She met with Ben-Gurion, the first Prime Minister of Israel, representing India — making her one of the earliest Indian representatives to engage with Israel. (The lecturer notes this was likely Ben-Gurion, Israel's first PM.)

Indira Priyadarshini Gandhi — Nehru's daughter, born 19 November 1917. Note: Indira Gandhi studied at Viswa Bharati University, Shantiniketan — the university founded by Tagore. Later she married Feroze Gandhi and became Prime Minister. She was known for a strong alliance with USSR (Russia); during the Bangladesh Liberation War, when Britain sent warships against India, the USSR dispatched its own fleet in India's defence. Nehru's Non-Aligned policy thus had practical benefits in both directions.

Gem
Indira Gandhi's full birth name was Indira Priyadarshini. She became "Indira Gandhi" only after marrying Feroze Gandhi. Her birth year: 1917, born on 19 November — exactly 5 days after her father's birthday (14 November).
Gem
The Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) — while Nehru championed not aligning with either bloc, his own family members effectively maintained both alliances: Vijayalakshmi Pandit (USA side) and Indira Gandhi (USSR side), giving India a strategic advantage.

PYQ

PYQ

PYQ
Lucknow Session 1916 — Nehru met Gandhi here for the FIRST time. This is a direct previous year question.
PYQ

PYQ

PYQ

PYQ
Individual Civil Disobedience 1940: 1st = Acharya Vinoba Bhave; 2nd = Nehru (with Pattabhi). Frequently tested in sequence questions.
PYQ
Books: (1) The Discovery of India — 1946 — Unity in Diversity; (2) Glimpses of World History — 1934 — letters to Indira from jail; (3) An Autobiography / Toward Freedom — 1936. All three have been tested. Don't miss The Unity of India as a 4th title.
