🎓 Education & Early Life
  • Born 23 January 1897, Cuttack, Odisha. Father: Janakinath Bose; Mother: Prabhavati Devi
  • From young age, deeply inspired by Swami Vivekananda — his spiritual guru
  • During school years, developed friction with British authorities
  • Father Janakinath Bose sent him to London to avoid constant confrontation with British
  • 1919: Left for London, England — went to Cambridge University
  • Cambridge University: studied for ICS exam — only 8 months of preparation
  • 1920: Wrote ICS (Indian Civil Service) exam — equivalent to today's IAS; exams only written in London (only wealthy families could attempt)
  • Secured 4th Rank in India — extraordinary achievement in just 8 months
  • April 1921: Resigned from ICS posting — could not serve British government while fighting for India

⚡ Major Contributions
CRITICAL
Bose resigned from one of India's most prestigious services (ICS/4th rank) to join the freedom struggle. After meeting Gandhi, he followed C.R. Das as political guru. He founded Forward Bloc (1939) with Muthuramalingam Thevar of Madurai, Tamil Nadu.

⚡ INA & Azad Hind — Deep Dive
CRITICAL
INA (Indian National Army) = Azad Hind Fauj (ஆசாத் ஹிந்த் ஃபௌஜ்): 'Azad' = Free/Liberated, 'Hind' = India, 'Fauj' = Army. Founded by Mohan Singh + Rash Behari Bose. Netaji took over in 1943 in Singapore.
Aspect Detail
Original Founders Mohan Singh and Rash Behari Bose (Ghosh) — in Singapore/Burma
Netaji's role Took over as Supreme Commander in 1943 in Singapore
Full name Indian National Army (INA) / Azad Hind Fauj (Free India Army)
Women's Regiment Founded by Netaji; Commander: Lakshmi Sahgal (doctor from Tamil Nadu, went to Singapore to study medicine)
Battle Cry "Chalo Delhi!" (March to Delhi!) — broadcast from Singapore radio
Provisional Govt Azad Hind Provisional Government declared in Singapore (1943) — "Free India Government"
Slogan "Jai Hind" — popularised by Netaji (original: Chembakaramam Pillai, Vienna, 1933; "Hind" suggested by Abdul Hassan, Hyderabad)
Territory Operated from Burma/Myanmar and Singapore; moved through Axis-held territories
⭐ Origin of "Jai Hind"

Step 1 — 1933: Tamil man Chembakaramam Pillai (சேம்பகராமன் பிள்ளை) said "Jai Hindustan Ke!" at a conference in Vienna, Austria. He had close friendships with Hitler and Egypt's Nasser. (Previously in syllabus, now removed but still asked in exams.)

Step 2: Netaji, inspired by Chembakaramam Pillai, adopted "Jai Hindustan" as INA battle cry.

Step 3: Abdul Hassan (Muslim, from Hyderabad, studying engineering in Germany) objected: "Hindustan sounds Hindu — Muslim soldiers won't join." He suggested "Hind" (just India, no religious connotation). Netaji agreed → "Jai Hind" became the official slogan.

CRITICAL
Two exam questions, two different answers: (1) "Who FIRST gave Jai Hind?" → Chembakaramam Pillai (Tamil, Vienna, 1933). (2) "Who SPREAD/POPULARISED Jai Hind nationwide?" → Netaji Subash Chandra Bose.
PYQ
VAO 2006 and 2016: "Who first gave the slogan Jai Hind?" → Chembakaramam Pillai (1933, Vienna). "Who spread it?" → Netaji. Two questions, two answers — do NOT mix.
🏃 The Great Escape — 1941
  • While under house arrest in Calcutta (1940–41), Bose planned daring escape
  • Shaved clean, then wore fake beard + moustache to look like a Muslim man
  • Dressed in large Afghan Muslim robe (colorful full-length garment) and turban
  • False identity: Rahmat Khan
  • Escaped from Calcutta home → through Afghanistan (contact: Bhagat Ram Talwar) → Germany
  • In Germany, met Hitler who offered limited support and suggested Japan
💎 GEM
Bose's disguise identity: Rahmat Khan — Afghan Muslim traveller with fake beard, robe, turban. Contact in Afghanistan: Bhagat Ram Talwar.
💎 GEM
Lakshmi Sahgal — from Tamil Nadu, went to Singapore to study medicine in 1943, joined INA Women's Regiment, became its commander. In 2002, Congress nominated her as their Presidential candidate against Abdul Kalam (who won). Two massive historical connections in one person!
🎯 Goals
  • Achieve complete independence for India — NO compromise (opposed Dominion Status)
  • Use military force if necessary — "Give me blood, I'll give you freedom"
  • Unite all Indians regardless of religion and caste under the INA (Jai Hind slogan was deliberately non-Hindu)
  • Liberate India through a military march from the northeast (Chalo Delhi!)
  • Establish a free, independent India's provisional government from abroad
  • Champion armed revolutionary approach alongside Congress's peaceful methods
📰 Newspapers & Publications
  • Bose did not found any major newspapers — his platform was through speeches, radio broadcasts, and books
  • From Singapore radio: "Chalo Delhi!" broadcasts — reached millions of Indians
  • The Azad Hind Government had its own official communications
TIP
Unlike Periyar (8 newspapers) or Nehru (The Independent, National Herald), Bose did NOT found newspapers. His primary communication was through radio broadcasts and books.

  • Lakshmi Sahgal — doctor from Tamil Nadu, went to Singapore, joined INA, became Women's Regiment Commander; later nominated as Presidential candidate by Congress (2002)

  • INA Women's Regiment — Lakshmi Sahgal (Tamil origin) was its commander — Tamil Nadu's direct role in INA
  • Parakram Divas (23 January) — national holiday connected to Tamil Nadu's Muthuramalingam Thevar who co-founded Forward Bloc with Bose
