🏺 Section 1: Archaeological Excavation — Tamil Nadu
Q1 An excavation revealed a structure of 6 rows and 9 bricks at a depth of 75 cm below surface level, with 3 large ring-wells stacked one above another. This is associated with which place?
MEDIUM Factual
✅ Answer
Korkai (கொற்கை) — Thoothukudi District, Tamil Nadu
Context: Korkai was one of the two major seaports of the Pandya Kingdom (the other being Kayal/Kayalpattanam), both located in present-day Thoothukudi district. The excavation uncovered:
  • A brick structure — 6 rows × 9 bricks — found at 75 cm below surface
  • 3 large ring-shaped structures (stacked) below — likely a ring-well / uraikkinaru (soaking jar / drainage system)
💎
LIVE GEM — Current Affairs Alert
At the time of this session, Tamil Nadu Chief Minister announced the new Cabinet. The Minister for Archaeology & Excavations is Rajmohan, who also holds School Education and Tamil Development portfolios. This is exam-ready current affairs — note it!
💎
LIVE GEM — Pandya Ports Memory Trick
Two Pandya ports in Thoothukudi district: (1) கொற்கை = Korkai | (2) காயல் = Kayal / Kayalpattanam. Both are recurring excavation spots in TNPSC Unit 6. Know the district for each!
🏆
PYQ ALERT
Archaeological excavation sites + their districts have been asked repeatedly in TNPSC exams. Vasavsamudram (previously asked in TNPSC — it is in Krishnagiri / Dharmapuri region) is another major site. Know: Site → District mapping.
💡
TIP — Key Sites to Know
Panchalankurichi (freedom struggle site), Vasavsamudram (excavation), Anaimalai, Korkai — all are TNPSC Unit 6 favourite spots. Match each to its district!
📜 Section 2: Tamil Nadu History — Anna & Periyar Timeline
Q2 Choose the correct statement(s): (I) Anna joined the Justice Party (Neetikatchi) in 1937. (II) Anna met Periyar for the first time in 1934.
HARD Trap/Trick
✅ Answer
Only Statement II is correct. (Answer Option B)
Statement I — WRONG: Anna joined the Justice Party (Neetikatchi) in 1935 (NOT 1937). The Justice Party's principle of creating a caste-free society appealed to Anna, who joined it in 1935.

Statement II — CORRECT: Anna met Periyar for the first time in 1934 at the Sengundar Conference held in Tiruppur. This was the beginning of Anna's discipleship under Periyar.
🪤
TNPSC TRAP
Examiners deliberately swap "1934 meet" and "1935 join" dates. Anna met Periyar in 1934 → joined Justice Party in 1935 → Later founded DMK in 1949 (September 17) — Periyar's birthday. Do not confuse these three key dates!
💎
LIVE GEM — Anna & Periyar Relationship
Anna considered Periyar his guru and greatest leader. When Anna founded DMK (1949), he declared: "As long as Periyar is alive, he is the Leader; I am only the General Secretary." DMK emerged from Dravidar Kazhagam, but Anna explicitly said it walks in Periyar's footsteps. — Teacher: September 17 = Periyar's Birthday = DMK Formation Date (1949)
🏆
PYQ ALERT
Anna's political timeline is a perennial TNPSC Unit 6 topic. Dates tested: 1934 (met Periyar), 1935 (joined Neetikatchi/Justice Party), 1949 (founded DMK).

MEDIUM Factual
✅ Answer
For 2016–17 specifically: Rajasthan topped registration (92%) under PMFBY. Tamil Nadu had achieved high coverage but Rajasthan led in % registration.
PMFBY Registration Rank (2016-17):
1st → Rajasthan (~92%) | 2nd → Nagaland | 3rd → Himachal Pradesh

TNPSC Exam Shortcut (Teacher's Note): In TNPSC exams, if Tamil Nadu is one of the options in agriculture/insurance questions, Tamil Nadu is usually the correct answer (~99% of the time). However, year-specific questions like "2016-17" may differ — always check the year carefully!
💎

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana:
• Fasal = Harvest / Crop (அறுவடை)
• Bima = Insurance / Protection (காப்பீடு)

For agriculture, infrastructure, welfare schemes — Tamil Nadu is frequently the correct answer in TNPSC papers. The state has pioneered many "firsts." But for questions tied to specific years or percentages, verify. When Tamil Nadu is an option and no year is specified, choose Tamil Nadu.
⚖️ Section 4: OBC / Backward Class Commissions
Q4 Choose the incorrect statement: (I) The First Backward Class Commission was constituted under the chairmanship of Kaka Kalelkar under Article 340. (II) It submitted its report in 1955.
HARD Trap/Trick
✅ Answer (Incorrect Statement)
Statement II is incorrect. (Answer Option A — only Statement I is correct)
Statement I — CORRECT: The First OBC Commission (India) was constituted in 1953 under Kaka Kalelkar under Article 340 of the Constitution. ✓

Statement II — INCORRECT: The commission submitted its report in 1956, NOT 1955. The report was submitted but never implemented — no one acted on it.
🪤
TNPSC TRAP — Classic Year Confusion
The exam tests 1955 vs 1956. The Kaka Kalelkar Commission report was submitted in 1956. Choosing 1955 costs you the mark. The report was famously shelved — never implemented. This is why Dr. B.R. Ambedkar resigned as Law Minister over OBC reservation not being implemented.
💎
LIVE GEM — All 4 OBC Commissions (India + Tamil Nadu)
Commission Year Chairperson
India — 1st OBC Commission 1953 Kaka Kalelkar (Report: 1956)
India — 2nd OBC Commission 1979 VP Mandal (Mandal Commission)
Tamil Nadu — 1st OBC Commission During Karunanidhi CM Sattanathan Commission
Tamil Nadu — 2nd OBC Commission During MGR CM Ambasankar Commission
Teacher noted: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar resigned as India's first Law Minister over the failure to implement OBC reservations — he was the most prominent champion of reservation rights.
🏆
PYQ ALERT
This exact question (Kaka Kalelkar + Article 340 + year of report) has appeared multiple times in TNPSC exams. Mandal Commission (1979) is equally important — its implementation in 1990 under PM VP Singh triggered national protests.
📜 Section 5: Constitutional Amendments
Q5 Which Constitutional Amendment Act provides 10% reservation in government jobs and educational institutions for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)?
MEDIUM Factual
✅ Answer
103rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 2019
EWS Reservation Details:
  • 10% reservation for those who are NOT in SC/ST/OBC categories
  • Annual family income below ₹8 lakh
  • Enacted by: 103rd Amendment Act, 2019
  • Note: This 10% EWS quota does NOT apply in Tamil Nadu (TN has its own reservation structure)
💎
LIVE GEM — Key Amendments Cheat List (93rd to 105th)
Teacher explicitly said: Know all amendments from 93rd to 105th (106th not yet implemented).
Amendment Key Change
93rd (2006) RTE — Free education for children (6-14 yrs)
99th (2014) National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC)
100th (2015) Land boundary agreement with Bangladesh
101st (2016) GST — Goods & Services Tax
103rd (2019) EWS — 10% reservation
104th (2020) Extension of SC/ST reservation in Parliament/Assemblies (25 more years)
105th (2021) State OBC lists — power restored to States
🏆
PYQ ALERT — GST Amendment
GST = 101st Amendment Act (2016) — this is asked repeatedly in TNPSC and SSC. Also know: Finance Commission, Income Tax — these are repeat Economics topics.
📉 Section 6: Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)
Q6 When did the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) launch / introduce the Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)?
MEDIUM Factual
✅ Answer
2010 (Answer Option D)
The Multidimensional Poverty Index was introduced in 2010 by the Oxford Poverty and Human Development Initiative (OPHI) in association with UNDP. It measures poverty across 3 dimensions — Health, Education, and Standard of Living.
💎

✅ Answer
Karnataka and Odisha had NOT achieved 100% by March 2017. (Correct options: D — Karnataka & Odisha)
Sikkim — had achieved 100% ✓
Tamil Nadu — had achieved 100% (since 1989) ✓
Karnataka — had NOT achieved ✗
Odisha — had NOT achieved ✗

The Pradhan Mantri UJALA Scheme / Saubhagya Scheme was launched to bring 100% electrification across all of India.
🚨

Haryana was the FIRST state in India to achieve 100% electrification — in 1970. Tamil Nadu followed in 1989 (under CM Karunanidhi). This is a frequently asked "first" in TNPSC.

📖 Section 9: Sangam Tamil Literature
Q9 Select the literary work that does NOT mention the "Velan Veriyattu" (Velan's ritual festival / trance dance festival).
MEDIUM Conceptual
✅ Answer
Mullaipattu (முல்லைப்பாட்டு) — Answer Option D
Velan Veriyattu = A ritual trance-dance performed by the Velan (priest of Murugan).

• Thirumurugatrupadai — about Murugan → mentions Velan Veriyattu ✓
• Pattinappalai — mentions Velan Veriyattu ✓
• Kurinjipattu — Kurinji = Murugan's land → mentions it ✓
• Mullaipattu — Mullai = forest/pastoral → Lord is Mayon (Vishnu/Krishna), NOT Murugan → does NOT mention Velan Veriyattu ✗
💎
LIVE GEM — Thinai (Land) & Deity System (Critical Exam Concept!)
In Sangam literature, each of the 5 Tinais (landscapes) has a presiding deity:
Tinai (நிலம்) Deity (தலைவன்) Theme
Kurinji (Hills) Murugan / Velan Love union
Mullai (Forest) Mayon (Vishnu/Krishna) Waiting
Marudam (Farmland) Indiran Infidelity
Neithal (Seashore) Varuna Longing
Palai (Desert) Korravai (Durga) Separation
Key rule: Velan Veriyattu is ONLY associated with Murugan → Only Kurinji & Murugan-related texts. Mullaipattu's god is Mayon, so NO Velan connection!
💎
LIVE GEM — "Velan" in Sangam Literature
In all of Sangam literature, only TWO names refer to Murugan: முருகன் (Murugan) and வேலன் (Velan). Sanskrit names like Kandan, Kartikeyar, Subramaniyar are NOT found in Sangam texts. This is a unique exam gem — know it!
🏆
PYQ ALERT
Tinai system + associated deities have appeared in TNPSC multiple times. Also tested: which Sangam text belongs to which tinai, and which customs are described in which text.
🏴 Section 10: Velu Nachiyar — Sivaganga Rani
Q10 Choose the incorrect statement about Velu Nachiyar: (I) With the help of Maruthu brothers, Velu Nachiyar was crowned queen. (II) She was the first female ruler in India to fight against British colonial power.
MEDIUM Factual
✅ Answer
Both statements are CORRECT. (Answer Option D — Neither is incorrect)
Statement I: Velu Nachiyar, with the help of Maruthu Pandiyar brothers (Chinna Maruthu & Periya Maruthu), recaptured Sivaganga and was crowned queen in 1780. ✓

Statement II: She IS the first female ruler in India to successfully fight and expel the British — unique because unlike others, she won and reclaimed her kingdom. ✓
💎
LIVE GEM — Why Velu Nachiyar is UNIQUE in Indian History
Every freedom fighter ultimately lost to the British — they were hanged, killed, or defeated. Velu Nachiyar is the ONLY ruler in Indian history who:
  1. Lost her kingdom to the British
  2. Built an army (including India's first woman suicide bomber — Kuyili)
  3. Fought back and defeated the British
  4. Recaptured her kingdom and was crowned queen again
Even Jhansi Rani (who fought later) was eventually defeated. Velu Nachiyar did all of this before Jhansi Rani was even born!
🏆
PYQ ALERT
Velu Nachiyar is a high-frequency TNPSC Unit 6 topic. Remember: Sivaganga Rani, Maruthu brothers support, crowned 1780, first female ruler to resist British in India.

🌱 Section 12: Agriculture — Navarai Pattam
Q12 What is the sowing period of "Navarai Pattam" (நவரிப்பட்டம்)?
MEDIUM Factual
✅ Answer
November to December (Answer Option C)
Navarai Pattam is one of Tamil Nadu's three traditional crop cultivation seasons (Pattams):

• Sowing: November–December
• Harvesting: February–March
• Crops: Watermelon (தர்பூசணி), Cucumber (வெள்ளரிக்காய்), and other short-duration crops grown in cool-to-warm season transitions

Memory Shortcut: "Nava = November" → Navarai starts in November!
💎
LIVE GEM — Tamil Nadu's 3 Pattams (Crop Seasons)
Pattam Sowing Harvest Season
Sornavarri (சொர்ணவாரி) April–May Aug–Sep SW Monsoon / Adi month
Samba / Thaladi Aug–Sep Jan–Feb NE Monsoon
Navarai (நவரிப்பட்டம்) Nov–Dec Feb–Mar Cool/dry season

💬 Section 13: Periyar — Views on Education & Examination
Q13 Choose the incorrect statement about Periyar: (I) Periyar said education should be given to all members of society. (II) Periyar did not give any opinion/comment about the examination system.
MEDIUM Conceptual
✅ Answer
Statement II is incorrect. (Answer Option B — only Statement I is correct)
Statement I — CORRECT: Periyar believed education is the only tool for human advancement — regardless of caste, religion, race, or language. ✓

Statement II — INCORRECT: Periyar DID comment on the examination system. He said the mark/rank-based examination system itself is wrong and should be abolished. He argued that ranking students by marks creates discrimination and inequality.
💎
LIVE GEM — Periyar's Vision on Education (Deep Insight)
Periyar's views (as explained by teacher):
  1. Universal education: Every person — regardless of caste/religion — deserves education
  2. Against mark system: Periyar opposed ranking students by marks because it creates new forms of discrimination (first-rank students get front seats, last-rank students pushed to the back — the same old hierarchy in new form)
  3. Against exam system itself: He felt the examination system was a tool that perpetuates inequality
Teacher connected this to Vijay's film "Nanban" where the protagonist questions rank-based photo placement — echoing Periyar's ideas.
Q14 Assertion–Reason: "Puli pasiththanna melivulla tharanuudaiyaar mannai menmaiyodu eynththa vagaiyil ulavokiyaro" — The Assertion states that those with small efforts (like mice) should not befriend those with great power (like tigers). The Reason explains this principle. Are both statement and reason correct and the reason properly explains the assertion?
HARD Conceptual
✅ Answer
Both Statement and Reason are correct, and Reason properly explains the Statement. (Answer Option A)
Verse meaning: "Those who have the humility of a mouse (small effort) living alongside those with the might of a tiger — is that wise?" → The verse advises: befriend equals or those more powerful, but do not try to maintain friendship with those far below your capability (as it leads to imbalance). The explanation (reason) given — that small-effort people should not befriend great-power people — correctly interprets the verse.

This verse is from Purananuru or a Sangam-era ethical poem (the teacher indicated it's from Tamil classical literature studied under Unit 6).
💎
LIVE GEM — Assertion-Reason Strategy for Literature Questions
When the assertion is a Tamil literary verse and the reason is its meaning:
  1. First verify if the meaning given IS the correct meaning of the verse
  2. If meaning is correct, both assertion and reason are usually right
  3. These questions appear in TNPSC Unit 6 under Tamil Literature and Ethics
