Political Associations & Organizations

Q1
MEDIUM FACTUAL 🏆 PYQ — TNPSC Gr.1 & Gr.2
When was the Madras Native Association (Chennai Vasikal Sangam / சென்னை வாசிகள் சங்கம்) founded, and by whom?
▾
✅ Answer
26 February 1852 — Founded by Gazulu Lakshminarasu (காஜுல லட்சுமி நரசு)
📌 Explanation

The Madras Native Association was the first major (largest) association formed in all of India (முதல் பெரிய சங்கம்). Its effort led to the passage of the Abolition of Torture Act (சித்திரவதை தடைச் சட்டம்), which abolished the practice of torture by authorities in the Madras Presidency.

🏆 PYQ ALERT
This question has been asked in TNPSC Group 2 exam (recent year). The association's name, founding date, and the law it helped pass are all commonly tested.
🪤 TNPSC TRAP
Examiners swap "First Political Association" and "First Major Association" to confuse you:
• First MAJOR/LARGEST Association in India → Madras Native Association (1852)
• First POLITICAL Association in India → Madras Mahajana Sabha (1884)
Both have been tested together in Group 4 exams with swapped answer options!
💎 Live Gem
Teacher's exact words: "Studying is important, but studying with clarity is far more important. You can study and still fail. Only those who study with clarity will pass." This distinction between rote reading and conceptual clarity is the core exam strategy.
Q2
HARD TRAP/TRICK 🏆 PYQ — TNPSC Gr.4
Choose the CORRECT statements about the Madras Mahajana Sabha (1884):
I. Its inaugural meeting was held on May 16, 1884.
II. G. Subramaniam, Veeraraghavachariar, and Ananthaselu Rangaiah were key figures who participated.
▾
✅ Answer
Both statements are correct → Answer Option C (Both I and II)
⚠️ CORRECTION NOTE — DATE TRAP
The question in the live session initially showed "March 16" — this was a deliberate trap. The teacher confirmed: the correct date is May 16, 1884 (not March 16). The teacher used this to check if students were paying attention. Several students missed it.
📌 Key Facts About Madras Mahajana Sabha
  • Founded: May 16, 1884
  • First President: P. Rangaiah Naidu (பி. ரங்கையா நாயுடு)
  • Significance: India's first political association (முதல் அரசியல் ரீதியான சங்கம்)
  • Precursor to Congress: Indian National Congress (founded Dec 28, 1885 in Bombay) adopted all of Sabha's demands
  • Demand 1: ICS exams should be held BOTH in London AND in India (not India alone)
  • Demand 2: Separate the Judiciary from the Executive — later enshrined in Article 50 of the Indian Constitution
💎 Live Gem — Constitutional Link
The teacher connected this 19th-century demand to the Constitution: The Madras Mahajana Sabha demanded separation of judiciary and executive in 1884 → British implemented it in 1882 Indian Council Act → Independent India codified it in Article 50 (DPSP). The teacher called this a "shortcut": remember 50-50 → Article 50 = separation of judiciary from executive.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
TNPSC has asked: "Who was the first President of Madras Mahajana Sabha?" — Answer: P. Rangaiah Naidu. Also asked in Group 4 as a "choose correct statement" question.

Section 02 · Freedom Struggle

Khilafat Movement (1919)

Q4
MEDIUM FACTUAL 🏆 PYQ
Where was the All India Khilafat Conference held in November 1919?
▾
✅ Answer
Delhi → Answer Option D
📌 Key Facts: Khilafat Movement
  • Khilafat (خلافت) = Arabic word meaning "Opposition/Caliphate" — refers to the movement to restore the Ottoman Caliph (spiritual head of all Muslims)
  • Founded in India by: Maulana Mohammad Ali and Maulana Shaukat Ali (Ali Brothers)
  • Gandhi's role: He supported the movement and chaired the All India Khilafat Conference in Delhi (November 1919)
  • Tamil Nadu Khilafat Conference: Held at Erode (not Madras, not Vaniambadi)
  • Erode conference leader: Maulana Shaukat Ali
🪤 TRAP — Tamil Nadu Location
Students guessed Madras or Vaniambadi. The Khilafat Conference in Tamil Nadu was held at Erode — the birthplace of Periyar (E.V. Ramasamy). Erode is in the Kongu region (western TN), not near Vaniambadi (Vellore district).
💎 Live Gem — Student Mnemonic
A student (Leodas) shared: "Valentine's Day → REST? No rest!" → to remember Indian Unrest was written by Valentine Chirol. The teacher loved this and confirmed it for use.
Section 03 · Freedom Struggle

Books, Authors & Historical Texts

Q5
HARD FACTUAL 💎 GEM
Who is the author of the book "Indian Unrest" (1910)? And who is known as the "Father of Indian Unrest"?
▾
✅ Answer
Author of "Indian Unrest": Valentine Chirol (published 1910)
"Father of Indian Unrest": Bal Gangadhar Tilak
📌 Explanation

"Indian Unrest" documents the rise of revolutionary nationalism among Indian youth against British rule in the early 1900s. Valentine Chirol, a British journalist, wrote it. He described how Indian youth's anger toward the British was growing. He called Bal Gangadhar Tilak the "Father of Indian Unrest" — meaning Tilak was the one who ignited this revolutionary fire. As a result, Tilak was arrested and sent to jail.

💎 Live Gem — Memory Trick
"Valentine → REST": Valentine's Day gives REST, but Valentine Chirol wrote about Indian UN-REST. This mnemonic was shared live and confirmed by the teacher as an effective memory tool.

Concept reminder: When studying History, years are extremely important. The teacher emphasized: "One concept from this topic — knowing the year + the connection between events — is what separates toppers from others."

Section 04 · Freedom Struggle

Gandhian Era — Satyagrahas & Civil Disobedience

Q7
HARD TRAP/TRICK 💎 GEM
What was the Tinkathia System (தின்கதியா முறை) practiced in Champaran, and why did Gandhi oppose it?
▾
✅ Answer
The Tinkathia System forced farmers to cultivate Indigo (நீலச்சாயச் செடி / அவுரி செடி) on 3 out of every 20 acres of their land — compulsorily for the British.
📌 Complete Explanation
  • Tinkathia = "Three Kattha" — farmers had to plant indigo on 3 katthas out of every 20 (i.e., 3/20 of land)
  • Indigo = blue dye plant (Avuri plant/அவுரி செடி) — Europeans needed it for textile dyeing; Indians had no use for it
  • Problem: British forced Indian farmers to grow indigo, depriving them of food crop cultivation on that land
  • Champaran Satyagraha (1917): Gandhi's FIRST Satyagraha in India (he had used it earlier in South Africa)
  • Gandhi was invited by: Raj Kumar Shukla (a local farmer/leader who traveled to Lucknow to request Gandhi's help)
  • State: Champaran is in Bihar
🪤 TNPSC TRAP — Gandhi's "Firsts" in India
Gandhi's "First" in IndiaEventYearNature
First Satyagraha in IndiaChamparan Satyagraha1917Non-cooperation with forced cultivation
First Civil DisobedienceKheda (Kaira) Satyagraha1918 (Mar-Apr)Farmers refused to pay taxes during famine
First Hunger StrikeAhmedabad Satyagraha1918Mill workers' wage dispute
Common mistake: Students answer "Champaran" for First Civil Disobedience — WRONG. Champaran = First Satyagraha. Kheda = First Civil Disobedience.
💎 Live Gem — Student Question Answered
Student asked: "Why did the British listen to Gandhi when he went on a hunger strike? Couldn't they just ignore it?"

Teacher's answer: Gandhi's power was Ahimsa (non-violence). The British strategy was to arrest leaders and use police to break up protests. But Gandhi's non-violent approach meant people wouldn't fight back — so the British couldn't justify arrests or use force. They couldn't figure out how to suppress it. His hunger strikes created moral and public pressure that the British couldn't easily counter with violence.
Q8
MEDIUM FACTUAL
Gandhi's first Civil Disobedience Movement in India — which Satyagraha was it, in which state, and in what year?
▾
✅ Answer
Kheda (Kaira) Satyagraha, 1918 — in Gujarat
Month: March–April 1918
📌 Why Kheda = Civil Disobedience?

In Kheda, there was a law (Famine Code) that said: if crop yield falls below 25%, farmers are exempt from paying taxes. But British officers were still forcibly collecting taxes during a famine year. Gandhi called on farmers to disobey this unjust enforcement of the law — hence Civil Disobedience (சட்டமறுப்பு இயக்கம்). The law itself existed; Gandhi defied its improper application.

Round Table Conferences (வட்டமேசை மாநாடுகள்)

Q9
MEDIUM CONCEPTUAL 💎 GEM
Where was the First Round Table Conference held? Who chaired it? Was it successful?
▾
✅ Answer
Held in London → All three Round Table Conferences were held in London.
Chaired by Ramsay MacDonald (British Prime Minister)
Result: NOT successful — Gandhi did not attend the First RTC.
📌 Round Table Conference Summary
ConferenceYearLocationGandhi?Outcome
1st RTC1930London❌ AbsentFailed — no Congress participation
2nd RTC1931London✅ PresentGandhi–Ambedkar clash on separate electorates
3rd RTC1932London❌ AbsentCongress boycotted

Purpose of RTCs: To review the Simon Commission Report (1928) and decide India's constitutional future. The Simon Commission was boycotted by most Indians because it had no Indian members.

💎 Live Gem — Simon Commission Context
The 1919 Government of India Act was valid for only 10 years → due for review by 1929. The British sent the Simon Commission to review it. Indians boycotted it. The RTCs were set up to analyse the Simon Report. All 3 were held in London (not Delhi, not Bombay). A "round table" means equal seating — everyone sat at a round table to symbolize equal status.

HARD CONCEPTUAL 💎 GEM
If the rate of inflation is in single digits (3%–9%), what type of inflation is it called?
▾
✅ Answer
Walking Inflation (நடக்கும் பணவீக்கம்)
📌 Complete Inflation Classification Table
TypeRateTamil NameNature
Creeping InflationBelow 3%தவலும் பணவீக்கம்Very slow, manageable
Walking Inflation3% – 9%நடக்கும் பணவீக்கம்Moderate, concerning

Inflation = When the value of money (currency) decreases, prices of goods increase. The more money in circulation, the higher inflation.

💎 Live Gem — Understanding Economics
Teacher's advice on Economics study strategy: "Don't memorize economics terms. UNDERSTAND them. If you understand what inflation is — money value down, prices up — then all the sub-types make logical sense. Knowledge wins competitive exams, not notes."

Key terms to understand (not memorize): Inflation, Deflation, Stagflation, GDP, GNP, NNI, CRR, SLR, Repo Rate.
💡 REAL-LIFE CONNECTION (Teacher's Example)
"Even today, petrol prices were raised 4 times this week. That IS inflation — the value of your rupee is buying less petrol than before." — Teacher used current events to make the concept tangible.

Section 07 · Economics

Banking — RBI, SBI & History

Q16
HARD TRAP/TRICK
Choose the correct statement(s):
I. The Central Bank of China was established in 1928.
II. The Reserve Bank of New Zealand was established in 1934.
▾
✅ Answer
Only Statement II is correct → Answer Option B (II only)
⚠️ CORRECTION — Central Bank of China
Statement I says 1928 — this is WRONG. The Central Bank of China was established in 1924 (not 1928). The current "Central Bank of China" is actually headquartered in Taiwan — because after the split between China and Taiwan, the original bank remained in Taiwan.
💎 Live Gem — Historical Context
The teacher explained the China-Taiwan split: China and Taiwan were once one nation. After WWII, when the People's Republic of China was formed, the original Nationalist government fled to Taiwan. The Central Bank of China (1924) stayed in Taiwan. So today, the "Central Bank of China" is in Taiwan, while mainland China has the "People's Bank of China."

Key fact: RBI was recommended by the Hilton Young Committee and established in 1934 (same year as Reserve Bank of New Zealand).
Q17
MEDIUM FACTUAL 💎 GEM
Who controls / is responsible for managing counterfeit / fake currency (போலியான பணம்) in circulation?
▾
✅ Answer
Reserve Bank of India (RBI) — Answer: Central Monetary Authority / RBI
📌 RBI's Role in Fake Currency

The RBI monitors, identifies, and controls fake currency in India. It also controls the volume of currency in circulation — too much money in circulation causes inflation; too little causes deflation. This is why the RBI (not the Government of India) controls money supply.

The 2016 Demonetisation (Nov 8, 2016) — when ₹500 and ₹1000 notes were banned — was partly aimed at removing fake currency (reportedly being pumped by Pakistan into India to destabilize the economy).

💎 Live Gem — Demonetisation Context
Teacher's complete explanation: Pakistan had allegedly prepared a large volume of fake Indian currency to flood India's economy. To counter this, PM Modi announced Demonetisation on November 8, 2016. The film "Dhurandhar" (recently released) depicts this story. RBI's normal annual job is to check and replace fake notes — Demonetisation was an extraordinary measure. Even 10 years later (November 2026 will be the 10th anniversary), fake currency still exists, managed by the RBI.

Section 08 · Polity / Current

Anti-Defection Law & Other Topics

Q19
MEDIUM CONCEPTUAL 💎 GEM
Does the Anti-Defection Law (கட்சி தாவல் தடை சட்டம்) apply when an elected MLA resigns from their party and joins another party?
▾
✅ Answer
NO — If the MLA resigns from their post FIRST and then joins another party, the Anti-Defection Law does NOT apply.
📌 When Does Anti-Defection Apply?

The Anti-Defection Law (10th Schedule, 1985) applies when a person holds an elected post (MLA/MP) and simultaneously supports or joins another party without resigning. If they resign first → free to join any party → no defection.

Recent example: AIADMK MLAs who resigned their positions and then joined another party — Anti-Defection law did NOT apply.

💎 Live Gem — Clear Conceptual Explanation
Teacher's analogy: "If you are working in Company X and while still employed you start supporting Company Y, that is defection. But if you resign from Company X and then join Company Y, that is your free choice — no defection." The public post is like employment — you can't serve two masters simultaneously.
