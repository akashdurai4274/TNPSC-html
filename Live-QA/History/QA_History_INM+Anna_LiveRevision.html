⚓ Royal Indian Navy (RIN) Revolt — 1946 Q1 – Q3
Q1
Medium Factual CRITICAL 🏆 PYQ
Which ship saw Indian sailors (sailors/ratings) revolt against racial discrimination by British officers?
பின்வரும் எந்த கப்பலில் ஆங்கிலேய அதிகாரிகளின் நிறவெறிக்கு எதிராக மாலுமிகள் போராட்டத்தில் இறங்கினர்?
  • A) HMIS Kathiawar
  • B) HMIS Bahadur
  • C) HMIS Talwar ✅
  • D) HMIS Hindustan
✅ Correct Answer
Option C — HMIS Talwar (HMS Talwar)
📌 Explanation
The Royal Indian Navy (RIN) Revolt began on 18 February 1946 in Bombay (Mumbai). The spark was lit on the ship HMIS Talwar, moored at Bombay. Indian sailor B.C. Dutt wrote "Quit India" on the ship's walls in anger against racial discrimination (salary disparity, food quality differences between British and Indian sailors). British officers punished him. Fellow Indian sailors rallied in his support, starting the revolt. This was called the Royal Indian Navy Revolt or Indian Naval Mutiny of 1946.
💎 LIVE GEM
This was the LAST major armed rebellion against the British in India. The revolt spread to every naval station in India — Bombay, Karachi, Madras, Calcutta, and others. It was not led by a single leader — it was a leaderless rebellion. The person who brought the revolt to a close was Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel (Iron Man of India). Key dates: Started — 18 Feb 1946; ended after Patel's intervention.
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 2 (2022) asked about the RIN Revolt — the ship name, starting location (Bombay), and year (1946) are frequently tested. SSC CGL also asked about who suppressed the revolt.
Q2
Medium Factual
Which ship at Karachi also joined the RIN Revolt?
ரின் கிளர்ச்சி காலத்தில் கராச்சியில் இருந்த கப்பலின் பெயர் என்ன?
✅ Correct Answer
HMIS Hindustan (from the options: Option D was correct for Karachi)
📌 Explanation
After starting in Bombay on HMIS Talwar, the revolt spread to Karachi. The ship stationed at Karachi that joined the revolt was HMIS Hindustan. Note: HMIS = His Majesty's Indian Ship. The revolt spread to approximately 78 ships, 20 shore establishments, and 20,000 naval ratings across India.
🪤 TRAP NOTE
Don't confuse: HMIS Talwar = Bombay (starting point). HMIS Hindustan = Karachi. Both appear as options in MCQs to confuse students.
Q3
Easy Factual
During the RIN Revolt, textile mill workers of which city joined in support of the naval ratings?
ரின் கிளர்ச்சி நடக்கும்போது எந்த நகரின் துணி ஆலை தொழிலாளர்கள் கடற்படை வீரர்களுக்கு ஆதரவாக போராட்டத்தில் இறங்கினர்?
  • A) Allahabad
  • B) Madras
  • C) Bengal
  • D) Bombay ✅
✅ Correct Answer
Option D — Bombay
📌 Explanation
Since the revolt started in Bombay, it was the textile mill workers of Bombay who came out in solidarity with the naval ratings. Bombay was a major textile/industrial hub at the time — the mill workers joined the struggle showing working-class solidarity with the naval mutineers.
💎 LIVE GEM
Concept shortcut: "First = Bombay" — any question asking WHERE the RIN revolt began or WHERE supporting workers joined = Bombay. Madras had its own naval presence but Bombay was the epicentre. This is a direct one-liner MCQ pattern.
📜 August Offer (1940) & Individual Satyagraha Q4 – Q5
Q4
Hard Trick/Trap TRAP 🏆 PYQ
Choose the correct statement(s):
(I) On August 8, 1940, Viceroy Wavell offered concessions to the Congress in exchange for support in WWII — this is called the "August Offer."
(II) On October 17, 1940, Individual Satyagraha was launched.
சரியான கூற்றை தேர்ந்தெடுக: (I) ஆகஸ்ட் 8, 1940ல் வேவல் பிரபு ஆகஸ்ட் சலுகை அறிவித்தார். (II) அக்டோபர் 17, 1940ல் தனிநபர் சத்தியாகிரகம் தொடங்கியது.
  • A) Both I and II ❌
  • B) Only II ✅
  • C) Only I
  • D) Neither
✅ Correct Answer
Option B — Only Statement II is correct
📌 Explanation
Statement I is WRONG: The August Offer was announced on August 8, 1940 — but it was announced by Viceroy Linlithgow, NOT Wavell. Wavell became Viceroy only after 1943. The Viceroy during WWII (1939–1943) was Lord Linlithgow.

Statement II is CORRECT: Individual Satyagraha began on October 17, 1940. Gandhi announced it. The first person to perform it was Acharya Vinoba Bhave. It was launched in response to the August Offer (which offered only Dominion Status, not full independence — the Congress demand).
💎 LIVE GEM
Viceroy Timeline (Must Know for All Exams):
• Lord Hardinge → Partition of Bengal Reunification (1911), WWI
• Lord Linlithgow → August Offer (1940), WWII
• Lord Wavell → 1943 onwards, Bengal Famine handling
• Lord Mountbatten → Partition Plan / June 3 Plan (1947)
Examiners frequently swap Linlithgow ↔ Wavell in August Offer questions!
🪤 TNPSC TRAP
The August Offer date (Aug 8, 1940) is correct, but the Viceroy name is the trap. It was Linlithgow, NOT Wavell. This exact trap appeared in TNPSC Group 2A 2023.
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 2 (2023) — Statement-based Q on August Offer and Individual Satyagraha dates. UPSC Prelims also tested Vinoba Bhave as first Individual Satyagrahi.
Q5
Medium Factual
When did Gandhi suspend (call off) the Individual Satyagraha?
காந்தியார் தனிநபர் சத்தியாகிரகத்தை எப்போது நிறுத்தினார்?
  • A) November 1940
  • B) December 1940
  • C) January 1941 ✅
  • D) March 1941
✅ Correct Answer
The Individual Satyagraha was suspended in December 1940 / January 1941
📌 Explanation
Gandhi launched Individual Satyagraha in October 1940. He suspended it (temporarily called it off) when he was invited to participate in the Second Round Table Conference. Before leaving, he announced a temporary suspension of the Civil Disobedience / Satyagraha movement. The suspension happened in December 1940.
🗓️ Partition Plan & Muslim League Acceptance Q6 – Q7
Q6
Medium Factual 🏆 PYQ
On which date did the Muslim League formally accept the Partition Plan (Mountbatten Plan)?
முஸ்லிம் லீக் பிரிவினை திட்டத்தை எப்போது ஏற்றுக்கொண்டது?
  • A) June 10, 1947 ✅
  • B) August 15, 1947
  • C) November 26
  • D) May 16
✅ Correct Answer
June 10, 1947
📌 Explanation
June 3, 1947 — Mountbatten Plan / June 3 Plan announced by Lord Mountbatten (Viceroy): India would be divided into India and Pakistan.
June 10, 1947 — Muslim League formally accepted the plan (just ~1 week after announcement).

Key Viceroy detail: Lord Mountbatten became Viceroy on March 22, 1947. His predecessor Lord Wavell was removed. PM Clement Attlee had announced on February 22, 1947 that Britain would quit India by June 1948.
💎 LIVE GEM
June 3 is also Kalaignar (M. Karunanidhi's) birthday! — Teacher noted this as a memory hook. Also note: June 3 Plan = Mountbatten Plan = Partition Plan — all three names refer to the same event. Mountbatten announced on June 3; Muslim League accepted June 10; India got independence August 15.
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 4 (2019) directly asked: "When did Muslim League accept the Mountbatten Plan?" Answer: June 10, 1947.
Q7
Medium Factual
On which date did Lord Mountbatten assume charge as Viceroy of India?
மவுண்ட் பேட்டன் பிரபு இந்தியாவின் வைசராயாக எப்போது பொறுப்பேற்றார்?
✅ Correct Answer
March 22, 1947
📌 Explanation
Lord Mountbatten took charge as Viceroy on March 22, 1947. He replaced Lord Wavell. British PM Attlee had announced on Feb 22, 1947 that Britain would leave India by June 1948. Mountbatten accelerated the process and India gained independence on August 15, 1947.
🪤 TRAP NOTE
Many students write "March 1947" but forget the exact date — March 22. The exam may have March 12 / March 22 / February 22 as options. March 22 = Mountbatten takes charge. February 22 = Attlee's announcement in Parliament.
🌍 Viceroys During World Wars Q8
Q8
Hard Conceptual CRITICAL 🏆 PYQ
Who was the Viceroy of India at the start of the Second World War?
இரண்டாம் உலகப்போர் தொடக்கத்தின் போது இந்தியாவின் வைசராயாக இருந்தவர் யார்?
  • A) Vienna
  • B) Burma
  • C) Lord Linlithgow ✅
  • D) Singapore
✅ Correct Answer
Option C — Lord Linlithgow
📌 Explanation
WWII started on September 1, 1939 (Germany invaded Poland). It ended in 1945 (Japan surrendered after atomic bombs on Hiroshima — Aug 6 — and Nagasaki — Aug 9). During this period, the Viceroy of India was Lord Linlithgow. He also announced the August Offer (1940).

For WWI: The Viceroy was Lord Hardinge. He was also the Viceroy when Bengal Partition was reunified (1911) — the original partition was done by Lord Curzon.
💎 LIVE GEM — Complete Viceroy Master List for Exams
Key Viceroy–Event pairing (must memorise):
• Lord Dufferin → Indian National Congress founded (1885)
• Lord Curzon → Partition of Bengal (1905)
• Lord Minto → All India Muslim League formed (1906)
• Lord Hardinge → Bengal Reunification (1911) + WWI Viceroy
• Lord Chelmsford → Jallianwala Bagh (1919), Rowlatt Act
• Lord Irwin → Gandhi–Irwin Pact (1931)
• Lord Willingdon → Government of India Act 1935 era
• Lord Linlithgow → August Offer (1940), WWII Viceroy
• Lord Wavell → 1943–1947, Bengal Famine relief
• Lord Mountbatten → Partition Plan, Indian Independence (1947)
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 2 (2023) asked: "Who was Viceroy during the Second World War?" — Lord Linlithgow. TNPSC Group 4 (2018) asked the same for WWI — Lord Hardinge.
Q8b
Medium Factual 🏆 PYQ
Which area in East Bengal (present Bangladesh) was worst affected by communal riots at the time of Partition — and where was Gandhi present on Independence Day (August 15, 1947)?
சுதந்திர தினத்தன்று காந்தியார் எங்கு இருந்தார்? கிழக்கு வங்காளத்தில் கொடும் இனக்கலவரத்தால் பாதிக்கப்பட்ட பகுதி எது?
  • A) Allahabad
  • B) Noakhali ✅
  • C) Bengal
  • D) Bombay
✅ Correct Answer
Noakhali (in present-day Bangladesh)
📌 Explanation
On August 15, 1947, while the rest of India celebrated independence, Gandhi was in Noakhali, East Bengal (now Bangladesh), working to restore peace after devastating Hindu–Muslim communal riots. Gandhi fasted and worked for communal harmony there, refusing to celebrate independence amidst the violence.

Manikatala — a place in Bengal — is also important in INM context: Hem Chandra Kanungo set up a bomb factory there after learning bomb-making in Paris, in connection with the Alipore Bomb Case (1908). Kanungo trained youth under 18 to throw bombs and target Judge Douglas Kingsford.
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 2 (2022) — "Where was Gandhi on Independence Day?" → Noakhali. This is a high-frequency question.
⚔️ Indian National Army (INA) & Netaji Q9 – Q11
Q9
Medium Trick/Trap TRAP 🏆 PYQ
Identify the INCORRECT statement(s):
(I) In 1942, Subhash Chandra Bose formed the Indian National Army in Singapore.
(II) Mahatma Gandhi called Subhash Chandra Bose "Father of the Nation."
தவறான கூற்றை கண்டுபிடி: (I) 1942ல் சுபாஷ் சந்திர போஸ் சிங்கப்பூரில் INA உருவாக்கினார். (II) காந்தியை தேசத்தந்தை என்று போஸ் அழைத்தார்.
  • A) Only Statement I ✅
  • B) Only Statement II
  • C) Both
  • D) Neither
✅ Correct Answer — INCORRECT statement is: Statement I only
Option A
📌 Explanation
Statement I is WRONG: The INA was formed in 1943, NOT 1942. Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose formed the Indian National Army (Azad Hind Fauj) in Singapore in 1943.

Statement II is CORRECT: It was Bose who addressed Gandhi as "Father of the Nation" (Rashtrapita). Before leaving for battle, Bose broadcast a message via Azad Hind Radio addressing Gandhi as "Father of the Nation" and seeking his blessings for the "last war of independence."
🪤 CRITICAL TRAP
Year trap: 1942 vs 1943. INA = 1943. Quit India Movement = 1942. Students often swap these years. The 0.5 mark difference on this one number can cost a candidate their selection!
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 2 (2025) — INA formation year and location (Singapore, 1943) asked. Also SSC CGL 2022.
Q10
Medium Factual 💎 Gem
From which city did Bose address Gandhi over the Azad Hind Radio, calling him "Father of the Nation"?
ஆசாத் ஹிந்த் ரேடியோ வழியாக காந்திக்கு போஸ் தனது உரையை எங்கிருந்து ஆற்றினார்?
  • A) Singapore
  • B) Berlin
  • C) Vienna
  • D) Rangoon (Burma) ✅
✅ Correct Answer
Option D — Rangoon (Yangon), Burma
📌 Explanation
Bose's journey: India → Afghanistan (Kabul) → Germany (Berlin) — where he learned about Japan's planned attack on British India → Singapore (1943) — formed INA → Japan (met PM Tojo, got support) → back to Singapore → addressed Gandhi via Azad Hind Radio from Rangoon, calling him "Father of the Nation" and seeking blessings for the "Last War of Independence."
💎 LIVE GEM
Netaji's Political Guru: Chittaranjan Das (C.R. Das) — note this for MCQs.
What happened to Netaji? A plane crash at Taihoku (Taipei) on August 18, 1945. Indian sources say he died; but no definitive evidence exists — still a historical mystery.
Azad Hind Radio was set up in Germany. The famous speech from Rangoon is where "Father of the Nation" for Gandhi originated.
Q11
Hard Conceptual
Mohan Singh, who first formed the Azad Hind Fauj (precursor to INA), was an officer of the British Indian Army. In which territory was he serving when he allied with the Japanese?
மோகன் சிங் எந்த பகுதியில் பிரிட்டிஷ் இந்திய ராணுவ அதிகாரியாக இருந்தார்?
  • A) Vienna
  • B) Burma
  • C) Malaya (present-day Malaysia) ✅
  • D) Singapore
✅ Correct Answer
Option C — Malaya (Malaysia)
📌 Explanation
Mohan Singh was an officer in the British Indian Army stationed in Malaya (now Malaysia). When Japan invaded and defeated the British forces in Malaya, Mohan Singh approached the Japanese for support against the British. With Japanese assistance, he formed the Azad Hind Fauj from captured Indian POWs. This initial force later came under Netaji's command, who expanded it into the full Indian National Army (INA) in Singapore in 1943.

Note (TNPSC vs SSC depth): SSC only needs basic INA facts. TNPSC Group 2 requires deeper knowledge: Mohan Singh → Malaya → initial INA → Netaji → Singapore → full INA.
🕯️ Jallianwala Bagh Massacre & Related Events Q12
Q12
Medium Factual 🏆 PYQ
Nationalist leaders Saifuddin Kitchlew and Dr. Satyapal were arrested on which date — an event that triggered the Jallianwala Bagh massacre?
சைபுதீன் கிச்சலு மற்றும் டாக்டர் சத்தியபால் எப்போது கைது செய்யப்பட்டனர்?
  • A) April 6
  • B) April 8
  • C) April 9, 1919 ✅
  • D) April 13
✅ Correct Answer
April 9, 1919
📌 Explanation
Sequence of events (crucial for MCQs):
• Rowlatt Act (1919) — Black Act giving British arbitrary arrest powers. Gandhi called for resistance.
• April 6, 1919 — National Hartal (National Strike) called by Gandhi, observed in Amritsar / Punjab.
• April 9, 1919 — British police arrested Saifuddin Kitchlew & Dr. Satyapal (who had organised the hartal in Amritsar).
• April 13, 1919 — Jallianwala Bagh massacre: General Reginald Dyer ordered troops to fire on a peaceful gathering demanding their release. This day was Baisakhi (Punjabi harvest festival — their equivalent of Pongal).
💎 LIVE GEM
April 13 = Baisakhi Day — the Punjabi harvest festival. The crowd at Jallianwala Bagh had gathered both to celebrate Baisakhi AND to protest the arrests of Kitchlew and Satyapal. This dual significance is sometimes tested: "What festival coincided with Jallianwala Bagh?" → Baisakhi.
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 4 (2019, 2023) — Arrest dates of Kitchlew & Satyapal; April 13 = Baisakhi connection. High-frequency question cluster.
📚 Perarignar Anna (C.N. Annadurai) — Tamil Nadu History Q13 – Q14 + Bonus
Q13
Medium Factual 🏆 PYQ
Match the following literary works with their category — all by C.N. Annadurai (Perarignar Anna):
First Essay / First Short Story / First Poetry / First Short Novel / First Novel
அறிஞர் அண்ணா அவர்களின் முதல் கட்டுரை, கவிதை, சிறுகதை, சிறுநாவல், நாவல் ஆகியவற்றை பொருத்துக.
✅ Complete Literary Works List
• First Essay: Velaikkaari (வேலைக்காரி) — Published in Tamilarasu magazine, March 1939
• First Short Story: Pengal Samattuvam (Women's Equality)
• First Poetry: Congress Oozhal (காங்கிரஸ் ஊழல்)
• First Short Novel: Komarattinodi
• First Novel: Vaazhgi Uthadu
• First Drama/Play: Velaikkaari (also a play)
• Published in magazine: Tamilarasu (தமிழரசு), March 1939
📌 Key Fact
Anna's book Ariya Mayai (ஆரியமாயை, 1943) led to a 6-month prison sentence awarded by the Tiruchirappalli (Trichy) Court. The book was a critique of the Aryan dominance theory. Published in 1943.
💎 LIVE GEM
Other Anna books for exams:
• Homeland (1957), Home Rule (1966), Ariya Mayai (1943)
• Ariya Mayai question trap: Year published = 1943; Year prison sentence = after Trichy court ruling. The question "1953 or 1943?" = 1943 is correct. 1953 is the trap.
• TNPSC 2025 Group 2 asked: A question about Anna's film-related work — always note his contributions to Tamil cinema (Parasakthi screenplay, dialogues).
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 2 (2025) — Anna's film contribution. TNPSC Group 4 (2022) — "Anna's first essay was published in which magazine?" → Tamilarasu (March 1939).
Q14
Medium Factual 🏆 PYQ
Match Anna's administrative reforms with the correct year:
(A) Renamed Secretariat from "Fort St. George" to "Thalaimai Seyalakam" (Head Quarters)
(B) Conducted 2nd World Tamil Conference in Chennai
(C) Introduced Two-Language Policy (replacing Three-Language Formula)
(D) Sub-Fellowship Award announced
சென்ட் ஜார்ஜ் கோட்டை → தலைமை செயலகம்; 2வது உலக தமிழ் மாநாடு; இருமொழிக் கொள்கை; சப் பெல்லோஷிப் விருது — இவற்றை சரியான ஆண்டுகளுடன் பொருத்துக.
✅ Answers
(A) Fort St. George → Thalaimai Seyalakam: 1967
(B) 2nd World Tamil Conference, Chennai: 1968
(C) Two-Language Policy introduced: January 23, 1968
(D) Sub-Fellowship Award announced: 1968
📌 Detailed Explanation
Official Language Amendment Bill: Introduced in Parliament on November 27, 1967 (Statement I) and passed on December 16, 1965 (Statement II). Note: Introduced AFTER it was passed — this is why Statement I (1967) is WRONG in the question format. The correct sequence: passed Dec 16, 1965 → amended Nov 1967.

Sri → Thiru / Srimathi → Thirumathi: Anna replaced Sanskrit honorifics with Tamil equivalents.

Two-Language Policy (Jan 23, 1968): Anna refused Three-Language Formula (Hindi compulsory). Tamil Nadu follows Two-Language Policy (Tamil + English). This is still relevant today — current attempts to reintroduce three-language policy face protests because of Anna's legacy.
💎 LIVE GEM — Sub-Fellowship Story
Anna visited 7 American universities to receive the Sub-Fellowship Award (given to best administrators). Americans at the event spoke in English to belittle him. When Anna took the mic, he deliberately used three conjunctions in one sentence: "There is no sense with 'because' because 'because' is a conjunction" — pointing out their own English grammar error (conjunctions cannot be used consecutively). This became known as "South Indian Bernard Shaw breaking English grammar" — showing intellectual brilliance in the very language they used to mock him.
First non-American to receive this award.
💎 LIVE GEM — World Tamil Conferences
• 1st World Tamil Conference: Malaysia (Kuala Lumpur)
• 2nd World Tamil Conference: Chennai, 1968 — by Anna
• 5th World Tamil Conference: MGR conducted
• 8th World Tamil Conference: J. Jayalalithaa conducted
• Semmozhimaanattu (Classical Tamil Conference): Kalaignar Karunanidhi conducted
⚠️ Teacher Correction Note
Teacher noted uncertainty about Anna's "First Essay" — between "Velaikkaari" and "Pengal Swatantram" / "Makhilir Gottam." The confirmed answer is Velaikkaari (வேலைக்காரி) published in Tamilarasu magazine, March 1939. Teacher asked students to remind for verification next day.
🏆 PYQ Alert
TNPSC Group 2A (2023) — Anna's Two-Language Policy date. TNPSC Group 4 (2019) — "In which year did Anna rename Fort St. George?" → 1967.
Q15
Easy Factual 💎 Gem
[Homework Question] Which scheme did Anna introduce that offered a gold/silver/bronze medal to inter-caste (cross-community) married couples? — Answer in comment section after video
சாதி மறுப்பு திருமணம் செய்தோருக்கு தங்கம்/வெள்ளி/வெண்கலம் பதக்கம் வழங்கும் திட்டத்தை அண்ணா அறிமுகப்படுத்தினார் — அந்த திட்டத்தின் பெயர் என்ன?

Dr. Ambedkar Inter-Caste Marriage Scheme (சாதிமறுப்பு திருமண உதவித்திட்டம்) — gold medal for inter-caste Hindu marriages; introduced to break caste barriers.

7. Sub-Fellowship Award — first non-American to receive it (1968)

  • August Offer (Aug 8, 1940): Announced by Viceroy Linlithgow (NOT Wavell); offered Dominion Status (rejected); triggered Individual Satyagraha.
  • Individual Satyagraha (Oct 17, 1940): Gandhi announced; Vinoba Bhave was the first to perform it.
  • Mountbatten Plan (June 3, 1947): India–Pakistan partition announced; Muslim League accepted June 10, 1947; Mountbatten became Viceroy March 22, 1947.
  • WWII Viceroy: Lord Linlithgow (1936–1943). WWI Viceroy: Lord Hardinge. Independence Viceroy: Lord Mountbatten.
  • INA formed by Netaji in Singapore in 1943; precursor by Mohan Singh in Malaya; Azad Hind Radio speech from Rangoon; "Father of the Nation" for Gandhi originated from Netaji.
  • Jallianwala Bagh: Kitchlew & Satyapal arrested April 9, 1919; massacre on April 13 (Baisakhi day); perpetrated by General Reginald Dyer; triggered by Rowlatt Act protests.
  • Anna's administrative reforms: Renamed Secretariat (1967); Sri→Thiru; Two-Language Policy (Jan 23, 1968); 2nd World Tamil Conference Chennai (1968).
  • Anna's literature: First essay = Velaikkaari in Tamilarasu (March 1939); Ariya Mayai (1943) = 6-month jail by Trichy Court; Sub-Fellowship Award (1968) — first non-American.
  • Gandhi on Independence Day = Noakhali (East Bengal), working on communal harmony; NOT celebrating in Delhi.
