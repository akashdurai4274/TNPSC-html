📍 Sites & Locations

Q1–Q3, Q34
Q01 MEDIUM Factual 🏆 PYQ
Which of the following Harappan sites are NOT located in present-day India?
(Options: Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Lothal, Kalibangan, Banawali, Rupar, Surkotada, Dholavira)
✅ ANSWER
Harappa and Mohenjo-daro — both are located in present-day Pakistan.
📌 EXPLANATION
The Harappan Civilisation spread across a vast region — majority of major sites fall in Pakistan (Punjab & Sindh provinces). Indian sites include: Lothal & Dholavira (Gujarat), Kalibangan (Rajasthan), Rupar (Punjab), Banawali (Haryana), Rakhigarhi (Haryana). Sites extending south reach Maharashtra; north reaches Haryana.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
This question type — identifying non-Indian Harappan sites — has appeared multiple times in TNPSC Group 2/4 papers. Mohenjo-daro (Pakistan) and Harappa (Pakistan) are the standard expected answers.
💎 LIVE GEM
Teacher's tip: "Harappan Civilisation covered Pakistan fully — that's the bulk. India only has a few sites. Gujarat (Lothal, Dholavira), Haryana (Rakhigarhi, Banawali), Rajasthan (Kalibangan) — that's the Indian portion. Everything in Punjab & Sindh = Pakistan."
Q02 MEDIUM Factual 🏆 PYQ 💎 Gem
Where is the Harappan site "Dholavira" located?
(Context: It is located on Khadir Island, Rann of Kutch, Gujarat)
✅ ANSWER
Dholavira is located on Khadir Island, in the Rann of Kutch, Gujarat.
📌 EXPLANATION
Many students confused Dholavira with Lothal (also in Gujarat). Lothal is on the Sabarmati tributary near Cambay Gulf — NOT in Rann of Kutch. Dholavira is specifically on Khadir Island in the Rann of Kutch (Great Rann). Remember: Dholavira = Rann of Kutch = Island.
⚠️ TRAP NOTE
Most students answered "Lothal" for "Rann of Kutch location" — this is WRONG. Lothal is near Cambay Gulf on the Bhogavo River / Sabarmati tributary. Dholavira = Khadir Island = Rann of Kutch.
💎 LIVE GEM
Teacher's memory trick: "Dholavira on an island in Rann of Kutch — visualise it as a fortress-city on a salt island. It also has the world's oldest signboard (inscription) found at its gates — 10 large Indus script signs."
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Dholavira's location (Khadir Island, Rann of Kutch) has appeared in TNPSC Group 2 (2019) and Group 4 exams. High-frequency topic.
Q03 MEDIUM Conceptual
Which of the following statements about the Harappan Civilisation is INCORRECT?
(1) All sites had urban civilisation. (2) Harappan towns depended on villages for food and workers. (3) Remains are found even in small villages. (4) Lothal's special feature is its port/dock.
✅ ANSWER
Statement (1) is INCORRECT: Not all Harappan sites were urban — the civilisation comprised 6 major cities + 200+ villages.
📌 EXPLANATION
The Harappan Civilisation had approximately 6 major cities and around 200 villages. Villages engaged in agriculture and supplied food and labour to urban centres. Lothal is indeed famous as a port/dockyard. So statements 2, 3, and 4 are correct; only statement 1 is false.
💎 LIVE GEM
Teacher: "Remember — 6 cities, 200 villages. That's the magic number for Harappan Civilisation. When they say 'all sites were urban' — that's the trap. Villages supported cities just like Keezhadi in Madurai shows a Tamil civilisation."

Q05 HARD Match the Following 🏆 PYQ 💎 Gem
Match the Harappan site with the river it is associated with:
Lothal – Kalibangan – Rupar – Harappa – Mohenjo-daro
✅ ANSWER
Lothal → Bhogavo (tributary of Sabarmati)
Kalibangan → Ghaggar (Hakra)
Rupar → Sutlej (Satluj)
Harappa → Ravi
Mohenjo-daro → Indus (Sindhu)
📌 EXPLANATION
Mohenjo-daro is situated on the banks of the Indus River — the most fertile land of the Indus, hence called "Garden of Sindhu." Harappa is on the Ravi. Kalibangan = Ghaggar (the dried-up Saraswati bed). Rupar = Sutlej. Lothal = Bhogavo/Sabarmati tributary near Gulf of Khambhat.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
This match-the-following question type (Sites ↔ Rivers) is one of the most frequently repeated in TNPSC Group 2 and Group 4 exams. Expect it in 2026.
💎 LIVE GEM — MUST MEMORISE TRIO
Teacher: "Memorise just 3 pairs — the rest you can guess:
1. Mohenjo-daro → Indus (Sindhu = fertile land of the Indus)
2. Harappa → Ravi
3. Rupar → Sutlej
Student shortcut shared: 'Rupa Sattun' (Rupar-Sutlej sounded like 'Rupa satt de' — pay up!)"
💡 TIP
Think of it this way: Mohenjo-daro = Indus civilisation's heart = Indus river. Just like Keezhadi = Vaigai River, Madurai. Connect location logic.

Q15 HARD Trick/Trap 🏆 PYQ 💎 Gem
Which of the following statements about Lothal is INCORRECT?
(1) Lothal is found in the Gulf of Cambay (Khambhat). (2) It is a port city; dock is 37m long and 216m wide. (3) This city was connected with the Bhogavo river. (4) Foreign trade was conducted from this port.
✅ ANSWER
Statement (2) is INCORRECT — The actual dock dimensions are approximately 37m long and 21.8m wide, not 216m wide. (Some sources give 216m long × 37m wide — verify with your textbook; key point: exact numbers differ).
📌 EXPLANATION
Lothal: (1) ✅ Located in Gulf of Cambay/Khambhat, Gujarat. (3) ✅ Connected with Bhogavo River (tributary of Sabarmati). (4) ✅ Foreign trade with Mesopotamia confirmed. (2) ❌ The dimension "216m wide" is fabricated — the actual dock is much smaller. Lothal's dockyard is a man-made structure, the world's earliest known.
💎 LIVE GEM
Teacher: "Lothal's river = Bhogavo (Pogava). Kalibangan's river = Ghaggar. These two are most confused in exams. Also: the word 'Lothal' in Gujarati = LOTH (dead) + AL (place) = 'Mound of the Dead' — same meaning as Mohenjo-daro! Both mean 'Mound of the Dead' in their respective languages."
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Lothal's features — dock, location, connected river — appear repeatedly in TNPSC exams. The false dimension in statement (2) is a standard exam trap.

Q06 HARD Trick/Trap
Which of the following statements about the Harappan Civilisation is INCORRECT?
(1) Peepal tree and Acacia tree were considered sacred. (2) Clay female figurines represent female deities. (3) The practice of cremating dead bodies was NOT followed at Harappa. (4) They worshipped the proto-form of Shiva.
✅ ANSWER
Statement (3) is INCORRECT: Harappans DID follow cremation — that's why the burial sites are called "mounds of the dead" (Mohenjo-daro). The site itself is named after this practice.
📌 EXPLANATION
(1) ✅ Peepal (sacred fig) and Acacia were sacred trees. (2) ✅ Female clay figurines = mother goddess worship. (3) ❌ WRONG — cremation WAS practiced. (4) ✅ Pashupati/Vishupati seal shows proto-Shiva. The name "Mohenjo-daro" literally means "Mound of the Dead" because of the burial/cremation mounds.
⚠️ TRAP NOTE
The phrase "was NOT followed" in statement (3) is a classic exam trap. Students read "cremation was not followed" and assume it's true — but Harappans DID cremate. The statement is FALSE because cremation WAS indeed practiced.
Q08 MEDIUM Factual
What type of pottery is associated with the Indus Valley Civilisation that shows the world's earliest evidence?
(Options: Cut, Knapped, Perforated, Polished — i.e., Incised, Knapped, Perforated, Polished)
✅ ANSWER
Polished pottery (Lustrous / Burnished Red Ware). The Harappans used red-coloured pottery with black painted designs — a hallmark of the civilisation.
📌 EXPLANATION
The Indus Valley pottery was made of red clay (hence red colour), decorated with black paint. The sophisticated wheel-made, polished pottery is considered among the earliest evidence of advanced ceramic technology globally. Animal motifs were painted on these vessels.
💎 LIVE GEM
Teacher: "The pots were red because of red clay. To distinguish them, they used black colour on top. Then drew animal shapes. This is what the 6th Standard textbook describes — red pots with black artwork. They were highly sophisticated for their time."
Q13 HARD Conceptual
Which of the following are CORRECT about the Harappan people and culture?
(1) More than one group existed in the Indus Valley Civilisation. (2) Various occupational groups existed: farmers, pastoralists, hunters, food-gatherers. (3) Exactly five groups existed. (4) Fort-style citadel city planning is still in practice today.
✅ ANSWER
Statements (1) and (2) are CORRECT. Statement (3) "exactly five groups" is wrong — number was unspecified/more. Statement (4) is discussed separately (Q29).
📌 EXPLANATION
One group had 30–40 members. The Indus Valley had multiple occupational groups: farmers, cattle-rearers, hunters, fishermen, food-gatherers. "Exactly five" is not mentioned in any standard textbook — it's a fabricated number. The civilisation had at least 4 distinct occupational groups.
🔴 CRITICAL — STUDENT NOTE
Teacher corrected students: "One group = 30 to 40 people. NOT 10–15. Many students answered 10–15 — that's wrong. 30–40 people formed one social group/community in the Indus Valley."

🐘 Animals, Seals & Symbols

Q09 MEDIUM Factual 🏆 PYQ
Name the four animals depicted on the Pashupati seal found at Mohenjo-daro.
✅ ANSWER
The four animals on the Pashupati seal: Elephant, Tiger (Pulli), Rhinoceros (Kaṇṭāmiruham), Buffalo (Erumei).
📌 EXPLANATION
The Pashupati (also called Vishupati) seal shows a figure seated in a yoga posture surrounded by four animals: elephant (east), tiger (west), rhinoceros (one side), buffalo (other side). This proto-Shiva figure is considered the earliest representation of Lord Shiva.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
This question appears in TNPSC Group 2/4 almost every cycle. The four animals must be memorised: Elephant + Tiger + Rhinoceros + Buffalo. All confirmed by the teacher.
💡 TIP
Confirmed: Elephant ✅ (river civilisation), Tiger ✅ (forest), Rhinoceros ✅ (extra/unique), Buffalo ✅ (confirmed). The seal confirms: (a) Elephant confirmed, (b) Tiger confirmed, (c) Rhinoceros confirmed — extra animal distinguishing this from the general list, (d) Buffalo confirmed.
Q10 EASY Factual 💎 Gem
Which animal was NOT known to the Harappan people?
✅ ANSWER
Horse — Harappans had no knowledge of horses.
📌 EXPLANATION
No horse bones or horse depictions found in any Harappan site. Also: Harappans had no knowledge of IRON (Bronze Age civilisation). Both "horse" and "iron" are the standard "unknown to Harappans" facts.
💎 LIVE GEM — HISTORICAL CONSEQUENCE
Teacher: "This is why Alexander the Great succeeded in India — he came on horseback, which Indians (Harappan descendants) were unfamiliar with. Alexander's intelligence said Indians had no horses but had elephants. Alexander thought: 'I have horses, they have elephants — I can win.' But when he saw 1000s of elephants, he was shocked! His army refused to go further. So lack of horse knowledge from Harappan times shaped Indian military history 1000+ years later!"

🏺 Trade, Economy & Material Culture

Q11, Q12, Q20, Q23–Q24, Q30, Q40, Q45, Q47
Q11 MEDIUM Factual
Which ancient inscription/text records trading connections between Mesopotamia and Harappa?
✅ ANSWER
Cuneiform tablets/letters (யூனிஃபார்ம் எழுத்துக்கள்) — Mesopotamian cuneiform records mention trade with "Meluhha" (identified as the Indus Valley).
📌 EXPLANATION
Mesopotamia (modern Iraq) is one of the world's oldest civilisations — 6500+ years old. Cuneiform writing records mention goods imported from Meluhha (Indus Valley): gold, carnelian beads, ivory, lapis lazuli. These cuneiform tablets are proof of Indus-Mesopotamia trade connections.
Q12 EASY Factual
Harappans used which stone to make blades and other tools?
(Options: Rohri chert, Steatite, Vidorium, Carnelian)
✅ ANSWER
Rohri chert — used for making blades and other cutting tools.
📌 EXPLANATION
Carnelian is a reddish semi-precious stone — used for making jewellery/beads, NOT tools. Steatite (soapstone) = used for seals. Rohri chert = used for blades/tools. The question tests whether students know the difference between decorative use vs. tool use.
Q20 HARD Assertion-Reason
Assertion (A): Indus Valley people did not use metal coins.
Reason (R): They did not have the technology to make metal coins.
✅ ANSWER
Both A and R are TRUE, and R is the correct explanation of A. Answer = B (Both true, R explains A).
📌 EXPLANATION
A is true: Harappans did NOT use metal coins — they used a barter system. R is also true: They lacked coin-minting technology (though they were skilled metalworkers, coinage was not developed). R correctly explains why A is true. This is a classic Assertion-Reason question where students must check: (1) Is A true? (2) Is R true? (3) Does R explain A?
⚠️ TRAP NOTE
Teacher warned: "Many students mark A as FALSE because they assume 'negative statement = wrong.' But 'they did NOT use metal coins' is a TRUE statement! They genuinely did NOT use coins. Don't let the negative phrasing confuse you."

Lapis Lazuli → Rajasthan / Shortugai
📌 EXPLANATION
Carnelian is a red semi-precious stone found at Lothal — used for beads (jewellery), NOT tools. Teacher confirmed: Answer = C (Carnelian = Lothal is the wrong match they were testing — Carnelian was found/processed at Lothal).
⚠️ TRAP NOTE
Carnelian is frequently confused with a tool-making material. It is a jewellery/bead stone. Steatite = seals. Rohri chert = tools/blades. Carnelian = beads/jewellery.
Q24 MEDIUM Factual 💎 Gem
What does the word "Loth" mean in the Gujarati word "Lothal"?
✅ ANSWER
"Loth" in Gujarati = Dead / Death. So "Lothal" = "Mound of the Dead" (same meaning as Mohenjo-daro in Sindhi).
💎 LIVE GEM
Teacher: "Lothal = Loth (dead) + Al (place) in Gujarati = 'Place of the Dead.' Mohenjo-daro = 'Mound of the Dead' in Sindhi. TWO different sites, TWO different languages — SAME meaning: Mound/Place of the Dead. Both were named because of burial mounds found there. Also: 'Lok' (लोक) in Hindi/Sanskrit = 'People' — so Lok Sabha = 'People's Assembly'. Different from 'Loth'!"

Q40 EASY Factual 🏆 PYQ
Which crop was NOT cultivated by the Harappan people?
✅ ANSWER
Sugarcane — Harappans did NOT cultivate sugarcane.
📌 EXPLANATION
Harappan cultivated crops: wheat, barley, sesame, mustard, peas, dates, cotton (first in the world — "Sindon" in Greek/Sindhi). Sugarcane cultivation came later in Indian history (associated with later Vedic/early classical period). Cotton was so associated with the Indus that Greeks called it "Sindon" (from Sindhu/Sindh).
🏆 PYQ ALERT
"Which crop did Harappans NOT cultivate?" — frequently asked. Sugarcane is the standard answer. Also note: cotton cultivation was FIRST done by Harappans — this is a positive PYQ fact.
Q45 EASY Factual

Agriculture (farming) — was the primary occupation. Secondary: cattle-rearing, then hunting, then fishing.
Q47 EASY Factual
Which of the following are TRUE about Indus Valley merchants in Mesopotamia?

✅ ANSWER
Both statements (1) and (2) are TRUE. Answer = Both are correct.
📌 EXPLANATION
Archaeological and textual evidence shows Indus traders stayed in Mesopotamia (trading colonies). Cotton — the Indus Valley's signature crop — was their primary export good. The Greeks called cotton "Sindon" (from Sindhu) because they first encountered it through Indus Valley trade.

Q36 MEDIUM Conceptual

✅ ANSWER
Both statements (1) and (2) are correct.

The Harappan society was matriarchal (mother-worshipping). Thousands of terracotta female figurines found = mother goddess worship. Q44 MEDIUM Conceptual
What is considered the primary cause of the decline of the Indus Valley Civilisation?
(Options: Climate change, Earthquake/tectonic, Aryan invasion, Floods)

Floods — considered the primary/most likely cause, ahead of the other theories.

Q46 EASY Factual
Dholavira is described as the oldest ___ city (fill in the blank from context of the session).
✅ ANSWER
Dholavira is described as the oldest Harappan city / one of the oldest planned cities with a three-part division (citadel, middle town, lower town).

Dholavira is unique in having a THREE-PART city plan (not just two). It also had the world's first known signboard (10 large Indus script letters found at its north gate). In 2021, it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Lothal — the port city of the Indus Valley Civilisation. It had the world's earliest man-made dockyard and conducted overseas trade with Mesopotamia.

📋 Session Summary

10 Key Takeaways From This Session
  • Harappa (Pakistan Punjab) & Mohenjo-daro (Pakistan Sindh) are NOT in India — both in Pakistan. Indian sites: Lothal/Dholavira (Gujarat), Kalibangan (Rajasthan), Rupar & Rakhigarhi (Haryana).
  • Mohenjo-daro = "Mound of the Dead" (Sindhi) = Indus River. Lothal = "Place of the Dead" (Gujarati) = Bhogavo River. Harappa = Ravi River. Kalibangan = Ghaggar.
  • Excavators: Charles Masson (first visitor 1826) → Alexander Cunningham (first recorded antiquities) → R.D. Banerji (major excavation) → Sir John Marshall (supervision). Mohenjo-daro: Mortimer Wheeler. Harappa: Daya Ram Sahni.
  • 6 major cities + 200 villages = the Harappan Civilisation structure. Not all sites were urban — statement "all sites were urban" is FALSE.
  • Pashupati Seal (Mohenjo-daro): 4 animals = Elephant + Tiger + Rhinoceros + Buffalo. This is proto-Shiva.
  • Harappans did NOT know: (1) Horse, (2) Iron. Bronze Age civilisation. Also did NOT use metal coins (barter system).
  • Lothal: only site with man-made dockyard, Gulf of Cambay, ivory scale, carnelian bead-making. Chanhu-daro: only site WITHOUT a citadel.
  • Dholavira (Khadir Island, Rann of Kutch, Gujarat): unique 3-part city, world's first signboard, UNESCO World Heritage 2021.
  • Indus script: undeciphered pictographic (सिधु script). Tamil Nadu CM offered $1 million prize for decipherment. ~400 signs identified, boustrophedon direction.
  • Decline cause (primary): Floods. Other factors: climate change, tectonic activity. Aryan invasion theory is outdated/discredited for modern TNPSC.
