01 What is History?
Q1
The word "History" is derived from which language and what does it mean?
வரலாறு என்ற சொல் எந்த மொழி சொல்லிலிருந்து பெறப்பட்டது? அதன் அர்த்தம் என்ன?
EASYFactualGr.4/VAO
✅ Answer
The word "History" is derived from the Greek word "Historia", which means "learning through inquiry / learning by investigation."
📌 Explanation
Historia (Greek) → learning by asking, investigating, and finding out through questioning. This reflects that knowledge of history is built by questioning sources and witnesses.
💡
TIP — Memory
Think of History = Investigation. You investigate the past to learn it.
Q2
Statement I: History is a chronological record of past events. Statement II: Time in history is measured in years (AD/BC). Are both correct?
கூற்று 1: வரலாறு என்பது கடந்த கால நிகழ்வுகளின் காலவரிசை பதிவு. கூற்று 2: வரலாற்றின் காலம் ஆண்டுகளில் கணக்கிடப்படுகிறது.
EASYFactual
✅ Answer
Both statements are correct.
📌 Explanation
History records the past in chronological order. Time is counted in years — traditionally as BC (Before Christ) and AD (Anno Domini). Modern secular usage replaces these with BCE (Before Common Era) and CE (Common Era).
💎
LIVE GEM — Modern Calendar Language
BC/AD were based on Jesus Christ's birth year. Modern historians now use BCE/CE (Common Era) to remove religious bias while referring to the same years.
02 Stone Age — Map-Based Questions
Q3
Which of the following places yielded Neolithic (New Stone Age) tools? (Options include: Mehrgarh, Maakara, Attirampakkam, Athichanallur, Daojali Hading)
புதிய கற்கால கருவிகள் கண்டுபிடிக்கப்பட்ட இடங்கள் எவை?
MEDIUMFactualGr.1/2
✅ Answer
Mehrgarh, Maakara, and Daojali Hading (Options 1, 2, 5)
📌 Explanation
Mehrgarh (Baluchistan, Pakistan) — pioneer settlement, precursor to Indus Valley Civilisation. Maakara — adjacent region also yielding Neolithic tools. Daojali Hading (Northeast India) — also confirmed Neolithic site. Attirampakkam = Palaeolithic. Athichanallur = Bronze/Iron Age.
🏆
PYQ ALERT
Map-based questions on Stone Age sites appear in TNPSC Group 2 (2018, 2022). Mehrgarh is the most-tested answer here.
💡
MAP TIP (Textbook p. 123 / New ed. p. 108)
Open the map and study the symbols: square = Palaeolithic, triangle = Bronze Age. Mehrgarh is in Pakistan (Baluchistan). Hathnagar/Hading is in West Bengal.
Q4
Which sites yielded Iron Age tools?
இரும்பு கால கற்கால கருவிகள் கண்டுபிடிக்கப்பட்ட இடம் எது?
MEDIUMFactualGr.1/2/4
✅ Answer
Hallur and Athichanallur (Thoothukudi district, Tamil Nadu)
💎
LIVE GEM — Memory Trick
Iron Age → Hallur. "Iron" is heavy (அல்லு = heavy in Tamil slang). So: Iron is heavy → Hallur. Both Hallur and Athichanallur = Iron Age. Athichanallur is in Thoothukudi, TN.
Q5
Which sites yielded Bronze Age tools?
வெண்கல காலத்தில் கண்டுபிடிக்கப்பட்ட கருவிகள் கண்டுபிடிக்கப்பட்ட இடங்கள் எவை?
MEDIUMFactualGr.1/2
✅ Answer
Lothal (Gujarat) and Athichanallur (Tamil Nadu). Answer: Options 1 and 4.
📌 Explanation
Lothal is an IVC site with a natural harbour on a tributary of the Sabarmati river, Gujarat. Athichanallur yields both Bronze and Iron Age artifacts — remember: South India (especially Athichanallur and Keeladi) has Bronze + Iron Age.
💎
LIVE GEM — Quick Table
Palaeolithic (Old Stone Age) → Chennai (Attirampakkam, Pallavaram), Bheembetka (MP), Hunsagi Valley (near Goa)
Neolithic (New Stone Age) → Mehrgarh, Maakara, Daojali Hading
Bronze Age → Lothal (Gujarat), Athichanallur
Iron Age → Hallur, Athichanallur
Athichanallur appears in both Bronze AND Iron Age — a common trap!
🪤
TNPSC TRAP
Athichanallur is often placed only in Iron Age. But it appears in both Bronze and Iron Age in the textbook. Watch options carefully.
Q6
Which sites yielded Palaeolithic (Old Stone Age) tools?
பழைய கற்கால கருவிகள் கண்டுபிடிக்கப்பட்ட இடங்கள் எவை?
MEDIUMFactualGr.2/4
✅ Answer
Bheembetka (Madhya Pradesh), Attirampakkam (Chennai), Hunsagi Valley (near Goa). Answer: Options 2, 3, 5.
📌 Explanation
Bheembetka (MP) has famous cave paintings of a woman holding a child with a bow aimed at a fleeing animal — sculpted ~2000 years ago. Attirampakkam is near Chennai. Hunsagi Valley is near Goa.
💎
LIVE GEM — Tamil Nadu Stone Age Logic
Chennai / Pallavaram / Attirampakkam = Palaeolithic (Old Stone Age)
Athichanallur / Keeladi (Thoothukudi/Madurai) = Bronze + Iron Age
So: if Chennai → Old Stone; if deep South TN → Bronze/Iron.
💡
TIP — Bheembetka (Group 4 Frequent)
Bheembetka is in Madhya Pradesh. It features cave paintings from the Palaeolithic era. Often tested in Group 4 as "which state has Palaeolithic cave art?"
03 Proto-History, Dating & Historical Sources
Q7
What is the period between the use of stone tools and the discovery of writing called?
கற்கருவிகளை பயன்படுத்தியதற்கும் எழுதும் முறைகளை கண்டுபிடித்ததற்கும் இடைப்பட்ட காலம் என்ன?
HARDTrapGr.1
✅ Answer
Proto-History (வரலாற்று தொடக்க காலம் — Proto-historic Period)
📌 Explanation
Three periods:
• Prehistoric = Before stone tools and writing (no record at all)
• Proto-historic = Stone tools used but writing not yet discovered (between the two)
• Historic = Writing discovered and used (records exist)
🔴
CRITICAL — Most-Missed Question
Students answer "Prehistoric period" — WRONG. The correct answer is Proto-historic / Proto-History. "Pre-historic" = before tools. "Proto-historic" = between tools and writing. Textbook p. 125 (old) / p. 110 (new).
🏆
PYQ ALERT — Group 1 Level
This distinction (Prehistoric / Proto-historic / Historic) is a confirmed Group 1 concept. Expect it as a Statement-based question.
Q8
Statement I: The period between History and Pre-history is called Proto-Historic period. Statement II: In the Proto-historic period, written records exist but we cannot understand their meaning.
கூற்று 1: வரலாற்றுக்கும் வரலாற்றுக்கு முந்தைய காலத்திற்கும் இடைப்பட்ட காலம் வரலாற்று தொடக்க காலமாகும். கூற்று 2: வரலாற்று தொடக்க காலத்தில் எழுதப்பட்ட பதிவுகள் உள்ளன — தற்போது அவற்றின் பொருளை நாம் புரிந்து கொள்ள முடியவில்லை.
HARDTrapGr.1
✅ Answer
Statement I is correct. Statement II is correct. → Answer: Both are correct.
📌 Explanation
Wait — textbook says: records exist in the Proto-historic period, BUT we cannot understand their meaning (they are pictographic/undeciphered scripts like IVC script). So Statement II is also correct. Answer: A (both correct) if such option exists, or "Statement I correct, Statement II correct."
🔴
CRITICAL — Teacher Correction During Live Class
Many students answered "A" (both correct) initially but were told to re-check. The teacher confirmed: Statement I ✅ correct. Statement II ✅ correct. The Indus Valley script is a real example — records exist but are undeciphered. Group 1 students who marked "A wrong" were making an error.
💎
LIVE GEM — IVC Script Connection
The Indus Valley script = perfect example of Proto-historic writing: it EXISTS on seals, but no one can read it yet. This is why the IVC period is classified as Proto-historic, not Historic.
Q9
Statement I: The science related to the history of coins is Numismatics. Statement II: The branch dealing with inscriptions on stone/copper plates is Epigraphy.
நாணவியல் — நாணயங்கள் பற்றிய அறிவியல். கல்வெட்டியல் — கல்வெட்டுகளில் பதிவு செய்யப்பட்ட செய்திகள் ஆராய்வதற்கான துறை.
EASYFactual
✅ Answer
Both statements are correct.
Basic definitions — Numismatics (நாணவியல்) = coins. Epigraphy (கல்வெட்டியல்) = inscriptions. Both are standard historical sources.
Q10
Statement I: "Dhamma" is a Sanskrit word. Statement II: In Prakrit it is called "Dharma."
தம்மா என்பது சமஸ்கிருத மொழிச்சொல். இது பிராகிருதத்தில் தர்மா எனப்படுகிறது.
HARDTrapGr.1/2
✅ Answer
Both statements are WRONG.
📌 Explanation
"Dhamma" is a Prakrit word (not Sanskrit). The Sanskrit equivalent is "Dharma." In English, it is called "Dharma." So Statement I is wrong (Dhamma ≠ Sanskrit) and Statement II is wrong (Prakrit does not call it "Dharma" — Prakrit calls it "Dhamma"; Sanskrit calls it "Dharma").
🔴
CRITICAL — Almost everyone got this wrong
Teacher said: "If you marked 'A' (both correct) you are wrong!" The trap is assuming Dhamma = Sanskrit. Dhamma = Prakrit. Dharma = Sanskrit. Dharma = English too. Textbook p. 127 — check glossary terms.
💎
LIVE GEM — Quick Fix
Asoka's edicts used Prakrit language (not Sanskrit) to reach common people. The word "Dhamma" in his edicts = Prakrit. Later scholars transliterated it to "Dharma" (Sanskrit/English).
🏆
PYQ ALERT
Dhamma vs Dharma language distinction has appeared in TNPSC Group 1 Prelims (2019).
04 Prehistoric Human Life — Tools & Animals
Q11
How did ancient (Palaeolithic) man gather his food?
பழங்கால மனிதன் தனது உணவை சேகரிக்க மேற்கொண்ட நடவடிக்கை எது?
EASYFactual
✅ Answer
By Hunting (வேட்டையாடுதல்)
Q12
Statement (Assertion): Palaeolithic man took dogs with him while hunting. Reason: While living in caves, dogs sensed approaching animals using their smell-sense and warned him.
கூற்று: பழைய கற்கால மனிதர்கள் வேட்டையாட செல்லும்போது நாய்களை உடன் சென்றனர். காரணம்: குகைகளில் தங்கியிருந்த போது விலங்குகள் வருவதை நாய்கள் தமது மோப்ப சக்தியினால் அறிந்து உணர்த்தின.
MEDIUMConceptualGr.2/4
✅ Answer
Both Assertion and Reason are correct — and the Reason explains the Assertion.
💎
LIVE GEM — Evolution of the Dog
2 lakh (200,000) years ago: no dog breed existed. The ancestor was the wolf (ஓநாய்). Early humans gradually domesticated wolves → over thousands of years wolves evolved (through human selection) into dogs. The dog is one of the fastest-evolving domesticated animals in evolutionary history. This is a Science + History crossover fact.
🔴
CRITICAL FACT — First Domesticated Animal
The dog was the first domesticated animal in human history. Humans have kept dogs for ~10,000+ years — from cave-dwelling days to the present day.
Q13
Find the wrong pair: (a) Palaeolithic – stone tools & cave paintings; (b) Copper plates – historical source; (c) Cats – first domesticated animal.
தவறான இணையை கண்டுபிடி: பழைய கற்காலம் – கற்கருவிகள் / பாறை ஓவியங்கள் / குகை சுவர்கள் | செப்பு தகடுகள் – வரலாற்று ஆதாரம் | பூனைகள் – முதலில் பழக்கப்படுத்தப்பட்ட விலங்கு
MEDIUMTrap
✅ Answer
Wrong pair: Cats – first domesticated animal. The correct answer is Dogs (not Cats).
First domesticated animal = Dog. Cats came much later in agricultural societies (to protect grain stores). Option (c) is wrong.
Q14
Find the wrong statement about cave paintings of Palaeolithic man.
பழைய கற்கால மனிதனின் குகை சுவர் ஓவியங்கள் பற்றிய தவறான கூற்று எது?
MEDIUMConceptual
✅ Answer
"They painted in many / multiple colours" — this is WRONG.
📌 Explanation
Correct facts: Paintings were on rocks/cave walls; hunting scenes were drawn; painted to show family members how to hunt. Wrong statement: They used many colours. Palaeolithic man could only use whatever natural pigment was available (from plants, minerals) — they did NOT have a full colour palette.
💎
LIVE GEM — Practical Logic
Teacher asked students to think practically: "If you want green paint 2000 years ago, you'd crush a leaf. That's all you get." So they used what nature gave them — NOT multiple designer colours. This eliminates the "many colours" option easily.
Q15
Match the following: Rock paintings / Written records / Asoka (famous ruler) / Religious literature
MEDIUMApplication
✅ Answer
Rock paintings → Way of life information (4) | Written records → Copper plates (1) | Asoka → Great king (2) | Religious literature → Thevaram / Saiva texts (3)
Answer: C (4-1-2-3). Thevaram is a Saiva (Shaiva) text — Shaivism follows Lord Shiva. Teacher noted: Saiva = relating to Shiva (not to be confused with vegetarian "saivam").
💎
LIVE GEM — Saivam vs Vainavam
சைவம் (Saivam) = worshippers of Shiva · வைணவம் (Vainavam) = worshippers of Vishnu.
Common student error: confusing "சைவம்" with vegetarianism ("saiva food"). In historical context, Saivam = a Hindu sect, NOT food preference. Thevaram and Periya Puranam are Saiva texts.
Q16
Statement I: Palaeolithic stone tools were found at Attirampakkam, Chennai. Statement II: Ancient artefacts excavated by archaeologists are preserved by the Archaeological department.
கூற்று 1: பழைய கற்கால கருவிகள் சென்னை அத்திரம்பாக்கத்தில் கண்டுபிடிக்கப்பட்டுள்ளன. கூற்று 2: பழங்கால மனிதர்கள் பயன்படுத்திய பொருட்கள் தொல்லியல் துறையினரால் பாதுகாக்கப்படுகின்றன.
EASYFactual
✅ Answer
Both correct. Reason also correctly explains the assertion.
Pallavaram and Attirampakkam (Chennai) are confirmed Palaeolithic sites in Tamil Nadu. The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) preserves such finds.
Q17
The objects excavated from ancient sites to understand past lifestyles are called what?
பண்டைய காலத்தில் வாழ்ந்த மனிதர்கள் பயன்படுத்திய பொருட்கள் அகழாய்வு மூலம் தோண்டி எடுக்கப்படுகின்றன — இவை என்னென்ன?
EASYFactual
✅ Answer
புதைப்படிமங்கள் / Fossils and Artefacts (answer option: Buried remains/artefacts — புதைப்படிவங்கள்)
The correct textbook answer is "Fossils/Buried artefacts" (புதைப்படிவங்கள்). Not "museums" or "bones" alone — the full category of excavated objects is called buried artefacts or fossils.
Q18
Where did Palaeolithic man mainly live?
பழைய கற்கால மனிதன் பெரும்பாலும் வாழ்ந்த இடங்கள் எது?
EASYFactual
✅ Answer
Caves (குகைகளில்)
Palaeolithic man lived in caves. He had no permanent settlements. He used the dog (domesticated from wolf) to guard the cave entrance and warn of approaching animals.
05 Asoka & Maurya Empire
Q19
Under whose reign did Buddhism spread to various parts of Asia?
யாருடைய ஆட்சியில் புத்த மதம் ஆசியாவின் பல்வேறு பகுதிகளுக்கு பரவியது?
EASYFactualAll groups
✅ Answer
Emperor Asoka (the Great)
Asoka sent his son Mahinda to Sri Lanka and his daughter Sanghamitra to Japan and other Asian countries to spread Buddhism. This was a key policy after his conversion following the Kalinga War.
Q20
After which war did Asoka embrace Buddhism and dedicate his life to spreading peace and righteousness?
_____ போருக்கு பின் அசோகர் புத்த மதத்தை தழுவி அமைதியையும் அறத்தையும் பரப்புவதற்கான தன் வாழ்வை அர்ப்பணித்தார்.
EASYFactualAll groups
✅ Answer
Kalinga War (கலிங்க போர்)
💎
LIVE GEM — Kalinga's Other Names
Kalinga is present-day Odisha.
Ancient name for the plains of Odisha: Utkala (உத்கல்).
This appears in the National Song "Jana Gana Mana": "Dravida Utkala Banga"
→ Dravida = South India | Utkala = Odisha | Banga = Bengal/Bangladesh
Utkala = Odisha is a confirmed Geography question in TNPSC Group 1.
💎
LIVE GEM — Kamarupa
Kamarupa = ancient name for present-day Assam. This is a PYQ from Geography section.
🏆
PYQ ALERT
Utkala = Odisha appeared in TNPSC Group 1 Geography and History paper. Kamarupa = Assam also tested.
Q21
The 24-spoked wheel in the Indian national flag — from whose emblem / pillar was it taken?
திரைவட குடியில் இடம் பெற்றுள்ள 24 ஆரக்கால் சக்கரம் அசோகர் நெறிவே தூண முத்திரையிலிருந்து பெறப்பட்டது.
MEDIUMFactualGr.1/2
✅ Answer
From Asoka's Dharma Chakra Pillar at Sarnath (சார்நாத் தூண). Sarnath is in Uttar Pradesh.
💎
LIVE GEM — Sarnath
Sarnath (Uttar Pradesh) has the Asoka Pillar and a Shanti Stupa. The Asoka Chakra (24 spokes) on our national flag comes from the Sarnath Lion Capital of Asoka.
🏆
PYQ ALERT
"Where is the Asoka Pillar from which the national flag emblem is taken?" = Sarnath, UP. Appeared in TNPSC Group 2 (2021).
Q22
Through whose historical research did Asoka's inscriptions become known to the world?
கீழ்கண்ட உற்றுள் யாருடைய வரலாற்று ஆய்வுகள் மூலம் அசோகரின் குறிப்புகள் வெளிவந்தன? (Options: William Jones, James Prinsep, Alexander Cunningham, Charles Allen)
HARDFactualGr.1
✅ Answer
William Jones, James Prinsep, and Alexander Cunningham (Options 1, 2, 3)
📌 Explanation
• William Jones — orientalist scholar, linguistic research
• James Prinsep — deciphered Brahmi script, enabling reading of Asoka's edicts
• Alexander Cunningham — first Director-General of ASI (Archaeological Survey of India), founded 1861
• Charles Allen — wrote the book "The Search for the Lost Emperor" about Asoka (he is NOT primarily a research scholar of edicts)
💎
LIVE GEM — The Book About Asoka
Charles Allen wrote "The Search for the Lost Emperor" — a popular book about how Asoka's story was rediscovered. "If he hadn't written it, Asoka might have remained a 'lost emperor' even today," said the teacher.
🪤
TNPSC TRAP
Charles Allen is IN the textbook but is NOT part of the "research scholars" group. He wrote a popular book. The three who made Asoka's inscriptions public = Jones + Prinsep + Cunningham.
Q23
Who wrote "The Search for the Lost Emperor" (about Asoka)?
சர்ச் ஆப் த இந்தியன் லாஸ்ட் எம்பரர் — இந்தியாவின் தொலைந்த ஒரு அரசர் — யாருடைய புத்தகம்?
MEDIUMFactualGr.1/2
✅ Answer
Charles Allen
💡
TIP — Memory
"Lost Emperor" → Charles Allen. If it were not for Charles Allen's book, Asoka would have truly remained "lost" to popular history.
Q24
What did Asoka consider would happen to his history if Charles Allen had not written/publicised it?
_____ செய்யவிட்டிருந்தால் அசோகரின் வரலாறு மறைக்கப்பட்ட வரலாறாகவே இருந்திருக்கும்.

Q59
What was the total area of IVC in square kilometres? How many large cities? How many villages?
சிந்துவெளி நாகரிகத்தின் பரப்பு — எத்தனை லட்சம் சதுர கிலோமீட்டர்? எத்தனை பெரிய நகரம்? எத்தனை கிராமங்கள்?
HARDFactualGr.1
✅ Answer
Area: 13 lakh sq km (1.3 million sq km) | Large cities: 6 | Villages: 200+
💎
LIVE GEM — Six Major Cities
The 6 major cities of IVC: Harappa, Mohenjodaro, Lothal, Shortugai, Rakhigarhi, Dholavira (and others like Kalibangan).
"Remember Lothal and Rakhigarhi especially — they are frequently tested."
Q60
What time period does the IVC belong to? What was the overall duration?
ஹரப்பா நாகரிகம் சார்ந்த காலம் எது? சிந்துவெளி நாகரிக காலம் எந்த காலம்?
MEDIUMFactualGr.1/2
✅ Answer
IVC period: 3300 BCE to 1900 BCE | Age: Bronze Age / Copper Age (also called Chalcolithic)
💡
TIP — Period Comparison
Mesopotamia: 3500–2000 BCE
IVC: 3300–1900 BCE → They were contemporaries and declined together!
Egypt: 3100–1100 BCE (lasted longer)
China: 1700–1100 BCE (came last)
Q61
The "Dancing Girl" statue of IVC was estimated to be how many years old? Who said this?
நடனமாடும் பெண் சிலை 3000 ஆண்டுகளுக்கு முற்பட்டது என கணித்தவர் யார்?
HARDFactual
✅ Answer
Estimated at over 3000 years old — statement made by John Marshall
Marshall reportedly said: "Looking at this, it feels like it could not have been made 3000 years ago — it looks so modern." This quote is in the textbook context box.
11 Miscellaneous & World History Connections
Q62
What is a Ziggurat? Who built it? For which god?
ஜிகரட் என்றால் என்ன? யார் கட்டினார்? எந்த கடவுளுக்கு?
HARDFactualGr.1
✅ Answer
Ziggurat = A stepped/tiered temple-tower built in Mesopotamia | Built by King Ur-Nammu in the city of Ur | Dedicated to the Moon God (Sin / Chandra Kadavul)
💎
LIVE GEM — Ziggurat = Twin Temple
Ziggurat is sometimes called "twin temple" or "double temple" in textbook context. Compare with: Abu Simbel (Egypt) = also twin temples. Ziggurat (Mesopotamia) ↔ Abu Simbel (Egypt) = common pair question.
Q63
The Great Pyramid of Giza — how many tons does a single block weigh?
கிசே பிரமிடு — எத்தனை டன் எடை?
HARDFactualGr.1
✅ Answer
Each block of the Great Pyramid of Giza (Cheops Pyramid) weighs approximately 15 tons.
Built by King Khufu (Cheops). Located at Giza, Egypt. One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Q64
Who were the first to invent writing in the world?
முதல் எழுத்து வடிவம் யாரால் உருவாக்கப்பட்டது?
MEDIUMFactualGr.1/2
✅ Answer
Sumerians (Mesopotamia) invented the first writing system — called Cuneiform script (~3200 BCE)

Q65
What archaeological tool/technique is used to survey land for buried sites? What carbon-based dating method is used?
MEDIUMFactual
✅ Answer
Land survey: Magnetic Scanner (Magnetic Survey / காந்தப்புல வருடி) | Dating method: Carbon-14 (C-14) dating / Radiocarbon dating
Q66
What animal designs/patterns appear on IVC pottery?
பானைகளில் காணப்படக்கூடிய வடிவம் எது?
EASYFactual
✅ Answer
Animal shapes / geometric animal designs (விலங்குகளின் வடிவியல் அமைப்பு) — dogs, bulls, birds etc. were painted on pots.
Q67
What is the special/unique feature of the Indus Valley Civilisation?
சிந்துவெளி நாகரிகத்தினுடைய சிறப்பு அம்சம் என்ன?
EASYFactualAll groups
✅ Answer
IVC is a Planned Urban Civilisation (திட்டமிடப்பட்ட நகர நாகரிகம்) — meaning cities were designed with a plan before construction, with grid streets, drainage, and public buildings.
🔴
CRITICAL — One-Line Answer to Memorise
"IVC = World's first PLANNED URBAN CIVILISATION." This is the most important single-line description of IVC. Include this in every essay/short answer.

13 2026 Exam Predictions
PREDICTION 01 · TNPSC Group 1 · HIGH PROBABILITY
"The script of the Indus Valley Civilisation has not been deciphered yet. This is why IVC is classified as ____ period." (Fill in blank)
Answer: Proto-Historic Period. This connects Q7+Q8 from this session. Expect as a statement-correction or fill-in-blank.
PREDICTION 02 · TNPSC Group 1/2 · HIGH PROBABILITY
"Match the following — Harappa : Granary :: Mohenjodaro : ___" or "Great Bath was found at ___"
Answer: Great Bath / Mohenjodaro. The Harappa-Granary and Mohenjodaro-Great Bath distinction is a classic exam pair.
PREDICTION 03 · TNPSC Group 1 · MEDIUM PROBABILITY
"Who among the following scholars was NOT associated with bringing Asoka's history to light — William Jones / James Prinsep / Alexander Cunningham / Ashutosh Mukherjee?"
Answer: Ashutosh Mukherjee (not associated). This is a negative-type exclusion question based on Q22.
PREDICTION 04 · TNPSC All Groups · HIGH PROBABILITY
"Dhamma as used in Asoka's edicts belongs to which language?" OR "Dharma in Sanskrit = ___ in Prakrit"
Answer: Dhamma = Prakrit. Dharma = Sanskrit/English. Q10 was the most-missed in this session — expect it in exam.
PREDICTION 05 · TNPSC Group 1/2 · MEDIUM PROBABILITY
"The IVC extended from ___ in the north-east to ___ in the south" OR "Which state forms the southern boundary of IVC?"
Answer: Afghanistan (NE) → Maharashtra (South). The four-direction extent question based on Q57.
