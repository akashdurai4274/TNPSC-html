🍱 Section 1 — Noon Meal Scheme (மதிய உணவு திட்டம்)
Q 01 MEDIUM Factual
Choose the correct statement(s) about the Justice Party / Neethi Katchi:
Statement 1: In 1921, the Noon Meal Scheme was first implemented in Chennai (Madras) schools for the first time.
Statement 2: This scheme was first implemented in the Adayar (Adyar) area.
▼
✅ Answer
Option A — Only Statement 1 is correct.
Statement 1 is TRUE. Statement 2 is FALSE (Adyar is wrong — it was implemented at a school in Ayiram Vilakku / Thousand Lights area).
📌 Explanation

In 1921, the Justice Party government under P.T. Thyagarayar (Pitthi Thyagarayar) introduced the Noon Meal Scheme for the first time — in a single school in the Ayiram Vilakku (Thousand Lights) area of Madras, not Adyar. This predates Kamarajar's statewide implementation. The scheme was later discontinued due to lack of funds and was revived and expanded by Kamarajar across Tamil Nadu.

💎 Live Gem — Noon Meal Evolution Timeline

The teacher gave a brilliant one-shot timeline of the Noon Meal scheme evolution:

  • 1921 P.T. Thyagarayar — First implementation, Thousand Lights, Madras (1 school only, later stopped)
  • 1950s–60s Kamarajar — Expanded Noon Meal Scheme across all Tamil Nadu to encourage school attendance
  • MGR era M.G. Ramachandran — Added nutritional value → called "Satthunavoo Thittam" (Nutritious Food Scheme)
  • Jayalalithaa era — Added potatoes (urulaikizhangu) to the meal
  • Kalaignar era — Added egg (muttai) along with potato
  • Recent CM Stalin — Introduced Morning Breakfast Scheme (Kalai Unavu Thittam)
🏆 PYQ Alert

The origin location of the Noon Meal Scheme (Thousand Lights vs. Adyar) has been used as a trap in TNPSC Group 2 & Group 4 exams. The "Adyar" option is a classic distractor.

🪤 TRAP NOTE

Most students associate Noon Meal Scheme only with Kamarajar. The exam tests whether you know P.T. Thyagarayar started it in 1921 — 26 years before Indian Independence!

🏛️ Section 2 — Justice Party Government Schemes & Years
Q 02 HARD Factual
Match the following — choose the correct year for each scheme/institution established by the Justice Party government:
(i) State Industries Assistance Act (Thozhil Valarchikkaana Maanila Arasu Udavi Thittam)
(ii) Public Service Commission (Pothu Thervaanayam — TNPSC)
▼
✅ Answer
Option B — Only Statement 2 (Public Service Commission) is correct.
• State Industries Act: 1922 (NOT 1923)
• Staff Selection Board: 1924 → became Public Service Commission in 1929
📌 Explanation

The Justice Party ruled Madras Presidency from 1920–1937 (with a break 1926–1930). During this tenure they passed several landmark Acts. The Madras Public Service Commission was the first PSC established in India, initially as a Staff Selection Board in 1924, formally upgraded in 1929.

💎 Live Gem — India's First PSC

The teacher emphasized: Tamil Nadu's TNPSC is the first Public Service Commission established in India — not just Tamil Nadu. It originated from the Justice Party's Staff Selection Board (1924), which became the PSC in 1929. This is a frequently tested fact.

🪤 TRAP NOTE

The year 1923 is a common distractor for the State Industries Act — the correct year is 1922.

📜 Section 3 — Justice Party History, Origin & Key Leaders
Q 03 MEDIUM Factual
In 1944, the Justice Party was converted into Dravidar Kazhagam. At which city's conference was this conversion resolution passed? Who proposed the resolution?
▼
✅ Answer
City: Salem
Date: 27th August 1944
Resolution proposed by: Periyar E.V. Ramasamy (Thanthai Periyar) — merged Justice Party + Self-Respect Movement into Dravidar Kazhagam
The resolution (theermanam) was moved by: Peraarignar Anna (C.N. Annadurai)
📌 Explanation

At the Salem Conference on 27 August 1944, Periyar announced the merger of the Justice Party and the Self-Respect Movement (Suyamariyathai Iyakkam) to form the Dravidar Kazhagam (DK). He declared that DK would not contest elections — its goal was to spread rationalism and self-respect among the masses, not seek votes. Anna (Annadurai) moved the formal resolution for this conversion.

Dravidar Kazhagam is the parent of all Dravidian parties in Tamil Nadu — DMK, AIADMK, and others trace their roots here.

💎 Live Gem — Critical Distinction

Two different persons, two different acts:

  • Periyar — announced & effected the conversion (changed Justice Party → Dravidar Kazhagam)
  • Peraarignar Anna (C.N. Annadurai) — moved the formal resolution (theermanam) at the Salem conference

This distinction has been specifically tested in TNPSC Group 2 exam. Don't confuse the two roles!

🏆 PYQ Alert

According to the teacher, this exact question — "Who moved the resolution to convert Justice Party to Dravidar Kazhagam?" — was asked in a previous TNPSC Group 2 paper. Answer: Anna (Annadurai).

Q 04 MEDIUM Factual
Under the Justice Party government, women were given the right to participate in elections (vote and contest) for the first time in Madras. In which year was this granted?
Options: 1920 / 1921 / 1922 / 1924
▼
✅ Answer
Option B — 1921
Women were granted voting rights AND the right to contest elections in Madras in 1921, under the Justice Party government.
📌 Explanation

The first direct elections in India (under the Montagu-Chelmsford reforms) were held in November 1920. Initially, only men with property qualifications could vote, and women had no electoral rights. In 1921, the Justice Party government passed two landmark laws: (1) the Noon Meal Scheme and (2) Women's Voting Rights and the right to contest elections — making Madras Presidency the first in India to grant women this right, 26 years before Indian Independence (1947).

💡 TIP — Connection to 106th Amendment

The teacher mentioned the 106th Constitutional Amendment (Women's Reservation Bill, 2023) in this context. Note: this amendment provides 33% reservation for women in Parliament and State Assemblies. However, it has not yet come into force (pending delimitation). Keep this updated fact in mind for current affairs questions.

Q 05 HARD Factual
Who was the first unofficial (non-official) Mayor / Chairman of the Madras Corporation? He was also known as "Vellai Udai Venthar" (Lord of White Robes) because of his love for white clothes.
▼
✅ Answer
P.T. Thyagarayar (Pitthi Thyagarayar)
One of the founding triumvirate of the Justice Party. Also the person who introduced the Noon Meal Scheme.
📌 Explanation

P.T. Thyagarayar was called "Vellai Udai Venthar" (King of White Attire) for always wearing white. He was a key founding figure of the Justice Party — one of three principal leaders:

  • Dr. T.M. Nair (Tharavath Madhavan Nair)
  • P.T. Thyagarayar
  • Raja of Panagal (Bobbili Raja / P. Ramarayaningar)

Note: P.T. Thyagarayar ≠ P.T. Rajan. P.T. Rajan was a different person who served as Chief Minister of Madras Presidency from April–August 1936.

The three most important leaders of the Justice Party (exam-critical):

🪤 TRAP NOTE

Don't confuse P.T. Thyagarayar with P.T. Rajan (later Chief Minister). They are two different people from the same party but different eras and roles.

Q 06 HARD Factual
The Madras Non-Brahmin Association was founded by two lawyers. Who were they, and in which year was it established?
▼
✅ Answer
Founders: P. Subramaniam and Purushothama Naidu
Year: 1909
📌 Explanation — The Origin Story of Justice Party

In 1908, Justice Sankar Nair (a Madras High Court Judge) gave a speech at the Madras University function calling for the abolition of the Varna system. Inspired by this, two lawyers — P. Subramaniam and Purushothama Naidu — founded the Madras Non-Brahmin Association in 1909.

The evolution chain:

  1. 1909 — Madras Non-Brahmin Association
  2. ~1912 — Became Madras United League
  3. ~1914 — Became Madras Dravidian Association
  4. ~1916 — Became South Indian Liberal Federation
  5. 1917 — Registered as Justice Party (Neethi Katchi) — for electoral purposes
💎 Live Gem — Why Justice Party Was Needed

The teacher explained the social context brilliantly: In the pre-Justice Party era, of 100 government vacancies, ~96 went to Brahmins (3% of the population) and only ~4 to non-Brahmins (97% of population). The Non-Brahmin movement sought social justice — not anti-religion, but equity in representation. This is the conceptual foundation of Tamil Nadu's reservation politics today.

Q 07 MEDIUM Factual
Who was the first President of the Madras United League (Madras Aikkiya Kazhagam)?
Options: Raja of Panagal / Dr. C. Natesanar / Veeraswami Naidu / Thuraiswami Naidu
▼
✅ Answer
Option A — Raja of Panagal
His original (full) name: P. Ramarayaningar (Bobbili Raja / Panagal Raja)
📌 Explanation

When the Madras Non-Brahmin Association evolved into the Madras United League (Madras Aikkiya Kazhagam), its first president was Raja of Panagal. He later became one of the most prominent Chief Ministers of Madras Presidency under the Justice Party — responsible for granting women voting rights and establishing the first PSC.

Q 08 HARD Factual
The books "Dravidian Virtues" (திராவிட தகுதிகள்) and "Non-Brahmin Letters" (பிராமணன் இல்லாத கடிதங்கள்) were published by the Madras Dravidian Association. Who published them?
Options: Natesanar / V.S. Srinivasa Sastri / Lord Willingdon / Dr. C. Karunagara Meenan
▼
✅ Answer
Option D — Dr. C. Karunagara Meenan
📌 Explanation

Around 1910–1912, the Madras Dravidian Association published two important books to spread awareness about non-Brahmin rights and Dravidian identity. Dr. C. Karunagara Meenan was the publisher. These books laid the intellectual groundwork for the Justice Party movement.

💎 Live Gem — Dr. T.M. Nair's British Honour

The teacher shared that Dr. T.M. Nair (Tharavath Madhavan Nair), one of the Justice Party's three pillars, received the "Kaiser-i-Hind" medal from the British — for his services as a ship's doctor on HMS Madras. He later returned it (as Gandhi did after Jallianwala Bagh). Periyar called him the "Dravidam Lenin" — drawing a comparison to Lenin (the Russian revolutionary who peacefully dismantled the Tsarist regime) for his role in dismantling Brahmin social dominance without bloodshed.

🏘️ Section 4 — Local Self-Government (73rd & 74th Constitutional Amendments)
Q 09 EASY Factual
The 73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments — in which year were they introduced?
Options: 1992 / 1995 / 1997 / 1990
▼
✅ Answer
1992
  • 73rd Amendment → Panchayati Raj (Rural Local Self-Government)
  • 74th Amendment → Municipalities / Nagarpalikas (Urban Local Self-Government)
📌 Key Facts

These two amendments added Part IX (Articles 243–243O) for Panchayats and Part IX-A (Articles 243P–243ZG) for Municipalities to the Constitution. The 73rd Amendment also added the 11th Schedule listing 29 subjects for Panchayats, and the 74th added the 12th Schedule with 18 subjects for municipalities.

🏆 PYQ Alert

This is a perennial question in all TNPSC exams. The year 1992 and the schedule numbers (11th for Panchayat, 12th for Municipality) are high-yield facts.

Q 10 MEDIUM Factual
Which of the following committees was appointed by the Planning Commission of India (Thittakuzu)?
Options: Ashok Mehta Committee / G.V.K. Rao Committee / L.M. Singhvi Committee / Balwantrai Mehta Committee
▼
✅ Answer
Option B — G.V.K. Rao Committee (1985)
📌 Explanation

The Planning Commission of India (est. 1950, dissolved 2014 → replaced by NITI Aayog) appointed the G.V.K. Rao Committee in 1985 to study Panchayati Raj. The other committees were appointed by the Central Government directly. Key distinction: Planning Commission → G.V.K. Rao.

📊 Section 5 — Panchayati Raj Committees (Year-Matching)
Q 11 HARD Factual
Which committee's report is known as the "Magna Carta of Panchayati Raj"?
Whose report said: "Panchayati Raj is like grass without roots (வேர்கள் இல்லாத புல்)"?
▼
✅ Answer
Magna Carta of Panchayati Raj → Balwantrai Mehta Committee (1957)
"Grass without roots" → G.V.K. Rao Committee (1985)
📌 All Four Committees — Year Chart
CommitteeYearKey Contribution / Quote
Balwantrai Mehta1957First committee on PR; recommended 3-tier system; "Magna Carta of Panchayati Raj"
Ashok Mehta1977Recommended 2-tier system; strengthening of PR
G.V.K. Rao1985"Grass without roots" (PR in name only); recommended constitutional status
L.M. Singhvi1986Recommended constitutional status for Panchayats — directly led to 73rd Amendment (1992)
💎 Live Gem — Shortcut Trick

"Grass without roots" → starts with 'G' → G.V.K. Rao
The teacher gave this shortcut: the phrase "Grass (வேர்கள்)" starts with G, just like G.V.K. Rao. Easy to remember!

🏆 PYQ Alert

The Balwantrai Mehta Committee and the 11th Schedule connection is a high-frequency TNPSC question. Also: L.M. Singhvi (1986) → recommended constitutional status → led to 73rd Amendment (1992) is a popular sequential question.

🪤 TRAP NOTE

Students often confuse Ashok Mehta (1977) with Balwantrai Mehta (1957). Both have "Mehta" in the name! Remember: Balwantrai = older, first, Magna Carta. Ashok = 1977, 2-tier recommendation.

Q 12 MEDIUM Factual
Which committee recommended giving Constitutional Status to Panchayati Raj institutions?
Options: G.V.K. Rao / Balwantrai Mehta / L.M. Singhvi / Ashok Mehta
▼
✅ Answer
Option C — L.M. Singhvi Committee (1986)
📌 Explanation

The L.M. Singhvi Committee (1986) recommended that Panchayati Raj institutions be given constitutional recognition and protection through a constitutional amendment. This recommendation was implemented through the 73rd Constitutional Amendment of 1992, which added Part IX to the Constitution.

Homework Q (from session): The Balwantrai Mehta Committee report is connected to which Schedule of the Constitution? Options: 3 / 5 / 9 / 11
Answer: 11th Schedule (lists 29 subjects for Panchayats under 73rd Amendment)
