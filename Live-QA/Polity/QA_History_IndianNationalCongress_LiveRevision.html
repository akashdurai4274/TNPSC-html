This session covered the Indian National Movement (1857–1947) in rapid-fire MCQ style, focused heavily on: Jallianwala Bagh & its aftermath, the "Magna Carta" series of declarations, chronological ordering of revolts/sessions, secret revolutionary societies, national songs, early press history, honorific titles of leaders, the Muslim League & Two-Nation Theory, Congress's organisational precursors, and biographical trivia on Naoroji, Tilak, Gokhale, Banerjee, Savarkar, Motilal Nehru and others. 🩸 Nehru, Jallianwala Bagh & Chauri Chaura

Q1MEDIUMTrick/Trap
What was the key event that drove Nehru into intensive/active politics? TA: நேரு தீவிர அரசியலில் ஈடுபட காரணமான முக்கிய நிகழ்வு எது?
✅ The Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (1919)
📌 Many students confuse this with the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920) — but the teacher clarified the exact, book-confirmed answer is Jallianwala Bagh.
[TRAP] Some reference books list "Non-Cooperation Movement" as an alternative — mark Jallianwala Bagh as the primary/correct answer for TNPSC.
[💎 LIVE GEM] In protest against Jallianwala Bagh, two people surrendered British-given titles: Gandhi surrendered his title "Kaisar-i-Hind", and Tagore surrendered his Knighthood. Keep these two paired and don't swap them!
🏆 PYQ ALERT This exact title-surrender pairing has repeatedly appeared as a TNPSC Group 2/4 MCQ.
Q2EASYFactual
Chauri Chaura incident — which year and how many policemen died? TA: சவுரி சவரா எந்த இயர்?
✅ February 1922 — 22 policemen killed
📌 Timeline: Non-Cooperation Movement started 1920 → ended at Chauri Chaura, Feb 1922.
[TIP] Memory hook: "22 in '22" — 22 policemen died in 1922.
🏆 PYQ ALERT Extremely frequent TNPSC question.
Q3EASYFactual

✅ 13 April 1919

📜 The "Magna Carta" (Mahasasanam) Series

TNPSC loves calling several different declarations India's "Magna Carta" — this is the single most trap-heavy theme of the session.

Q4HARDConceptual
What is referred to as the "Magna Carta of the Moderates"? TA: இந்தியாவின் மேக்னகார்ட்டா என மிதவாதிகளால் வரவேற்கப்பட்டது எது?
✅ The August Declaration, 1917 (Montagu's Declaration)
📌 "Mahasasanam" (மகாசாசனம்) = "Great Decree / Magna Carta". Moderates had been demanding reforms for years; the August Declaration finally addressed those demands, so they called it their own "Magna Carta."
[💎 LIVE GEM] There are a total of 7 documents nicknamed "Mahasasanam / Magna Carta" in Indian history. Six were recalled live:
  • India's Magna Carta — associated with Part III (Fundamental Rights) [NEEDS EXPANSION — teacher did not confirm exact document/year]
  • Indians' Magna Carta — Queen Victoria's Proclamation, 1858
  • Education's Magna Carta — Charles Wood's Despatch, 1854
  • Moderates' Magna Carta — August Declaration, 1917
  • Poligars' (Palayakkarar) Magna Carta — 1801, by the Marudu Brothers
  • Consumers' Magna Carta — Consumer Protection Act, 1986 (Consumer Day = 24 December)
The teacher noted a 7th exists but could not recall it live — flag this for extra self-study.
[TRAP] Don't confuse "Consumer Day" (24 December) with the enactment year (1986) — TNPSC often asks the Act year separately from the Day.
🏆 PYQ ALERT The Charles Wood's Despatch (1854) = "Magna Carta of English/Indian Education" is a TNPSC evergreen.
Q5EASYFactual
Which document is called the "Magna Carta of Indians" and in which year was it issued?
✅ Queen Victoria's Proclamation, 1858 (read out by Lord Canning)

🔢 Chronology & Matching Sets

Q6MEDIUMApplication
Arrange in correct chronological order: Mangal Pandey Revolt (Barrackpore) · Rani of Jhansi's defeat · Vellore Mutiny · Meerut Revolt
✅ Vellore Mutiny (1806) → Mangal Pandey Revolt (1857, Barrackpore) → Rani of Jhansi's defeat → Meerut Revolt (end of 1857 uprising)
📌 Vellore Mutiny (1806) is a separate, earlier event and is NOT part of the 1857 Revolt — a very common trap.
[TNPSC TRAP] Students often place Vellore Mutiny inside the 1857 sequence — it happened 51 years earlier and is unrelated to the Great Revolt.
Q7MEDIUMApplication
Match: Surat Split · Bengal Partition · Minto-Morley Reforms · Formation of Muslim League — with their years.
✅ Bengal Partition: 1905 · Muslim League founded: 1906 · Minto-Morley Reforms: 1909 · Surat Split: 1907
[TRAP] The Minto-Morley Reforms (1909) is often confused with the Muslim League founding year (1906) since both are "1909-ish" sounding — verify separately.
Q8EASYApplication
Match: Swaraj Party formed · 1858 Act ends East India Company rule · 1909 Act (separate Muslim electorate) · 1919 Act (Dyarchy in provinces) · 1935 Act (Provincial Autonomy)
✅ Swaraj Party — 1923 (Motilal Nehru & C.R. Das) · 1858 Act — ends Company rule · 1909 Act — Communal/separate electorate for Muslims · 1919 Act — Dyarchy in provinces · 1935 Act — Provincial Autonomy
Q9HARDTrick/Trap
Which pair is INCORRECTLY matched: Licensing Regulations · Vernacular Press Act · Press Act · [related law]?
✅ Press Act, 1910 is separate — the FIRST press-control law was the Vernacular Press Act, 1878 (not 1910)
📌 Sequence: Vernacular Press Act (1878, Lord Lytton) came before the later Press Act (1910). TNPSC frequently swaps these two dates.
Q10HARDApplication

✅ Delhi Durbar — Lord Lytton, 1911 (14th George's coronation Durbar — see note) · Indian Association — Surendranath Banerjee · Servants of India Society — Gokhale, 1905 · Drain of Wealth Theory — Dadabhai Naoroji

📰 Press & Newspaper History

Q20HARDTrick/Trap
What was India's first REGIONAL-language newspaper? TA: முதல் இந்திய பிராந்திய செய்தித்தாள் எது?
✅ Sambad Kaumudi (started by Raja Ram Mohan Roy)
📌 Not to be confused with Mirat-ul-Akbar, which was India's first PERSIAN newspaper — also by Ram Mohan Roy.
[💎 LIVE GEM] Ram Mohan Roy alone founded BOTH "firsts": Sambad Kaumudi = first regional-language paper; Mirat-ul-Akbar = first Persian-language paper. Keep the language distinction crystal clear — same founder, two different "first" titles.
Q21EASYFactual
Which was India's very first newspaper (any language)?
✅ Bengal Gazette (1780)
Q22HARDTrick/Trap
Assertion-Reason: Tilak was editor of "Vande Mataram" and criticised Christianity and Islam harshly.
✅ First statement is FALSE (Tilak's papers were Kesari & Mahratta, not "Vande Mataram") — second statement (criticism of Christianity/Islam) is TRUE
[💎 LIVE GEM] Language trick: Kesari = Marathi-language paper; Mahratta = English-language paper (despite the "Maratha-sounding" name!). Tilak ran exactly these two — no third paper.

📝 Topic Summary — 10-Point Recap

  • Nehru's shift to intense politics is linked to Jallianwala Bagh (1919), not the Non-Cooperation Movement.
  • Chauri Chaura (Feb 1922, 22 policemen killed) ended the Non-Cooperation Movement.
  • Several unrelated documents are each nicknamed a "Magna Carta" — always check WHICH one a question is asking about (Moderates', Education's, Indians', Consumers', Poligars').
  • Vellore Mutiny (1806) is NOT part of the 1857 Revolt sequence — a classic chronology trap.
  • Both national songs (Vande Mataram, Jana Gana Mana) were first sung by Tagore, both at Calcutta sessions, but 15 years apart (1896 and 1911).
  • Ram Mohan Roy founded both India's first regional-language paper (Sambad Kaumudi) and first Persian paper (Mirat-ul-Akbar).
  • Title-matching is a huge scoring area: Lion of Punjab = Lala Lajpat Rai (NOT Tilak); Grand Old Man of India = Naoroji; Tamil Nadu's Grand Old Man = G. Subramania Iyer.
  • Muhammad Iqbal — not Bhagat Singh — coined "Inquilab Zindabad", and also proposed the Two-Nation Theory (1930), ahead of the Lahore Resolution (1940).
  • Three separate ICS trivia threads (Satyendranath Tagore = first ICS officer 1863; Surendranath Banerjee = dismissed 1895; Aurobindo Ghosh = failed horse-riding test) are frequently mixed together in MCQs.
  • The No-changers vs Pro-changers split (post-1922) is a key organisational fact: No-changers stayed loyal to Gandhi's programme; Pro-changers formed the Swaraj Party.

🔮 2026 Prediction — Likely TNPSC/SSC Questions

  • "Who first sang Vande Mataram / Jana Gana Mana, and where?" in either direct or assertion-reason format.
