1

Law — Introduction & Meaning

4.1.1 & 4.1.2 — Definitions, Root Word, Purposes
Q1 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO 🏆 PYQ
The term 'Law' is derived from which root word, and what does it mean?
சட்டம் என்ற சொல் எந்த மூல சொல்லிலிருந்து உருவானது? அதன் பொருள் என்ன?
✅ Answer
The term 'Law' is derived from an old Teutonic root word 'lag', which means "something which lies fixed or evenly."
🔴 CRITICAL

The root is Teutonic (Germanic), not Latin or Greek. Don't confuse with 'lex' (Latin).

💚 TIP

Mnemonic: Teutonic = T for "Tied down" / Fixed — law lies fixed like a foundation stone.

Q2 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ 💎 GEM
According to John Austin, what is Law? According to Salmond?
ஜான் ஆஸ்டின் மற்றும் சால்மண்ட் படி சட்டம் என்றால் என்ன?
✅ Answer
Austin: "Law is the command of the sovereign."
Salmond: "Law is the collection of rules which the state recognizes and applies in the administration of Justice."
🏆 PYQ ALERT

Austin's definition appeared in TNPSC Group 4 (2019) and Group 2 (2016). Expect match-the-following or fill-in-blank format.

💎 GEM

Austin's positivist view says law is law regardless of morality (command theory). Salmond's is more pragmatic — linking law to justice administration. This difference is a frequent comparison question.

🪤 TRAP

Bodin said "Law is the command of the sovereign" — the textbook attributes this to Austin. Don't confuse Bodin (who said it in a different context) with Austin's formal definition.

Q3 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO
What are the two kinds of laws based on what they regulate?
ஒழுங்குபடுத்துவதன் அடிப்படையில் சட்டம் எத்தனை வகைப்படும்?
✅ Answer
Physical law — regulates nature.
Human law — regulates human life / human action.
Q4 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2 💎 GEM
List any five purposes of Law as given in the textbook.
பாடப்புத்தகத்தில் கொடுக்கப்பட்ட சட்டத்தின் நோக்கங்களை ஐந்து கூறுக.
✅ Answer
1. Protect basic human rights
2. Promote fairness
3. Help resolve conflicts
4. Promote justice
5. Promote order and stability
6. Promote desirable social and economic behaviour
7. Represent the will of the majority
8. Protect the rights of minorities
💎 GEM

TNPSC sometimes asks: "Which of the following is NOT a purpose of law?" — Be careful with invented options like "punish the innocent" or "promote inequality."

Q5 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO 🏆 PYQ
Aristotle said: "If there is no law, even man will behave like a ___."
அரிஸ்டாட்டில் கூற்றில் சட்டம் இல்லாவிடில் மனிதன் எவ்வாறு நடந்துகொள்வான்?
✅ Answer
A beast. Aristotle pointed out that without law, even man will behave like a beast.
🏆 PYQ ALERT

This quote has appeared as a fill-in-the-blank in TNPSC Group 4 Prelims (2018). Very commonly tested.

2

Classification of Laws

4.1.3 — 8 types with key features
Q6 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ
What are Private Laws? Who described the state's role in private laws?
தனியார் சட்டங்கள் என்றால் என்ன? தனியார் சட்டங்களில் அரசின் பங்கை யார் விவரித்தார்?
✅ Answer
Private laws determine the relationship between citizens and regulate relations among one another. In private laws, the parties concerned are private individuals, and the state acts as an impartial arbiter — described by Holland.
🔴 CRITICAL

The scholar who described state's role in private law is Holland (not Salmond or Austin).

Q7 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ 💎 GEM
Distinguish between Private Law and Public Law.
தனியார் சட்டம் மற்றும் பொது சட்டம் — வேறுபாடு கூறுக.
✅ Answer
Private Law: Regulates relationships between private individuals; state acts as impartial arbiter above both parties.

Public Law: Determines the relation of citizens to the state; state is both an arbiter AND one of the parties interested.
💎 GEM

Key difference: In Public Law, the state is a party to the dispute; in Private Law, the state is only an arbiter. This is a common match-the-following or assertion-reason question.

Q8 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO 🏆 PYQ
What are Constitutional Laws? Give examples.
அரசியலமைப்பு சட்டங்கள் என்றால் என்ன? உதாரணம் தருக.
✅ Answer
Constitutional laws are the basic laws according to which the government in a state conducts itself. They define, interpret, and regulate the functions of the government.

Examples: Election of President, powers and functions of the Supreme Court, method of appointment of the Governor.
Q9 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO
What are Statute Laws? Who frames them?
சட்டமன்றச் சட்டங்கள் என்றால் என்ன? யார் இயற்றுகின்றனர்?
✅ Answer
Statute laws are laws framed by the Legislative Assembly or by the Parliament. In democratic governments, most laws are framed by Parliaments.
Q10 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ 💎 GEM
What are Ordinances? Who issues them, and in what situation?
அவசரச் சட்டங்கள் (Ordinances) என்றால் என்ன? யார், எந்தச் சூழ்நிலையில் பிறப்பிக்கிறார்?
✅ Answer
Ordinances are issued by the executive branch of the government. They are temporary by nature and are issued by the President in the absence of Parliament, especially to face emergencies.
💎 GEM

In India, Article 123 of the Constitution empowers the President to issue ordinances. A Governor can also issue ordinances under Article 213. Ordinances must be approved by Parliament within 6 weeks of its reassembly.

🪤 TRAP

Students confuse: Ordinances are temporary executive orders — not permanent laws. They lapse if not ratified by Parliament.

Q11 MEDIUM Factual Gr.4/VAO
Common laws rest on _____ but are enforced by courts like statute law. In which country are common laws popular?
வழக்காறு சட்டங்கள் (Common Laws) எதன் அடிப்படையில் அமைந்தவை? எந்த நாட்டில் பிரபலம்?
✅ Answer
Common laws rest on customs but are enforced by courts like statute law. Common laws are popular in England.
Q12 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2
What are Administrative Laws? When do administrative courts intervene?
நிர்வாக சட்டங்கள் என்றால் என்ன? நிர்வாக நீதிமன்றம் எப்போது தலையிடும்?
✅ Answer
Administrative laws interpret and govern the office and responsibilities of government servants. When a dispute arises between a citizen and a government servant, the administrative court resolves the issue using administrative laws.
Q13 HARD Conceptual Gr.1 💎 GEM
What governs International Law in the absence of a formal legal framework? What examples of international law are given?
சர்வதேச சட்டத்தை நிர்வகிப்பது எது? உதாரணங்கள் தருக.
✅ Answer
International law is backed by public opinion and the rules of the UNO. It determines the conduct of civilized states in their relations with other states. Examples: Sea Law (governing international maritime borders) and Air Law (requiring aircraft to fly with permission of respective nations).
💎 GEM

Unlike domestic law, international law has no central enforcement agency — it relies on consent, diplomatic pressure, and international bodies (UN, ICJ). This is why it is sometimes called "soft law."

Sources of Law

4.1.4 — Customs, Religion, Judiciary, Equity, Scientific Commentaries, Legislature
Q14 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ 💎 GEM
Name the six sources of law. Which classical example is given for customs as a source of law in Tamil Nadu?
சட்டத்தின் ஆறு ஆதாரங்களை கூறுக. தமிழ்நாட்டில் வழக்காற்றிலிருந்து சட்டம் உருவான உதாரணம் என்ன?
✅ Answer
Six sources of law: Customs, Religion, Judicial decisions, Equity, Scientific commentaries, Legislature.

Tamil Nadu example: The Bull-taming sport (Jallikattu) culture of Tamils resulted in the creation of the Jallikattu Law in 2017 — a classical example of custom becoming law.
🏆 PYQ ALERT

Jallikattu Law 2017 — TNPSC asked about this in current affairs context in 2018. The connection to Article 29(1) (cultural rights) and Article 21 (right to life of animals) makes it a high-yield concept.

💎 GEM

Jallikattu is conducted in Alanganallur, Madurai district, during the month of January with Pongal festival. The 2014 Supreme Court judgment held animals as sentient beings with right to life under Article 21. Article 29(1) protects cultural rights. This contradiction led to the 2017 legislation.

Q15 HARD Factual Gr.1
What is the origin of Hindu law and Mohammedian law? What is the source of divine law for Christians?
இந்து சட்டம் மற்றும் இஸ்லாமிய சட்டம் எங்கிருந்து உருவானது? கிறிஸ்தவர்களுக்கான தெய்வீக சட்டம் என்ன?
✅ Answer
Hindu law: Traced in the Code of Manu.
Mohammedian law: Traced in Shariat law.
Christian: The Ten Commandments were the first law given by the Lord Almighty — considered the basis of law for Christians.
🔴 CRITICAL

God is the ultimate source of Divine law. Divine law is revealed through man from God — this is frequently tested as a definition question.

Q16 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2
What is Equity as a source of law? What does it mean in English Law?
சட்டத்தின் ஆதாரமாக நியாயசாஸ்திரம் (Equity) என்றால் என்ன?
✅ Answer
When laws are ambiguous and do not fit, principles of equity are applied — cases are decided according to common sense and fairness. In English Law, equity means the body of rules originally enforced only by the Court of Chancery.
Q17 HARD Factual Gr.1 💎 GEM
What are Scientific Commentaries as a source of law? Name the scholars cited by A. Appadurai.
அறிவியல் விளக்கவுரைகள் (Scientific Commentaries) சட்டத்தின் ஆதாரமாக இருப்பது எப்படி? A. அப்படுரை குறிப்பிடும் அறிஞர்கள் யாவர்?
✅ Answer
Scientific commentaries on law are accepted as correct law over time — initially they are arguments, but their authority grows more authoritative than judicial decisions.

Scholars cited by A. Appadurai:
England: Coke and Blackstone
America: Story and Kent
India: Vijnaneswara and Apararka
💎 GEM

These pairs are match-the-following bait: England→Coke & Blackstone | America→Story & Kent | India→Vijnaneswara & Apararka. The quote is from scholar A. Appadurai.

Q18 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ
Name the three main sources from which the Indian Constitution was derived.
இந்திய அரசியலமைப்பின் மூன்று முக்கிய ஆதாரங்கள் யாவை?
✅ Answer
i) Government of India Act, 1935
ii) UK Constitution
iii) US Constitution
🏆 PYQ ALERT

TNPSC Group 2 (2016, 2019) and Group 4 (2019) asked about sources of the Indian Constitution. Note: The textbook lists these three but India's Constitution drew from many more sources (Irish, Australian, etc.) — the lesson specifically mentions only these three.

🪤 TRAP

Students often write "French Constitution" or "Australian Constitution" — those are correct broadly but this textbook passage specifically names only GOI Act 1935, UK, and US. Answer as per lesson for TNPSC.

Law, State, Morality & Public Opinion

4.1.5 & 4.1.6 — Distinctions and Relationships
Q19 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2 💎 GEM
State three key distinctions between Law and Morality as given in the textbook.
சட்டம் மற்றும் நெறிமுறை (Morality) வேறுபாட்டை மூன்று புள்ளிகளில் தருக.
✅ Answer
1. Laws are enforced by the state — disobedience leads to punishment. Morality can only lead to social boycott at most.
2. Morality covers both internal and external affairs of man; Law covers only external affairs.
3. Law punishes only those who violate by external actions; morality deals with intentions too.
💎 GEM

Plato's quote: "The best state is that which is nearest in virtue to the individual." This links state, law, and morality — a bad state creates bad citizens. Used in Assertion-Reason questions.

Q20 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO
What is a Moral Law? Give examples of evils that moral laws aim to eliminate.
தார்மீக சட்டம் என்றால் என்ன? உதாரணங்கள் தருக.
✅ Answer
A Moral Law is a law framed with the purpose of eliminating evils such as drinking of wine, gambling, theft, dacoity, and murder. Laws based on morality remain permanent.
Q21 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2
What is Public Opinion? How is it related to law in a democracy?
பொதுக் கருத்து என்றால் என்ன? ஜனநாயகத்தில் சட்டத்துடன் அதன் தொடர்பு என்ன?
✅ Answer
Public opinion is the opinion held by people for the common welfare. In democracy, laws are framed based on public opinion support. People use peaceful demonstrations to express opinions. Common welfare and social progress are primary considerations of public opinion.
5

Citizenship

4.2 — Introduction, City-state, Marshall's Analysis, India, Global Citizenship
Q22 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO 🏆 PYQ
How did Aristotle define Citizenship?
அரிஸ்டாட்டில் குடியுரிமையை எவ்வாறு வரையறுத்தார்?
✅ Answer
Aristotle defined citizenship as: "He who has the power to take part in the deliberative or judicial administration of any state."

He also defined citizenship with reference to birth place, family lineage, and culture.
🏆 PYQ ALERT

Aristotle's definition of citizenship is a frequently tested quote in TNPSC exams. Fill-in-blank format: "He who has the power to take part in the _____ or _____ administration of any state."

Q23 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2 💎 GEM
What are the three divisions of Citizenship according to Marshall's Analysis?
மார்ஷல்லின் பகுப்பாய்வின்படி குடியுரிமையின் மூன்று பிரிவுகள் யாவை?
✅ Answer
According to Marshall (liberal-social democrat), citizenship has three essential divisions:
1. Civil — right to freedom
2. Political — right to participate in political decision-making
3. Social — standard of living and enjoying its fruits (social services)
💎 GEM

Marshall linked citizenship to social class in the context of capitalism. He stressed social services because mere civil and political rights are not enough if economic inequalities are stark. This is a nuanced UPSC-level concept tested in Group 1.

Q24 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ
In which Part of the Indian Constitution are provisions about Citizenship found? How many ways can citizenship be acquired?
இந்திய அரசியலமைப்பில் குடியுரிமை விதிகள் எந்த பகுதியில் உள்ளன? எத்தனை வழிகளில் குடியுரிமை பெறலாம்?
✅ Answer
Citizenship provisions are found in Part II of the Indian Constitution.

Citizenship can be acquired by: Birth, Descent, Registration, Naturalisation, or Inclusion of territory — five ways.
🏆 PYQ ALERT

TNPSC Group 4 (2017, 2019) asked about ways to acquire Indian citizenship. The five methods are directly from the Citizenship Act, 1955.

🔴 CRITICAL

Citizenship is in Part II (Articles 5–11), NOT Part III (Fundamental Rights). Don't confuse!

Q25 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2
Distinguish between National Citizenship and Global Citizenship.
தேசிய குடியுரிமை மற்றும் உலகளாவிய குடியுரிமை வேறுபாடு கூறுக.
✅ Answer
National Citizenship: Assumes the state can provide protection and rights needed to live; focused within national boundaries.

Global Citizenship: Deals with citizenship across national boundaries; requires cooperative action by people and governments of many states. It reminds us that states are interconnected and resolves socio-economic inequalities.
Q26 HARD Factual Gr.2 💎 GEM
What does the Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007 state about maintenance amount and penalties?
பெற்றோர் மற்றும் மூத்த குடிமக்கள் பராமரிப்பு சட்டம் 2007 பராமரிப்புத் தொகை மற்றும் தண்டனை பற்றி என்ன கூறுகிறது?
✅ Answer
Key provisions of the Act:
• Established the Maintenance Tribunal for speedy relief
• Maximum maintenance: ₹10,000 per month
• Maintenance includes: food, clothing, residence, medical attendance and treatment
• Lawyers are not allowed to present cases before the Tribunal
• Abandoning a senior citizen: Fine of ₹5,000 or imprisonment of 3 months or both
• Condition: Person must be unable to maintain themselves from their own earnings
💎 GEM

The Act was passed in 2007. State governments appoint a maintenance officer to represent elderly interests. The prohibition on lawyers in Tribunal proceedings ensures speedy, affordable justice.

🏆 PYQ ALERT

The maximum maintenance amount (₹10,000), prohibition of lawyers in Tribunal, and fine for abandoning (₹5,000) are frequently tested figures.

Rights & Duties — Types of Rights

4.3 — Natural, Moral, Legal, Contractual, Human Rights
Q27 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO 🏆 PYQ
T.H. Green defined Rights as ___________. What are the five features of Rights?
T.H. கிரீன் உரிமையை எவ்வாறு வரையறுத்தார்? உரிமையின் ஐந்து பண்புகள் யாவை?
✅ Answer
T.H. Green: "Rights are powers necessary for the fulfilment of man's vocation as a moral being."

Features of Rights:
1. Important for societal development
2. Available to all irrespective of caste, creed, race, or gender
3. Rights and duties are interrelated — "No duties, no rights"
4. Rights are justiciable
5. It is the duty of the state to protect rights
Q28 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ
Name the five types of Rights. Which are the three sub-types of Legal Rights?
உரிமையின் ஐந்து வகைகளையும் சட்ட உரிமையின் மூன்று உட்பிரிவுகளையும் கூறுக.
✅ Answer
Five types of Rights: Natural, Moral, Legal, Contractual, Human Rights

Three sub-types of Legal Rights:
i) Civil Rights — right to lead civilized social life; includes Right to life, liberty, equality
ii) Political Rights — right to participate in political process; right to vote, get elected, hold public office
iii) Economic Rights — provide economic security
🔴 CRITICAL

Right to life, liberty and equality = Civil Rights (Legal). This is a frequent trap — students wrongly say "Natural Rights."

Q29 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ 💎 GEM
What are Human Rights? When is International Human Rights Day celebrated?
மனித உரிமைகள் என்றால் என்ன? சர்வதேச மனித உரிமை நாள் எப்போது கொண்டாடப்படுகிறது?
✅ Answer
Human Rights are the rights of the highest order. They are protected and supported by international and national laws and treaties. International Human Rights Day is celebrated on December 10 every year.
🏆 PYQ ALERT

December 10 — International Human Rights Day. This date appeared in TNPSC Group 4 (2018) and SSC CGL (2022). Don't confuse with World Human Rights Day observed by different bodies on different dates.

💎 GEM

December 10, 1948 is when the UN General Assembly adopted the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR). The date commemorates this landmark adoption.

Q30 EASY Factual Gr.4/VAO
What are Contractual Rights? How do they originate?
ஒப்பந்த உரிமைகள் என்றால் என்ன? அவை எவ்வாறு உருவாகின்றன?
✅ Answer
Contractual Rights originate from the practice of promise-keeping. They apply to particular individuals to whom contractual promises have been made.
7

Fundamental Rights (India)

Part III of Constitution — Bill of Rights, Fundamental Rights, Writs, New Rights
Q31 MEDIUM Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ 💎 GEM
When was the US Bill of Rights adopted? Who proposed it? What influenced it?
அமெரிக்க உரிமைகள் சாசனம் எப்போது ஏற்றுக்கொள்ளப்பட்டது? யார் முன்மொழிந்தார்? எதனால் பாதிக்கப்பட்டது?
✅ Answer
Adopted: December 15, 1791
Proposed by: James Madison
Influenced by: George Mason's 1776 Virginia Declaration of Rights and the 1689 English Bill of Rights
It derives from: Magna Carta (1215) and the English Bill of Rights (1689)
💎 GEM

Chain of influence: Magna Carta (1215) → English Bill of Rights (1689) → George Mason's Virginia Declaration (1776) → US Bill of Rights (1791). Knowing this chain impresses examiners in Group 1 mains.

Q32 HARD Factual Gr.2 🏆 PYQ
How long did the Constituent Assembly take to frame the Indian Constitution?
அரசியலமைப்பு சட்ட சபை இந்திய அரசியலமைப்பை வடிவமைக்க எவ்வளவு நேரம் எடுத்தது?
✅ Answer
The Constituent Assembly took 2 years, 11 months, and 20 days to frame the Constitution.
🏆 PYQ ALERT

Exact duration is a TNPSC favourite: 2 years, 11 months, 20 days. This appeared in Group 4 (2016) and Group 2 (2018).

🪤 TRAP

5. Prohibition — Prevent lower court from exceeding jurisdiction

💎 GEM

Q45 HARD Conceptual Gr.1
Explain the difference between Political Obligation and Political Authority, with example.
அரசியல் கடமை மற்றும் அரசியல் அதிகாரம் — வேறுபாடு உதாரணத்துடன் விளக்குக.
✅ Answer
When the state has political authority, it has the right to compel non-compliers. Example: If the state has authority to levy taxes, it can compel citizens to pay taxes.

Political obligation goes beyond legal compulsion — even if the state does not enforce its authority, it remains the moral duty of citizens to comply with laws.
🔴 CRITICAL

Political obligation = moral duty (even without enforcement). Political authority = legal right of state to compel. Moral duty survives even when enforcement is absent.

Q53 HARD Conceptual Gr.1
Explain the divine theory of political obligation.
அரசியல் கடமையின் தெய்வீகக் கோட்பாட்டை விளக்குக.
✅ Answer
The Divine Theory holds that political authority and obligation are derived from God. According to this theory, rulers are God's representatives on earth and citizens are divinely obligated to obey them. The state's authority is sacred and divinely ordained.

This theory was dominant in medieval Europe and is associated with the concept of "Divine Right of Kings."
🔴 CRITICAL

The Divine theory is contrasted with the Social Contract theory (Locke, Rousseau, Hobbes) in which authority is derived from the consent of the governed — not divine ordinance. [EXPANDED FROM KNOWLEDGE]

Q54 MEDIUM Conceptual Gr.2 💎 GEM
What is Locke's Utilitarian justification of political obligation?
லாக்கின் பயன்பாட்டு நியாயமாக்கல் என்ன?
✅ Answer
Locke's utilitarian justification argues that political obligation exists because obeying the state provides the greatest good (utility) for citizens. Citizens obey laws not out of divine command but because the state protects their natural rights (life, liberty, property). When the state fails to do so, citizens have the right to withdraw their obligations (right to revolution). [EXPANDED FROM KNOWLEDGE]
💎 GEM

Locke's justification is the philosophical foundation of liberal democracy: Citizens obey the state because it benefits them. If the state becomes tyrannical, the social contract is broken and rebellion is justified.

📝 Topic Summary — 10 Key Takeaways

  1. Law derives from the Teutonic root 'lag' meaning "fixed/evenly." Austin: "Command of sovereign"; Salmond: "Rules recognized by the state for administration of justice."
  2. Eight types of law: Private, Public, Constitutional, Statute, Ordinance, Common, Administrative, International — each serving a distinct function in governance.
  3. Six sources of law: Customs, Religion, Judicial decisions, Equity, Scientific commentaries, Legislature — Jallikattu Law 2017 is a textbook example of custom becoming law.
  4. Indian Constitution's primary sources: Government of India Act 1935, UK Constitution, US Constitution — it is described as a classical example of borrowing best provisions.
  5. Citizenship in India (Part II) can be acquired in five ways: birth, descent, registration, naturalisation, inclusion of territory. Marshall identified three components: civil, political, social.
  6. Five types of Rights: Natural, Moral, Legal (Civil + Political + Economic), Contractual, Human. Human Rights Day = December 10.
  7. Fundamental Rights are in Part III. Article 32 (SC) and Article 226 (HC) provide constitutional remedies. Five writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Certiorari, Quo Warranto, Prohibition.
  8. New rights — RTI Act 2005 (transparency/accountability), Right to Privacy (Article 21 — 2017 SC ruling), Rights of Transgenders (Third Gender — SC direction).
  9. Political Obligation = duty to comply with constitutional obligations. Even without state enforcement, it is the moral duty of citizens. Moral obligations (caring for parents, hospitality) are not legally enforceable.
  10. Maintenance and Welfare of Parents Act 2007: Maximum maintenance ₹10,000/month; abandonment penalty ₹5,000 or 3 months imprisonment; lawyers barred from Tribunal.
