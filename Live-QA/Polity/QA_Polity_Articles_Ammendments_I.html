ERA 01

Amendments 1–10 · 1951–1961
01Amendment
1st Constitutional Amendment Act
1951
[CRITICAL] [PYQ]
▾
Core Objective

Added the Ninth Schedule to the Constitution to protect land reform laws from judicial review. Added three more grounds for restricting freedom of speech and expression: public order, friendly relations with foreign states, and incitement to an offence. Empowered the state to make special provisions for socially and economically backward classes.

🔴
CRITICAL
The 9th Schedule was created specifically to shield land reform laws from Article 19 (right to property) challenges. This is the FIRST amendment — always asked in "which amendment added 9th Schedule?"
🔵
PYQ
TNPSC 2019: "Which amendment added the Ninth Schedule?" → Answer: 1st Amendment, 1951.
🟡
TRAP
Many confuse 9th Schedule with 10th Schedule (Anti-Defection). 9th = land reforms (1st Amendment). 10th = defection (52nd Amendment).
Year1951
Key AdditionNinth Schedule
Article AffectedArts. 15, 19, 85, 87, 174, 176, 341, 342, 372, 376
PurposeProtect land reform laws; expand state power for backward classes
? Q: Why was the 1st Amendment passed just months after the Constitution came into force? ▾
The Supreme Court had struck down several state land reform laws as violating the right to property (Art.19). To protect these social-welfare laws, the 1st Amendment created the Ninth Schedule — laws placed there are immune from fundamental rights challenge. This also established the precedent of Parliament responding to SC judgments with constitutional amendments.
💎
LIVE GEM
The 1st Amendment is the origin of the "Parliament vs Judiciary" conflict that would explode in the 24th, 25th, and 42nd Amendments — a chain reaction worth memorising.

24Amendment
24th Constitutional Amendment Act
1971
[CRITICAL][PYQ]
▾

Affirmed Parliament's power to amend any part of the Constitution including Fundamental Rights. Made it compulsory for the President to give assent to a Constitutional Amendment Bill. This was in response to the Supreme Court's Golaknath judgment (1967).

🔴
CRITICAL
The 24th Amendment directly overturned the Golaknath case (1967), which said Parliament cannot amend Fundamental Rights. The Supreme Court later upheld this amendment in Kesavananda Bharati (1973) but added the Basic Structure doctrine as a limit.
? Q: What is the Golaknath–24th Amendment–Kesavananda chain? ▾
1967: Golaknath case — SC rules Parliament CANNOT amend Fundamental Rights.
1971: 24th Amendment — Parliament overrules Golaknath; claims unlimited amending power.
1973: Kesavananda Bharati case — SC upholds 24th Amendment BUT introduces Basic Structure Doctrine — Parliament can amend anything except the Basic Structure of the Constitution.
💎
LIVE GEM
This three-part chain is the most important judicial-constitutional sequence in Indian history. TNPSC Group 1 Mains may ask for a full analytical essay on this.
Year1971
Articles AmendedArts. 13, 368
ContextResponse to Golaknath v. State of Punjab (1967)

42Amendment
42nd Constitutional Amendment Act — "Mini Constitution"
1976
[CRITICAL][PYQ][GEM]
▾

The most comprehensive amendment ever passed. Known as the "Mini Constitution". Enacted on the recommendations of the Swaran Singh Committee. Key changes:

  • Added Socialist, Secular, Integrity to the Preamble
  • Added Fundamental Duties (new Part IV-A, Art. 51-A) — 10 duties originally
  • Made President bound by Cabinet's advice
  • Added 3 new DPSPs: equal justice, free legal aid, worker participation in management
  • Shifted 5 subjects from State List → Concurrent List: Education, Forests, Wild animal/bird protection, Weights & Measures, Administration of justice
  • Empowered Centre to deploy armed forces in any state for law and order
🔴
CRITICAL
"Socialist, Secular, Integrity" were NOT in the original Preamble — added by 42nd Amendment (1976). This is one of the most tested facts in all Indian polity exams.
💎
LIVE GEM
Education was moved from State List to Concurrent List by this amendment — making central government involvement in education (like NCERT, UGC, IITs, NEP) constitutionally valid. Critical for Tamil Nadu's arguments over state autonomy in education.
? Q: Why is the 42nd Amendment called the "Mini Constitution"? ▾
Because it was the most sweeping constitutional change ever — it amended 59 Articles, added Parts IV-A and XIV-A, and changed the very character of the Constitution (from secular to explicitly "secular"; added fundamental duties, changed DPSP-FR balance). It also tried to make Parliament's powers practically unlimited, though the 44th Amendment later clawed back several changes.
Year1976 (during Emergency)
Based onSwaran Singh Committee recommendations
Preamble words addedSocialist, Secular, Integrity
New Part addedPart IV-A (Fundamental Duties — Art. 51-A)

44Amendment
44th Constitutional Amendment Act
1978
[CRITICAL][PYQ]
▾

A landmark post-Emergency restoration amendment. Key changes:

  • Deleted Right to Property from Fundamental Rights (Art. 31 deleted); made it a legal right under Art. 300-A
  • Replaced "internal disturbance" with "armed rebellion" for national emergency (Art. 352)
  • President must declare emergency only on written recommendation of the Cabinet
  • President can send Cabinet advice back once for reconsideration, but reconsidered advice is binding
  • Restored provisions on freedom of press
🔴
CRITICAL
Right to Property: Originally Art.19(1)(f) + Art.31. The 44th Amendment DELETED these and moved it to Art. 300-A as a mere legal right. Now Parliament can take property by law without paying compensation (unlike earlier).
🟡
TRAP
"Internal disturbance" → "Armed rebellion": This is a major change in the Emergency provisions. The 1975 Emergency was declared on grounds of "internal disturbance." After the 44th Amendment, this is no longer sufficient — requires "armed rebellion."

In the 1992 Indra Sawhney (Mandal) judgment, the SC ruled total reservation cannot exceed 50%. Tamil Nadu already had 69%. So Parliament placed the TN law in the 9th Schedule to protect it from judicial review. This is THE most important TNPSC-specific constitutional fact.
💎
LIVE GEM
Tamil Nadu's 69% reservation: BC = 30%, MBC = 20%, SC = 15%, ST = 1%, SCA = 3%. This exceeds the 50% SC ceiling. The 9th Schedule protection is why it still stands. Always connect the 76th Amendment to Indra Sawhney in TNPSC answers.

[CRITICAL][PYQ]
▾

Extended the ban on readjustment of seats in the Lok Sabha and state assemblies for another 25 years (i.e., up to 2026), with the objective of encouraging population-limiting measures.

99Amendment
99th Amendment — National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC)
2014
[CRITICAL]
▾

Provided for the establishment of the National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC) to replace the collegium system for appointment of SC and HC judges.

🔴
CRITICAL
The 99th Amendment was struck down by the Supreme Court in 2015 (Supreme Court Advocates-on-Record case) as violating the Basic Structure doctrine (judicial independence). The collegium system was restored. This is the most recent major test of the Basic Structure doctrine.
? Q: What is the Collegium System and why was NJAC struck down? ▾
The Collegium System: SC and HC judges are appointed by a collegium of senior judges (not by executive). This was judicially evolved through three judges cases (1981, 1993, 1998). The NJAC (99th Amendment) tried to give the executive a role in appointments. The SC struck it down saying judicial independence (which is part of Basic Structure) would be compromised if the executive had a say in judicial appointments.

? Q: Why was the Anglo-Indian nomination discontinued by the 104th Amendment? ▾
The Anglo-Indian community is now very small (estimated under 200 in India). The nomination was designed as a transitional measure at Independence for a community caught between British and Indian identity. Having been extended for 70 years beyond its original 10-year plan (1950–1960), Parliament decided the community is now sufficiently integrated and the provision was no longer justified. The 104th Amendment ended this historical provision permanently.
105Amendment
105th Amendment — States Restored Power to Identify OBCs
2021
[CRITICAL][PYQ]
▾

Also known as the OBC Bill. Restored the power of State Governments and Union Territories to identify and notify their own list of Socially and Educationally Backward Classes (SEBCs/OBCs). This overturned the effect of a Supreme Court ruling in the Maratha reservation case (2021) which had interpreted the 102nd Amendment as giving this power solely to the Central Government/NCBC.

💎

Tamil Nadu maintains a state OBC list with 138+ communities. Without the 105th Amendment, TN's state OBC list would have no constitutional backing. This amendment directly protects Tamil Nadu's reservation framework for state jobs and education.

106Amendment
106th Amendment — Women's Reservation 33% (Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam)
2023
[CRITICAL][PYQ][GEM]
▾

Popularly known as the Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam. Reserves 33% (one-third) of all seats for women in the Lok Sabha, State Legislative Assemblies, and the Delhi Legislative Assembly — including seats reserved for SCs and STs. Reservation will last for 15 years from the date of commencement.

🔴
CRITICAL
IMPLEMENTATION CONDITION: The 33% women's reservation will be implemented ONLY AFTER (1) the next Census is conducted AND (2) subsequent delimitation exercise is completed. As of 2024, these have not yet occurred, so the reservation is not yet in force.
🟡
TRAP
Common trap: The 106th Amendment does NOT immediately give 33% seats to women. It requires Census + Delimitation first. Political parties and advocates have noted this means implementation is likely post-2029 at the earliest.
💎
LIVE GEM
Currently, women's representation in Lok Sabha is ~15%. The 73rd and 74th Amendments already mandate 33% women in Panchayats and Municipalities. This 106th Amendment extends the principle to Parliament and state assemblies. Tamil Nadu has been ahead nationally on women's representation in local bodies (50% reservation in TN).

? Q: Why is the 106th Amendment considered historic yet incomplete? ▾
Historic: It's the first time women's reservation has been constitutionally guaranteed at the parliamentary level. The Women's Reservation Bill had been pending since 1996 (27 years!). Passed unanimously in both Houses in the new Parliament building (Sansad Bhavan) in September 2023.

Incomplete: The "implementation after Census and Delimitation" clause means it's a promise for the future, not a present reality. Critics argue this was a deliberate design to delay — as Census 2021 was delayed (COVID) and delimitation is politically sensitive (southern states fear losing seats).
💎
LIVE GEM
TNPSC Group 1 Mains may ask: "Critically analyse the 106th Amendment." Key angle: Constitutional guarantee vs deferred implementation — historic intent but uncertain timeline.
