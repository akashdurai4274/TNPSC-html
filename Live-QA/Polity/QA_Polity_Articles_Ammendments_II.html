1st Act
1951		 Added Ninth Schedule to insulate land reform laws from judicial review. Introduced three new grounds of reasonable restriction on freedom of speech (Article 19(2)): Public Order, Friendly relations with foreign states, and Incitement to an offence. Empowered state to execute special provisions for advancement of SEBCs.
[CRITICAL]
Triggered by landmark State of Madras v. Champakam Dorairajan (1951) case regarding communal reservations in Tamil Nadu.
[PYQ]
Questioned repeatedly on what restrictions were added to Art 19(2).
2nd Act
1952		 Readjusted scale of representation in Lok Sabha, providing that one member could represent more than 7,500,000 persons.
[TIP]
Deals strictly with early demographic balancing acts in early general elections.
3rd Act
1954		 Empowered Parliament to control production, supply, and distribution of foodstuffs, cattle fodder, raw cotton, cotton seed, and raw jute in public interest. Altered Entry 33 of Concurrent List to centralize essential commodities powers.
4th Act
1955		 Made quantum of compensation given in lieu of compulsory acquisition of private property non-justiciable (beyond court scrutiny). Expanded state monopoly over trade.
[TRAP]
Do not confuse with the 44th Amendment which deleted Right to Property completely. This merely restricted compensation challenges.
5th Act
1955		 Empowered President to fix time-limits for State Legislatures to express views on proposed central laws affecting state boundaries (Article 3). Crucial for understanding federal dynamics under Article 3.
6th Act
1956		 Introduced taxes on sale or purchase of goods taking place during inter-state trade or commerce. Restricted state powers over same. Laid early foundations for Union control over inter-state indirect taxation streams.
7th Act
1956		 Implemented recommendations of States Reorganisation Commission. Abolished classification of states into Part A, B, C, and D states. Reorganized India into 14 States and 6 UTs. Allowed common High Courts for two or more states.
[CRITICAL]
Altered Articles 1, 2, 3, 4, and 4th schedule entirely. Essential historical turning point for federal setups.
8th Act
1959		 Extended period of reservation of seats for SCs, STs, and Anglo-Indians in Lok Sabha and Assemblies by 10 more years (up to 1970). First of the series of periodic 10-year extensions given to constitutional reservation models.
9th Act
1960		 Facilitated cession of Indian territory of Berubari Union (West Bengal) to Pakistan as provided under Indo-Pakistan Agreement (1958).
[CRITICAL]
Led to historic Berubari Union Case (1960) where SC ruled Preamble is not part of Constitution and Article 3 cannot be used to cede territory to foreign countries.
10th Act
1961		 Incorporated Dadra and Nagar Haveli into Indian Union as a Union Territory post-liberation from Portuguese rule. Integrated external territories into Article 1 frameworks.
11th Act
1961		 Changed election procedure of Vice-President by establishing Electoral College instead of joint meeting of both Houses. Provided that vacancy in electoral college cannot challenge Presidential/VP elections. Streamlined process to avoid legislative logjams during high executive elections.
12th Act
1962		 Incorporated Goa, Daman, and Diu into Indian Union as Union Territories following Operation Vijay. Further elimination of colonial enclaves on western coast.
13th Act
1962		 Conferred statehood status upon Nagaland and made special protection provisions under newly inserted Article 371A. Asymmetric federalism mechanism initiated for tribal protections.
14th Act
1962		 Incorporated former French territory of Puducherry into Indian Union. Enabled creation of Legislatures and Councils of Ministers for certain UTs. Direct impact on Puducherry governance model frequently referenced in regional exams.
15th Act
1963		 Increased retirement age of High Court judges from 60 to 62 years. Enabled retired HC judges to sit as ad-hoc judges of same court.
[PYQ]
Frequently asked in match the following style matrices alongside Supreme Court age provisions.
16th Act
1963		 Included "Sovereignty and Integrity of India" within forms of oaths/affirmations for lawmakers, ministers, judges, and CAG. Included it as a ground of restriction on Article 19. Enacted in backdrop of secessionist trends to fortify unity frameworks.
17th Act
1964		 Prohibited acquisition of land under personal cultivation unless market-value compensation is provided. Added 44 more land reform acts to Ninth Schedule. Led to intensive legal standoffs culminating in basic structure challenges later.
18th Act
1966		 Clarified that power of Parliament under Article 3 to form new states includes power to form a state/UT by uniting part of any state/UT to another. Purely technical clarification regarding territorial adjustments.
19th Act
1966		 Abolished system of Election Tribunals and vested power to hear election petitions directly within High Courts. Judicial streamlining to expedite election-related dispute resolutions.
20th Act
1966		 Validated appointments of certain district judges in Uttar Pradesh which had been struck down by Supreme Court. Curative amendment designed to protect administrative finality in regional judiciaries.
21st Act
1967		 Included Sindhi as the 15th constitutionally recognized language within Eighth Schedule.
[PYQ]
The entry sequence of languages added to Eighth Schedule is highly testable. Remember Sindhi was first.
22nd Act
1969		 Facilitated creation of a new autonomous State of Meghalaya within boundaries of State of Assam. Innovative institutional framework preceding full-scale statehood deployment.
23rd Act
1969		 Extended reservation metrics for SCs/STs and Anglo-Indians in legislative chambers for another ten years (up to 1980). Second regular cyclical extension of administrative reservations.
24th Act
1971		 Affirmed absolute power of Parliament to amend any part of Constitution including Fundamental Rights by amending Articles 13 and 368. Made it mandatory for President to give assent to Amendment Bills.
[CRITICAL]
Passed to completely nullify structural impact of Golaknath Case (1967) judgment.
25th Act
1971		 Curtailed Fundamental Right to property. Mandated that laws giving effect to DPSPs under Article 39(b) or (c) cannot be challenged for violating Articles 14, 19, or 31. Introduced Article 31C. Declared that where Article 31C steps in, Fundamental Rights step out. Substantive challenge in Kesavananda Bharati.

29th Act
1972		 Inserted two land reform legislations from Kerala into protective parameters of Ninth Schedule. Another precursor piece directly challenged in historic basic structure litigation.
30th Act
1972		 Abolished financial threshold criteria (Rs 20,000) for filing civil appeals to Supreme Court. Replaced it with requirement that case must involve a substantial question of law. Optimized highest court resources toward systemic legal issues rather than monetary disputes.
31st Act
1973		 Increased total elective strength of Lok Sabha from 525 to 545 seats to accommodate population growth recorded in census metrics.

Important baseline for tracking demographic representation ceilings in parliamentary history.
32nd Act
1973		 Inserted specialized regional equitable safeguards for Telangana zone inside Andhra Pradesh under Article 371D. Direct historic precedent to future state divisions in Deccan area.
33rd Act
1974		 Stipulated that Speaker or Chairman can accept resignation of legislators only if fully satisfied it is completely voluntary and genuine. Formulated to block forced political resignations during high-tension regional agitations.
34th Act
1974		 Added 20 distinct agrarian tenure and land redistribution laws enacted across states into protected Ninth Schedule lists. Continuous systemic insulating of socialist wealth distribution strategies from judicial interventions.
35th Act
1974		 Terminated protectorate status of Sikkim, updating it to Associate State of Indian Union. Added Tenth Schedule detailing terms of association.
[TRAP]
Do not confuse with the 36th amendment. 35th made it an Associate State and added an older temporary Tenth Schedule.
36th Act
1975		 Elevated Sikkim to status of a full-fledged 22nd state of Indian Union. Completely omitted temporary Tenth Schedule.
[PYQ]
Extremely frequent question regarding precise year and amendment index of Sikkim full integration.
37th Act
1975		 Provided a Legislative Assembly and Council of Ministers framework for Union Territory of Arunachal Pradesh. Strategic institutional maturation of frontier strategic zones.
38th Act
1975		 Made declaration of National Emergencies by President completely non-justiciable. Allowed simultaneous proclamations on distinct grounds. Passed immediately prior to emergency declarations to construct systemic defense structures.
39th Act
1975		 Placed election disputes of President, Vice-President, Prime Minister, and Lok Sabha Speaker beyond purview of judiciaries. Passed in response to Allahabad HC Judgment in Indira Gandhi election dispute. Struck down partially in Indira Gandhi v. Raj Narain.
40th Act
1976		 Empowered Parliament to define and dynamically alter limits of territorial waters, continental shelf, EEZ, and maritime zones. Sovereign maritime economic control stabilization vector.
41st Act
1976		 Raised upper retirement age limit of members of State Public Service Commission (SPSC) and JPSC from 60 to 62 years.
[CRITICAL]
Direct structural connection to TNPSC setup operations. Do not forget age remains 65 for UPSC.
42nd Act
1976		 Known as the Mini-Constitution. Implemented Swaran Singh recommendations. Added Socialist, Secular, Integrity to Preamble. Added Part IVA (Fundamental Duties). Shifted 5 subjects to Concurrent list (Education, Forests, Weights/Measures, Wild Animals, Admn of Justice). Bound President to Cabinet advice.
[CRITICAL]
Most expansive amendment in history. Altered structural balance of powers comprehensively across federal tiers.
43rd Act
1977		 Restored jurisdiction of Supreme Court and High Courts regarding judicial review powers and issuance of constitutional writs. First corrective rolling back of extreme centralization measures introduced during emergency.
44th Act
1978		 Replaced "internal disturbance" with "armed rebellion" for National Emergency. Made written cabinet advice mandatory. Deleted Right to Property from Part III, re-housing it as Article 300A legal right. Ensured Articles 20 & 21 cannot be suspended.
[CRITICAL]
Massive counter-balancing act restoring basic constitutional liberties and accountability dynamics.
45th Act
1980		 Extended reservation parameters for SC/ST quotas and Anglo-Indian representations by another 10-year block (until 1990). Routine decade extension baseline adjustment.
46th Act
1983		 Plugged structural loopholes in sales tax systems to stop commercial tax evasion maneuvers in inter-state consignments. Technical fiscal tightening instrument.
47th Act
1984		 Included specified land reform acts belonging to Assam, Bihar, Haryana, Tamil Nadu, UP, and West Bengal into protected Ninth Schedule. Direct protection of TN regional land ceiling laws from local litigation.
48th Act
1984		 Permitted extension of President's Rule in Punjab beyond standard limits without meeting specific stringent statutory conditions. Specialized state security situation amendment.
49th Act
1984		 Accorded constitutional sanctity and structured framework to Autonomous District Council systems operating inside Tripura. Sixth Schedule strengthening mechanism for indigenous tribal populations.
50th Act
1984		 Authorized Parliament to modify/restrict Fundamental Rights of personnel working in military intelligence or telecommunication networks. Modified Article 33 to guarantee extreme operational security protocols.

💎 Live Case Analysis & High-Yield Cards

How does the 105th Amendment protect state-level autonomy in Tamil Nadu?

The 102nd Amendment was interpreted by the Supreme Court as centralizing the notification of Backward Classes exclusively under the President. The 105th Amendment clarified and restored the concurrent power of states to maintain their independent state lists. This prevents administrative disruption to Tamil Nadu's existing reservation matrices.

💎 Live Classroom Gem
In Mains, link this directly to federalism. States argue local governments are best positioned to observe localized social stratification, making central-only lists counter to federal principle.
Why is the 76th Amendment considered a unique shield for Tamil Nadu's 69% reservation?

The 1992 Indira Sawhney judgment placed a strict 50% ceiling on total reservations. To preserve its 69% model, Tamil Nadu passed a state act and secured its placement in the Ninth Schedule via the 76th Constitutional Amendment Act, attempting to insulate it from judicial review.

💎 Live Classroom Gem
Always analyze this alongside the I.R. Coelho (2007) judgment. The SC ruled laws added to the Ninth Schedule after April 24, 1973, can still be challenged if they violate the Basic Structure.
