GROUP 1 · PARLIAMENT
Q1
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.2/4 SSC
Which Article deals with Sessions of Parliament, Prorogation, and Dissolution?
பாராளுமன்ற அமர்வுகள், ஒத்திவைப்பு மற்றும் கலைப்பு பற்றி எந்த சரத்து விவரிக்கிறது?
A) Art. 82 B) Art. 83 C) Art. 84 D) Art. 85 ✓
✅ ANSWER
Article 85 — Sessions of Parliament, Prorogation and Dissolution
📌 EXPLANATION
Prorogation = Adjournment with a fixed date announced (session suspended for a period).
Dissolution = Ending of Lok Sabha without a fixed date (no date given for reconvening).

The teacher introduced a key memory formula: Central Govt Article + 89 = State Govt Article
→ Art. 85 (Parliament Sessions) + 89 = Art. 174 (State Legislature Sessions)
The same concept (Sessions/Prorogation/Dissolution) for State Legislatures is covered under Article 174.
💎 LIVE GEM — The +89 Formula
Teacher's Golden Trick: For nearly all Central Government articles, adding 89 gives you the equivalent State Government article.
Examples: Art. 85 (Parliament Sessions) + 89 = Art. 174 (State Legislature Sessions) | Art. 76 (AG of India) + 89 = Art. 165 (Advocate General of State) | Art. 123 (Ordinance by President) + 89 = Art. 213 (Ordinance by Governor)
⚠️ Teacher noted: This formula works reliably up to around Article 140. Beyond 140, some deviations occur.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
This concept (Parliament Sessions) appeared in TNPSC Group 2 exams. The distinction between Prorogation (fixed date) and Dissolution (no date, Lok Sabha dissolved) is a favourite one-liner trap.
GROUP 2 · SPEAKER / CHAIRMAN SALARIES
Q2
Hard Factual TNPSC Gr.1/2 SSC
Which Article deals with the Salaries and Allowances of the Speaker, Deputy Speaker (Lok Sabha) and Chairman, Deputy Chairman (Rajya Sabha)?
தலைவர், துணை தலைவர் (மக்களவை) மற்றும் சபாநாயகர், துணை சபாநாயகர் (மாநிலங்களவை) ஆகியோரின் சம்பளம் மற்றும் படிகள் பற்றி எந்த சரத்து?
A) Art. 98 B) Art. 99 C) Art. 97 ✓ D) Art. 110
✅ ANSWER
Article 97 — Salaries and Allowances of the Chairman, Deputy Chairman, Speaker, and Deputy Speaker
📌 EXPLANATION
Lok Sabha = Speaker (Sabhapathi / சபாநாயகர்) + Deputy Speaker (Deputy Sabhapathi)
Rajya Sabha = Chairman (Sabhapath / சேர்மன்) + Deputy Chairman (Deputy Sabhapathi / துணை சேர்மன்)

Key related articles to memorize:
• Art. 93 — Election of Speaker and Deputy Speaker of Lok Sabha (very frequently asked)
• Art. 97 — Salaries & Allowances of Speaker, Deputy Speaker, Chairman, Deputy Chairman
• Art. 110 — Definition of Money Bill (அப்படி கேட்பாங்க — asked in TNPSC very often)
💎 LIVE GEM
Article 110 — Money Bill (மணி பில்): The teacher stressed this as a standalone high-frequency question. "TNPSC students — Article 110 is Money Bill. Don't forget this."
Also: Article 93 = Election of Lok Sabha Speaker — asked "adikadi" (repeatedly) in exams.
GROUP 3 · SUPREME COURT & JUDICIARY
Q3
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.2/4 SSC
Which Article states that the law declared by the Supreme Court shall be binding on all courts in India?
உச்ச நீதிமன்றத்தால் அறிவிக்கப்பட்ட சட்டம் அனைத்து நீதிமன்றங்களுக்கும் கட்டுப்படும் என்று எந்த சரத்து விவரிக்கிறது?
A) Art. 139 B) Art. 142 (some students answered this) C) Art. 141 ✓ D) Art. 143
✅ ANSWER
Article 141 — Law declared by Supreme Court to be binding on all courts
📌 EXPLANATION
The Supreme Court is the apex court for the entire India. Whatever law/judgment the Supreme Court declares becomes binding on all subordinate courts — High Courts, District Courts, Subordinate Courts.

Related Supreme Court articles:
• Art. 141 — SC law binding on all courts
• Art. 142 — Enforcement of decrees of Supreme Court (not the same!)
• Art. 123 — Ordinance power of President (central govt)
• Art. 213 — Ordinance power of Governor (state govt)
🪤 TRAP NOTE
Students commonly confuse Art. 141 with Art. 142. Art. 141 = SC's declared law binding on ALL courts. Art. 142 = enforcement of decrees by Supreme Court for complete justice. These are tested with very similar answer options.
💎 LIVE GEM — Article 123 (Ordinance)
Teacher spontaneously added: Article 123 = Ordinance-making power of the President (Avara Sattam / அவசர சட்டம் in English = Ordinance).
Using +89 trick: Art. 123 + 89 = Art. 213 = Ordinance-making power of the Governor.

Bonus: Teacher mentioned Special Leave Petition (SLP) was asked in Group 2 recently — students should check the article for SLP (Article 136).
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Art. 141 appeared in TNPSC Group 2 (2017, 2019). Art. 123 (Ordinance by President) also appeared in Group 2/4 exams multiple times.
GROUP 4 · LAW OFFICERS
Q4
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.2/4 SSC
Which Article describes the Advocate General of a State?
மாநில அட்வகேட் ஜெனரல் பற்றி எந்த சரத்து விவரிக்கிறது?
A) Art. 76 (AG of India) B) Art. 167 (wrong — some students) C) Art. 165 ✓ D) Art. 163
✅ ANSWER
Article 165 — Advocate General of a State
📌 EXPLANATION
Just as individuals hire lawyers for their cases, governments have their own legal representatives:

• Art. 76 — Attorney General of India = Central Govt's top legal officer, highest law officer in India
• Art. 165 — Advocate General of a State = State Govt's legal officer

Formula: Art. 76 + 89 = Art. 165 ✓

Key fact about AG of India: He/She can attend Parliament sessions but cannot vote.
💎 LIVE GEM
Attorney General of India — the highest law officer of India — has the right to address Parliament (both Houses) but has NO right to vote. This distinction is frequently tested.
Students who answered Art. 167 were told: "If you put 167, consider your answer wasted — 165 is the correct answer."
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Art. 76 vs. Art. 165 (AG vs. Advocate General) is a classic TNPSC comparison question. Also asked in SSC CGL: "Who is the highest law officer of India?" → AG of India (Art. 76).
GROUP 5 · UNION TERRITORIES
Q5
Hard Conceptual TNPSC Gr.1/2
Which Article gives the President the power to make regulations for Union Territories?
யூனியன் பிரதேசங்களுக்கான விதிமுறைகளை உருவாக்க ஜனாதிபதியின் அதிகாரம் பற்றி எந்த சரத்து?
A) Art. 238 B) Art. 239 C) Art. 240 ✓ D) Art. 242
✅ ANSWER
Article 240 — Power of President to make regulations for certain Union Territories
📌 EXPLANATION
Union Territories are under the direct control of the President of India. Each UT is administered through a Lieutenant Governor (LG) who acts under the power of the President.

Key Facts:
• India currently has 8 Union Territories (as of 2024)
• Only 2 UTs have Legislatures: Delhi and Puducherry (Pondicherry)
• All other 6 UTs have no legislature (population too small)
• Even if Delhi/Puducherry elects a CM, the Lt. Governor has more power than the CM in UTs
• This is because UTs fall directly under the President (Central Govt)
💎 LIVE GEM — UT Legislature Trick
Only Delhi and Puducherry have State Legislatures among all 8 UTs. In these UTs, even though a CM is elected, the Lieutenant Governor holds more power than the Chief Minister — because UTs are under Presidential control.
Total UTs = 8 (Students who wrote 7 or 9 are considered "in coma stage" by the teacher 😄)
Current Total: 28 States + 8 UTs = 36 divisions

GROUP 8 · EMERGENCY PROVISIONS
Q8
Easy Factual TNPSC Gr.2/4 SSC
Which Article deals with Financial Emergency in India?
நிதி நெருக்கடி பற்றி எந்த சரத்து கூறுகிறது?
A) Art. 352 B) Art. 356 C) Art. 92 (wrong answer) D) Art. 360 ✓
✅ ANSWER
Article 360 — Financial Emergency (never proclaimed in India till date)
📌 EXPLANATION
Three types of Emergency in India:
• Art. 352 — National Emergency (Rashtriya Avasara Kalanilaai / தேசிய அவசர கால நிலை) — External aggression or armed rebellion
• Art. 356 — State Emergency / President's Rule (Rajya Avasara Kaala Nilai) — Constitutional machinery in a state fails
• Art. 360 — Financial Emergency (Nidhi Nerukadi Nilai / நிதி நெருக்கடி) — Threat to financial stability of India

CRITICAL FACT: Art. 360 (Financial Emergency) has NEVER been proclaimed in India till date!
Art. 352 and Art. 356 have been invoked, but Art. 360 has NOT.
💎 LIVE GEM — National Emergency History
National Emergency (Art. 352) was proclaimed THREE times:
1. 1962 — Indo-China War (External threat)
2. 1971 — Indo-Pakistan War (External threat)
3. 1975 — Internal disturbance (by Indira Gandhi — internal political emergency)

The question asked: "Which year was National Emergency NOT proclaimed?" — Answer is 1965 (Indo-Pakistan 1965 war did NOT lead to a National Emergency proclamation).
⚠️ Read questions carefully: "which was NOT proclaimed" vs "which WAS proclaimed" — a classic exam trap!
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Art. 360 (Financial Emergency never invoked) is a standalone PYQ in TNPSC Group 2/4 and SSC. "Which emergency was never proclaimed in India?" is a direct question type.
Q9
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.1/2
In the following years — 1962, 1965, 1971, 1975 — in which year was National Emergency NOT proclaimed?
1962, 1965, 1971, 1975 ஆண்டுகளில் எந்த ஆண்டில் தேசிய அவசர நிலை அறிவிக்கப்படவில்லை?
A) 1962 B) 1965 ✓ C) 1971 D) 1975
✅ ANSWER
1965 — National Emergency was NOT proclaimed during the 1965 Indo-Pakistan War
📌 EXPLANATION
The 1965 Indo-Pakistan War happened but did NOT lead to a National Emergency under Art. 352. The Emergency was proclaimed in 1962 (China war), 1971 (Pakistan war), and 1975 (internal).

There are two types of National Emergency:
• External National Emergency — external aggression (1962, 1971)
• Internal National Emergency — internal armed rebellion (1975)
🪤 TRAP NOTE
The question says "was NOT proclaimed" — many students see "1962, 1971, 1975" and answer hastily. The keyword is "NOT". 1965 war happened but NO emergency was declared. Always read the complete question!
GROUP 9 · FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS DURING EMERGENCY
Q10
Hard Conceptual TNPSC Gr.1/2 SSC
Under which Articles can the President NOT suspend Fundamental Rights even during a National Emergency?
எந்த சூழ்நிலையிலும் குடியரசு தலைவரால் எந்த சரத்துகளின் கீழ் வழங்கப்பட்ட உரிமைகளை தடை செய்ய முடியாது?
✅ ANSWER
Articles 20 and 21 — These two Fundamental Rights can NEVER be suspended, not even during National Emergency
📌 EXPLANATION
Part III of the Constitution contains 6 Fundamental Rights available to every individual.
During National Emergency (Art. 352) or State Emergency (Art. 356), some Fundamental Rights can be suspended automatically.

BUT — Two rights that can NEVER be taken away under any emergency:
• Art. 20 — Protection in respect of conviction for offences (No ex-post-facto law, no double jeopardy, no self-incrimination)
• Art. 21 — Protection of life and personal liberty (Right to Life)

These two rights are permanent and irrevocable — even in emergency.
💎 LIVE GEM
Teacher said: "Even if life itself goes, these rights don't go — like a farmer who refuses to leave his land even in hardship."
Mnemonic: "20-21 = Two for Life" — Art. 20 protects against criminal conviction abuse; Art. 21 protects your life and liberty. Both are ABSOLUTE — no President, no emergency can remove them.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
This is a high-frequency TNPSC and SSC question: "Which Fundamental Rights cannot be suspended even during emergency?" → Art. 20 and 21. Appeared in TNPSC Group 1 prelims and SSC CGL multiple times.
GROUP 10 · OFFICIAL LANGUAGES (8th SCHEDULE)
Q11
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.2/4 SSC
Which language was added as an Official Language by the 21st Constitutional Amendment Act?
21வது சட்டதிருத்தத்தினால் ஆட்சி மொழியாக சேர்க்கப்பட்ட மொழி எது?
A) Konkani B) Manipuri C) Santhali D) Sindhi ✓
✅ ANSWER
Sindhi — added by the 21st Constitutional Amendment Act
📌 EXPLANATION
The 8th Schedule (Eighth Schedule) of the Constitution lists Official Languages.

At the time of Constitution adoption: 14 languages
Current total: 22 Official Languages

Three key Amendments that added languages:
• 21st Amendment → Added Sindhi (1 language added)
• 71st Amendment → Added Konkani, Manipuri, Nepali (3 languages added)
• 92nd Amendment → Added Bodo, Dongri, Maithili, Santhali (4 languages added)

Total added: 1 + 3 + 4 = 8 → 14 + 8 = 22 ✓
💎 LIVE GEM — Language Amendment Shortcut
Easy memory formula: 21 → 71 → 92 (adds 1, 3, 4 languages respectively)
• 21st: Sindhi (1)
• 71st: Konkani, Manipuri, Nepali (3)
• 92nd: Bodo, Dongri (Dogri), Maithili, Santhali (4)
Note: Options A, B, C (Konkani, Manipuri, Santhali) were added by DIFFERENT amendments — classic exam trap!
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Official Languages + Amendment number is a repeatedly asked TNPSC question. "Which amendment added Sindhi?" (21st) and "Which amendment added Bodo and Maithili?" (92nd) both appeared in Group 2 exams.
GROUP 11 · HIGH COURT JURISDICTION
Q12
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.2 SSC
Which Constitutional Amendment empowered Parliament to establish a common High Court for two or more States and Union Territories?
இரண்டு அல்லது அதற்கு மேற்பட்ட மாநிலங்களுக்கும் யூனியன் பிரதேசங்களுக்கும் பொதுவான உயர்நீதிமன்றத்தை நிறுவ பாராளுமன்றத்திற்கு அதிகாரம் வழங்கிய திருத்த சட்டம் எது?
A) 5th Amendment B) 7th Amendment (1956) ✓ C) 12th Amendment D) 15th Amendment
✅ ANSWER
7th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1956 — established common High Court for 2 or more States/UTs
📌 EXPLANATION
The 7th Amendment (1956) was a major amendment that reorganized states. It allowed Parliament to create a single High Court to have jurisdiction over two or more States or Union Territories.

This is why a single court sometimes covers multiple jurisdictions — e.g., one HC for Goa + Daman & Diu, etc.
💎 LIVE GEM — 7th Amendment Importance
The 7th Amendment (1956) is a multifaceted amendment — it:
1. Reorganized states on linguistic basis (States Reorganisation Act 1956)
2. Allowed common High Court for multiple states/UTs
3. Also related to the concept of one Governor for two states (see Q16)
The year 1956 is key — remember "7th Amendment = 1956 = States Reorganisation."
GROUP 12 · 7TH SCHEDULE — DISTRIBUTION OF POWERS
Q13
Hard Conceptual TNPSC Gr.1/2
By what majority can Parliament amend any list contained in the 7th Schedule of the Constitution?
ஏழாவது அட்டவணையில் அடங்கியுள்ள எந்த பட்டியலையும் திருத்தம் செய்ய நாடாளுமன்றம் எந்த பெருமான்மையை பின்பற்ற வேண்டும்?
✅ ANSWER
Special Majority (2/3rd of members present and voting) + Ratification by at least half of State Legislatures
📌 EXPLANATION
The 7th Schedule of the Constitution deals with Distribution of Powers between Centre and States:
• Union List (மத்திய பட்டியல்) — Only Central Govt can legislate
• State List (மாநில பட்டியல்) — Only State Govt can legislate
• Concurrent List (பொதுப்பட்டியல்) — Both Centre & State can legislate

To amend any of these lists:
→ Parliament needs Special Majority (2/3 of members present & voting) AND
→ Ratification by at least half the State Legislatures (14 out of 28 states)
💎 LIVE GEM — Types of Majority
Types of Majority in Indian Parliament:
• Simple Majority — More than half of present & voting members
• Absolute Majority — More than half of total membership (regardless of attendance)
• Special Majority (Art. 368) — 2/3 of present & voting members
• Special Majority + State Ratification — For 7th Schedule and federal structure changes

7th Schedule amendment requires the most difficult procedure — both Parliament special majority AND half-state ratification.
GROUP 13 · RESERVATION — EWS (103rd AMENDMENT)
Q14
Hard Factual TNPSC Gr.1/2 SSC
Which Article was added to the Constitution by the 103rd Constitutional Amendment Act for providing 10% EWS reservation to economically weaker sections?
103வது சட்டதிருத்தம் மூலம் பொருளாதாரத்தில் பின்தங்கிய பிரிவினருக்கு 10% இட ஒதுக்கீடு வழங்க எந்த சரத்து சேர்க்கப்பட்டது?

Article 15(6) and Article 16(6) — both added by the 103rd Constitutional Amendment Act

The 103rd Amendment Act (2019) introduced 10% reservation for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) in education and government jobs for those NOT covered under existing SC/ST/OBC reservations.

Two new sub-clauses were inserted:
• Art. 15(6) — EWS reservation in educational institutions

Reservation in Constitution — Evolution:

Teacher's exact words: "Two articles were born from the 103rd Amendment — Art. 15(6) and Art. 16(6)."
🏆 PYQ ALERT
The 103rd Amendment (EWS reservation) appeared in TNPSC Group 2 (2021) and SSC CGL (2022). The Article numbers 15(6) and 16(6) are important one-liners.
GROUP 14 · BASIC STRUCTURE DOCTRINE
Q15
Hard Conceptual TNPSC Gr.1/2 SSC
In which case did the Supreme Court rule that Parliament cannot amend Fundamental Rights under Article 368 — establishing the "Basic Structure Doctrine"?
ஆர்டிகல் 368ன் கீழ் பண்டமூல உரிமைகளை திருத்த முடியாது என்று உச்ச நீதிமன்றம் எந்த வழக்கில் தீர்ப்பளித்தது?
✅ ANSWER
Golak Nath vs. State of Punjab (1967) — SC held Parliament cannot amend Fundamental Rights under Art. 368
📌 EXPLANATION
Article 368 = Amendment of the Constitution (the article that defines how the Constitution can be amended).

Golak Nath vs. State of Punjab (1967): This case arose from land reform laws. The Golak Nath family owned 500 acres of land. Land ceiling laws required returning all above 30 acres to the government. They challenged this in court. The Supreme Court ruled that Parliament cannot amend Fundamental Rights through Art. 368.

This led to a conflict between Parliament and Judiciary — eventually resolved by the Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973) which established the "Basic Structure Doctrine" — Parliament can amend but cannot destroy the basic structure of the Constitution.
💎 LIVE GEM — Constitutional Cases Series
Teacher promised a dedicated video on landmark constitutional cases. Key cases to know:
1. Golak Nath vs. Punjab (1967) — FRs cannot be amended
2. Kesavananda Bharati vs. Kerala (1973) — Basic Structure Doctrine established
3. Minerva Mills vs. Union of India (1980) — Struck down parts of 42nd Amendment
4. Indra Sawhney vs. Union of India (1992) — 50% reservation cap confirmed
These cases are TNPSC Group 1 and SSC CGL staples.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Golak Nath Case + Kesavananda Bharati Case are recurring TNPSC Group 1/2 and SSC questions. "Which case established Basic Structure Doctrine?" = Kesavananda Bharati (1973). "Which case said FRs cannot be amended?" = Golak Nath (1967).
GROUP 15 · 42ND AMENDMENT — CONCURRENT LIST
Q16
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.2 SSC
Which subject was transferred to the Concurrent List by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment (1976)?
42வது சட்டதிருத்தம் 1976 மூலம் பொதுப்பட்டியலுக்கு மாற்றப்பட்ட விஷயம் எது?
A) Education B) Forests C) Marriage (NOT transferred) → Correct = Education ✓ D) Weights & Measures
✅ ANSWER
Education, Forests, Weights & Measures, Administration of Justice, Protection of Wild Animals & Birds — all 5 transferred to Concurrent List. "Marriage" was NOT transferred.
📌 EXPLANATION
The 42nd Amendment (1976) — called the "Mini-Constitution" — transferred 5 subjects from the State List to the Concurrent List:
1. Education
2. Forests
3. Weights and Measures
4. Administration of Justice (including constitution of all courts except SC and HCs)
5. Protection of Wild Animals and Birds

Marriage was NOT transferred — this was the trap in the question!
💎 LIVE GEM — 42nd Amendment Nickname
The 42nd Amendment (1976) is nicknamed "Mini-Constitution" because it made the most sweeping changes to the Constitution. It was passed during the Emergency period (1975-77). Key additions:
• Added "Socialist," "Secular," and "Integrity" to the Preamble
• Added Fundamental Duties (Art. 51A)
• Extended Parliament's tenure from 5 to 6 years (later reversed by 44th Amendment)
• Transferred 5 subjects to Concurrent List
Many parts of the 42nd Amendment were struck down in Minerva Mills case (1980).
GROUP 16 · PRESIDENT'S RECONSIDERATION POWER
Q17
Hard Factual TNPSC Gr.1/2 SSC
Which Constitutional Amendment states that the President may once send back Cabinet advice for reconsideration?
குடியரசு தலைவர் கேபினட் வழங்கும் ஆலோசனையை ஒருமுறை பரிசீலனைக்கு திருப்பி அனுப்பலாம் என்று எந்த திருத்தம் கூறுகிறது?
A) 41st Amendment B) 42nd Amendment C) 44th Amendment (1978) ✓ D) 45th Amendment
✅ ANSWER
44th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1978 — President can send back Cabinet advice once for reconsideration
📌 EXPLANATION
Article 74 — Council of Ministers headed by the Prime Minister shall aid and advise the President.

Originally, the President was bound by Cabinet advice. The 44th Amendment (1978) added a provision: The President may return the advice once for reconsideration. However, if the Cabinet sends back the same advice again, the President is bound to act on it.

The 44th Amendment also reversed some provisions of the 42nd Amendment (e.g., restored 5-year term of Parliament).
💎 LIVE GEM — 44th Amendment vs 42nd
The 44th Amendment (1978) was a corrective to the 42nd Amendment (1976). Key reversals:
• Restored Parliament's term from 6 years back to 5 years
• Gave President the power to return Cabinet advice once
• Restored Right to Property from Fundamental Right to a legal right (Art. 300A)
Passed by the Janata Government after Emergency ended.
GROUP 17 · GOVERNOR FOR MULTIPLE STATES
Q18
Medium Factual TNPSC Gr.2 SSC
Which Constitutional Amendment allows the same person to be Governor of two States simultaneously?
ஒரே நேரத்தில் ஒருவர் இரண்டு மாநிலங்களுக்கு ஆளுநராக இருக்கலாம் என்று எந்த சட்டதிருத்தம் கூறுகிறது?
A) 6th Amendment B) 7th Amendment (1956) ✓ C) 12th Amendment D) 14th Amendment
✅ ANSWER
7th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1956 — allows same Governor to serve two States
📌 EXPLANATION
The 7th Amendment (1956) (States Reorganisation) enabled one person to serve as Governor of more than one state simultaneously — to save administrative cost and ensure efficiency.

Real-world example: Tamil Nadu and Kerala sometimes share a Governor. Recently, Tamilisai Soundararajan was simultaneously Governor of Telangana and Puducherry. Currently, Tamil Nadu's Governor RN Ravi also served Kerala.

GROUP 19 · HOMEWORK QUESTION
Q20
Hard Conceptual TNPSC Gr.1/2
[HOMEWORK] The power to amend the Constitution is contained in which Part of the Constitution?
அரசியலமைப்பை திருத்தும் அதிகாரம் எந்த பகுதியில் உள்ளது?
A) Part 9 B) Part 12 C) Part 20 D) Part 21
✅ ANSWER
Part XX (Part 20) — Amendment of the Constitution (Article 368)
📌 EXPLANATION
Part XX (Part 20) of the Indian Constitution contains Article 368 which provides for the amendment of the Constitution. This is the ONLY article in Part XX.

The Constitution had 395 Articles when it came into force on January 26, 1950. Currently it has 440+ Articles due to various amendments.
⚠️ CRITICAL — Total Articles
At commencement (Jan 26, 1950): 395 Articles. Currently: 440+ Articles (due to insertions by amendments). This number keeps growing with new amendments.
