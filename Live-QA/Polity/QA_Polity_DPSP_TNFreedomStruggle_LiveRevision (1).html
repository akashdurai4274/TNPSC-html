Q1 Medium Factual

Who served as the Mayor of Madras (Chennai) in 1939?
1939ஆம் ஆண்டு சென்னை மேயராக பணியாற்றியவர் யார்?
APeriyar (E.V. Ramasamy)
BS. Satyamurthy
CSrinivasa Iyengar
DG. Subramaniam
✅ Correct Answer
B — S. Satyamurthy
Served as Mayor of Madras in 1939
📌 Explanation
S. Satyamurthy was a prominent Congress leader from Tamil Nadu who served as Mayor of Madras in 1939. He is most famously known as the political guru (mentor) of Karmaveerar Kamarajar. Satyamurthy was a brilliant orator and nationalist who deeply influenced Kamarajar's political career.
💎
Live Gem — Political Guru Chain
The teacher expanded this into a memorable "Political Guru Chain" — a high-frequency exam pattern:

Kamarajar's Political Guru → S. Satyamurthy
Mahatma Gandhi's Political Guru → Gopal Krishna Gokhale
Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose's Political Guru → C.R. Das (Chitta Ranjan Das)
Netaji's Spiritual/Other Guru mentioned by students → Muthurama­lingam Thevar (noted by students)

Note: The teacher confirmed Netaji's political guru is C.R. Das — NOT Gokhale. This is a frequent TRAP in exams.
🏆
PYQ Alert
Political Guru relationships have been directly asked in TNPSC Group 2A (2018) and Group 4 (2019) exams. The Kamarajar–Satyamurthy link is a top-repeat question.
Q2 Hard Factual
TNPSC Gr.1 TNPSC Gr.2
Who donated her jewels in support of the Neel Statue (James Neill) removal agitation?
நீல் சிலை சத்தியாகிரகத்தை ஆதரிப்பதாக தனது நகைகளை வழங்கியவர்?
AManju Vasini
BPadmavathi Ammal (Padmanabha Iyer's wife)
CPadmavathi Asher
DSwarnam Ammal
✅ Correct Answer
B — Padmavathi Ammal
Donated her jewels to fund the Neel Statue Agitation (1927)
📌 Explanation — Full Story
Who was James Neill? A British military officer associated with the 1857 Revolt. During the Kanpur (Cawnpore) massacre — where many British soldiers were killed — Neill retaliated by massacring thousands of innocent Indians, including children. He is thus known as the perpetrator of the "Kanpur Massacre."

The Neill Statue: In 1920s, a statue of James Neill was erected on Mount Road (now Anna Salai), Chennai — an act Indians considered insulting, as the man had slaughtered their people.

The Agitation (1927): A person named Somayajulu from Tirunelveli led the Neel Statue Removal Agitation. Padmavathi Ammal donated her jewellery to fund this protest movement.

Outcome: After Rajaji became CM in 1937, the statue was removed and placed in the Madras Museum — where it can be visited today.
🏆
PYQ Alert
This question was directly asked in TNPSC Group 2 exam. The "jewels donated for agitation" angle is a classic trick question. Padmavathi Ammal is the answer — not Manjuvasini.
💎
Live Gem — James Neill's History
The teacher connected the Neill Statue agitation to the 1857 Great Revolt — linking it to the Kanpur Massacre perpetrated by James Neill. This historical context helps remember WHY the statue was controversial. The statue remained until 1937 when Rajaji's Congress government removed it.
Q3 Hard Factual
TNPSC Gr.1 TNPSC Gr.2
Who was the first woman representative of the All India Congress Party from Tamil Nadu?
அகில இந்திய காங்கிரஸ் கட்சியின் தமிழ் மாநிலத்தின் முதல் பெண் பிரதிநிதி யார்?
AKrishnambal Jagannathan
BS.N. Sundarambal
CNagammaiyar (Periyar's first wife)
DMuthulakshmi Reddy
✅ Correct Answer
A — Nagammaiyar
First woman member of the Tamil Nadu Congress Committee (TNCC)
📌 Explanation
Nagammaiyar was the first wife of Periyar (E.V. Ramasamy). She was elected as the first woman representative of the All India Congress Party from Tamil Nadu. She participated actively in all of Periyar's freedom struggle movements.

Notable connection: In 1921, when Gandhi announced the Temperance Movement (Kallu Kadai Mariyaal — Liquor Shop Picketing), a British journalist asked Gandhi when he would stop this movement. Gandhi famously replied: "The power to stop this movement is not in my hands; it is in the hands of two women from Erode." — referring to Nagammaiyar and Periyar's sister.
💎
Live Gem — Gandhi's Famous Quote about Nagammaiyar
Gandhi's statement about "two women from Erode" having the power to stop the Temperance Movement is a deeply significant fact. The two women were: (1) Nagammaiyar (Periyar's first wife) and (2) Periyar's younger sister. This shows the immense power these women wielded in the freedom struggle — a fact rarely discussed in standard textbooks but frequently asked in TNPSC.
⚠️
Trap Note
Students commonly confuse this with Krishnambal Jagannathan or Muthulakshmi Reddy. The keyword is "First Woman Representative of TNCC (Tamil Nadu Congress Committee)" = Nagammaiyar.
Q4 Medium Application
TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.1
Who is described in the following statements?
(i) She launched the "Land for Tillers' Freedom" (LAFTI) programme in 1981.
(ii) She presided over the Coral Jubilee (75th anniversary) of the Vedaranyam Salt March in 2006.
இவர் 1981ல் நிலமற்றவர்களுக்கு நிலம் திட்டத்தை துவங்கினார். இவர் 2006ல் வேதாரணம் உப்பு சத்தியாகிரகத்தின் பவள விழாவிற்கு தலைமை தாங்கினார்.
✅ Correct Answer
Krishnambal Jagannathan
📌 Explanation
Krishnambal Jagannathan was a prominent freedom fighter and social reformer from Tamil Nadu.

Vedaranyam Salt March (1930): Rajaji (C. Rajagopalachari) led approximately 100 people on a march from Thiruchirapalli to Vedaranyam (April 13 to April 28, 1930) to defy the British salt tax — parallel to Gandhi's Dandi March.

Krishnambal Jagannathan's Role: She participated in this Salt Satyagraha and was also involved in the Individual Satyagraha. She notably participated in the Quit India Movement (1942).

Pavazhavizha = Coral Jubilee: Pavazhavizha = 75 years anniversary (Coral Jubilee). In 2006, the 75th year of the Vedaranyam Salt March was celebrated, and Krishnambal Jagannathan presided over it.

Q5 Medium Factual + Assertion-Reason
TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.4
Assertion: E.V. Ramasamy (Periyar) organised an Anti-Hindi Conference in Salem.
Reason: When the first Congress ministry came to power, Rajaji abolished the Sales Tax.
கூற்று: பெரியார் சேலத்தில் இந்தி எதிர்ப்பு மாநாட்டை நடத்தினார். காரணம்: காங்கிரஸின் முதல் அமைச்சரவையின் போது ராஜாஜி விற்பனை வரியை ரத்து செய்தார்.
ABoth Assertion and Reason are correct, and Reason explains Assertion
BBoth Assertion and Reason are correct, but Reason does NOT explain Assertion
CAssertion is correct but Reason is WRONG
DAssertion is wrong but Reason is correct
✅ Correct Answer
C — Assertion is correct, Reason is WRONG
📌 Explanation
Assertion (CORRECT): Periyar, along with Bharathidasan and Maraimalai Adigal, opposed the Hindi imposition. When Rajaji became CM in 1937 and made Hindi compulsory in 125 schools across Madras, Periyar organised an Anti-Hindi conference in Salem and led protests. Periyar marched students from Salem to Madras and protested in front of Iraiyavar School. He was arrested and later imprisoned in Vellore.

Reason (WRONG): Rajaji did NOT abolish the Sales Tax. What actually happened: When Rajaji became CM in 1937 (after Congress's first election win), he banned liquor (alcohol). To compensate for the revenue lost from the liquor ban, he INTRODUCED the Sales Tax (Virpanai Vari) — not abolished it.
🚨
Critical — Common Mistake
Rajaji INTRODUCED Sales Tax (not abolished it) to compensate for revenue lost from the liquor ban. Many students get this reversed. Assertion ✅ + Reason ❌ = Answer C.
💎
Live Gem — Why Congress Avoided Elections from 1920 to 1937
The teacher posed a bonus question: "Why did the Congress Party not contest elections from 1920 to 1937 (17 years)?" — This was part of Gandhi's Non-Cooperation Movement strategy where Congress boycotted British institutions including legislatures. Congress first contested elections in 1937 under the Government of India Act, 1935. This context helps answer related questions about Rajaji becoming the first Congress CM.
Q6 Hard Factual
TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.1
During the Quit India Movement (1942), where did the clash between Congress volunteers and the British Army take place in Tamil Nadu?
வெள்ளையனை வெளியேற்றும் போராட்டத்தின் போது காங்கிரஸ் தொண்டர்களுக்கும் ராணுவத்துக்கும் இடையே மோதல் நடைபெற்ற இடம் எது?
AErode
BMadras (Chennai)
CSalem
DMadurai
✅ Correct Answer
D — Madurai
The clash between Congress volunteers and the British Army occurred in Madurai during the Quit India Movement (1942)
📌 Explanation
The Quit India Movement was launched on August 8, 1942 in Bombay (not 1943 as some options may suggest). Kamarajar represented Tamil Nadu at the Bombay session. On his way back, he received information at Arakkona­m Railway Station that Congress leaders were being arrested. He escaped from there, went underground, mobilised people, and later voluntarily surrendered for arrest. During this movement, the major clash between Congress volunteers and the British Army took place in Madurai.
💎
Live Gem — Kamarajar's Escape Story
The Full Kamarajar–Arakkona­m Story:
• Gandhi held the Quit India session in Bombay, Aug 8, 1942
• Each state sent only select representatives — Kamarajar went from TN
• The British arrested all Congress leaders at railway stations the next morning
• Kamarajar received a tip-off at Arakkona­m Railway Station (before reaching Madras Central)
• He was the only leader to escape arrest that day
• He went underground, organised the masses with Gandhi's instructions
• Later, he voluntarily surrendered himself for arrest

This story explains why the WRONG answer in exam options often includes "Kamarajar went underground and NEVER got arrested" — he DID eventually get arrested.

📜 DPSP — Directive Principles of State Policy
Q9 – Q14
💡
DPSP Quick Intro
DPSP = Directive Principles of State Policy (அரசுக்கு வழிகாட்டும் நெறிமுறைகள்)
• Located in Part IV of the Indian Constitution
• Articles 36 to 51
• Borrowed from the Irish Constitution
• NON-JUSTICIABLE — Courts cannot enforce these
• These are ADVISORY to the government (not binding like Fundamental Rights)
• Fundamental Rights = Individual rights | DPSP = State/Government guidelines
Q9 Medium Conceptual
TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.4 SSC
Choose the CORRECT statement regarding Article 37 of the Indian Constitution.
(i) DPSP does NOT control/bind the government.
(ii) Courts CAN question whether DPSP provisions are implemented.
அரசு நெறிமுறை கோட்பாடுகள் அரசாங்கத்தை கட்டுப்படுத்தாது. நடைமுறைப்படுத்துவது குறித்து நீதிமன்றங்கள் கேள்வி எழுப்ப முடியும்.
✅ Correct Answer
Only Statement (i) is correct
DPSP does NOT bind the government. Courts CANNOT question non-implementation of DPSP.
📌 Explanation — Article 37
Art. 37 Article 37 itself states the nature of DPSP:
• DPSP are NOT enforceable in any court
• Courts CANNOT compel the government to implement DPSP
• DPSP is like advice to the government — if followed, good; if not, courts cannot intervene
• Analogy used by teacher: "Like a parent advising a child — 'if you study, you'll get a government job' — but no one can force you to follow the advice."
Key Rule: Fundamental Rights = Justiciable (Courts enforce them) | DPSP = Non-Justiciable (Courts cannot enforce them). Article 37 explicitly states this distinction.
💎
Live Gem — Small vs Capital Letters in Constitution
Critical insight about Constitutional Articles:
• Original Constitution (Jan 26, 1950) used small letters: 1a, 1b, 1c
• Capital letters (21A, 39A, etc.) = Added LATER by Constitutional Amendment

So: Article 21A (Right to Education for 6–14 yr olds) was NOT in the original Constitution — it was added by the 86th Constitutional Amendment, 2002. Capital letter = amendment-added article. This is a high-value exam distinction!

Q12 Hard Trick/Trap — Incorrect Statement
TNPSC Gr.2 TNPSC Gr.4
Choose the CORRECT statement about Article 40:
(i) The government should establish Panchayati Raj institutions.
(ii) The 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992 implemented Panchayati Raj.
ஆர்டிகிள் 40 — பஞ்சாயத்துராஜ் அமைப்புகளை ஏற்படுத்த வேண்டும். 73வது அரசியலமைப்பு திருத்த சட்டம் 1993ம் ஆண்டு நடைமுறைப்படுத்தப்பட்டது.
✅ Correct Answer — Statement I is correct, Statement II has an error
Statement I ✅ | Statement II ❌ (Year is wrong)
The 73rd Amendment was PASSED in 1992 but came into FORCE on April 24, 1993
🚨
Critical — Year Trap for Art. 40 / 73rd Amendment
73rd Constitutional Amendment:
• Passed by Parliament: 1992
• Came into Force: April 24, 1993

Exam options often say "1993" for the amendment — if the question asks when it was "enacted/passed," answer is 1992. If "when it came into force/implemented," answer is 1993. Read the question carefully!
💎
Live Gem — Art. 40 is a Perennial TNPSC Favourite
The teacher explicitly said: "Article 40 comes in TNPSC exams every year — like Pongal, Diwali, Christmas, Eid — it comes without fail!" This is the Panchayati Raj Article — extremely high frequency in TNPSC exams. Combined with:
• Art. 40 = DPSP for Panchayats
• 73rd Amendment 1992/1993 = Made Panchayati Raj a constitutional reality
• National Panchayati Raj Day = April 24 (commemorating the date it came into force)
