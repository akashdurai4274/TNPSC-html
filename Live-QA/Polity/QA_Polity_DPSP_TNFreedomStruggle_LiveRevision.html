Tamil Nadu Freedom Struggle — Key Personalities TN HISTORY

Q 01 MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.2/2A Gr.4/VAO
Who served as the Mayor of Madras (Chennai) in 1939?
Options: Periyar / S. Satyamurthi / Srinivasa Nadar / G. Subramaniam
1939ஆம் ஆண்டு சென்னை மேயராக பணியாற்றியவர் யார்?
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
S. Satyamurthi (எஸ். சத்தியமூர்த்தி)
📌 EXPLANATION
S. Satyamurthi served as the Mayor of Madras in 1939. He was a prominent Congress leader and orator from Tamil Nadu.
💎 LIVE GEM — Political Guru Chain
The teacher drew a direct link between political gurus:
Kamarajar's political guru = S. Satyamurthi
Mahatma Gandhi's political guru = Gopal Krishna Gokhale
Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose's political guru = C.R. Das (Chittaranjan Das)
Note: Some students incorrectly answered "Muthuramaling Thevar" for Netaji — this is WRONG. Muthuramaling Thevar was a Netaji supporter/follower, not his political guru.
Q 02 HARD Factual TNPSC Gr.2/2A Gr.1
Who was registered / enrolled at Bala Mandir in 1949 under the encouragement of Kamarajar?
Options: Vanchivadani / Padmabai Ammal / Padmathi / Shoornath Ammal
1949ல் பால மந்திரில் காமராஜரின் ஊக்கத்தின் கீழ் யார் பதிவு செய்தார்?
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Option D — Vanchivadani (வஞ்சுவாசினி)
📌 EXPLANATION
Vanchivadani was S. Satyamurthi's disciple (shishya). While Q1 asked about the guru (Satyamurthi), Q2 asks about the disciple — this is a direct conceptual follow-on. In 1949, under Kamarajar's encouragement, Vanchivadani enrolled at Bala Mandir to do social work for the freedom movement.
🪤 TRAP — Guru vs Disciple
Questions Q1 and Q2 are linked: Q1 = Guru (Satyamurthi), Q2 = Disciple (Vanchivadani). Exam setters place these back-to-back to test if you confuse the roles. Always note the year (1949) and the institution (Bala Mandir).
Q 03 HARD Factual TNPSC Gr.2 Gr.1
Who donated her jewellery to support the Neel Statue (Neil Statue) Removal Satyagraha?
Options: Vanchivadani / Padmabai Ammal / Padmathi Ashir / Sornath Ammal
நீல் சிலை சத்யாரகத்தை ஆதரிப்பதாக தனது நகைகளை வழங்கியவர் யார்?
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Padmabai Ammal (பத்மாவாசினி / பத்மாபாய் அம்மாள்) — Option B
🏆 PYQ ALERT — TNPSC Group 2 Previous Paper
This exact question was asked in a previous TNPSC Group 2 exam. The answer is Padmabai Ammal who donated her jewellery to fund the Neil Statue Removal agitation (1927).
💎 LIVE GEM — James Neill Story (Background)
Who was James Neill? A British officer stationed in Madras. During the 1857 Great Revolt (Kanpur Massacre), he massacred thousands of innocent Indians and children at Kanpur as revenge. Later (around 1920), a statue of James Neill was erected on Mount Road (now Anna Salai), Chennai.

Why was the statue controversial? Indians were outraged that a man who killed Indian civilians had a statue on Indian soil. A protest was led by Somayajulu (from Tirunelveli) to remove the statue. Padmabai Ammal donated her jewellery to fund this agitation.

When was it finally removed? In 1937, after Rajaji became Chief Minister, the statue was removed and placed in the Madras Museum — where it can still be seen today.
Q 04 HARD Factual TNPSC Gr.2/2A Gr.1
Who was the FIRST WOMAN REPRESENTATIVE of the All India Congress Party from Tamil Nadu?
அகில இந்திய காங்கிரஸ் கட்சியின் தமிழ் மாநிலத்தின் முதல் பெண் பிரதிநிதி யார்?
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Nagammaiyar (நாகம்மையார்) — Option A
📌 EXPLANATION
Nagammaiyar was the first wife of Periyar (E.V. Ramasamy). She was elected as the first woman representative of the Tamil Nadu Congress Committee (TNCC). She was also a key leader in the Temperance Movement of 1921.
💎 LIVE GEM — Temperance Movement 1921 & Two Women of Erode
When journalists asked Gandhi when he would stop the Temperance Movement (1921 — campaign against alcohol / liquor shops), Gandhi replied:
"The power to stop this movement is not in my hands — it is in the hands of two women of Erode."

Those two women were:
1. Nagammaiyar (Periyar's first wife)
2. Periyar's younger sister (name to be filled in by students — teacher asked this as a live challenge question)

Periyar's full name: E.V. Ramasamy (ஈவேரா — NOT the Sun News anchor!)
🚨 CRITICAL — Tricky Options
Students should NOT confuse this with S.N. Sundarambal. Option "Sundarambal" was listed but is INCORRECT for this question. The correct answer is Nagammaiyar (Periyar's first wife).
Q 05 HARD Application / Statement-Based TNPSC Gr.2 Gr.1
Identify the personality described by the following statements:
1. She initiated the "Land for the Tillers' Freedom" programme in 1981.
2. In 2006, she presided over the coral jubilee (75th anniversary) of the Vedaranyam Salt March.
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Krishnambal Jagannathan (கிருஷ்ணாம்பால் ஜகாநாதன்)
📌 EXPLANATION
Coral Jubilee = 75 years. The Vedaranyam Salt March was conducted in 1930. 2006 − 1930 = 76 years ≈ 75th anniversary (Coral Jubilee). Krishnambal Jagannathan presided over this event. She also launched the "Land for Tillers' Freedom" (LAFTI) scheme in 1981 for landless labourers.
💎 LIVE GEM — Vedaranyam March Details
Rajaji's Salt March (Tamil Nadu):
• Started: 13 April 1930
• Ended: 28 April 1930
• Route: Tiruchirappalli → Vedaranyam
• Approximately 100 marchers participated
• Krishnambal Jagannathan also participated in the Quit India Movement (1942) — she worked actively in the "Vellaiyanai Veliyeru" (Quit India) agitation.

Jubilee types (important for exam):
Silver = 25 yrs | Pearl = 30 yrs | Ruby = 40 yrs | Gold = 50 yrs | Diamond = 60 yrs | Coral/Platinum = 75 yrs | Centenary = 100 yrs
🏆 PYQ ALERT — TNPSC Group 2
This question was previously asked in a TNPSC Group 2 exam. The key identifier is the 1981 LAFTI scheme combined with the 2006 Vedaranyam jubilee.

Anti-Hindi Agitation, Quit India Movement & Key Facts TN HISTORY

Q 06 MEDIUM Conceptual / Assertion-Reason TNPSC Gr.2
Statement (Assertion): Periyar held an Anti-Hindi Conference in Salem.
Reason: During the first Congress ministry (Rajaji), the Sales Tax was abolished.
Choose the correct answer.
தந்தை பெரியார் சேலத்தில் இந்தி எதிர்ப்பு மாநாட்டை நடத்தினார். காங்கிரஸின் முதல் அமைச்சரவையின் போது ராஜாஜி விற்பனை வரியை ரத்து செய்தார்.
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Assertion is CORRECT. Reason is WRONG. → Answer: Option C (A is true, R is false)
💎 LIVE GEM — 1937 Congress & Rajaji's Actions
Why 1937 was the FIRST time Congress contested elections: From 1920 to 1937 (17 years), the Congress party did NOT contest elections. In 1937 they first contested and won; Rajaji became CM.

What Rajaji actually did as CM:
1. He BANNED liquor (alcohol prohibition) in Madras.
2. To compensate for lost revenue, he INTRODUCED the Sales Tax.
3. He did NOT abolish it — he INTRODUCED it. Hence the Reason in the question is FALSE.

Periyar's Anti-Hindi Agitation (1937):
Rajaji made Hindi compulsory in 125 Madras schools. Periyar, along with Bharathidasan and Maraimalai Adigal, led protests from Salem, marched students to Madras, protested at Irayananar School. Periyar was arrested and sent to Vellore prison.
🚨 CRITICAL — Rajaji's Policy: Introduced vs Abolished
Rajaji INTRODUCED Sales Tax (to replace liquor revenue) — he did NOT abolish it. This is a frequent exam trap. Assertion ✓ | Reason ✗
Q 07 MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.2/2A Gr.4/VAO
During the Quit India Movement, at which place did the clash between Congress volunteers and the military take place?
Options: Erode / Madras / Salem / Madurai
வெள்ளையனை வெளியிக்கத்தின் போது ராணுவத்துடனான காங்கிரஸ் தொண்டர்களின் மோதல் நடைபெற்ற இடம் எது?
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Madurai (மதுரை)
Q 08 HARD Trick / Incorrect Statement TNPSC Gr.2 Gr.1
Choose the INCORRECT statement:
A. The Quit India Resolution was passed on 8 August 1943.
B. Kamarajar went underground and rallied the people for the Quit India cause.
தவறான கூற்றுகளை தேர்வு செய்க: ஆகஸ்ட் 8, 1943ஆம் ஆண்டு வெள்ளையனை வெளியேறு தீர்மானம் நிறைவேற்றப்பட்டது.
✅ CORRECT ANSWER (= the INCORRECT statement)
Statement A is INCORRECT → Correct answer = Option A only
🚨 CRITICAL — Year Error: 1942 not 1943
The Quit India Resolution was passed on 8 August 1942 (NOT 1943) at Bombay, by Gandhi. Statement A says 1943 — that is the error. Statement B about Kamarajar going underground is CORRECT.
💎 LIVE GEM — Kamarajar's Escape at Arkonam
After the Bombay session (August 8, 1942), the British arrested Congress leaders at every railway station. Kamarajar was on the train from Bombay back to Madras. At Arkonam (Arakkonam) railway station, he received a tip that police were waiting at Chennai Central. He escaped at Arkonam, went underground, mobilised people for the Quit India cause with Gandhi's instructions, and then voluntarily surrendered himself for arrest. This is a high-value narrative fact for Gr.1/Gr.2.

Directive Principles of State Policy — Introduction & Article 37 POLITY · DPSP

Q 09 EASY Conceptual TNPSC Gr.4/VAO Gr.2/2A
Choose the correct statement about Article 37 of the Indian Constitution (DPSP):
A. DPSP does NOT control the government; courts cannot question non-implementation.
B. DPSP controls the government; courts can question it.
அரசு நெறிமுறை கோட்பாடுகள் அரசாங்கத்தை கட்டுப்படுத்தாது — எந்த நீதிமன்றமும் கேள்வி எழுப்ப முடியாது.
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Option A — DPSP does NOT control the government; courts CANNOT question non-implementation.
💎 LIVE GEM — DPSP vs Fundamental Rights (Key Distinction)
Fundamental Rights = Given to every INDIVIDUAL citizen. If violated, any individual can go to court.
DPSP = Advisory instructions given to the GOVERNMENT by the Constitution. They are like a policy wish-list — "If the government follows these, the nation will be better."

Teacher's analogy: "It's like advice — 'Study properly and you can become a government officer.' Your dad says it. If you don't follow it, he can't drag you to court!"

DPSP Location: Part IV, Articles 36–51 of the Indian Constitution.
Nature: Non-justiciable (not enforceable by courts).

DPSP — Articles 38 to 45 (High-Yield) POLITY · DPSP

Q 10 MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.4/VAO Gr.2/2A
Which Article says the government should make efforts to improve the well-being of the people and maintain social order?
Options: 39 / 39A / 38 / 37
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Article 38
💡 TIP — Memory Trick from Teacher
"Order Poduvar — 38 vayasula!"
Think of someone placing an ORDER at age 38. Article 38 = Social ORDER / Well-being of people.
Teacher's mnemonic: "At 38 years old, he placed his order" → Article 38 = Social Order maintenance.
💎 LIVE GEM — Article 39A
Article 39A = The State shall provide free legal aid to poor/weaker sections to ensure they can access justice. This is a Directive Principle that resulted in the Legal Services Authorities Act.
Q 11 MEDIUM Factual / Year-Based Trap TNPSC Gr.2/2A Gr.4/VAO
Choose the correct statement about Article 40 (Panchayati Raj):
A. Government should establish Panchayati Raj systems.
B. 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act of 1993 operationalised Panchayati Raj.
ஆர்டிகள் 40 — பஞ்சாயத்துராஜ் அமைப்புகளை ஏற்படுத்த முயற்சிகள் மேற்கொள்ள வேண்டும். 73வது சட்டத்திருத்தம் 1993ல் நடைமுறைப்படுத்தப்பட்டது.
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Statement A = CORRECT. Statement B = INCORRECT (year is wrong).
🚨 CRITICAL — 73rd Amendment: 1992 vs 1993
The 73rd Constitutional Amendment ACT was passed in 1992.
It came into force/effect in 1993.
Questions often trap students by asking about the year. "Passed" = 1992. "Operationalised/Implemented" = 1993. The statement in this question says "1993 was enacted" — this is partially misleading; the Act was 1992 but implemented 1993. Know BOTH years.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
Article 40 + Panchayati Raj + 73rd Amendment is asked repeatedly in TNPSC exams every year. The teacher described it as "coming like Pongal every year." Know Art. 40 → 73rd Amendment → 1992/1993.
Q 12 MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.4/VAO Gr.2/2A
Which Article states that workers should be given the right to participate in the management of factories/industries?
Options: 43 / 45 (etc.)
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Article 43A
💡 TIP — Article 43A vs 43B
Article 43A = Workers' participation in management of factories.
Article 43B = Promotion of Co-operative Societies (Cooperative Societies) — Gandhi's dream. This was added later by amendment.
Do NOT confuse these two sub-articles.
Q 13 MEDIUM Factual TNPSC Gr.4/VAO Gr.2/2A
Which Article says the government should provide free and compulsory education to children between ages 6 to 14?
Options: 44 / 45 / 46 / 47
ஆறு முதல் 14 வயது வரை உள்ள குழந்தைகளுக்கு இலவச கட்டாய கல்வி வழங்க வேண்டும் என எந்த விதி கூறுகிறது?
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
Article 45
💎 LIVE GEM — Article 21A vs Article 45 Connection
Original DPSP (Art. 45): Free and compulsory education for children 6–14 years — this was a Directive (advisory).
86th Constitutional Amendment, 2002 added Article 21A which made this a Fundamental Right (Right to Education Act, 2009).
So the progression is: Art. 45 (DPSP) → Art. 21A (Fundamental Right after amendment).

Also related: Fundamental Duties (Art. 51A) — added by the 42nd Amendment (teacher set this as the comment-section question for students to answer).
🚨 CRITICAL — Article 45 vs Article 21A
Art. 45 in DPSP = policy directive for 6–14 education.
Art. 21A = Fundamental Right to Education (added by 86th Amendment, 2002).
Both relate to the same subject but are in DIFFERENT parts of the Constitution.
Q 14 MEDIUM Factual / Scheme-Based TNPSC Gr.2/2A Gr.4/VAO
In which year was the Maternity Benefit Act enacted?
Options: 1960 / 1961 / 1962 / 1963
பேர்கால உதவி சட்டம் எப்போது இயற்றப்பட்டது?
✅ CORRECT ANSWER
1961 (Option B)
💎 LIVE GEM — TN Maternity Scheme: Muthu Lakshmi Ammal Scheme
Tamil Nadu's Muthulakshmi Reddy Maternity Benefit Scheme (மகப்பேறு உதவி திட்டம்) provides ₹18,000 to pregnant women in THREE instalments of ₹6,000 each.

Purpose: Nutritional support for mother and child (pre-natal and post-natal).
Beneficiaries: Below-poverty-line pregnant women.

Current Maternity Leave: Paid maternity leave in India = 26 weeks (≈ 6 months) for government and private sector employees (Maternity Benefit Amendment Act, 2017 extended it from 12 to 26 weeks).
