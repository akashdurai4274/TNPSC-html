§1 · Languages & 8th Schedule (Q25–Q30)
Q25
Which Schedule of the Indian Constitution deals with languages?
EASY Factual Gr.2 / Gr.4
✅ Answer
8th Schedule (Eighth Schedule) — Part XVII of the Constitution (Articles 344–351)
Part XVII of the Constitution begins at Article 363 (as noted by teacher). The 8th Schedule lists officially recognised languages. Part XVII covers Articles 343–351 dealing with Official Language.
🏆 PYQ ALERTFrequently asked in TNPSC Gr.2, Gr.4, and Group 1 Prelims. "Which Schedule lists official languages?" is a standard question.

💎 Live Gem — National Anthem vs National Song

Q85
What is "Constitution Day" (Indian Constitution Day / Samvidhan Divas)? Don't confuse it with Republic Day!
MEDIUM Trick/Trap All Exams
✅ Answer
November 26 (1949) = Constitution Day (Samvidhan Divas) — the day the Constitution was adopted by the Assembly. January 26 (1950) = Republic Day — the day it came into force.
💎 Live Gem — Critical Distinction
This is one of the most common TNPSC mistakes. Candidates write January 26 for both. Remember: Adopted (Nov 26, 1949) ≠ Enforced/Implemented (Jan 26, 1950). Constitution Day was officially declared in 2015 by the Modi government.
🏆 PYQ ALERTConstitution Day vs Republic Day distinction has appeared in multiple years of TNPSC Group 2 and Group 4.
