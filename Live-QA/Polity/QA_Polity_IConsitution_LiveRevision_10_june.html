⚖️ Part I: Indian Constitutional Amendments (Unit 5)

Q1
Medium Factual / Structural TNPSC Gr. 2/4 & SSC
Through which Constitutional Amendment Act was the States Reorganisation Act enacted?
எந்த சட்டதிருத்தம் மூலம் மாநிலங்களின் மறுச்சீரமைப்பு சட்டம் நிறைவேற்றப்பட்டது?
✅ Correct Answer: Option B · 7th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1956

Official Explanation: Following independence in 1947, India required structural organization. Three key commissions were set up sequentially to evaluate linguistic boundaries: the S.K. Dhar Commission (1948), the JVP Committee (1948), and ultimately the Fazl Ali Commission (1953). Based on the recommendations of the Fazl Ali Commission, the States Reorganisation Act was implemented. On November 1, 1956, India was reorganized into 14 States and 6 Union Territories based primarily on linguistic lines. This monumental change was constitutionalized via the 7th Amendment Act of 1956.

💎 Live Faculty Memory Trick / Gem

Mnemonic Formula from Live Stream: Think of the target baseline of states created: 14 States. Divide 14 by 2, and you instantly get 7! Hence, 7th Amendment = 14 States enacted in the year 1956.

🪤 TNPSC / SSC Trap Alert

Do not confuse the implementation date. The Act was passed in 1956, but structural panels started as early as 1948. SSC and TNPSC often trap candidates by switching the structural findings: The Dhar Commission and JVP Committee firmly rejected pure linguistic division as a primary threat to security, whereas the Fazl Ali Commission strategically accepted it while safeguarding national unity.

Q2
Easy Factual TNPSC Gr. 4
By which Constitutional Amendment Act was the name of the official language "Oriya" renamed to "Odia"?
எந்த திருத்த சட்டத்தின் மூலம் ஒரியாவுக்கு ஒடியா என்று பெயர்மாற்றம் செய்யப்பட்டது?
✅ Correct Answer: Option C · 96th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2011

Official Explanation: The alteration changed the spelling and phonetic designation of both the state name (from Orissa to Odisha) and its language listed under the Eighth Schedule. The language name "Oriya" was formally altered to "Odia" by the 96th Constitutional Amendment Act of 2011.

Q3
Medium Conceptual Matched Pair TNPSC Gr. 1/2
Select the correctly matched pairs regarding the official languages and their corresponding Constitutional Amendments:
சரியான இணையை தேர்வு செய்க: (அலுவல் மொழி - சட்டதிருத்தம்)

Given choices in discussion: 21st Amendment introduced Sindhi | 71st Amendment introduced Konkani, Manipuri, Nepali.

✅ Correct Answer: Option B · Statement 2 Only Correct (Evaluating individual pairings)

Official Explanation: India has no singular National Language (தேசிய மொழி கிடையாது). Instead, Schedule 8 (எட்டாவது அட்டவணை) contains 22 Official Languages (அலுவல் மொழிகள்). The structural historical evolution is critical for exams:
• The original constitution in 1950 recognized only 14 languages.
• The 21st Amendment Act (1967) added exactly 1 language: Sindhi.
• The 71st Amendment Act (1992) added 3 languages: Konkani, Manipuri, and Nepali.
• The 92nd Amendment Act (2003) added 4 languages: Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, and Santhali.
Hence, if a pairing states Konkani was appended via the 21st, it is demonstrably incorrect.

🏆 PYQ Alert (TNPSC 2018-2024)

The mathematical equation of Language Amendments is a classic question. Notice the pattern: 21 + 71 = 92! The 21st Amendment and the 71st Amendment sum up perfectly to the 92nd Amendment, which is the third major linguistic adjustment block.

Q4
Hard Conceptual Trap TNPSC Gr. 1/2 / SSC CGL
Which of the following statements is/are correctly matched?
1. 86th Amendment Act: Made elementary education a Fundamental Right.
2. 42nd Amendment Act: Removed Right to Property from Fundamental Rights and made it a legal right.
பின்வருவனவற்றுள் எவை சரியாகப் பொருந்தியுள்ளது?
1. 86-வது திருத்தம்: தொடக்கக் கல்வியை அடிப்படை உரிமையாக்கியது.
2. 42-வது திருத்தம்: சொத்துரிமையை அடிப்படை உரிமையிலிருந்து நீக்கி சட்டபூர்வ உரிமையாக்கியது.
✅ Correct Answer: Option A · 1 Only Is Correct

Official Explanation: Statement 1 is flawless: The 86th Constitutional Amendment Act of 2002 introduced Article 21A, making free and compulsory elementary education a Fundamental Right for children aged 6 to 14.
Statement 2 is a classic examiner trap: The Right to Property was initially an absolute Fundamental Right under Article 19(1)(f) and Article 31. However, it was deleted from the Fundamental Rights list and transformed into a mere legal right under Article 300A not by the 42nd, but by the 44th Amendment Act of 1978 under the Morarji Desai led Janata Government.

🪤 The 42nd vs 44th Definitive Distinction Trap

Students in the live stream chat continuously selected "Both 1 and 2".
Faculty Fix: The 42nd Amendment (1976) under Indira Gandhi is the "Mini-Constitution" that expanded centralized reach. The 44th Amendment (1978) was passed by the opposing government to correct executive overreaches and strip property status down to a legal right. Remember: 4+4 = 8 (1978).

Q5
Medium Conceptual / Polity TNPSC Unit 5 / Social Justice
What does the 93rd Constitutional Amendment Act, 2005 deal with?
93-வது அரசியலமைப்பு சட்ட திருத்தம் எதை பற்றி கூறுகிறது?
✅ Correct Answer: Option B · Extension of Reservations in Educational Institutions

Official Explanation: The 93rd Amendment Act, 2005 enables the state to make special provisions for the advancement of socially and educationally backward classes (OBCs, SCs, and STs) regarding their admission to educational institutions, including private aided or unaided institutions (except minority institutions). It added Article 15(5).

💎 Live Gem · Ambedkar's Stance on Structural Equality

Deep Faculty Analysis from Stream: During the live interaction, the teacher illuminated a vital historical fact. When Dr. B.R. Ambedkar introduced reservations, he initially contextualized a political timeline constraint of 10 years for legislative seats, hoping India would achieve structural socio-economic parity by then. However, because systemic caste barriers and unequal educational access persisted, subsequent amendments extended it. The 93rd expanded educational reservations, and recently the 104th Amendment Act extended political reservations for SC/STs in the Lok Sabha and State Assemblies for another 10 years until 2030.

Q6
Medium Factual / Multi-Statement TNPSC Gr. 1/2
Choose the incorrect statement from the following choices:
1. The 69th Amendment designated Delhi as the National Capital Territory (NCT).
2. The 52nd Amendment introduced the Anti-Defection Law for the first time.
தவறான கூற்றுகளை தேர்வு செய்க:
1. 69-வது திருத்தம் தில்லி யூனியன் பிரதேசத்தை தில்லியின் தேசிய தலைநகர பகுதியாக மாற்றியது.
2. 52-வது திருத்தம் அரசியலமைப்பில் கட்சித்தாவல் தடை சட்டத்தை முதல் முறையாக அறிமுகப்படுத்தியது.
✅ Correct Answer: Option D · Neither 1 nor 2 (Both statements are completely correct)

Official Explanation: Both provisions stand completely validated by constitutional history.
• The 69th Amendment Act, 1991 accorded special status to Delhi, naming it the National Capital Territory and creating a 70-member legislative assembly.
• The 52nd Amendment Act, 1985 enacted the comprehensive Anti-Defection Law, adding the 10th Schedule to check political opportunism.

🪤 Structural Mechanism Trap

Faculty Breakdown: An ordinary citizen or independent figure can change political affiliations without triggering structural legislative disqualification if they aren't holding an elected house seat. However, if an elected MLA or MP wins a seat on Ticket 'A' and subsequently defects or votes against party whips to align with Party 'B', they face immediate disqualification under the 10th Schedule. This prevents arbitrary shifts in legislative majorities.

Q7
Easy Factual / High-Yield TNPSC & SSC All Exams
Which Constitutional Amendment altered the voting age limit, lowering it from 21 to 18 years?
அரசியலமைப்பின் பின்வரும் திருத்தங்களில் எது வாக்காளர்களின் வயதை 21-லிருந்து 18 ஆக குறைப்பதுடன் தொடர்புடையது?
✅ Correct Answer: 61st Constitutional Amendment Act, 1988

Official Explanation: Enacted during the tenure of Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi, the 61st Constitutional Amendment Act of 1988 amended Article 326 to expand the democratic base by reducing the voter eligibility baseline from 21 years down to 18 years, taking effective structural force in 1989.

Q8
Medium Scheme + Polity Integration TNPSC Unit 9 / Policy Manual
Which of the following statements is/are correct?
1. Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA) aims to provide computer education even in rural areas.
2. Education was moved from the State List to the Concurrent List via the 42nd Amendment Act of 1976.
பின்வரும் கூற்றுகளில் எது/எவை சரியானது?
1. சர்வ சிக்ஷா அபியான் கிராமப்புறங்களிலும் கணினி கல்வியை வழங்க முயல்கிறது.
2. 42-வது திருத்தம் 1976 மூலம் கல்வியானது பொதுப்பட்டியலில் (Concurrent List) சேர்க்கப்பட்டது.
✅ Correct Answer: Option C · Both 1 and 2 are correct

Official Explanation:
1. Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA), launched in 2001, is a flagship universal elementary education program. It integrates technology deployment to reduce the digital divide across rural schools.
2. Education was originally a state subject. The landmark 42nd Amendment Act of 1976 transferred five subjects—Education, Forests, Weights and Measures, Protection of Wild Animals and Birds, and Administration of Justice—from the State List to the Concurrent List, empowering both Center and State legislatures.

Q9
Easy Factual Formula TNPSC Gr. 4 / SSC
Which specific Article governs the procedure for the Amendment of the Indian Constitution?
இந்திய அரசியலமைப்பின் எந்த பிரிவில் அரசியலமைப்பு திருத்தம் நடைமுறை குறித்து தரப்பட்டுள்ளது?
✅ Correct Answer: Article 368 (Part XX)

Official Explanation: Part XX, under Article 368, grants Parliament the authority to amend the Constitution by way of addition, variation, or repeal, subject to the "Basic Structure" doctrine.
The surrounding options represent critical Emergency Provisions:
• Article 352: National Emergency (தேசிய அவசரநிலை)
• Article 356: State Emergency / President's Rule (மாநில அவசரநிலை)
• Article 360: Financial Emergency (நிதி அவசரநிலை)

💎 Live Mnemonic Trick

Mathematical Visual Trick from Live Stream: Add the first two digits of the article: 3 + 6 = 9. However, the last digit is written as 8. This intentional variance or "mistake" signifies that you must perform a correction or adjustment—meaning a Constitutional Amendment!

Q10
Hard Current Constitutional Law TNPSC Gr. 1/2 Core
The 103rd Constitutional Amendment Act of India deals primarily with which provision?
இந்தியாவின் 103-வது அரசியலமைப்பு திருத்தம் எது சம்பந்தமானது?
✅ Correct Answer: Option B · 10% Reservation for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)

Official Explanation: The 103rd Amendment Act, 2019 introduced a 10% reservation limit targeting individuals belonging to the Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) among the unreserved general category. It amended Articles 15 and 16 to insert economic criteria alongside historical social backwardness.

💎 Live Gem · Social Justice Jurisprudence in Tamil Nadu

Deep Interactive Faculty Explanation: A student asked why EWS isn't actively implemented in Tamil Nadu recruitment. The faculty clarified that Tamil Nadu's political model operates on a unique social justice paradigm. Dr. Ambedkar's framework defines reservations as a tool for structural caste representation rather than poverty alleviation. Tamil Nadu protects its unique 69% reservation matrix under the 9th Schedule via the 76th Amendment, viewing purely economic reservations outside of caste criteria as contrary to traditional social justice principles.

📜 Part II: History & Culture of Tamil Nadu (Unit 8)

Q11
Medium Historical Reform movements TNPSC Unit 8 Core
Where was the "Sakya Buddhist Society" (Sakya Buddha Sangam) founded by Pandit Iyotheethassar?
சாக்கிய பௌத்த சங்கம் என்னும் அமைப்பு எங்கு நிறுவப்பட்டது?
✅ Correct Answer: Option A · Madras (Chennai)

Official Explanation: Pandit Iyotheethassar, a pioneer of anti-caste activism and the Dravidian movement, inspired key thinkers like Thanthai Periyar. Seeking an egalitarian alternative to social hierarchies, he traveled to Sri Lanka alongside Colonel H.S. Olcott of the Theosophical Society to embrace Buddhism. Upon his return, he founded the Sakya Buddhist Society in Madras in 1898 to reconstruct a casteless identity.

🏆 PYQ Extension Point

Pandit Iyotheethassar also launched the iconic weekly journal "Oru Paisa Tamilan" (ஒரு பைசா தமிழன்) in 1907, later dropping "Oru Paisa" to run it simply as Tamilan until his death in 1914. He established the Advaidananda Sabha in 1876.

Q12
Medium Freedom Movement Factual TNPSC History
On which exact date did the Vedaranyam Salt March, led by C. Rajagopalachari, successfully reach its destination?
வேதாரண்யம் உப்பு சத்தியாகிரக நடைபயணம் எப்போது வேதாரண்யத்தை சென்றடைந்தது?
✅ Correct Answer: April 28, 1930

Official Explanation: In coordination with Mahatma Gandhi's Dandi March, C. Rajagopalachari led the Salt Satyagraha in Tamil Nadu. The march commenced from Trichy on April 13, 1930, and arrived at Vedaranyam in Tanjore district on April 28, 1930, taking exactly 15 days.

🪤 Historical Details Trap Box

Historical Context: The British collector of Tanjore during the march was J.A. Thorne, who issued strict warnings against providing food or shelter to the satyagrahis. Despite these threats, local residents welcomed the marchers warmly. The procession started with exactly 100 hand-picked volunteers.

Q13
Medium INC Session Matrix Modern India / TN History
Which Indian National Congress session held in 1927 adopted a resolution for "Purna Swaraj" (Complete Independence) for the first time?
1927-ல் நடைபெற்ற எந்த காங்கிரஸ் மாநாட்டில் முதன்முறையாக பூரண சுயராஜ்ஜியம் (முழு சுதந்திரம்) குறிக்கோளாக அறிவிக்கப்பட்டது?
✅ Correct Answer: Option C · Madras Session, 1927

Official Explanation: The 1927 INC Session met in Madras under the presidency of Dr. M.A. Ansari. This session formally adopted a resolution demanding complete national independence, setting the stage for the definitive Lahore Session in 1929.

Q14
Easy Literature Core TNPSC General Tamil / Unit 8
Who is the author of the critically acclaimed Tamil short novel titled "Yaanai Doctor" (The Elephant Doctor)?
யானை டாக்டர் என்னும் குறும்புதினத்தின் ஆசிரியர் யார்?
✅ Correct Answer: Option A · Jeyamohan

Official Explanation: The short story "Yaanai Doctor" by writer Jeyamohan centers on Dr. V. Krishnamurthy, dedicated to the conservation and medical treatment of wild elephants in the Western Ghats (Anamalai/Topslip region). It highlights environmental ethics and conservation.

🪤 Character vs Author Distinctions

Trap Variant: Examiners often list Dr. Krishnamurthy as an option. Remember, Dr. Krishnamurthy is the real-life inspiration and main character of the book, but the author who penned the novel is Jeyamohan. Jeyamohan also wrote the historic socio-political novel Vellai Yaanai (White Elephant), which touches on historical labor issues in Madras.

📋 HIGH-YIELD REVISION SUMMARY (10 BULLET POINTS)

  • The 7th Amendment (1956) executed the States Reorganisation Act, giving rise to 14 states and 6 UTs based on language.
  • The 96th Amendment (2011) officially substituted the designation "Oriya" with "Odia".
  • There is no National Language in India; Schedule 8 preserves 22 recognized Official Languages.
  • The timeline sequence of language additions follows a helpful mathematical pattern: 21st CAA + 71st CAA = 92nd CAA.
  • The 86th Amendment (2002) inserted Article 21A, making education for ages 6–14 a Fundamental Right.
  • The 44th Amendment (1978) moved the Right to Property to Article 300A as a legal right, correcting errors associated with the 42nd Amendment.
  • The 93rd Amendment (2005) introduced special reservation clauses for backward classes within educational institutions.
  • The 61st Amendment (1988) lowered the minimum voting age from 21 down to 18 years under Rajiv Gandhi's tenure.
  • Pandit Iyotheethassar established the Sakya Buddhist Society in 1898 in Madras to advance social equality.
  • The Vedaranyam Salt March commenced from Trichy on April 13, 1930, and successfully reached Vedaranyam on April 28, 1930.
