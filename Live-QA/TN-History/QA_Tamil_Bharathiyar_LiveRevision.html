Section 1 · Biography & Personal Facts
Q1
EASY Factual 🏆 PYQ
Who said "நாடும் மொழியும் நமதிரு கண்கள்" — "Both the Nation and the Language are our two eyes"?
Tamil: நாடும் மொழியும் நமதிரு கண்கள் என்று கூறியவர் யார்?
✅Mahakavi Subramaniya Bharathiyar (மகாகவி சுப்பிரமணிய பாரதியார்)
This famous quote is one of Bharathiyar's most celebrated patriotic lines, equating the nation and its language as equal and essential — like two eyes of the body.
💎
LIVE GEM
The teacher connected Q1 and Q7, which both involve this same quote — Q1 asks for the matching question form, Q7 gives you the statement and asks who said it. Recognizing this pattern saves time in exams.
🏆
PYQ ALERT
This exact quote has appeared in TNPSC General Tamil sections multiple times. Know it in both formats: "Who said this?" and "Choose the matching question form."
Q2
EASY Factual
Who sang "நெஞ்சை அல்லும் சிலப்பதிகாரம்" — "The heart-piercing Silappathikaram"?
Tamil: நெஞ்சை அல்லும் சிலப்பதிகாரம் என்று பாடியவர் யார்?
✅Bharathiyar (பாரதியார்)
Bharathiyar praised the classical epic Silappathikaram with this phrase, highlighting how emotionally moving it is. This is a previous year question (TNPSC).
💡
TIP
"நெஞ்சை அல்லும்" (heart-piercing / heart-wrenching) → Bharathiyar. Also: "நெஞ்சு வேண்டும்" → Vairamuthu. Know the difference!

EASY Factual 🏆 PYQ

✅December 11, 1882 (டிசம்பர் 11, 1882)
💡
SHORTCUT TIP — LIVE GEM 💎
Remember the number 11: Born on December 11 → Also received the title "Bharathi" at age 11 → Also died on September 11. Three 11s to remember him!
Q4
EASY Factual
When did Bharathiyar pass away, and what was the cause of his death?
Tamil: பாரதியார் எப்போது இறந்தார்? காரணம் என்ன?
✅September 11, 1921 — due to dysentery (வயிற்று போக்கு / diarrhea)
A temple elephant struck him with its trunk in the Parthasarathy temple, Triplicane. After this injury, weakened health led to dysentery, and he passed away on September 11, 1921.
🪤
COMMON TRAP
Most students say "He died because an elephant trampled him" — THIS IS WRONG. The elephant only pushed/struck him with its trunk. He actually died of dysentery (diarrhea) sometime later. Only 14 people attended his funeral.
💎
LIVE GEM
Teacher's analogy: "Just like Newton's apple — the apple didn't kill Newton, it inspired him. Similarly, the elephant didn't kill Bharathiyar; dysentery did. School books simplify for children."

Q7
EASY Factual
What are the names of Bharathiyar's parents?
Tamil: பாரதியாரின் பெற்றோர் பெயர்கள் என்ன?
✅Father: Chinnaswami Iyer (சின்னசுவாமி ஐயர்) · Mother: Lakshmi Ammal (லட்சுமி அம்மாள்)
His mother Lakshmiammal passed away when Bharathiyar was only 5 years old. He wrote a poem about this grief when he was 7.
💎
LIVE GEM — Beautiful Poem
The poem Bharathiyar wrote at age 7 about his mother who died when he was 5: "என்னை ஈன்று எனக்கு ஐந்து பிராயத்தில் ஏங்கவிட்டு விண்ணெய்திய தாய்" — "O Mother who bore me and went to heaven when I was just five years old, leaving me longing." This may appear as a fill-in-the-blank.
Q8
EASY Factual
At what age did Bharathiyar receive the title "Bharathi"? When exactly was it conferred?
Tamil: பாரதியாருக்கு "பாரதி" பட்டம் எத்தனை வயதில் வழங்கப்பட்டது?
✅Age 11, in the year 1893, by the King of Ettayapuram (எட்டையாபுர மன்னன்)
The title was given to both Subramaniya Bharathi and Somasundara Bharathi at the same event. The Ettayapuram king recognized his poetic genius at this young age.
💎
LIVE GEM
The teacher noted this was a Group 2 question that appeared previously. Two key facts in one: Age = 11, Year = 1893. Both Subramaniya Bharathi and Somasundara Bharathi received the title at the same event.

✅Thangammal (தங்கம்மாள்) and Shakuntala (சகுந்தலா)
His wife Chellamma (செல்லம்மா) — also called Valli (வள்ளி), his pet name for her — appears in his songs as "Kannamma" (கண்ணம்மா).
Q10
MEDIUM Factual
At what age did Bharathiyar begin writing Tamil poetry? What poem did he write at age 7?
Tamil: பாரதியார் எத்தனை வயதில் கவிதை எழுத ஆரம்பித்தார்?
✅Age 7 — wrote a poem mourning his mother who died when he was 5
💎
LIVE GEM
Key word is "பிராயம்" (பிராயத்தில் = at the age of). The poem says "ஐந்து பிராயத்தில்" = at age five. So: Mother died when he was 5; he wrote the poem at age 7. Two different ages — don't confuse them.
Q11
MEDIUM Factual
In which year did Bharathiyar go to Pondicherry, and why did he go?
Tamil: பாரதியார் பாண்டிச்சேரிக்கு எப்போது சென்றார்? ஏன்?
✅1908 — to escape arrest by the British due to his revolutionary journalism in "India" newspaper
In 1908, during the Swadeshi movement, V.O. Chidambaram Pillai (Vaauci) and Subramanya Siva were arrested. The British also issued an arrest warrant for Bharathiyar due to his articles in "India" journal. He escaped to Pondicherry (then under French rule).
💎
LIVE GEM
His friend in Pondicherry who welcomed him was Kuvalai Kannan (குவளைக் கண்ணன்) — the French government initially refused to give him a house. Kuvalai Kannan arranged a house near a cow shed for Bharathiyar to stay. This is out-of-textbook data!

MEDIUM Factual 🏆 PYQ

Tamil: பாரதியார் மகாத்மா காந்தியை எப்போது சந்தித்தார்?
✅1919 — at Rajaji's house in Chennai
This is a 7th standard textbook fact. Bharathiyar met Gandhi at the residence of C. Rajagopalachari (Rajaji) in Chennai in 1919.
Q13
HARD Factual
Who attended Bharathiyar's final rites? How many people were present?
Tamil: பாரதியாரின் இறுதி சடங்கில் கலந்துகொண்டவர்கள் யாவர்?
✅Only 14 people — including Paralasu Nellaiappar (பரலேசு நெல்லையப்பர்) and Kamal Kannan (கமலக் கண்ணன்)
Kamal Kannan was the one who helped transport the ailing Bharathiyar in a bullock cart to the hospital. Kuvalai Kannan was the Pondicherry helper; Kamal Kannan was the Chennai helper.
💎

Two different helpers:
• Kuvalai Kannan (குவளைக் கண்ணன்) → helped in Pondicherry
• Kamal Kannan (கமலக் கண்ணன்) → helped in Chennai; transported him in a bullock cart during final illness
Q14
HARD Factual
In which year were Bharathiyar's works nationalized by the Tamil Nadu government? Who was the Chief Minister then, and from whom were the works purchased?
Tamil: பாரதியாரின் நூல்கள் எந்த ஆண்டு தமிழ்நாடு அரசால் நாட்டுடைமையாக்கப்பட்டன?
✅1949 — by Chief Minister Omandur Ramaswami Reddiar (ஓமந்தூர் ராமசாமி ரெட்டியார்), purchased from A.V. Meiyappa Chettiar (ஏவிஎம் மெய்யப்ப செட்டியார்)
💎
LIVE GEM
AVM = A.V. Meiyappa Chettiar (the famous film studio owner). He had purchased Bharathiyar's manuscripts/rights. When nationalized in 1949, anyone could freely use Bharathiyar's works — that's why they are copyright-free today.

EASY Factual 🏆 PYQ
What are the three great works (மூப்பெரும் இலக்கியங்கள்) Bharathiyar composed while in Pondicherry?
Tamil: பாரதியார் புதுவையில் இயற்றிய மூப்பெரும் இலக்கியங்கள் யாவை?
✅1. Kuyil Paattu (குயில் பாட்டு) · 2. Kannan Paattu (கண்ணன் பாட்டு) · 3. Paanchaali Sabatham (பாஞ்சாலி சபதம்)
🏆

Q16
MEDIUM 🪤 TRAP
The lines "நாட்டினிலும் காட்டினிலும் நாளெல்லாம் நன்றொளிக்கும் பாட்டினிலும் நெஞ்சை பறிகொடுத்தேன் பாவியேன்" appear in which work?
✅Kuyil Paattu (குயில் பாட்டு)
🪤
MAJOR TRAP — LIVE DISCUSSION
Almost ALL students answered "Paappa Paattu" — WRONG! The correct answer is Kuyil Paattu. Teacher stressed this repeatedly.
• Paappa Paattu = About children/women
• Kuyil Paattu = About beauty of music/nature (the kuyil/cuckoo is the symbol)
💎
LIVE GEM
Kuyil Paattu is about the beauty of music. Kannan Paattu is a Pillai Tamil (Pillaittamizh — a genre where a divine being is treated as a child) — Bharathiyar treated Lord Krishna as his child in this work.
Q17
EASY Factual
What is Tamil's first prose work (தமிழின் முதல் உரைநடை இலக்கியம்) — and who wrote it?
✅Gnanaratham (ஞானரதம்) — by Bharathiyar
Gnanaratham is classified as Tamil's first prose work (உரைநடை இலக்கியம்). It falls under the category of prose literature (உரைநடை — prose / non-verse writing).
Q18
EASY Factual
What was the first journal in which Bharathiyar published a poem? (First poem journal) Year?
Tamil: பாரதியாரின் முதல் பாடல் வெளிவந்த இதழ் எது?
✅Viveka Banu (விவேக பானு) — 1904, published in Madurai
V.O. Chidambaram Pillai (Vaauci) was associated with this journal. After working at Viveka Banu, Bharathiyar got a major offer from Sooddesa Mittiran and moved there.
Q19
MEDIUM Factual 🏆 PYQ
In which year did Bharathiyar join Sooddesa Mittiran (சுதேசமித்திரன்) as sub-editor?
Tamil: பாரதியார் சுதேசமித்திரன் இதழில் துணையாசிரியராக சேர்ந்த ஆண்டு?
✅1904
Sooddesa Mittiran was founded in 1891 and became a daily newspaper in 1899. G.S. Iyer (ஜிஎஸ் ஐயர்) was the chief editor. Bharathiyar joined as sub-editor (துணையாசிரியர்) in 1904.
💎
LIVE GEM — Key Story
G.S. Iyer restricted Bharathiyar from writing against the British (fearing newspaper ban). Because Bharathiyar had strong views, he left and co-founded "India" journal — where he had total freedom. This explains why "India" was eventually banned and he fled to Pondicherry in 1908.
Q20
MEDIUM 🪤 TRAP
In which journal did Bharathiyar translate Bankim Chandra Chatterjee's "Vande Mataram" into Tamil?
Tamil: வந்தே மாதரம் பாடலை தமிழில் மொழிபெயர்த்து பாரதியார் எந்த பத்திரிகையில் வெளியிட்டார்?
✅Vijaya (விஜயா) journal
💎
LIVE GEM — Smart Shortcut
Vande Mataram praises India as a mother (female form). Vijaya = the journal for women readers. So: Vande Mataram (female/mother theme) → Vijaya (women's journal). India = the general national journal. This shortcut prevents confusion between the two journals.
🪤
TRAP
Many will answer "India" journal — WRONG. It was "Vijaya." Know: India journal = general patriotic writing; Vijaya = women-focused journal where Vande Mataram Tamil translation appeared.

Q23

Q24

Q25
MEDIUM Factual
What were Bharathiyar's English-language journals/magazines?

✅1. Balabaratham (பாலபாரதம்) — monthly magazine · 2. Karmayogi (கர்மயோகி) — also an English journal
💎
LIVE GEM
Balabaratham = monthly (மாத இதழ்). Karmayogi = also English. These are the two English periodicals by Bharathiyar. Tamil journals: India, Vijaya, Sooddesa Mittiran (as sub-editor), Sooriyodayam.
Q26
EASY Factual
What is Bharathiyar's dream magazine that he could never launch due to lack of funds?
✅Sithiravali (சித்திராவலி) — a pictorial / illustrated magazine with political cartoons
Bharathiyar wanted to launch India's first Tamil illustrated magazine with political cartoons. He even planned the first Tamil newspaper to publish political caricatures in red ink. Due to financial difficulties, Sithiravali could never be launched. The "India" journal did publish some caricatures in red color.

CRITICAL

Section 3 · Tributes, Quotes & Descriptions by Others
Q29
HARD Factual 🏆 PYQ
"Tilak sowed the seed — it sprouted as Bharathiyar; Gandhi sowed the seed — it sprouted as Rajaji." Who said this?
Tamil: திலகர் விதைத்த விதை பாரதியாக முழைத்தது; காந்தி விதைத்த விதை ராஜாஜியாக முழைத்தது — என்று கூறியவர் யார்?
✅Salem Vijayaraghavachariar / Salem Vijayaraghavachariyar (சேலம் விஜயராகவாச்சாரியார்)
💎
LIVE GEM — Important Distinction
Many students said "Rajaji" — WRONG! It was Salem Vijayaraghavachariyar (Rajaji's political guru). He made this comparative statement about both. Rajaji is being compared in the statement, so Rajaji could NOT have said it about himself.
🏆
PYQ ALERT
This appeared in TNPSC Group 2 papers. The TNPSC school textbook version says: "Tilak sowed seed → Bharathi; Gandhi sowed seed → Namakkal Kavignar" — attributed differently. The out-of-textbook version (Rajaji connection) = Salem Vijayaraghavachariyar.

Q31

📝 Session Summary — 10 Key Takeaways

  • Bharathiyar: Born Dec 11, 1882 in Ettayapuram (then Tirunelveli dist.); died Sep 11, 1921 — NOT from elephant attack, but from dysentery.
  • Three landmark works from Pondicherry: Kuyil Paattu + Kannan Paattu + Paanchaali Sabatham. All composed 1908–1919.
  • Key distinction: "India" journal = Bharathiyar (general patriotic); "Vijaya" journal = women-focused; Vande Mataram Tamil translation → appeared in Vijaya.
  • Mahakavi title: officially given by Va. Ramaswami Iyengar; frequently popularized by Krishnaswami Iyer. Two different answers for two different questions.
  • Paralasu Nellaiappar = called Bharathiyar "younger brother"; his actual brother Sanmugasundaram Pillai = only person in India called "Vande Mataram Pillai."
  • "Tilak seed → Bharathi; Gandhi seed → Rajaji" quote = Salem Vijayaraghavachariyar (NOT Rajaji himself).
  • Bharathiyar's works nationalized in 1949 by CM Omandur Ramaswami Reddiar — bought from AVM Meiyappa Chettiar.
  • TRAP: "துறைதோறும் தமிழுக்கு" = Bharathidasan (NOT Bharathiyar). "Kamarajar midday meal" year = 1955 (NOT 1956).
  • Bharathidasan praised Gnanaratham: "No one on this four-directional earth can write such a work." Bharathidasan = Russian-influenced; Bharathiyar = Walt Whitman (USA) influenced in prose poetry.
  • Complete special names list including unusual ones: குப்பம்மாள் செல்வன் (aunt's son), சாவித்திரி (pen name for women's topics), முண்டாசு கவி (turban poet), பைந்தமிழ் தேர்பாகன் — all examinable.
