PART I The Union and its Territory Art. 1–4
🔴 CRITICALAll Groups🏆 PYQ
Art. 1–4Name, Territory, Admission & Formation of States
✅ KEY CONTENT
  • Art.1 — India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States. Territory = States + UTs (1st Sched) + acquired territories.
  • Art.2 — Parliament may admit new states (not yet in India) by law.
  • Art.3 — Parliament can form new states, alter boundaries/names of existing states. Needs President's recommendation + state legislature consultation (NOT consent).
  • Art.4 — Laws under Art.2 & 3 are not constitutional amendments (pass by simple majority).
🔴 CRITICALIndia = Union of States NOT Federation. Art.2 = new states from outside. Art.3 = reorganise existing states. State consent NOT required under Art.3.
🏆 PYQTelangana (2014), Jharkhand/Uttarakhand/Chhattisgarh (2000) = Art.3. "India is described as?" → Union of States.
🪤 TRAP NOTEArt.4 says laws under Art.2 & 3 are NOT deemed to be amendments under Art.368 → they pass by simple majority.

✅ KEY CONTENT

Art. 72Pardoning Powers of the President — 5 Types
✅ 5 PARDONING POWERS
  • Pardon — Complete acquittal; removes both punishment and conviction
  • Reprieve — Temporary delay in execution of sentence (e.g. death row, appeal pending)
  • Respite — Lesser sentence due to special reason (pregnancy, physical disability)
  • Remit — Reduce quantum of sentence without changing its character
  • Commute — Change punishment to a less severe form (death → life imprisonment)
🔴 CRITICALPresident (Art.72) CAN pardon death sentence. Governor (Art.161) CANNOT pardon death sentence — biggest distinction. President acts on advice of Cabinet; Governor acts on advice of State Cabinet.

PART IXB 🆕 NEW Co-operative Societies Art. 243ZH–243ZT
🆕 NEWGr.2
Art. 243ZH–243ZTCo-operative Societies — Added by 97th Amendment 2011
✅ KEY CONTENT

Part IXB (12 articles: Art.243ZH to 243ZT) added by 97th Constitutional Amendment Act 2011. Constitutionalised Panchayati Raj. Added Part IX (Art.243–243O), 11th Schedule (29 subjects). 3-tier system, 5-year elections, State Election Commission, 1/3 reservation for women. Effective April 24, 1993.
74th — 1992
Municipalities — Part IXA, 12th Schedule
Constitutionalised urban local bodies. Added Part IXA (Art.243P–243ZG), 12th Schedule (18 subjects). Ward Committees, Municipal Councils, Corporations. Effective June 1, 1993.
🆕 76th — 1994
Tamil Nadu OBC Reservation (9th Schedule)
Added Tamil Nadu Backward Classes, SC & ST (Reservation of Seats in Educational Institutions & in Public Services) Act 1993 to 9th Schedule (69% reservation in TN). Crucial for Tamil Nadu TNPSC aspirants.
🆕 77th — 1995
Reservation in Promotion for SC/ST
Added Art.16(4A) — State can provide reservation in promotion for SC/ST if they are not adequately represented. Response to Indra Sawhney case (Mandal case) 1992.
🆕 79th — 1999
SC/ST Reservation Extended to 2010
Extended SC/ST reservation in Parliament and State Legislatures under Art.334 to 2010.
86th — 2002
Right to Education — Art.21A
Added Art.21A — free and compulsory education 6–14 years. Added 11th Fundamental Duty (Art.51A(k)). Amended Art.45 (below 6 yrs). RTE Act 2009 enacted.
🆕 89th — 2003
Separate SC and ST Commissions
Bifurcated old National Commission for SC/ST (Art.338) into: Art.338 = National Commission for Scheduled Castes + Art.338A = National Commission for Scheduled Tribes.
🆕 91st — 2003
Council of Ministers Size Capped
Council of Ministers (Centre + States) capped at 15% of Lok Sabha/Assembly strength. Anti-defection merger rule changed from 1/3 to 2/3. Added Art.75(1A) and Art.164(1A).
🆕 92nd — 2003
4 Languages Added to 8th Schedule
Added Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, Santhali to 8th Schedule (19th–22nd languages). Total = 22 languages. Last addition to 8th Schedule so far.
🆕 93rd — 2005
Reservation in Private Educational Institutions
Added Art.15(5) — State can make reservation in private unaided educational institutions (except minority institutions) for OBC, SC, ST. Response to Inamdar case 2005. Central Educational Institutions (Reservation) Act 2006.
97th — 2011
Co-operative Societies — Part IXB
Added Part IXB (Art.243ZH–243ZT) and Art.43B to DPSP. For democratic functioning of co-operatives. Partially struck down by SC 2021 (Rajendra Shah case).
🆕 98th — 2012
Art.371J — Hyderabad-Karnataka Region
Added Art.371J — special provisions for Hyderabad-Karnataka region (now Kalyana-Karnataka region). Established a separate development board; reservation in educational institutions and public employment for locals.
🆕 100th — 2015
Land Boundary Agreement with Bangladesh
Exchange of territories between India and Bangladesh (enclaves). Added territories to 1st Schedule. First time India gave away its territory by constitutional amendment (100th enclaves exchanged).
101st — 2016
GST — Goods and Services Tax
Introduced Art.246A — concurrent legislative power on supply of goods and services. Created GST Council (Art.279A). Replaced multiple indirect taxes. "One Nation One Tax" reform.
🆕 102nd — 2018
National Commission for Backward Classes (Art.338B)
Added Art.338B — National Commission for Backward Classes (OBCs) gets constitutional status. Added Art.342A — President specifies OBC list. Amended Art.366(26C) defining SEBCs.
103rd — 2019
10% EWS Reservation (Art.15(6), Art.16(6))
10% reservation for Economically Weaker Sections (general category) in education and public employment. Added Art.15(6) and Art.16(6). Upheld by SC 5-judge bench in Nov 2022 (3-2 majority).
🆕 104th — 2020
Anglo-Indian Nomination Removed; SC/ST Reservation Extended
Removed reservation of 2 Anglo-Indian nominated seats in LS and State Assemblies (Art.331 and 333 amended). Extended SC/ST political reservation in Parliament and State Legislatures till 2030 (Art.334 extended from 2020 to 2030).
🆕 105th — 2021
OBC List with States — Art.342A Amended
Restored States' power to maintain their own OBC lists. The 102nd Amendment had inadvertently given only Centre the power to notify OBCs. 105th Amendment corrected this — both Centre and States can maintain OBC lists.
🆕 106th — 2023
Women's Reservation — 33% in LS and State Assemblies
Added Art.330A and Art.332A — 1/3 seats reserved for women in Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies. Also reserved within SC/ST quotas. Called "Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam." Effective only after next delimitation exercise (after Census).
LANDMARK CASES Famous Constitutional Cases — TNPSC Must Know
1950 — SC Case
A.K. Gopalan v. State of Madras
SC held Art.21 "procedure established by law" meant any law enacted by Parliament, even if unfair. Art.19 and Art.21 were seen as mutually exclusive.
🏆 PYQ: First major FR case. Later overruled by Maneka Gandhi 1978.
1951 — SC Case
Champakam Dorairajan v. State of Madras
First case on fundamental rights vs DPSP. SC held FR prevails over DPSP. Led to 1st Constitutional Amendment 1951 and addition of Art.15(4).
🏆 PYQ: "Which case led to 1st Amendment?" → Champakam Dorairajan.
1958 — SC Case
State of Madras v. V.G. Row
SC laid down "reasonableness" test for restrictions on Art.19 freedoms. Restrictions must be proportionate and reasonable, not arbitrary.
🏆 Important for Art.19 restriction jurisprudence.
1967 — SC Case
Golaknath v. State of Punjab
SC held Parliament CANNOT amend Fundamental Rights. FRs are sacrosanct. Parliament has no power under Art.368 to abridge FRs.
🏆 PYQ: Led to 24th Amendment 1971. Overruled by Kesavananda 1973.
1970 — SC Case
R.C. Cooper v. Union of India (Bank Nationalisation Case)
SC struck down Bank Nationalisation Act 1969. Held that deprivation of property must meet Art.14 and Art.19 standards. Led to 25th Amendment.
🏆 PYQ: Indira Gandhi's bank nationalisation challenged. Landmark on property rights.
1973 — SC Case (Most Important)
Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala
Largest ever SC bench (13 judges), decided 7:6. Parliament CAN amend the Constitution including FRs but CANNOT destroy the BASIC STRUCTURE. Basic Structure = supremacy of Constitution, democratic republic, secularism, separation of powers, federalism, unity & integrity, judicial review, FR, rule of law, parliamentary system.
🏆 PYQ: Most important constitutional law case ever. "Basic Structure doctrine = ?" → Kesavananda Bharati 1973. Date = April 24, 1973.
1975 — SC Case
Indira Gandhi v. Raj Narain
SC struck down 39th Amendment that shielded PM's election from judicial review. Held "free and fair elections" and "judicial review" are part of Basic Structure. Art.329A struck down. Led to declaration of Emergency 1975.
🏆 PYQ: "Which case struck down 39th Amendment?" → Indira Gandhi v. Raj Narain 1975.
1978 — SC Case
Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India
SC overruled AK Gopalan. Art.21 "procedure established by law" must be fair, just and reasonable. Arts.14, 19, and 21 are interconnected (golden triangle). Procedure must pass test of reasonableness under Art.14 and test of freedoms under Art.19. Expanded right to life enormously.
🏆 PYQ: "Golden Triangle of FRs?" → Arts.14, 19, 21 (Maneka Gandhi 1978). "Procedure in Art.21 must be?" → Fair, just, reasonable.
1980 — SC Case
Minerva Mills v. Union of India
SC struck down 42nd Amendment provisions that gave DPSPs absolute precedence over FRs (Art.31C as amended). Held that balance between FRs and DPSPs is part of Basic Structure. Also struck down provisions curtailing judicial review power.
🏆 PYQ: "Minerva Mills struck down which amendment?" → Parts of 42nd Amendment.
1980 — SC Case
Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab
SC upheld constitutional validity of death penalty. Established "rarest of rare cases" doctrine for awarding death penalty. Art.21 does not prevent death penalty if procedure is fair and just.
🏆 PYQ: "Rarest of rare doctrine?" → Bachan Singh 1980. Death penalty constitutional in India.
1981 — SC Case
Waman Rao v. Union of India
SC held that 9th Schedule laws added before April 24, 1973 (Kesavananda date) cannot be challenged. But laws added after that date can be challenged if they violate Basic Structure. Confirmed Kesavananda principle for 9th Schedule.
🏆 PYQ: "9th Schedule laws can be challenged?" → Only those added after April 24, 1973.
1992 — SC Case
Indra Sawhney v. Union of India (Mandal Case)
SC upheld 27% reservation for OBCs (Mandal Commission) but struck down reservation in promotion for OBCs. Held 50% is the outer limit for reservations (SC/ST + OBC combined). "Creamy layer" concept for OBCs.
🏆 PYQ: "50% ceiling for reservation?" → Indra Sawhney 1992. "Creamy layer concept?" → Mandal case. Led to 77th Amendment (Art.16(4A) — reservation in promotion for SC/ST).
1993 — SC Case
Kihoto Hollohan v. Zachillhu (Speaker's Power Case)
SC upheld 10th Schedule (Anti-Defection Law) as constitutional. But held Speaker's decision is subject to judicial review (not immune). Speaker's decision reviewable by HC under Art.226 and SC under Art.136.
🏆 PYQ: "10th Schedule upheld by?" → Kihoto Hollohan 1993. "Speaker's defection decision reviewable?" → Yes (by HC/SC).
1994 — SC Case
S.R. Bommai v. Union of India
SC restricted misuse of Art.356 (President's Rule). Held: President's Rule subject to judicial review. Floor test is the only way to test majority. Secularism is a Basic Structure element — states doing anti-secular activities can be put under President's Rule.
🏆 PYQ: Most important case on Art.356. "SR Bommai relates to?" → President's Rule (Art.356). "Secularism is Basic Structure?" → Yes, SR Bommai 1994.
1997 — SC Case
L. Chandra Kumar v. Union of India
SC held that Administrative Tribunals (Art.323A/323B) CANNOT oust the jurisdiction of HC under Art.226 and Art.227. HC superintendence over tribunals is part of Basic Structure. All tribunal orders are subject to HC review.
🏆 PYQ: "Tribunals and HC jurisdiction?" → L. Chandra Kumar 1997. Tribunals cannot replace HCs.
2002 — SC Case
TMA Pai Foundation v. State of Karnataka
SC (11-judge bench) ruled on minority educational institutions (Art.30). Held that aided minority institutions can make reservations in management quota. Linguistic minorities also have Art.30 rights. State can regulate but not dominate minority institutions.
🏆 PYQ: "TMA Pai Foundation relates to?" → Minority educational institutions (Art.30).
2005 — SC Case
P.A. Inamdar v. State of Maharashtra
SC held State cannot impose reservations on private unaided institutions (minority or non-minority). Led to 93rd Constitutional Amendment 2005 adding Art.15(5) to allow reservation in private unaided non-minority educational institutions.
🏆 PYQ: Led directly to 93rd Amendment 2005. "Art.15(5) added in response to?" → Inamdar case.
2007 — SC Case
I.R. Coelho v. State of Tamil Nadu (9th Schedule Case)
SC (9-judge bench) held that laws added to 9th Schedule after April 24, 1973 CAN be challenged if they violate Basic Structure or fundamental rights that form part of the identity of the Constitution.
🏆 PYQ: "IR Coelho relates to?" → 9th Schedule judicial review. After 1973 = can be challenged. Before 1973 = cannot be challenged.
2014 — SC Case
NALSA v. Union of India (Transgender Rights)
SC recognised transgender persons as third gender. Held Art.21 (right to life), Art.19(1)(a) (freedom of expression of gender identity), and Art.14 (equality) protect transgender rights. Directed government to take welfare measures.
🏆 PYQ: "NALSA relates to?" → Transgender rights. "Third gender recognised by?" → NALSA 2014.
2017 — SC Case (Most Important Recent)
Justice K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India (Privacy Case)
SC (9-judge bench) unanimously held that Privacy is a Fundamental Right under Art.21. Overruled M.P. Sharma (1954) and Kharak Singh (1962) which had held no right to privacy exists. Privacy includes bodily integrity, informational privacy, identity, and dignity.
🏆 PYQ: "Right to Privacy declared FR in?" → Puttaswamy 2017. "Aadhaar judgment year?" → 2018 (upheld with conditions). Privacy = Art.21.
2018 — SC Case
NCT of Delhi v. Union of India (Delhi Governance)
SC (5-judge bench) held that the elected government of Delhi (not the Lieutenant Governor) is the real executive authority. LG bound by advice of CoM on matters not transferred to Centre. LG cannot stall decisions made by elected govt.
🏆 PYQ: "Delhi governance case year?" → 2018. "LG bound by?" → Advice of elected Delhi CoM (except on law, order, land).
2019 — SC Case
Indian Young Lawyers Association v. State of Kerala (Sabarimala Case)
SC held (4-1) that excluding women of menstruating age (10–50 years) from Sabarimala temple violates Art.17 (untouchability), Art.14 and Art.21. Constitutional morality must prevail over popular morality. Later referred to 9-judge bench for broader questions on essential religious practices.
🏆 PYQ: "Sabarimala case year?" → 2019. "Sabarimala relates to?" → Art.25-26 (freedom of religion) vs Art.14,17,21.
2022 — SC Case
Janhit Abhiyan v. Union of India (EWS Reservation Case)
SC (5-judge bench, 3-2 majority) upheld 103rd Constitutional Amendment (10% EWS reservation). Held it does not violate Basic Structure. 50% ceiling can be breached for EWS. SC/ST/OBC exclusion from EWS valid.
🏆 PYQ: "EWS reservation upheld by?" → Janhit Abhiyan 2022. "103rd Amendment?" → EWS 10% reservation.
2023 — SC Case
Supreme Court Advisory Opinion on Art.370 (Re: J&K Reorganisation)
SC (5-judge bench) upheld abrogation of Art.370 on December 11, 2023. Held J&K has no internal sovereignty. President had power to abrogate Art.370. Directed elections to J&K UT by September 2024. Also directed restoration of statehood to J&K.
🏆 PYQ: "Art.370 abrogation upheld by SC on?" → December 11, 2023. "J&K statehood to be restored?" → Yes (SC direction).
2024 — SC Case 🆕
Sub-Classification of SC/ST Reservations
SC (7-judge bench, 6-1) held that States CAN sub-classify SCs and STs into further categories (e.g., giving preference to most disadvantaged within SC/ST). Overruled E.V. Chinnaiah (2004) case which had held all SCs must be treated as one group.
🆕 LATEST: States can now create "creamy layer" concept within SC/ST or give preference to most backward sub-group. Punjab, Tamil Nadu, and other states with sub-classification laws are now valid.

📝 Master Summary — 15 Must-Know Takeaways

  • India = Union of States (Art.1) — NOT Federation. 28 States + 8 UTs. Art.3 allows state reorganisation without state's consent (only consultation).
  • Single Citizenship — India (Art.11). USA = Double Citizenship. Citizenship Act 1955 + CAA 2019.
  • 6 Fundamental Rights (Art.12–35) — from USA. Art.32 = Heart & Soul (Ambedkar). 5 Writs: H-M-P-C-Q. Art.226 (HC) = wider scope than Art.32.
  • Art.14–18 (Equality): Art.14 = British + American concept. Art.17 = No Untouchability. Art.18 = No Titles. Art.15(6)/16(6) = EWS 10% (103rd Amend 2019).
  • Art.19 = 6 Freedoms (originally 7). Art.21 = most expansive (privacy, livelihood, health). Art.20 & 21 CANNOT be suspended during Emergency (44th Amend 1978).
  • DPSP (Art.36–51) — Non-justiciable. From Irish Constitution. 3 types: Socialistic/Gandhian/Liberal-Intellectual. Art.39(equal pay), Art.44(UCC), Art.40(Panchayats), Art.48A(Environment).
  • 11 Fundamental Duties (Art.51A) — From USSR. 42nd Amend 1976 (10 duties) + 86th Amend 2002 (11th duty: educate child). Non-justiciable. Citizens only.
  • 7th Schedule (3 Lists): Union (97+), State (66), Concurrent (47). Education + Forests moved to Concurrent by 42nd Amend 1976. Residuary = Parliament.
  • 8th Schedule = 22 Languages. Originally 14. Tamil in original 14. Last 4 added: 92nd Amend 2003. India has NO national language (only official language = Hindi in Devanagari).
  • 3 Emergencies: National (Art.352) — declared 3 times (1962, 1971, 1975); President's Rule (Art.356) — SR Bommai 1994 limits misuse; Financial (Art.360) — NEVER declared.
  • Amendment (Art.368) — 3 methods. Basic Structure doctrine (Kesavananda Bharati 1973) — cannot be destroyed. Maneka Gandhi 1978 = Golden Triangle (Art.14+19+21).
  • Key retirement ages: SC Judge = 65 yrs | HC Judge = 62 yrs. President/Governor term = 5 years. Voting age = 18 (61st Amend 1988). CAG = 6 yrs or 65 yrs.
  • Schedules: 11th (29 subjects, 73rd Amend) | 12th (18 subjects, 74th Amend) | 10th (Anti-Defection, 52nd Amend) | 9th (immune laws, 1st Amend) | 7th (3 Lists).
  • 🆕 New Facts: Art.370 abrogated Aug 5, 2019 (SC upheld Dec 11, 2023). 106th Amend 2023 = Women 33% reservation. 102nd Amend 2018 = NCBC constitutional. Sub-classification SC/ST upheld by SC 2024.
