SECTION 03
The Constitution Making Process — Timeline & Key Demands

India needed a constitution book (rule book) after independence. The struggle to form a Constituent Assembly — the body that would write this book — went through multiple phases:

TIMELINE 01
Demands for a Constituent Assembly — Step by Step
1922
Mahatma Gandhi expressed the idea that Indians should form a Constituent Assembly for their own constitution (as a thought, not a formal demand).
1928
Motilal Nehru published the Nehru Report — proved Indians are capable of drafting a constitution, countering British claims.
1934
🏆 M.N. Roy (Communist leader) — FIRST OFFICIAL DEMAND to the British for a Constituent Assembly. Rejected by British.
1935
Indian National Congress (INC) officially demanded a Constituent Assembly. Rejected.
1937
Faizpur session of INC — Nehru presided; Congress met in a village for first time. (Note: Nehru's 1938 demand came at this session's follow-up.)
1938
Jawaharlal Nehru demanded CA elected by adult franchise. Rejected.
1939
Gandhi wrote article "One Way" in his journal Harijan advocating for Constituent Assembly. World War II began; British gave India no choice.
PYQ ALERT 🏆 "Who was the first to demand a Constituent Assembly for India?" → Answer: M.N. Roy (1934). This has been asked in Group 1, 2, and 4.
TNPSC TRAP ⚠ Many students write Gandhi (1922) or Nehru (1938) as the first to demand a Constituent Assembly. Wrong! Gandhi only expressed an idea/concept. Nehru demanded it but in 1938. M.N. Roy in 1934 made the FIRST OFFICIAL DEMAND to the British. The key word is "official demand" or "first to demand."
TIMELINE 02
British Responses — All Rejected Until 1946
1940
August Offer (8 Aug 1940) by Viceroy Linlithgow — Promised Dominion Status (not full independence). Rejected. Gandhi called it "Post-dated cheque."
1942
Cripps Mission (22 Mar 1942) — 3 members: Cripps, Lawrence, Alexander. Offered post-war CA + Dominion status. Rejected. Gandhi: "Post-dated cheque of a failing/crashing bank." Nehru: "Reading this caused severe mental distress."
1946
Cabinet Mission — Same 3: Lawrence + Alexander + Cripps. Finally recommended Constituent Assembly. British accepted. CA election held.
TNPSC TRAP ⚠ Cripps appears in BOTH the 1942 Cripps Mission AND the 1946 Cabinet Mission. Don't confuse them. The 1942 Cripps Mission was rejected; the 1946 Cabinet Mission was accepted (which led to the CA).
PYQ ALERT 🏆 "Gandhi described which offer as a post-dated cheque?" → Cripps Mission (1942). Also asked: "post-dated cheque of a failing bank" — same answer.
MEMORY TRICK 🧠 Order of demands: G-N-R-C-N-G-C-C = Gandhi(1922) → Nehru Report(1928) → Roy(1934) → Congress(1935) → Nehru(1938) → Gandhi Harijan(1939) → Cripps(1942) → Cabinet mission(1946).
Trick phrase: "Good News Reaches Congress, Nehru Got Cabs Cheap"

SECTION 05
Preamble (முகவுரை) — The Identity Card of the Constitution
WHAT IS THE PREAMBLE?
The First Page — Introduction to the Constitution
The Preamble is the introductory/opening statement of the Constitution. Like the first page of a book that summarises its content, the Preamble tells the world what kind of nation India is and what it values. It begins with "WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA..." (இந்திய மக்களாகிய நாம்...).

Sourced from: USA's Constitution (concept borrowed by Nehru).
CRITICAL FACT ❗ The Preamble order after 42nd Amendment: SOVEREIGN → SOCIALIST → SECULAR → DEMOCRATIC → REPUBLIC
Original (before 1976): Sovereign → Democratic → Republic (no Socialist, Secular, Integrity in this position)
THE PREAMBLE TEXT (Key Elements)
WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA...
...having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC and to secure to all its citizens:

JUSTICE — Social, Economic, Political
LIBERTY — of thought, expression, belief, faith, worship
EQUALITY — of status and opportunity
FRATERNITY — assuring dignity of the individual and unity & integrity of the Nation.

IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949...
MEMORY TRICK 🧠 Preamble key words: S-S-S-D-R & J-L-E-F
= Sovereign·Socialist·Secular·Democratic·Republic + Justice·Liberty·Equality·Fraternity
Mnemonic: "Sensible Students Should Do Research, Just Like Every Fearless"
3 types of Justice in Preamble: Social, Economic, Political (from French Revolution + Russia inspired)
42ND AMENDMENT & PREAMBLE
AspectDetail

SECTION 06
Salient Features of the Indian Constitution
#FeatureExplanation
1Longest Written ConstitutionWorld's longest written & handwritten constitution. Originally 395 Articles; now 450+.
2Rigid & FlexibleSome articles are rigid (cannot be easily changed, e.g. federal structure). Some are flexible (simple majority). India has both (नेगिलும் + नेगिलா).
3Federal with Unitary BiasCentre & States share power (federal). But Centre is stronger — called "Quasi-federal." Borrowed from Canada.
4Parliamentary SystemTwo Houses (Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha). PM & Cabinet accountable to Parliament. Borrowed from UK.
5Secular StateNo state religion. All religions treated equally. Added by 42nd Amendment.
6Independent JudiciaryJudges cannot be removed/controlled by any politician or President. This ensures justice.
7Fundamental RightsGuaranteed rights for all citizens — cannot be taken away except during Emergency.
8DPSPDirective Principles of State Policy — guidelines for the State to achieve social/economic justice.
9Single CitizenshipOnly ONE citizenship (Indian). No dual citizenship unlike USA. From UK.
10Universal Adult FranchiseAll citizens 18+ can vote — regardless of gender, wealth, religion, caste.
11Independent Constitutional BodiesElection Commission, UPSC/TNPSC, Finance Commission — not under any Minister's control.
12Emergency ProvisionsPresident can suspend certain rights during National/State Emergency.
MEMORY TRICK 🧠 Rigid vs Flexible: Rigid = can't easily touch (like a "rigid" rod). Flexible = can bend. India's constitution is BOTH — some articles need special majority (rigid), others need simple majority (flexible). Neither fully rigid like USA nor fully flexible like UK.

SECTION 08
Union, States & Union Territories (Articles 1–4)
ArticleWhat It SaysKey Content
Article 1Name and Territory of IndiaIndia = "India, that is Bharat" (dual name accepted). Territory = 28 States + 8 UTs currently.
Article 2Admission / Establishment of New StatesParliament can admit territories OUTSIDE India (from foreign countries) into India. e.g. 2015 — land exchange with Bangladesh (100th Amendment)
Article 3Changes WITHIN IndiaFormation/alteration of states within India — can increase/decrease area, change names, merge, split. e.g. Andhra Pradesh → Telangana (2014). Needs Presidential recommendation; State's opinion taken (but NOT binding).
Article 4Laws under Articles 2 & 3Laws made under Articles 2 & 3 are NOT constitutional amendments (not under Article 368); they are ordinary laws.
TNPSC TRAP ⚠ Article 2 vs Article 3:
• Article 2 = Territory from outside India (foreign territory joining India or India giving land to foreign country)
• Article 3 = Changes within India (splitting one state, merging, renaming)
Students confuse these. Remember: Art.2 = External; Art.3 = Internal.
PYQ ALERT 🏆 "Under which Article can Parliament create a new state?" → Article 3. "Under which Article was Telangana created?" → Article 3. "Which case is linked to Article 2?" → Berubari Union Case (1960) — transfer of Berubari to Pakistan.
CONNECT 🔗 Berubari Union Case (1960): India transferred Berubari area to Pakistan using Article 2. Question arose: Does transfer of territory require Constitutional Amendment? SC said YES — 9th Constitutional Amendment (1960) was passed. Same case also said Preamble is not part of Constitution — two PYQ-relevant points from one case!

SECTION 09
State Reorganisation — The Journey from Princely States to 28 States
THE INTEGRATION CHALLENGE
565 Princely States Had to Join India or Pakistan
After 1947, ~565 princely states had to merge with either India or Pakistan. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel (with Secretary V.P. Menon) handled the integration. 562 merged quickly. 3 resisted: Hyderabad, Junagadh, Jammu & Kashmir.

• Hyderabad: Police Action (Operation Polo), Sep 1948 → merged with India
• Junagadh: Plebiscite (Feb 1948) → people voted to join India
• J&K: King Hari Singh signed Instrument of Accession 26 Oct 1947 after Pakistani attack → merged with India with special status demand
PYQ ALERT 🏆 "Who was the ruler of J&K when it acceded to India?" → Maharaja Hari Singh. "Date of Instrument of Accession?" → 26 October 1947.
CONNECT 🔗 — Article 370 & J&K J&K merger came with a condition: Special Status (own constitution, own flag, separate laws). This became Article 370. Ambedkar refused to draft it; N. Gopalaswami Ayyangar drafted it. Article 370 was operative from 1950 to 31 Oct 2019 when it was revoked. J&K was split into 2 UTs: J&K UT + Ladakh UT.
THREE STATE REORGANISATION COMMITTEES
CommitteeYearRecommendation
S.K. Dhar CommissionJune 1948Said: Do NOT reorganise states on linguistic basis. Use administrative convenience instead.
JVP CommitteeDec 1948 (formed) → Apr 1949 (report)Agreed with Dhar: Linguistic basis NOT advisable. "J" = Jawaharlal Nehru, "V" = Vallabhbhai Patel, "P" = Pattabhi Sitaramayya
Fazl Ali CommissionDec 1953 (formed) → Sep 1955 (report)Recommended HOW to reorganise linguistically (not IF). After Potti Sriramulu's death forced Andhra creation, this commission mapped out all states.
CRITICAL FACT ❗ Potti Sriramulu: Demanded separate Andhra state (Telugu-speaking people) from Madras Province. Started fast-unto-death on 19 Oct 1952. Died on 25 Dec 1952. His death led to massive protests → Andhra Pradesh created on 1 Oct 1953 as the FIRST linguistic state of India.
PYQ ALERT 🏆 "First linguistic state formed in India?" → Andhra Pradesh (1 Oct 1953). "Who fasted for Andhra?" → Potti Sriramulu. "Which committee finally led to SRA 1956?" → Fazl Ali Commission.

7th Constitutional Amendment + States Reorganisation Act = 14 States + 6 UTs
Based on Fazl Ali Commission's report, the 7th Constitutional Amendment + States Reorganisation Act came into force on 1 Nov 1956. It abolished the A, B, C, D classification and created:
• 14 States + 6 Union Territories

This is the foundation. Later additions brought us to 28 states + 8 UTs.

Q: On which date was India's Constitution adopted? / What is Constitution Day?
26 November 1949 = Constitution Day (Samvidhan Divas). Celebrated officially since 2015. 26 January 1950 = Republic Day (when it came into force). The difference between these two dates is a current affairs + polity crossover question for 2026.

31 Oct 2019. J&K split into 2 UTs. This is a high-profile recent development tested since 2020. With 8 UTs now (up from 7), direct factual questions on current UT count are expected.

389 total → 299 after partition. The 42nd, 44th, 86th amendments are increasingly tested. Watch for questions on which words were added to the Preamble in 1976.

Andhra Pradesh (1 Oct 1953). Potti Sriramulu angle. With Telugu states frequently in news (AP + Telangana), this may resurface in Group 4/VAO 2026.
EXAMINER'S TRAP MCQ 🎯 Q: "Which of the following correctly identifies the temporary President of the Constituent Assembly?"
A) Dr. Rajendra Prasad    B) Jawaharlal Nehru    C) Sachchidananda Sinha    D) B.R. Ambedkar

Trap: 90% of students answer A (Rajendra Prasad) because they know he was "President of CA." But Rajendra Prasad was the PERMANENT President. The TEMPORARY President was Sachchidananda Sinha (C). The examiner specifically adds "temporary" to trap those who only know the permanent president.
SECTION 13
Quick MCQ Drill — 10 Questions
Q1. Who was the FIRST person to officially demand a Constituent Assembly for India?
A) Mahatma Gandhi
B) Jawaharlal Nehru
C) M.N. Roy
D) Motilal Nehru
✅ Answer: C — M.N. Roy (1934)
📌 M.N. Roy made the first formal/official demand to the British for a Constituent Assembly in 1934.
🪤 Trap: Gandhi is often chosen because he expressed the idea in 1922, but that was a concept, not a formal demand to the British.
Q2. Under which Cabinet Mission recommendation was the Constituent Assembly formed?
A) August Offer (1940)
B) Cripps Mission (1942)
C) Cabinet Mission (1946)
D) Wavell Plan (1945)
✅ Answer: C — Cabinet Mission (1946)
📌 Lawrence, Alexander, and Cripps formed the Cabinet Mission. Their recommendation finally led to the CA being formed.
🪤 Trap: Cripps was in BOTH 1942 mission AND 1946 mission. Students confuse Cripps Mission with Cabinet Mission.
Q3. Gandhi described which proposal as "Post-dated cheque of a failing bank"?
A) August Offer
B) Cripps Mission
C) Cabinet Mission
D) Wavell Plan
✅ Answer: B — Cripps Mission (1942)
📌 Gandhi called the 1942 Cripps Mission "a post-dated cheque on a crashing bank." He called the August Offer simply a "post-dated cheque."
🪤 Trap: "Post-dated cheque" alone refers to August Offer; "post-dated cheque of a FAILING BANK" is Cripps Mission.
Q4. How many members were in the Constituent Assembly when the Constitution was adopted on 26 Nov 1949?
A) 389
B) 299
C) 284
D) 292
✅ Answer: C — 284 members signed
📌 On 26 Nov 1949 (signing day), 284 out of 299 present members signed the Constitution.
🪤 Trap: 389 is total CA members; 299 is post-partition count. 284 signed on adoption day.
Q5. Who was the Temporary President of the Constituent Assembly?
A) Dr. Rajendra Prasad
B) Sachchidananda Sinha
C) Jawaharlal Nehru
D) B.R. Ambedkar
✅ Answer: B — Sachchidananda Sinha
📌 Sachchidananda Sinha was elected as Temporary President at the 1st meeting (9 Dec 1946), proposed by Acharya Kripalani (French convention).
🪤 Trap: Rajendra Prasad was Permanent President. The question says "temporary" — that's Sinha.
Q6. Which case held that the Preamble IS a part of the Indian Constitution?
A) Berubari Union Case (1960)
B) Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973)
C) LIC Case (1995)
D) Golaknath Case (1967)
✅ Answer: B — Kesavananda Bharati (1973)
📌 1973 SC said Preamble IS part of Constitution and can be amended, but basic structure cannot be changed.
🪤 Trap: Berubari (1960) said Preamble is NOT part of Constitution. These two cases have OPPOSITE rulings.
Q7. How many times has the Preamble of the Indian Constitution been amended?
A) Never
B) Once
C) Twice
D) Three times
✅ Answer: B — Once (42nd Amendment, 1976)
📌 Only the 42nd Constitutional Amendment (1976) amended the Preamble by adding Socialist, Secular, and Integrity.
🪤 Trap: Some students say "never" thinking the Preamble cannot be amended. It CAN be — but only once so far.
Q8. The first linguistically organised state of India was:
A) Tamil Nadu (1969)
B) Gujarat (1960)
C) Andhra Pradesh (1953)
D) Maharashtra (1960)
✅ Answer: C — Andhra Pradesh (1 Oct 1953)
📌 After Potti Sriramulu's fast-unto-death and death on 25 Dec 1952, Andhra Pradesh was formed on 1 Oct 1953 as India's first linguistic state.
🪤 Trap: Tamil Nadu (Madras→Tamil Nadu) is a name change (1969), not a new state formation. Gujarat is 1960 (split from Bombay).
Q9. Which Article gives Parliament power to form new states within India by altering existing states?
A) Article 1
B) Article 2
C) Article 3
D) Article 4
✅ Answer: C — Article 3
📌 Article 3 allows formation, alteration, merger, or division of states WITHIN India. Article 2 is for territories outside India.
🪤 Trap: Article 2 is for new states from OUTSIDE. Article 3 is for changes WITHIN India. Many students confuse these two.
Q10. The Drafting Committee of the Constituent Assembly had how many members?
A) 5
B) 6
C) 7
D) 8
✅ Answer: C — 7 members
📌 Seven members: Ambedkar (chairman), Munshi, Alladi, Saadullah, Gopalaswami, N. Madhava Rao (replaced Mitter), T.T. Krishnamachari (replaced Khaitan).
🪤 Trap: Old textbooks say 8. The correct answer is 7. Two members were replacements — leading some texts to count both original and replacement = 8 total names, but only 7 were active at any time.
