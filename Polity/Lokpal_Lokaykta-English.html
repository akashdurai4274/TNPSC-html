Section 01

Evolutionary Origin & Etymology

RULE 01

The Scandinavian Root of Anti-Corruption Institutions

The conceptual blueprint of anti-corruption ombudsman devices globally tracks back entirely to the institution established in Sweden. The foundational concept was translated into domestic jurisprudence to build structural shields preventing systemic executive malfeasance.

[CRITICAL FACT] Literal Linguistic Meaning

In legal etymology, LokPal translates literally to the "Protector of the People" or "Guardian of the Public" (மக்கள் பாதுகாவலர் / மக்கள் காவலன்).

[MEMORY TRICK] Scandinavian Anchor

Remember: "Sweden's Ombudsman" = India's LokPal. Ombudsman explicitly implies an institutionalized anti-corruption officer (ஊழல் எதிர்ப்பு அதிகாரி) designated to block and police administrative corruption.

Section 02

Indian Legislative Evolution & Passage Timeline

RULE 02

Coining and Recommendations

The trajectory from an academic concept to a operational law spans multiple legislative iterations, changing political environments, and specific administrative reform mandates.

[PYQ ALERT] Structural Origins in India

1. The exact term "LokPal" was formally coined and put forward within Indian discourse in the year 1963 by the renowned jurist Dr. L. M. Singhvi.
2. The official operational implementation of a statutory LokPal and LokAyukta body was structurally recommended by the First Administrative Reforms Commission (ARC) established in 1966, which initially sat under the chairmanship of Morarji Desai.

[TNPSC TRAP] Dual Purpose Commission Confusion

Do not falsely deduce that the 1966 First ARC was set up solely for the LokPal bill. The commission possessed a multi-tier mandate, prominently analyzing structural Centre-State Relations and submitting targeted executive recommendations alongside the ombudsman framework.

RULE 03

The Ten-Attempt Legislative Hurdles

The LokPal Bill holds a unique position in parliamentary history due to repetitive legislative failures before successfully transforming into an enforceable statute.

[CRITICAL FACT] Structural Failure Statistics

The LokPal Bill was introduced into the Parliament of India an extraordinary total of 10 times. It collapsed, lapsed, or faced defeat exactly 9 times across its historical timeline before successfully passing on the 10th attempt.

TNPSC Sample Concept Test: Chronological Legitimacy
  • A. The First LokPal Bill was introduced in Parliament in 1963 by L.M. Singhvi.
  • B. The LokPal bill passed successfully on its 9th parliamentary attempt.
  • C. The LokPal and LokAyuktas Act received Presidential assent on January 1, 2014, and commenced enforcement on January 16, 2014.
  • D. The First ARC recommending LokPal was led by L.M. Singhvi in 1966.
Explanation: L.M. Singhvi coined the term in 1963, but the first bill hit the floor in 1968. The bill succeeded on its 10th presentation, not the 9th. The 1966 ARC was chaired initially by Morarji Desai. Thus, option C is chronologically correct.
Section 03

Institutional Composition & Selection Committee

RULE 04

The "1 + 8" Structural Framework

The overarching LokPal panel operates strictly as a multi-member statutory commission configured by fixed categorical ratios.

[CRITICAL FACT] Statutory Ratios

The body consists precisely of 1 Chairperson and a maximum of 8 Members. The structural composition of the 8 members is strictly divided by a mandatory 50% Rule:

  • 50% Judicial Members: Exactly 4 Members must be drawn from judicial backgrounds (former Supreme Court Judges or High Court Chief Justices).
  • 50% Categorical Balance: Exactly 4 Members must be systematically pulled from SC, ST, OBC, Minority communities, or Women.
[PYQ ALERT] Chronology of Executive Chairpersons

• First Inaugural Chairperson (2019): Justice Pinaki Chandra Ghose (பினாகி சந்திரகோஸ்).
• Incumbent Sitting Chairperson (Current 2025+): Justice Ajay Manikrao Khanwilkar (அஜய் மாணிக்கராவ்).

RULE 05

The Five-Member Select Committee Composition

The appointment of the Chairperson and members is executed by the President, but driven entirely by the binding decisions of a specialized 5-member Selection Committee.

Section 04

Service Conditions, Qualifications & Removal Dynamics

RULE 06

Eligibility Criteria and Disqualifications

To maintain complete institutional neutrality, strict entry criteria, age limits, and subsequent employment bans are applied.

[CRITICAL FACT] Age and Experience Caps

• Minimum Entry Age: Candidates must have completed a minimum of 45 years of age.
• Domain Experience: Requires at least 25 years of highly specialized experience in fields like anti-corruption policy, public administration, vigilance, or law.

[TNPSC TRAP] Professional Disqualification Conditions

An individual is completely disqualified from consideration if they have ever been previously dismissed or removed from any State or Central Government service (பதவி நீக்கம் செய்யப்பட்டுருக்க கூடாது). Furthermore, upon assuming office, they are completely barred from holding any other profit-yielding office or political assignment.

RULE 07

Tenure, Remuneration, and Removal

The structural autonomy of the office is maintained by linking their salaries and terms directly to top judicial offices.

[PYQ ALERT] Tenure & Financial Charges

• Official Tenure Terms: Service is fixed for 5 Years or until reaching 70 Years of age (5 ஆண்டுகள் அல்லது 70 வயது வரை), whichever occurs earlier.
• Chairperson Salary: Equivalent to the Chief Justice of India (CJI).
• Member Salaries: Equivalent to a sitting Judge of the Supreme Court.
• Financial Source: All salaries and costs are charged completely to the Consolidated Fund of India (இந்திய திரட்டு நிதி).

[CRITICAL FACT] Removal Protocol Mechanics

The President of India holds the exclusive constitutional power to execute a removal order. However, the President cannot act independently. Removal can only occur after a formal inquiry and recommendation by the Supreme Court of India confirming charges of misbehaviour or incapacity.

Section 05

Jurisdictional Purview & Operational Restrictions

RULE 08

The Investigative Ambit & Statutory Exemptions

While the LokPal possesses wide anti-corruption investigative powers across all categories of public employees, specific high-level exclusions exist to protect state security.

[CRITICAL FACT] Personnel Jurisdiction Boundaries

• Inclusions: Includes the Prime Minister, Union Ministers, Members of Parliament, and all Central Government personnel spanning Groups A, B, C, and D officers.
• Foreign Funding Purview: The LokPal has the explicit power to investigate any individual or non-governmental entity receiving foreign contributions exceeding ₹10 Lakhs within a single calendar year.
• Absolute Judicial/Electoral Exclusions: Sitting Judges of the Supreme Court of India and all Election Commissioners are completely excluded from LokPal jurisdiction (உட்படாதவர்கள்: தேர்தல் ஆணையர்கள் & உச்சநீதிமன்ற நீதிபதிகள்).

[TNPSC TRAP] Prime Minister Investigation Safeguards

The LokPal can investigate the Prime Minister, but this is subject to strict statutory requirements:
1. Voting Threshold: Investigation approval requires a mandatory 2/3rd Majority vote of the full LokPal bench (3-ல் 2 பங்கு உறுப்பினர்கள்).
2. Transparency Restrictions: All investigative hearings must be recorded via video, but the footage must remain strictly confidential and protected from public disclosure.
3. Subject Matter Exemptions: The LokPal is completely barred from investigating the PM on matters concerning International Relations, Atomic Energy, or Space Science (சர்வதேச உறவுகள், அணுசக்தி, விண்வெளி - விசாரிக்க முடியாது).

RULE 09

Judicial Status, Timelines & Penalty Clauses

The commission operates with specialized judicial powers and strict timelines to prevent endless delays in anti-corruption investigations.

[CONNECT] Core Enforcement Powers

• Court Status: Holds powers equivalent to a Civil Court (உரிமையியல் நீதிமன்றம்). It can order asset confiscation and refer cases directly to agencies like the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC).
• Preliminary Investigation Deadline: Must be completed within 60 Days.
• Final Case Disposition: The entire case must be resolved within a maximum limit of 1 Year.

[CRITICAL FACT] False Complaints Penalties

To prevent malicious or politically motivated cases, any citizen filing an intentionally false or fabricated complaint faces a mandatory penalty of up to 1 Year of Imprisonment alongside a fine of ₹1 Lakh (ஓராண்டு சிறை மற்றும் 1 லட்சம் ரூபாய் அபராதம்).

Section 06

The State LokAyukta Implementation Matrix

RULE 10

State Autonomy and Legislative Variance

While the Union level operates under a single uniform LokPal framework, State-level LokAyuktas vary significantly in administrative structures, tenure rules, and naming conventions.

[PYQ ALERT] First Mover States Matrix

• First State to Pass Legislation: Odisha passed the LokAyukta Bill in 1970, but delayed its actual implementation until 1983.
• First State to Implement Operations: Maharashtra established and launched operational LokAyukta structures in 1971, making it the pioneer state for active implementation.

[CRITICAL FACT] Tamil Nadu Specific Milestones

The state of Tamil Nadu officially passed and established its provincial LokAyukta institutional framework in the year 2018.

[TNPSC TRAP] Dual-Tier Structural Divergence

The institutional design varies dynamically across states. Certain states operate a dual-tier model consisting of a LokAyukta and an Upa-LokAyukta (லோகாயுக்தா மற்றும் உபலோகாயுக்தா). Examples include Rajasthan, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh. Service conditions also differ: LokAyukta tenure is generally fixed at 5 Years or 65 Years of age, with no eligibility for reappointment.
