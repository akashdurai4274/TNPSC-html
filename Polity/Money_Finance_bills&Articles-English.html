Section 04

Topic Overview & Exam Significance

The distinction between a Money Bill (பண மசோதா) under Article 110 and a Financial Bill (நிதி மசோதா) under Article 117 is one of the most frequently tested areas in the TNPSC Indian Polity syllabus. This topic untangles the complex procedural mechanisms of the Indian Parliament, clarifying exactly what constitutes financial legislation and resolving the common confusion surrounding state-level emergency declarations.

  • Exam Weightage: Typically 1 to 2 questions in Group 1, Group 2/2A, and Group 4/VAO exams.
  • Core Philosophy: Do not just blindly memorise the numbers; understand the fundamental criteria defining each bill. Learn to decouple Article 110 of the Constitution from Rule 110 of the State Legislative Assembly procedures.

Section 06.1

1. Money Bill — Definition, Certification & Procedures

RULE 01

The Three Pillars of Article 110

A bill is categorized strictly as a Money Bill if it contains only provisions dealing with all or any of the matters enumerated under Article 110. According to the lecture transcript, these focus on three central pillars:

  • Taxation (வரிவிதித்தல்): The imposition, abolition, remission, alteration, or regulation of any tax by the Central Government.
  • Borrowing (கடன் வாங்குதல்): The regulation of borrowing money or the giving of any guarantee by the Union Government (e.g., from the Reserve Bank of India).
  • Consolidated Fund (ஒருங்கிணைந்த நிதியம்): The custody of, payment into, or withdrawal of money from the Consolidated Fund of India.
[CRITICAL FACT] Absolute Exclusions from Article 110

A bill is NOT deemed to be a Money Bill by reason only that it provides for:
1. The imposition of fines or other pecuniary penalties (அபராதங்கள்).
2. The demand or payment of fees for licences or fees for services rendered.
3. The imposition, abolition, remission, alteration, or regulation of any tax by any local authority or body for local purposes (உள்ளூர் சபையால் விதிக்கப்படும் வரிகள்).

[PYQ ALERT] 🏆 The Speaker's Certificate

If any question arises whether a bill is a Money Bill or not, the decision of the Speaker of the Lok Sabha (சபாநாயகர்) is final. Their certificate is attached to the bill when it is transmitted to the Rajya Sabha and presented to the President for assent.

[TNPSC TRAP] The Rajya Sabha Power Myth

Common Mistake: Thinking that the Rajya Sabha can reject or amend a Money Bill like an ordinary bill.
Correct Law: A Money Bill can only be introduced in the Lok Sabha. The Rajya Sabha has virtually zero legislative leverage. It must return the bill within a strict timeline of 14 Days with or without recommendations. The Lok Sabha can reject all recommendations, and the bill passes anyway.

[CONNECT] Deadlock Resolution & Prior Assent

• Prior Assent Required: A Money Bill can only be tabled with the prior recommendation/permission of the President of India.
• No Joint Sitting: Since the Lok Sabha holds supreme, overriding powers, there is no possibility of a deadlock. Therefore, a Joint Sitting (கூட்டு கூட்டம்) is completely barred under Article 108 for Money Bills.

Practice MCQ Concept Test:
  • A. Rajya Sabha can retain a Money Bill for up to 30 days without returning it.
  • B. In case of a deadlock over a Money Bill, the President summons a Joint Sitting.
  • C. The Speaker of the Lok Sabha possesses sole, absolute power to certify a bill as a Money Bill under Article 110.
  • D. A bill imposing local municipal taxes is certified as a Money Bill.
Section 06.2

2. Financial Bills — Categories, Article 117(1) vs 117(3)

RULE 02

The General Scope of Revenue and Expenditure

Any bill dealing with broad fiscal matters, public revenue, or government expenditure that does not meet the restrictive criteria of Article 110 is designated as a Financial Bill under Article 117. These are closely connected to the Annual Financial Statement (பட்ஜெட் கூட்டத்தொடர்) presented every February (e.g., the recent Budget session of 2025), which tracks general national income and departmental expenditures.

[EXCEPTION] "All Money Bills are Financial Bills, but..."

Remember the classic constitutional aphorism: "All Money Bills are Financial Bills, but not all Financial Bills are Money Bills." This is because Financial Bills contain non-money provisions alongside financial matters, whereas Money Bills contain only matters listed in Article 110.

Procedural Parameter Financial Bill (Type I) — Art. 117(1) Financial Bill (Type II) — Art. 117(3)
Core Substantive Content Contains any matter from Article 110 plus general non-financial legislative subjects. Contains provisions involving expenditures from the Consolidated Fund of India, but none of the matters from Article 110.
House of Initial Introduction Lok Sabha Only (மக்களவை மட்டும்) Either House of Parliament (இரு அவைகளிலும் யார் வேணாலும்)
President's Prior Recommendation Mandatory for introduction. Not required for introduction; required only before consideration for passing.
Rajya Sabha Powers Full ordinary powers (Can amend or reject). Full ordinary powers (Can amend or reject).
Joint Sitting Mechanism Permissible (கூட்டு கூட்டம் உண்டு) Permissible (கூட்டு கூட்டம் உண்டு)
Section 06.3

3. Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly Rule 110 — The Great Confusion

RULE 03

Decoupling Article 110 from House Rules

One of the biggest misconceptions among aspirants is conflating Constitutional Article 110 with the frequently reported announcements made by the Chief Minister under "Rule 110" in the Tamil Nadu State Legislative Assembly.

[CRITICAL FACT] The Source of Assembly Rules

Under Article 208 of the Indian Constitution, each State Legislature has the autonomous power to frame rules to regulate its procedure and conduct of business. The Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly has framed a total of 23 Chapters and 292 Rules under this provision.

[TNPSC TRAP] The Numerical Coincidence Danger

• Constitutional Article 110: Defines a Union Money Bill in the Indian Constitution.
• Assembly Rule 110 (சட்டப்பேரவை விதி 110): It is merely the 110th procedural rule inside the rulebook created under Article 208.
The Rule 110 Mechanism: It allows the Chief Minister (முதலமைச்சர்) to make a dynamic ministerial statement on a policy matter or an emergency decree on the floor of the House without any immediate debate, discussion, or voting among the members.

Practice MCQ Analytical Test:
  • A. The Tamil Nadu Chief Minister makes policy declarations under Constitutional Article 110.
  • B. State Assembly Rule 110 is a procedural rule framed under Article 208, allowing ministerial statements without debate.
  • C. Rule 110 announcements require mandatory voting in the State Assembly within 14 days.

Section 10

Quick MCQ Drill (TNPSC Standard Model)

Q1. Which Constitutional Article defines the essential parameters of a Money Bill in India?
A) Article 112
B) Article 117
C) Article 110
D) Article 208
✅ Answer: C) — Article 110
📌 Why: Article 110 specifically outlines the strict conditions under which a bill is deemed a Money Bill.
🪤 Trap: Choosing Article 117 by confusing generic Financial Bills with restrictive Money Bills.
Q2. Who holds the absolute authority to decide whether a bill is a Money Bill or not?
A) The Prime Minister
B) The President of India
C) The Speaker of the Lok Sabha
D) The Union Finance Minister
✅ Answer: C) — The Speaker of the Lok Sabha
📌 Why: Under the Constitution, the Speaker's certificate is final and unchallengeable.
🪤 Trap: Choosing the President because of their mandatory "prior recommendation" power.
Q3. For how many days can the Rajya Sabha maximum delay a certified Money Bill?
A) 30 Days
B) 14 Days
C) 6 Months
D) 60 Days
✅ Answer: B) — 14 Days
📌 Why: If the Rajya Sabha does not return the bill within 14 days, it is deemed passed by both Houses.
🪤 Trap: Confusing this with the 6-month window allowed for ordinary deadlocks before a joint sitting.
Q4. Which of the following matters is completely excluded from the definition of a Money Bill?
A) Alteration of a central tax
B) Imposition of local municipal taxes
C) Regulation of central borrowing
D) Appropriation from Consolidated Fund
✅ Answer: B) — Imposition of local municipal taxes
📌 Why: Article 110 explicitly excludes fees, licence costs, and local municipal taxes from its scope.
🪤 Trap: Believing that because it is a "tax," local body taxes must automatically be part of Article 110.
Q5. Under which Article can a Financial Bill (Type II) be introduced in either House of Parliament?
A) Article 117(1)
B) Article 110
C) Article 117(3)
D) Article 112
✅ Answer: C) — Article 117(3)
📌 Why: Article 117(3) governs expenditures from the Consolidated Fund and can originate in either House.
🪤 Trap: Selecting 117(1), which acts like a Money Bill and can only be introduced in the Lok Sabha.
Q6. In case of an operational deadlock over a Financial Bill under Article 117, what is the remedy?
A) The Lok Sabha overrides the Rajya Sabha automatically after 14 days.
B) The Bill instantly lapses.
C) The President can summon a Joint Sitting under Article 108.
D) The Speaker overrides the dispute.
✅ Answer: C) — The President can summon a Joint Sitting under Article 108.
📌 Why: Financial Bills allow ordinary legislative recourse, including joint sittings, which are barred for Money Bills.
🪤 Trap: Applying the 14-day Money Bill override rule to general Financial Bills.
Q7. The procedural "Rule 110" used in the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly is framed under which Constitutional Article?
A) Article 110
B) Article 117
C) Article 208
D) Article 112
✅ Answer: C) — Article 208
📌 Why: Article 208 empowers state legislatures to create internal procedural rules. Rule 110 is simply the 110th rule within that rulebook.
🪤 Trap: Falling for the numerical trick and choosing Article 110.
Q8. What is the operational effect when a statement is made under State Assembly Rule 110?
A) It triggers a mandatory secret vote.
B) It allows the statement to be recorded directly without debate or discussion.
C) It requires immediate Rajya Sabha verification.
D) The house is adjourned for 14 days.
✅ Answer: B) — It allows the statement to be recorded directly without debate or discussion.
📌 Why: Rule 110 protects government announcements from intermediate floor debates or opposition arguments during the declaration.
🪤 Trap: Assuming it acts like a Money Bill and carries a 14-day delay or voting clause.
Q9. Which of the following bills requires the prior recommendation of the President before introduction?
A) Money Bill and Financial Bill (Type I)
B) Financial Bill (Type II) only
C) Ordinary Bills only
D) All Legislative Assembly resolutions
✅ Answer: A) — Money Bill and Financial Bill (Type I)
📌 Why: Both Article 110 and Article 117(1) require prior presidential recommendation to be introduced.
🪤 Trap: Believing that Financial Bill Type II also needs prior consent before it can be tabled.
Q10. Who determines the internal rules of business and number of rules within a State Legislative Assembly?
A) The Parliament of India
B) The State Assembly itself under Article 208
C) The Governor by executive order
D) The Supreme Court of India
✅ Answer: B) — The State Assembly itself under Article 208
📌 Why: Article 208 gives state houses full autonomy to draft their internal regulations, creating frameworks like TN's 292 rules.
🪤 Trap: Assuming the Governor sets these rules as part of their general executive assembly control.
