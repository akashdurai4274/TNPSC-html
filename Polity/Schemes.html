C-01
PM Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)
Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
FEB 2019
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
Small and marginal farmers lacked supplemental income support to meet farming input costs, leading to agrarian distress and debt cycles.
Objective
  • Provide income support of ₹6,000/year to all farmer families
  • Reduce dependency on informal moneylenders for crop inputs
  • Strengthen the financial base of small & marginal farmers
Key Features
  • ₹2,000 transferred in 3 equal instalments directly to bank accounts via DBT
  • Covers all landholding farmer families (institutional landholders excluded)
  • Funded entirely by Central Government (100% centrally sponsored)
  • Aadhaar-linked beneficiary verification; PM-KISAN portal for self-registration
Target Beneficiaries
All landholding farmer families across India (~14 crore families)
Budget / Outlay
₹75,000 crore annually (approx.); over ₹3.04 lakh crore disbursed by 2024

Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs
JUN 2015
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
Rapid urbanisation created massive slum populations and housing deficits in Indian cities. Existing schemes like JNNURM were insufficient in coverage and impact.
Objective
  • Ensure "Housing for All" in urban areas by 2022 (extended)
  • Provide affordable housing through 4 verticals: CLSS, AHP, BLC, ISSR
  • Slum rehabilitation with private sector participation
Key Features
  • 4 Verticals: In-Situ Slum Rehabilitation (ISSR), CLSS (Credit Linked Subsidy), AHP (Affordable Housing in Partnership), BLC (Beneficiary-Led Construction)
  • Interest subsidy up to 6.5% on home loans for EWS/LIG
  • Technology Innovation Grant for green/disaster-resistant housing
  • Covers all 4,041 statutory towns

EWS (income <₹3 lakh), LIG (₹3–6 lakh), MIG (₹6–18 lakh) categories in urban areas
Budget / Outlay
₹1,11,893 crore (centre share); 1.12 crore houses sanctioned by 2023
⚠ TRAP
CLSS (Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme) is a COMPONENT of PMAY-U, not a separate scheme. PMAY-U 2.0 was announced in Union Budget 2024–25 for 1 crore additional urban poor.
💡 TIP
The "Technology Sub-Mission" under PMAY-U promotes green building materials, construction technology and disaster-resistant features — often asked in exams.

C-05
PM Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana (PMGKAY)
Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food & Public Distribution
MAR 2020
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
COVID-19 lockdown severely disrupted livelihoods and food security of the poor. Emergency free food grain distribution was needed to prevent hunger during the pandemic.
Objective
  • Provide free additional food grains (over NFSA entitlement) to NFSA beneficiaries
  • Ensure no poor goes hungry during economic distress
  • Merged with NFSA from January 2023 for permanent free grain distribution
Key Features
  • 5 kg free grain per person per month (rice/wheat/coarse grains) under NFSA
  • Extended multiple times; merged with NFSA from Jan 1, 2023 (5 years)
  • Covers 81.35 crore NFSA beneficiaries; One Nation One Ration Card enabled
  • Government bears full cost — ₹2 lakh crore+ spent on free grain
Target Beneficiaries
81.35 crore beneficiaries under National Food Security Act 2013
⚠ TRAP
PMGKAY was originally a COVID-19 emergency measure but became permanent from Jan 2023. Under merged PMGKAY+NFSA, Priority Households get 5 kg FREE, Antyodaya Anna Yojana families get 35 kg FREE per month.
💡 TIP
The merger of PMGKAY with NFSA was announced for 5 years (2023–2028), making India's free food grain programme the world's largest such scheme covering ~81 crore people.
💡 MNEMONIC: "GARIB = Grain At Really Incredible Bulk"
Reminds you it's a food grain scheme for the poor, free of cost
C-06
Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM)
Ministry of Jal Shakti
AUG 2019
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
Only 17% of rural households had tap water connections in 2019. Women walked kilometres daily to fetch water, affecting health, education and productivity.
Objective
  • Provide Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) to every rural household by 2024
  • Ensure adequate quantity (55 lpcd) of prescribed quality water on regular basis
  • Promote community participation through Village Water & Sanitation Committees
Key Features
  • Target: 19.27 crore rural households; 14+ crore connected by 2024
  • Funding: 90:10 (Himalayan/NE states), 50:50 (others); UTs fully funded by Centre
  • Convergence with SBM, MGNREGS, PMKSY for grey water management
  • Jal Jeevan Mission (Urban) also launched for cities — separate scheme
Budget / Outlay
₹3.60 lakh crore (2019–2024); largest water supply scheme in the world
⚠ TRAP
JJM replaced the National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP). Also note: JJM Urban is a SEPARATE scheme under MoHUA launched in 2021. Do not confuse them.
💡 TIP
Target norm under JJM: 55 litres per capita per day (lpcd). Har Ghar Jal is the brand name. Launched on Independence Day 2019 (Aug 15) by PM Modi from Red Fort.
💡 MNEMONIC: "JJM = Jal for Joy Mission — 55 lpcd, Har Ghar Jal"
55 lpcd water norm + tagline Har Ghar Jal = brand of Jal Jeevan Mission
C-07
Mahatma Gandhi NREGS (MGNREGS)
Ministry of Rural Development
FEB 2006
UPA/INC
▼
Why Launched
Rural unemployment and seasonal migration of agricultural labourers was a persistent problem. A rights-based guaranteed employment framework was needed to address rural poverty.
Objective
  • Guarantee 100 days of wage employment per year to every rural household
  • Create durable assets (roads, ponds, check dams) in rural areas
  • Strengthen the livelihood resource base of the rural poor
Key Features
  • Legally guaranteed 100 days employment; extended to 150 days in drought years
  • One-third of beneficiaries must be women; SC/ST given priority
  • Unemployment allowance if work not provided within 15 days of demand
  • Social audit mandatory; Job cards issued to all eligible households
Target Beneficiaries
Any adult member of a rural household willing to do unskilled manual work
Budget / Outlay
₹73,000 crore (2023–24 BE); ~5–6 crore households employed annually
⚠ TRAP
NREGS was enacted as NREG ACT 2005 and implemented from Feb 2, 2006. It was renamed MGNREGS in 2009 after Gandhi's name. The ACT (2005) and LAUNCH DATE (2006) are different — common exam trap!
💡 TIP
MGNREGS is the world's largest social protection/public works programme. It started in 200 districts, expanded to all districts by April 2008. Gram Sabha approves works; Gram Panchayat executes at least 50% of works.
💡 MNEMONIC: "100-15-1/3" = 100 days guaranteed, 15 days to provide work, 1/3 women mandatory
Three key numbers for MGNREGS that appear frequently in exams

C-08
PM Street Vendor's AtmaNirbhar Nidhi (PM SVANidhi)
Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs
JUN 2020
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
COVID-19 lockdown devastated street vendors who lost working capital overnight and couldn't access formal credit due to lack of collateral.
Objective
  • Provide affordable collateral-free working capital loan to street vendors
  • Enable their reintegration into the formal financial system
  • Reward digital transactions with cashback incentives
Key Features
  • Loan tiers: ₹10,000 (1st), ₹20,000 (2nd), ₹50,000 (3rd) — on timely repayment
  • Interest subvention at 7% p.a.; 1-year tenure
  • Monthly cashback up to ₹100 for digital transactions via UPI
  • Vendors identified via ULB survey; certificate of vending issued
Target Beneficiaries
~50 lakh street vendors in urban areas as of 2020
⚠ TRAP
PM SVANidhi is for STREET VENDORS specifically — do not confuse with PM Mudra Yojana (for micro-enterprises broadly) or PMEGP (for small industries). SVANidhi is urban-focused; Mudra covers rural & urban.
💡 TIP
PM SVANidhi was first launched exclusively for COVID rehabilitation of street vendors. The scheme was later extended. It has a unique digital incentive component — ₹1,200/year cashback on digital payments.
💡 MNEMONIC: "SVANidhi = Street Vendor Atmanirbhar NIDHI (Fund)"
NIDHI = fund/treasury; scheme name itself is the mnemonic for its purpose
C-09
PM Mudra Yojana (PMMY)
Ministry of Finance / MUDRA Ltd. (under SIDBI)
APR 2015
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
Micro and small enterprises — the backbone of employment — were excluded from formal bank credit due to lack of collateral and documentation, forcing them to depend on usurious moneylenders.
Objective
  • Provide collateral-free loans to non-corporate, non-farm micro enterprises
  • Fund the unfunded and under-funded micro entrepreneurs
  • Create formal credit ecosystem for 5.77 crore micro enterprises
Key Features
  • 3 loan products: Shishu (<₹50,000), Kishore (₹50K–5L), Tarun (₹5L–10L); 4th: Tarun+ (₹10L–20L) added 2024
  • No collateral required; Mudra card (RuPay-linked debit card) issued
  • Available through banks, NBFCs, MFIs, RRBs, small finance banks
  • Credit Guarantee through MUDRA Credit Guarantee Fund
Target Beneficiaries
Non-corporate small business owners, artisans, small traders, vegetable vendors — anyone in non-farm informal sector
⚠ TRAP
MUDRA = Micro Units Development & Refinance Agency. It's a refinancing institution, not a direct lending bank. Also: Shishu-Kishore-Tarun names are frequently mixed up in MCQs. Shishu = smallest (up to ₹50K), Tarun = largest (up to ₹10L).
💡 TIP
Since 2015 launch, MUDRA has sanctioned loans worth ₹27+ lakh crore to 45+ crore accounts. Women borrowers account for ~68% — a key statistic for exams.
💡 MNEMONIC: "SKT = Shishu Kid, Tarun Teen" (child to teen growth = loan size growth)
Shishu (child) = smallest loan, Kishore (adolescent) = medium, Tarun (teen/youth) = largest
C-10
Skill India Mission / PMKVY
Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE)
JUL 2015
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
India's demographic dividend was at risk — the working-age population was growing but without marketable skills, threatening unemployment and productivity. Only 4.7% of the workforce had formal vocational training (vs. 52% in USA, 75% in Germany).
Objective
  • Skill 40 crore people by 2022 through various programmes
  • Create a demand-driven, outcome-based skilling ecosystem
  • Align skilling with industry requirements through National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF)
Key Features
  • PMKVY (flagship): Short-term training + Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL); ₹8,000 average training cost
  • Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Kendras (PMKK): Model ITIs in every district
  • National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS) linked
  • PMKVY 4.0 (2022–26): Focus on Industry 4.0, AI, coding, robotics, drones
Target Beneficiaries
Youth aged 15–45 years; PMKVY 4.0 targets 1 crore youth (2022–2026)
⚠ TRAP
Skill India Mission ≠ PMKVY. Skill India is the umbrella mission; PMKVY is one scheme under it. MSDE was created as a NEW ministry in 2014 specifically for skilling.
💡 TIP
National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) implements PMKVY. Sector Skill Councils (SSCs) set standards for 30+ sectors. NSQF levels 1–10 align qualifications nationally.
💡 MNEMONIC: "SKILL = Sector-based Kaushal Integrated Livelihood Learning"
Skill India focuses on sector-based, demand-driven skilling for livelihood

Ministry of Electronics & IT (MeitY)
JUL 2015
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
India lagged in digital infrastructure, internet penetration and e-governance. Bridging the digital divide and creating a knowledge economy required a coordinated national programme.

  • Transform India into a digitally empowered society and knowledge economy

Make in India
Department for Promotion of Industry & Internal Trade (DPIIT)
SEP 2014
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
India's share in global manufacturing was just 2% in 2014 against a target of 25%. Weak investment climate, poor infrastructure and complex regulations deterred manufacturing FDI.
Objective
  • Increase manufacturing sector's contribution to GDP from 15% to 25% by 2025
  • Create 100 million additional manufacturing jobs by 2022
  • Make India a global manufacturing hub and improve Ease of Doing Business
Key Features
  • Covers 27 sectors including electronics, auto, pharma, defence, textiles
  • Investor Facilitation Cell (IFC); Invest India as official investment promotion agency
  • FDI reforms in 25+ sectors; reduced compliance burden
  • Linked to PLI Scheme (Production Linked Incentive) for targeted manufacturing boost

Make in India was launched on Sep 25, 2014 — NOT on Independence Day. Symbol is a lion made of cogs/gears. Do not confuse with Startup India (Jan 2016) or Atma Nirbhar Bharat (May 2020).
💡 TIP
Make in India 2.0 focuses on 27 sectors — reduced from original 25 to add new emerging sectors. DPIIT (earlier DIPP) is the nodal ministry. Lion logo symbolises strength, courage and manufacturing.
💡 MNEMONIC: "Make in India = Sep 2014, Lion Logo, 25% GDP target"
Month (Sep), Year (2014), Symbol (Lion), Goal (25% manufacturing GDP)

BJP/NDA

India had massive entrepreneurial potential but startups faced regulatory hurdles, limited access to funding, and lack of mentorship ecosystems.

C-14
PM POSHAN Shakti Nirman (Mid-Day Meal → PM POSHAN)
Ministry of Education
SEP 2021
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
The Mid-Day Meal Scheme (1995) was renamed and revamped as PM POSHAN in 2021 to improve nutritional outcomes and boost school enrolment and attendance.
Objective
  • Provide nutritious cooked meals to children in government schools (Classes 1–8)
  • Improve nutritional status, reduce hunger and boost school attendance
  • Extend to pre-primary (Bal Vatika) classes in government schools
Key Features

Links the scheme name to its nutrition and school attendance goals
C-15
Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP)
WCD + Health + Education Ministries (tri-ministerial)
JAN 2015
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
India's declining Child Sex Ratio (CSR) from 945 (2001) to 918 (2011) per 1000 boys, combined with low female education rates, required urgent intervention.
Objective
  • Prevent gender-biased sex selective elimination through enforcement of PC-PNDT Act
  • Ensure survival and protection of the girl child
  • Promote education and participation of girls
Key Features
  • Initially launched in 100 districts with low CSR; expanded to all districts
  • Multi-sectoral convergence across WCD, Health and Education ministries
  • SBM convergence for girls' toilets in schools
  • Beti Bachao campaign: community mobilisation against sex determination
⚠ TRAP
BBBP launched Jan 22, 2015 at Panipat, Haryana — a state with one of the lowest sex ratios. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana was launched the SAME DAY (Jan 22, 2015) as a companion financial scheme for girl child savings.
💡 TIP
CSR improved from 918 (2014–15) to 934 (2019–20) — a positive outcome of BBBP. PM Modi launched BBBP himself at Panipat citing Haryana's alarmingly low sex ratio of 879.
💡 MNEMONIC: "BBBP = Bachao (Save) + Padhao (Educate) = B+P"
Two action words: Bachao (protection) and Padhao (education) = dual objective of the scheme
C-16
Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)
Ministry of Finance (Post Office / Banks)
JAN 2015
BJP/NDA
▼
Why Launched
Girl children had no dedicated savings and investment vehicle. Families needed incentives to invest in daughters' education and marriage rather than treating daughters as financial burdens.
Objective
  • Provide a high-interest savings account for girl child's future education and marriage
  • Encourage financial planning for girl child from birth to adulthood
  • Offer tax benefits to incentivise savings for girl children
Key Features
  • Account opened in name of girl child below 10 years; up to 2 girls per family
  • Interest rate: 8.2% p.a. (2024); best among small savings schemes
  • Minimum deposit ₹250/year; maximum ₹1.5 lakh/year; Tenure: 21 years from opening
