Q6
HARD Factual 💎 GEM
The Chennai Municipal Corporation — India's oldest local self-government institution — was established in which year? Connect: Who founded Chennai city? When did it become a Province? When did Madras become a State? When was its name changed to Tamil Nadu? When was the district renamed Chennai?
▼
✅ ANSWER — COMPLETE CHENNAI TIMELINE
1. 1639 — Francis Day (of East India Company) acquired land from Chennappa Nayak and founded Madras city.
2. 1688 — Madras Municipal Corporation established (~40 years later; St. George's Fort area).
3. 1801 — Madras became a Province (associated with Lord Wellesley).
4. 1956, November 1 — Madras became a separate State (States Reorganisation).
5. 1969, January 14 (Pongal Day) — CM Arignar Anna renamed the state from Madras to Tamil Nadu.
6. 1996 — CM Kalaignar (M. Karunanidhi) renamed Madras district to Chennai (in memory of Chennappa Nayak, at the request of his descendants).
💎 LIVE GEM — COMPLETE DATE CHAIN
The teacher drilled this as a 6-fact revision chain. Any one of these 6 dates could appear as a standalone question. Memorise all 6 in sequence: 1639 → 1688 → 1801 → 1956 → 1969 → 1996.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
All 6 dates have appeared across TNPSC exams (1688 = very frequent). "Who renamed Madras to Tamil Nadu?" → Arignar Anna, 1969 — appeared in Group 4 paper. "Who renamed Madras district to Chennai?" → Kalaignar, 1996.
📊 Group 2 — Population Thresholds for Urban Bodies
Q7
MEDIUM Factual 💎 GEM
What is the minimum population required to classify an area as a Municipality (நகராட்சி) in Tamil Nadu?
▼
✅ ANSWER
A Municipality (நகராட்சி) requires a population of more than 1,00,000 (1 Lakh).
💎 LIVE GEM — COMPLETE POPULATION SHORTCUT TABLE
The teacher gave a brilliant shortcut system — memorise all four together:

🏙️ Municipal Corporation (மாநகராட்சி) → > 10 Lakh (1 Million)
🏘️ Municipality (நகராட்சி) → > 1 Lakh
🚌 Town Panchayat / Peruratchi (பேரூராட்சி) → > 10,000 — "Bus ticket = ₹10 → 10,000 people"
🌾 District Panchayat / Mavatta Uratchi (மாவட்ட ஊராட்சி) → 50,000

BUS ₹10 → 10,000 | NAGAR → 1 Lakh | MA.NAGAR → 10 Lakh
🪤 COMMON TRAP
Students confuse Municipality (1 Lakh) with Town Panchayat (10,000). Many write 50,000 for Municipality — that's WRONG. 50,000 is for District Panchayat.
Q8
EASY Factual
What is the population threshold for a Municipal Corporation (மாநகராட்சி)?
▼
✅ ANSWER
More than 10 Lakh (1 Million) population → Municipal Corporation.
💡 TIP
The head of a Municipal Corporation is called the Mayor (மேயர்). If a district question is political: Mayor rules. If administrative: Collector (District Collector) rules. Both are true — different domains.
Q9
EASY Factual
What is the population threshold for a Town Panchayat / Peruratchi (பேரூராட்சி)?
▼
✅ ANSWER
More than 10,000 population → Town Panchayat (Peruratchi / பேரூராட்சி).
💎 LIVE GEM — BUS TICKET TRICK
"பேருந்து" (Bus) → "பேரூராட்சி" (Peruratchi). Basic bus ticket minimum in Tamil Nadu = ₹10. So Peruratchi = 10,000 people! The teacher used this brilliantly — pēruṇdu ticket ₹10 → pēruratchi 10,000.
Q10
EASY Factual
What is the population threshold for a District Panchayat (மாவட்ட ஊராட்சி)?
▼
✅ ANSWER
50,000 population → District Panchayat (Mavatta Uratchi / மாவட்ட ஊராட்சி).
Q11
HARD Application 💎 GEM
Match the Urban Local Body with its administrative officer: (1) Municipal Corporation (2) Municipality (3) Town Panchayat/Peruratchi (4) Panchayat Union (ஊராட்சி ஒன்றியம்)
▼
✅ ANSWER
1. Municipal Corporation → IAS Officer / Commissioner
2. Municipality → Commissioner (ஆணையர்)
3. Town Panchayat (Peruratchi) → Executive Officer (EO / செயல் அலுவலர்)
4. Panchayat Union → Block Development Officer (BDO / பிடிஓ / வட்டார வளர்ச்சி அலுவலர்)
💎 LIVE GEM — OFFICER SHORTCUT
Teacher's logic: "Peruratchi only needs a regular bus-fare — so only an Executive Officer (EO) is needed, not an IAS officer!" Big body → big officer. Small body → smaller officer. The answer to this common matching question: 1-IAS, 2-Commissioner, 3-EO, 4-BDO.
🏆 PYQ ALERT
This matching-type question (Local body ↔ Administrative Officer) is a recurring question in TNPSC Group 4 papers. Answer: Option A (All four correct in sequence).

⚙️ Group 7 — Obligatory & Optional Functions of Local Bodies
Q31
MEDIUM Conceptual
Which of the following is NOT an Optional Function (விருப்பப் பணி) of a local body? (A) Laying out parks/gardens (B) Establishing a library (C) Street lighting (D) Establishing a playground
▼
✅ ANSWER
C — Street lighting is NOT an optional function. It is an Obligatory Function (அவசியப் பணி).
💡 EXPLANATION
Obligatory Functions (must do): Drinking water supply, Road maintenance, Street lighting, Sanitation/cleaning streets
Optional Functions (may do): Parks, Libraries, Playgrounds, Cultural facilities

"Can a town exist without street lights? No — it's a necessity. Can it exist without a library? Yes, for a while — so that's optional." — Teacher's explanation.

💎 LIVE GEM — GREEK QUARTET + SHORTCUT
The four founders of Greek political thought (in ORDER):
சாக் → பிரி → அரி → அள்ளு
சாக்ரடீஸ் (Socrates) → பிளாட்டோ (Plato) → அரிஸ்டாட்டில் (Aristotle) → அலெக்சாண்டர் (Alexander)

Tamil shortcut: "Chakka pirichchu arisi allu" (சாக்க பிரிச்சு அரிசி அள்ளு) = Soak rice, separate, take rice, scoop — kitchen memory!

• Aristotle = Father of Political Science AND Father of Zoology
• Alexander = Student of Aristotle, world conqueror

Master the three "-land" countries with ONE mnemonic:

1️⃣ New Zealand (நியூசிலாந்து) → "New" thing = First country to give voting rights to women (1893)
2️⃣ Switzerland (சுவிட்சர்லாந்து) → "Switch" = Switch is Direct — click switch = law enacted directly by people = Direct Democracy
3️⃣ Iceland (ஐஸ்லாந்து) → "Ice" preserves things longest = World's oldest parliament (Althing, since 930 AD)

NEW → 1893 Women Vote | SWITCH → Direct Democracy | ICE → Oldest Parliament

San Marino (சான் மரினோ) — a small country within Italy — has the world's oldest written constitution still in use.
🚨 CRITICAL NOTE
DO NOT confuse with Iceland (oldest parliament) or Greece (birthplace of democracy). San Marino → Oldest constitution currently in use. No shortcut provided by teacher — must memorise: San Marino, located in Italy.

Q45
HARD Conceptual
Find the INCORRECT statement about democracy: (A) People have significant power in democratic systems (B) In Direct Democracy, people have law-making power (C) In democratic systems, decision-making power is concentrated with the leader (D) In Direct Democracy, all law amendments are approved by the people
▼
✅ ANSWER
Statement A is INCORRECT (in typical Representative Democracy, significant power is NOT directly with the people — it's with elected representatives and leaders).
💡 EXPLANATION
B, C, D are all correct statements about Direct Democracy (Switzerland model). A is technically the incorrect one — in India's democracy, a regular citizen cannot pass laws or force a vote. Power is with elected officials. The teacher confirmed D is correct for Direct Democracy (Switzerland).
Q46
EASY Conceptual
In Direct Democracy (நேரடி மக்களாட்சி), who has the power to make laws?
▼
✅ ANSWER
The voters / citizens themselves (வாக்காளர்கள்) — not their representatives. This is why Switzerland is the best example: citizens directly vote on laws.
🪤 TRAP NOTE
"People's representatives" → WRONG answer for Direct Democracy. In India (Representative/Indirect Democracy), MLAs/MPs make laws. In Direct Democracy, citizens themselves do. Don't mix these up.
Q47
HARD Factual
Features of democratic systems: Which is INCORRECT? (A) Roman Empire — Civilizational growth and global spread (B) Manu Island — Self-governance under monarchy (C) 1615 — Written Magna Carta by United States (D) Aristotle — Foundation of political philosophy
▼
✅ ANSWER
Statement C is INCORRECT. The Magna Carta was NOT by the United States — it was by King John of England (UK) in 1215 (not 1615). The USA didn't even exist in 1215/1615.
💎 LIVE GEM
America was "discovered" by Columbus ~1492 and became independent in 1776. The Magna Carta (1215) was signed by King John of England. These two events are centuries apart — so connecting Magna Carta with "United States" is always wrong.
