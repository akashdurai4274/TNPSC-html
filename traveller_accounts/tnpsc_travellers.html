Traveller Profiles

Every Foreign Traveller — Complete Profiles

Each profile includes origin, period, places visited, king/ruler met, travelogue name, and TNPSC-targeted exam questions.

Mg
Megasthenes
🇬🇷 Greek · Ambassador · Seleucid Empire
302–298 BCE Ambassador Pataliputra Indica

The earliest and most important Greek ambassador to India, sent by Seleucus I Nicator to the court of Chandragupta Maurya. His work Indica is the foundational Greek account of India.

King MetChandragupta Maurya (Sandrokottos)
CapitalPataliputra (modern Patna, Bihar)
BookIndica (preserved in fragments)
Key NotesDescribed 7 castes of India; noted absence of slavery; described the royal Mauryan army with war elephants; described Pataliputra's wooden walls and 570 towers. Mentioned south Indian kingdoms as "Pandaea" (Pandya) ruled by a queen.
TN LinkFirst Greek to mention Pandya kingdom — called it "Pandaea" — and described pearl trade from south India
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Who was Megasthenes? He was the Greek ambassador sent to the court of Chandragupta Maurya by Seleucus I Nicator.
What is the name of Megasthenes' travelogue? — Indica.
Which south Indian kingdom did Megasthenes refer to as "Pandaea"? — Pandya Kingdom.
Megasthenes described India as having how many castes? — Seven castes.
St
Strabo
🇬🇷 Greek · Geographer · Pontus (modern Turkey)
1st Century BCE Geographer Geographica

Did not visit India personally but compiled all existing Greek accounts about India in his monumental Geographica — a crucial secondary source for ancient Indian history.

Period64 BCE – 24 CE
BookGeographica (17 volumes)
Key NotesPreserved information from Megasthenes, Nearchus, and others; described India's physical geography; accounts of Indian rivers (Indus, Ganges); described trade between India and Rome.
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Strabo compiled ancient Indian accounts in which book? — Geographica.
Strabo's Geographica contains how many volumes? — 17 volumes.
Pl
Pliny the Elder
🇮🇹 Roman · Author · Como, Roman Empire
1st Century CE Roman Scholar India (via trade) Naturalis Historia

Roman naturalist who wrote Naturalis Historia. Did not visit India but documented Indo-Roman trade extensively. Famously complained that India was draining Rome of its gold.

BookNaturalis Historia (Natural History)
Key NotesRecorded that Rome paid India 550 million sesterces annually for luxury goods — spices, muslin, precious stones. Documented pepper, silk, ivory trade. Mentioned Muzaris (Muchiri/Cranganore) as a major Indian port.
TN LinkDescribed Tamil ports, pepper trade from Malabar; mentioned Tamil kings sending embassies to Rome
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Who complained that trade with India was draining Rome of gold? — Pliny the Elder.
Pliny's major work documenting India trade is called? — Naturalis Historia.
Which port did Pliny mention as the busiest Indian trade port? — Muzaris (Cranganore/Muchiri).
Pt
Claudius Ptolemy
🇪🇬 Greek-Egyptian · Geographer · Alexandria
2nd Century CE Geographer Geographia South India

Alexandrian geographer whose Geographia contains the earliest map-like description of South India, identifying Tamil ports and rivers with remarkable accuracy.

BookGeographia
Key NotesMentioned "Sopatma" (Mahabalipuram area), "Chaberis" (Kaveri mouth/Kaveripattinam), "Poduca" (Pondicherry/Arikamedu), "Camara" (possibly Kaveripattanam). Created early map of India.
TN LinkIdentified Arikamedu as "Poduca" — a major trading post; mentioned Kaveri river and Cauvery delta ports
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Ptolemy identified "Poduca" as which modern Tamil Nadu location? — Arikamedu (near Pondicherry).
Ptolemy called the Kaveri river by which name? — Chaberis.
Ptolemy's geographical work is called? — Geographia.
Fa
Fa-Hien (Faxian)
🇨🇳 Chinese · Buddhist Monk · China
399–414 CE Buddhist Pilgrim North India · Sri Lanka Record of Buddhist Kingdoms

First Chinese Buddhist monk to travel to India in search of Buddhist scriptures. He visited during the reign of Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) of the Gupta dynasty.

King MetChandragupta II (Vikramaditya) — Gupta Empire
PlacesPeshawar, Taxila, Mathura, Pataliputra, Varanasi, Bodh Gaya, Kapilavastu, Sarnath, Sri Lanka
BookFo-Kwo-Ki (Record of Buddhist Kingdoms)
Key NotesDescribed Gupta society as prosperous and peaceful; said no capital punishment existed; observed that people were honest and vegetarian in the central lands; found Buddhist monasteries flourishing; visited sacred Buddhist sites.
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
During whose reign did Fa-Hien visit India? — Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) of Gupta dynasty.
Fa-Hien's travelogue is called? — Fo-Kwo-Ki / Record of Buddhist Kingdoms.
What did Fa-Hien observe about Gupta society? — Prosperous, no capital punishment, people honest and vegetarian.
Which Chinese pilgrim visited India between 399–414 CE? — Fa-Hien.
HT
Hiuen Tsang (Xuanzang)
🇨🇳 Chinese · Buddhist Scholar · Henan, China
629–645 CE Buddhist Pilgrim All India incl. South India Si-Yu-Ki

The most important Chinese traveller to India. He spent 16 years in India during the reign of Harsha and travelled extensively including South India and Tamil regions. Called the "Prince of Pilgrims".

King MetEmperor Harsha of Kanauj; also Pulakesi II (Chalukya); visited Pallava kingdom
PlacesTaxila, Kanauj, Nalanda, Bodh Gaya, Kanchipuram, Mahabalipuram area, Sri Lanka via south India route
BookSi-Yu-Ki (Records of the Western Regions) · Also: Datang Xiyu Ji
Key NotesDescribed Nalanda University in detail — 10,000 students, 1,500 teachers; praised Harsha as a virtuous ruler; described Kanchipuram (Conjeeveram) as a great city; described the Pallava kingdom's prosperity; noted decline of Buddhism in many parts.
TN LinkVisited Kanchipuram — described it as a thriving Buddhist centre; described Pallava king Narasimhavarman; noted Tamil Nadu's wealth and trade
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
During whose reign did Hiuen Tsang visit India? — Harsha of Kanauj (also Pulakesi II Chalukya).
Hiuen Tsang's travelogue is named? — Si-Yu-Ki (Records of the Western Regions).
Which Tamil city did Hiuen Tsang describe as "Kanchipuram"? — Kanchipuram (Pallava capital).
Hiuen Tsang described which university in detail? — Nalanda University.
Which Chinese traveller is called the "Prince of Pilgrims"? — Hiuen Tsang.
IT
I-Tsing (Yijing)
🇨🇳 Chinese · Buddhist Monk · Shandong, China
671–695 CE Buddhist Pilgrim South India · Nalanda · Sri Vijaya Buddhist Records

Chinese monk who travelled to India by sea route via Sri Vijaya (Sumatra). He studied at Nalanda for 10 years and wrote about South Indian Buddhist practices.

RouteSea route via Sri Vijaya (Sumatra) — not overland like Fa-Hien
PlacesNalanda, South India ports, Sri Lanka; studied at Nalanda 10 years
BookRecord of Buddhist Practices sent from the South Sea; Biographies of Eminent Monks
Key NotesDescribed 56 Buddhist monks who visited India; detailed accounts of Nalanda's curriculum; described South Indian Buddhist monasteries; noted that Buddhism was weakening in India but thriving in South-East Asia.
TN LinkDescribed Buddhist centres in South India including Kanchipuram region; documented sea trade routes from Tamil ports
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Which Chinese pilgrim took the sea route via Sumatra to reach India? — I-Tsing (Yijing).
I-Tsing studied at which Indian university for 10 years? — Nalanda University.
I-Tsing noted Buddhism was thriving in which region? — South-East Asia (Sri Vijaya).
AM
Al-Masudi
🇮🇶 Arab · Historian-Geographer · Baghdad, Abbasid Caliphate
10th Century CE (916 CE) Arab Geographer West India · Deccan · South India Muruj al-Dhahab

Called the "Herodotus of the Arabs," Al-Masudi was an Arab historian and traveller who visited India in 916 CE and wrote extensively about Indian society, trade, and religion.

Period896–956 CE · Visited India c. 916 CE
BookMuruj al-Dhahab wa Ma'adin al-Jawhar (Meadows of Gold and Mines of Gems)
Key NotesDescribed the caste system; documented Hindu temples; described the Deccan kingdoms; wrote about Indian Ocean trade; described the western coast of India; mentioned the kingdom of the Rashtrakutas; described Indian customs of sati.
TN LinkDescribed Tamil trade along western coast; wrote about pepper, spices, and textile trade from south India
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Al-Masudi is called the "Herodotus of the Arabs." His major work is? — Muruj al-Dhahab (Meadows of Gold).
Al-Masudi visited India during the reign of which dynasty? — Rashtrakutas (Deccan).
What Indian social practice did Al-Masudi describe in his accounts? — The caste system and sati.
AB
Al-Biruni (Abu Rayhan)
🇺🇿 Persian · Scholar · Khwarezm (modern Uzbekistan)
1017–1030 CE Persian Scholar North India · Punjab · Sindh Kitab al-Hind

The greatest medieval Islamic scholar and the first true Indologist. He came to India with Mahmud of Ghazni and spent 13 years studying Sanskrit, mathematics, astronomy, and Indian philosophy.

Came withMahmud of Ghazni (after his invasions)
PlacesMultan, Thaneswar, Punjab, Sindh, Kashmir region
BookKitab al-Hind (Book of India / Tahqiq ma lil-Hind)
Key NotesLearned Sanskrit; translated Sanskrit texts into Arabic; described Hindu philosophy, mathematics, and astronomy with deep respect; criticized caste discrimination; noted India's intellectual achievements; described India's physical geography and climate accurately.
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Al-Biruni's famous book about India is? — Kitab al-Hind (Tahqiq ma lil-Hind).
Al-Biruni came to India with which ruler? — Mahmud of Ghazni.
Which language did Al-Biruni learn to study Indian texts? — Sanskrit.
Who is considered the first true "Indologist"? — Al-Biruni.
IB
Ibn Battuta
🇲🇦 Moroccan · Explorer · Tangier, Morocco
1333–1347 CE Explorer Delhi · Deccan · Malabar · Tamil Ports Rihla

The greatest medieval Muslim traveller — called the "Marco Polo of the Muslim World." Served as a Qadi (judge) under Muhammad bin Tughluq in Delhi and later travelled to South India and China.

King MetMuhammad bin Tughluq (Delhi Sultanate) — served as his ambassador to China
PlacesDelhi, Daulatabad, Malabar Coast, Quilon (Kollam), Calicut, Hormuz, Maldives, China
BookRihla (Travels / A Gift to Those Who Contemplate the Wonders of Cities)
Key NotesDescribed Muhammad bin Tughluq as both a tyrant and generous; described mass transfer of capital from Delhi to Daulatabad; described Malabar pepper trade; described Calicut as a great port; provided vivid accounts of sultanate court life and punishments; described Hindu customs in South India.
TN LinkVisited Tamil Nadu ports; described Quilon and Calicut as major trade centres; noted Tamil merchants dealing in spices and textiles
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Ibn Battuta served as a Qadi under which Delhi Sultanate ruler? — Muhammad bin Tughluq.
Ibn Battuta's famous travelogue is called? — Rihla.
Which South Indian port did Ibn Battuta describe as the greatest? — Calicut (Kozhikode).
Ibn Battuta is from which country? — Morocco (Tangier).
As whose ambassador was Ibn Battuta sent to China? — Muhammad bin Tughluq's ambassador.
MP
Marco Polo
🇮🇹 Italian · Merchant-Explorer · Venice, Italy
1288–1294 CE Merchant Explorer South India · Pandya Kingdom · Coromandel The Travels of Marco Polo

The Venetian merchant-explorer who passed through South India on his return journey from China. He described the Pandya kingdom of South India and Tamil Nadu's pearl fisheries in detail.

Periodc. 1288–1294 CE (returning from China via sea)
PlacesMalabar Coast, Pandya territory (Tamil Nadu), Coromandel Coast, Cape Comorin, Ceylon (Sri Lanka)
BookIl Milione (The Travels of Marco Polo)
Key NotesDescribed pearl fisheries at Mannar (between TN and Sri Lanka) in great detail; described South Indian practice of going about barely clothed; noted that the king of Ma'abar (Pandya kingdom) wore only a cloth around his waist but was covered in jewels; described the diamond mines of Golconda; described Indian Ocean trade extensively.
TN Link"Ma'abar" = Coromandel Coast (Tamil Nadu). Described Pandya king's court, pearl fisheries, idolatry and Hindu temple practices in Tamil regions
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Marco Polo called the Coromandel Coast of Tamil Nadu by which name? — Ma'abar.
Which pearl fishery location in Tamil Nadu did Marco Polo describe? — Mannar (between Tamil Nadu and Sri Lanka).
Marco Polo's travelogue is called? — Il Milione (The Travels of Marco Polo).
Which South Indian dynasty's kingdom did Marco Polo describe as "Ma'abar"? — Pandya Kingdom.
Marco Polo was from which city in Italy? — Venice.
NC
Nicolo de Conti
🇮🇹 Italian · Merchant · Venice, Italy
1420–1421 CE Merchant Vijayanagara · South India Account (via Poggio Bracciolini)

Venetian merchant who visited the Vijayanagara Empire and described it in detail. His account was recorded by the Papal secretary Poggio Bracciolini in the work De Varietate Fortunae.

King MetVijayanagara ruler (Deva Raya I era)
PlacesVijayanagara (Hampi), Malabar, South India
Key NotesDescribed Vijayanagara city as very large, with a circumference of 60 miles; described the king's army of 90,000 soldiers; described elephant corps; noted Hindu customs and sati; described South Indian temples.
TN LinkDescribed Vijayanagara's influence over Tamil regions; documented Telugu and Tamil cultural practices
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Nicolo de Conti described which empire's capital in South India? — Vijayanagara Empire (Hampi).
Nicolo de Conti's account was recorded by whom? — Poggio Bracciolini (in De Varietate Fortunae).
AR
Abdur Razzaq
🇮🇷 Persian · Ambassador · Herat, Timurid Empire (Persia)
1443–1444 CE Persian Ambassador Vijayanagara · Calicut · Mangalore Matla-us-Sadain

Persian ambassador sent by Shah Rukh of the Timurid dynasty to Calicut and Vijayanagara. His account is one of the most important descriptions of the Vijayanagara Empire at its peak.

King MetDeva Raya II — Vijayanagara Empire
PlacesCalicut (Kozhikode), Mangalore, Vijayanagara (Hampi city)
BookMatla-us-Sadain wa Majma-ul-Bahrain
Key NotesDescribed Vijayanagara city in extraordinary detail — its markets, bazaars, temples; said the city was as large as a great metropolis; described Calicut as a great port city with merchants from all over the world; described the prosperity of Deva Raya II's reign; noted Hindu temples with precious gems; described the Dasara festival at Vijayanagara.
TN LinkDescribed Tamil traders in Calicut; documented Vijayanagara's rule over Tamil regions; noted the prosperity of south Indian trade routes
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Abdur Razzaq visited Vijayanagara during the reign of which king? — Deva Raya II.
Abdur Razzaq's travelogue is named? — Matla-us-Sadain.
Abdur Razzaq was sent as ambassador by which ruler? — Shah Rukh (Timurid/Persian ruler).
Which festival at Vijayanagara did Abdur Razzaq famously describe? — Dasara (Mahanavami) festival.
DB
Duarte Barbosa
🇵🇹 Portuguese · Official · Lisbon, Portugal
1500–1516 CE Portuguese Official Malabar · Vijayanagara · Coromandel Book of Duarte Barbosa

Portuguese official who spent years in South India as an interpreter and administrator. His book provides one of the best accounts of South India in the early 16th century.

PeriodServed in Portuguese India c. 1500–1516
PlacesMalabar Coast, Coromandel Coast, Vijayanagara territory, Tamil ports
BookLivro de Duarte Barbosa (Book of Duarte Barbosa)
Key NotesDetailed description of South Indian trade goods: pepper, ginger, cinnamon, precious stones; described Vijayanagara as the greatest Hindu empire; described the caste system in South India; documented Tamil weaving industry and textile exports; noted Brahmin influence.
TN LinkDescribed Tamil ports (Pulicat, Nagapattinam, Tuticorin); documented pearl fisheries; described the Nayaka administration in Tamil regions
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Duarte Barbosa was a Portuguese official who described which empire as the "greatest Hindu empire"? — Vijayanagara Empire.
Which Tamil Nadu ports did Duarte Barbosa describe? — Pulicat, Nagapattinam, Tuticorin.
Duarte Barbosa's book on South India is called? — Livro de Duarte Barbosa (Book of Duarte Barbosa).
DP
Domingo Paes
🇵🇹 Portuguese · Merchant-Chronicler · Portugal
1520–1522 CE Portuguese Merchant Vijayanagara Empire Chronica dos reis de Bisnaga

Portuguese horse-trader and chronicler who visited Vijayanagara during its golden age under Krishna Deva Raya. His account is considered the most vivid description of Vijayanagara at its peak glory.

King MetKrishna Deva Raya — Vijayanagara Empire (greatest king)
PlacesVijayanagara (Hampi), Goa, Malabar Coast
BookChronica dos reis de Bisnaga (Chronicle of the Kings of Bisnaga)
Key NotesCalled Vijayanagara "as large as Rome"; compared Krishna Deva Raya to Alexander the Great; described the Dasara/Mahanavami festival in vivid detail (9-day celebrations); described the king's court — 100,000 soldiers, 11,000 women; described the city's bazaars selling gems and silk; noted the abundance of fresh water and food.
TN LinkKrishna Deva Raya's empire included Tamil regions; described Vijayanagara's Telugu and Tamil subjects; noted Nayak governors in Tamil territory
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Domingo Paes visited Vijayanagara during whose reign? — Krishna Deva Raya.
Domingo Paes compared Vijayanagara city to which European city? — Rome.
Domingo Paes compared Krishna Deva Raya to which conqueror? — Alexander the Great.
Which festival at Vijayanagara did Domingo Paes describe in extraordinary detail? — Mahanavami (Dasara) festival.
What is the title of Domingo Paes' chronicle? — Chronica dos reis de Bisnaga.
FN
Fernão Nunes (Nuniz)
🇵🇹 Portuguese · Horse Merchant · Portugal
1535–1537 CE Portuguese Merchant Vijayanagara Chronicle of Vijayanagara

Portuguese horse merchant who wrote a historical chronicle of Vijayanagara kings. His account provides detailed information about the succession of Vijayanagara rulers.

PeriodVisited c. 1535–1537 CE (post Krishna Deva Raya era)
Key NotesWrote a chronicle of Vijayanagara rulers from 1336 to his time; described revenue system; described the role of Nayaks (governors) in Tamil regions; gave a history of the Sangama, Saluva, and Tuluva dynasties of Vijayanagara.
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Fernão Nunes wrote a chronicle covering Vijayanagara from which year? — 1336 CE (founding of Vijayanagara).
Fernão Nunes described the role of which administrative officers in Tamil territory? — Nayaks (governors).
TR
Sir Thomas Roe
🇬🇧 British · Ambassador · England
1615–1618 CE British Ambassador Mughal Court · Ajmer · Agra Embassy of Sir Thomas Roe

First official British ambassador to the Mughal court of Jahangir. He came seeking trading rights for the East India Company. His journal is one of the best accounts of Jahangir's court.

King MetEmperor Jahangir (Mughal Empire) — at Ajmer and Agra
PlacesSurat, Ajmer, Agra — all within the Mughal Empire
BookEmbassy of Sir Thomas Roe to the Court of the Great Mogul (Journal)
Key NotesDescribed Jahangir's personality and drinking habits; noted Mughal court's love of painting; described the political rivalry among Jahangir's sons; noted Nur Jahan's enormous influence over the emperor; described the East India Company's early trading struggles; provided insights into Mughal administration and wealth.
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Thomas Roe was the first British ambassador to the court of which Mughal emperor? — Jahangir.
Thomas Roe came to India representing which organization? — British East India Company.
Thomas Roe described the enormous influence of which Mughal queen? — Nur Jahan.
In which cities did Thomas Roe meet Jahangir? — Ajmer and Agra.
JT
Jean-Baptiste Tavernier
🇫🇷 French · Gem Merchant · Paris, France
1638–1668 CE (6 voyages) French Gem Merchant Mughal India · Golconda · South India Travels in India

French gem merchant who made six voyages to India over 30 years. He was the first European to describe the famous Kohinoor diamond and gave detailed accounts of Golconda diamond mines and South Indian trade.

King MetShah Jahan, Aurangzeb (Mughal); Golconda Sultans
PlacesSurat, Golconda, Bijapur, Agra, Delhi, Dacca, Masulipatnam (Tamil/AP coast)
BookLes Six Voyages de J.B. Tavernier (Six Voyages in Turkey, Persia and India)
Key NotesFirst European to describe the Kohinoor diamond; described Golconda diamond mines; described the Taj Mahal under construction; compared Indian and European textile trade; gave detailed account of Indian gemstone trade; noted Aurangzeb's administration and contrast with Shah Jahan; described Indian Ocean trade routes.
TN LinkVisited Masulipatnam (Machilipatnam) and Coromandel Coast; described Tamil and Telugu textile industries; documented diamond trade from Golconda affecting South Indian economy
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
Which French traveller was the first European to describe the Kohinoor diamond? — Jean-Baptiste Tavernier.
Tavernier made how many voyages to India? — Six voyages (over 30 years).
Tavernier's book about his Indian travels is called? — Les Six Voyages (Six Voyages).
Which diamond mines did Tavernier describe in detail? — Golconda diamond mines.
Tavernier described the Taj Mahal in which state? — Under construction (during Shah Jahan's reign).
FB
François Bernier
🇫🇷 French · Physician-Philosopher · Anjou, France
1656–1668 CE French Physician Mughal India · Kashmir · Delhi Travels in the Mogul Empire

French physician who served in the Mughal court as physician to Dara Shikoh (heir of Shah Jahan). He witnessed the Mughal war of succession and Aurangzeb's rise to power.

King MetShah Jahan, Dara Shikoh (served as physician), Aurangzeb
PlacesDelhi, Agra, Kashmir, Surat, Bengal, Bihar
BookTravels in the Mogul Empire (Histoire de la dernière révolution des états du Grand Mogol)
Key NotesProvided eyewitness account of Shah Jahan's illness and succession war; described the execution of Dara Shikoh; described Kashmir's beauty extensively; criticized Mughal land ownership (no private property in land); described Bengal's rich agriculture; wrote about Indian philosophy and compared it with European thought.
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
François Bernier served as physician to which Mughal prince? — Dara Shikoh (son of Shah Jahan).
Bernier's book about the Mughal Empire is called? — Travels in the Mogul Empire.
Which Mughal emperor's succession war did Bernier witness firsthand? — Aurangzeb's war against Dara Shikoh.
Bernier criticized Mughal land ownership on what grounds? — Absence of private property in land (all land belonged to the Emperor).

William Hawkins
🇬🇧 British · Captain · England
1608–1611 CE English Captain Mughal Court · Agra Purchas His Pilgrimage

English sea captain who was the first Englishman to visit the Mughal court of Jahangir. He could speak Turkish which greatly pleased Jahangir, who initially gave him a royal welcome.

King MetEmperor Jahangir (at Agra)
PlacesSurat, Agra — Mughal capital
BookAccount published in Purchas His Pilgrimage (compiled by Samuel Purchas)
Key NotesFirst Englishman at Mughal court; Jahangir liked him because he spoke Turkish; Hawkins even married an Armenian woman in India; described Jahangir's alcoholism; noted that Portuguese influence blocked English trading rights; ultimately failed to secure trading concessions; described Surat's trade importance.
⚡ TNPSC Exam Questions
William Hawkins was the first Englishman to visit the court of which Mughal emperor? — Jahangir.
Why did Jahangir initially favour William Hawkins? — Hawkins could speak Turkish, which pleased Jahangir.
William Hawkins' account was published in which compilation? — Purchas His Pilgrimage (by Samuel Purchas).

Practice MCQs

TNPSC-Style MCQ Practice

Q.01 · Prelims Level
Which Chinese traveller visited India during the reign of Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) of the Gupta dynasty?
A Hiuen Tsang
B I-Tsing
C Fa-Hien
D Ma Huan
✓ Answer: C — Fa-Hien. He visited India in 399–414 CE during Chandragupta II's reign, described as the Gupta golden age with prosperity, no capital punishment, and thriving Buddhism. His book is Fo-Kwo-Ki.
Q.02 · Prelims Level
Megasthenes referred to the Pandya kingdom of South India as:
A Panduranga
B Pandaea
C Ma'abar
D Tamilakam
✓ Answer: B — Pandaea. Megasthenes in his Indica referred to the Pandya kingdom as "Pandaea," described as ruled by a queen, and noted for its pearl trade. "Ma'abar" was Marco Polo's term for the Coromandel Coast.
Q.03 · Prelims Level
Which traveller is called the "Prince of Pilgrims" and described Kanchipuram as a great Buddhist centre?
A Fa-Hien
B I-Tsing
C Hiuen Tsang
D Al-Biruni
✓ Answer: C — Hiuen Tsang. Called the "Prince of Pilgrims," he visited Kanchipuram (Pallava capital) during 629–645 CE, described it as a thriving city and Buddhist centre. His travelogue Si-Yu-Ki is the most important account of 7th century India.
Q.04 · Mains Level
Match the travelogue with its author:

1. Indica 2. Rihla 3. Si-Yu-Ki 4. Kitab al-Hind
A 1-Hiuen Tsang, 2-Ibn Battuta, 3-Megasthenes, 4-Al-Biruni
B 1-Megasthenes, 2-Ibn Battuta, 3-Hiuen Tsang, 4-Al-Biruni
C 1-Al-Biruni, 2-Megasthenes, 3-Ibn Battuta, 4-Hiuen Tsang
D 1-Marco Polo, 2-Fa-Hien, 3-Ibn Battuta, 4-Al-Masudi
✓ Answer: B — Indica=Megasthenes (Greek ambassador, 302 BCE); Rihla=Ibn Battuta (Moroccan traveller, 1333 CE); Si-Yu-Ki=Hiuen Tsang (Chinese pilgrim, 629 CE); Kitab al-Hind=Al-Biruni (Persian scholar, 1017 CE).
Q.05 · Prelims Level
The Portuguese traveller who compared Vijayanagara to Rome and Krishna Deva Raya to Alexander the Great was:
A Duarte Barbosa
B Abdur Razzaq
C Domingo Paes
D Fernão Nunes
✓ Answer: C — Domingo Paes (1520–1522). He visited Vijayanagara during Krishna Deva Raya's reign (the golden age) and compared the city to Rome and the king to Alexander. His chronicle: Chronica dos reis de Bisnaga.
Q.06 · Prelims Level
Which French gem merchant was the first European to describe the Kohinoor diamond?
A Jean-Baptiste Tavernier
B François Bernier
C Thomas Roe
D Domingo Paes
✓ Answer: A — Jean-Baptiste Tavernier. He made 6 voyages to India (1638–1668), described the Kohinoor diamond and Golconda mines, and also described the Taj Mahal under construction. His book: Les Six Voyages.
Q.07 · Mains Level
Consider these statements about Al-Biruni:
1. He came to India with Mahmud of Ghazni.
2. He wrote Kitab al-Hind after learning Sanskrit.
3. He described Vijayanagara empire in detail.
Which statements are correct?
A 1 and 3 only
B 1 and 2 only
C 2 and 3 only
D 1, 2 and 3
✓ Answer: B — Statements 1 and 2. Al-Biruni came with Mahmud of Ghazni (1017 CE) and wrote Kitab al-Hind after learning Sanskrit. Statement 3 is WRONG — Al-Biruni preceded Vijayanagara (founded 1336 CE) by 300 years. Vijayanagara was described by Abdur Razzaq, Domingo Paes, and Nicolo de Conti.
Q.08 · Prelims Level
Marco Polo called the Coromandel Coast (Tamil Nadu) by which name in his travelogue?
A Pandaea
B Tamilakam
C Ma'abar
D Indica
✓ Answer: C — Ma'abar. Marco Polo called the Coromandel Coast (Tamil Nadu) "Ma'abar" in his travelogue Il Milione. He described the Pandya kingdom's pearl fisheries at Mannar and noted the king's jewel-covered body.
Q.09 · Mains Level
Which traveller described Muhammad bin Tughluq as both a generous and tyrannical ruler, and served as his ambassador to China?
A Al-Biruni
B Abdur Razzaq
C Ibn Battuta
D Al-Masudi
✓ Answer: C — Ibn Battuta (Moroccan, from Tangier). He served as Qadi (judge) under Muhammad bin Tughluq and was later sent as his ambassador to China. His travelogue Rihla is one of the most important medieval accounts of the Delhi Sultanate.
Q.10 · Prelims Level
The Persian ambassador who visited Vijayanagara during Deva Raya II's reign and described the Dasara festival was:
A Al-Masudi
B Ibn Battuta
C Abdur Razzaq
D Al-Biruni
✓ Answer: C — Abdur Razzaq. Persian ambassador from Herat (Timurid Empire), sent by Shah Rukh. Visited Calicut and Vijayanagara in 1443–1444 during Deva Raya II's reign. His Matla-us-Sadain described the Dasara festival in detail.
